Common misconception Fact
1 Chlorine decolourises a dry blue or red litmus paper. Only moist chlorinecan decolourise the dry blue or red litmus. Dry chlorine does not bleach as nascent oxygen is not formed in that case.
2 Chlorine occurs in free state as well as in combined state. Chlorine occurs only in combined state not in free state because of high chemical activity, high electro negativity, high salt forming tendency

Introduction

Chlorine was first synthesized by Scheele and the elemental nature of this element was established by Davy. Chlorine is not only a member of the halogen family, but also considered as the typical halogen. It is diatomic and highly reactive gas. In the elemental state, chlorine generally exists in a combined state rather than in a free state
Symbol: Cl
Atomic Number: 17
Atomic Weight: 35.5
Electronic Configuration: 2, 8, 7 (K, L, M)

Occurace

Free State

Chlorine does not occur in nature in the free state because of :

  1. high chemical activity

  2. high electronegativity

  3. high salt forming tendency

Combined State

It is widely distributed as chlorides.

  1. Common salt (sodium chloride, NaCl). It is found in large quantities in sea - water and as rocl salt.

  2. Potassium chloride, KCl.

  3. Potassium magnesium chloride (carnallite), \( KCl. MgCl_2.6H_2O \) .

  4. Silver chloride, AgCl (in mineral horn silver).

Preparation of Chlorine

Oxidation of concentrated hydrochloric acid using oxidising agents.

Chlorine can be prepared by the oxidation of conc. hydrochloric acid by one of the following oxidizing agents ; manganese (IV) oxide, lead (IV) oxide, red lead, potassium permanganate, potassium dichromate and bleaching powder \( (CaOCl_2) \) .

Laboratory Preparation of Chlorine

Oxidation of hotconc. HC/by \( MnO_2 \)

The main reactants are manganese dioxide (an oxidising agent) and conc. hydrochloric acid. The reaction between them is as follows:
\( MnO_2 + 4 HCl \ \rightarrow \ MnCl_2 + 2 H_2 O + Cl_2 \)
The impurities are present in the gas. As the gas is contaminated with HC1 and water. It can be purified by passing the gas through washer bottles that contains:

  1. Water - which absorbs moisture.

  2. \( Conc. H_2SO_4 \) - which absorbs moisture

The chlorine gas is collected in the gas jar by upward displacement of air.
By warming manganese (IV) oxide and sodium chloride with concentrated sulphuric acid.
The main reactants are common salt (NaCl), manganese (IV) oxide \( (MnO_2), \ Conc.H_2SO_4 \)

  1. \( \begin{align} 2NaCl + 2 H_2 SO_4 \ \rightarrow \ 2 Na HSO_4 + 2 HCl \\\\[3pt] MnO_2 + H_2 SO_4 \ \rightarrow \ MnSO_4 + H_2 O +(O) \\\\[3pt] 2 HCl + (O) \ \rightarrow \ H_2 O + Cl_2 \end{align} \)
    Overall reaction : \( MnO_2 + 2 NaCl + 3 H_2 SO_4 \ \rightarrow \ 2 Na HSO_4 + MNSO_4 + 2 H_2 O + Cl_2 \)

  2. Copper (II) oxide, CuO
    Copper (II) oxide oxidises conc. hydrochloric acid to give chlorine in the presence of oxygen. \( CuO + O_2 + 6HCl \ \rightarrow \ Cu Cl_2 + 3 H_2 O + 2 Cl_2 \uparrow \)

By decomposing bleaching powder, \( CaOCl_2 \) with a mineral acid.
Bleaching powder can be decomposed by excess of dilute mineral acids at ordinary temperature to obtain chlorine.
\( CaO Cl_2 + H_2 SO_4 (dil) \ \rightarrow \ Ca SO_4 + H_2 O + Cl_2 \uparrow \)
Dilute nitric acid or atmospheric carbon dioxide can be used to obtain chlorine gas
\( CaO Cl_2 + 2HNO_3 (dil) \ \rightarrow \ Ca (NO_3)_2 + H_2 O + Cl_2 \uparrow \)
Collection of the gas : Gas is collected in the gas jar after passing through water conc. \( H_2SO_4 \) by upward displacement of air.

Physical Properties

  1. Colour : Greenish yellow.

  2. Odour : Pungent smell.

  3. Solubility : Sparingly soluble in water.

  4. Density : Heavier than air. Vapour density 35.5

  5. Atomicity : Two.

Chemical Properties of Air

Reaction with air

Under normal conditions chlorine does not react with oxygen chlorine is non combustible and also not a supporter of combustion.

Reaction with water

Chlorine dissolves in water. A mixture of hydrochloric acid and hypochlorous acid is formed. This reaction is responsible for the bleaching action of chlorine since HOC: dissociates giving nascent oxygen
\( \begin{align} Cl_2 + H_2 O \rightarrow HCl + HOCl \\\\[3pt] HOCl \ \rightarrow \ HCl + [O] \end{align} \)

Reaction with metals

Chlorine reacts with most of the metals. \( 2Na + Cl_2 \ \rightarrow \ 2 NaC1 \)
Ex : Burning magnesium ribbon reacts with chlorine gas corresponding metallic chloride \( MgCl_2 \) is formed with the evolution of light energy.
\( Mg + Cl_2 \ \rightarrow \ MgCl_2 \)

Reaction with non metals

At high temperature chlorine reacts with some non metals.
Ex:
\( \begin{align} 2P + 3Cl_2 \ \rightarrow \ 2PCl_3 \\\\[3pt] 2P + 5Cl_5 \ \rightarrow \ 2PCl_5 \end{align} \)
Corresponding non - metallic chlorides are formed.

Reaction with alkalies : (Cold and dilute)

Reaction with cold dilute NaOH the products are sodium chloride and sodium hypochorite
\( Cl_2 + 2NaOH \ \rightarrow \ 2 NaCl + Na OCl + 3 H_2 O \)

Reaction with alkalies : (Hot and Concentrated)

\( 3 Cl_2 + 6 NaOH \ \rightarrow \ 5 NaCl + Na ClO_3 + 3 H_2 O \)

Reaction with Other Compounds

Reaction of chlorine with excess of ammonia produces products ammonium chloride and nitrogen
\( 8 NH_3 + 3 Cl_2 \ \rightarrow \ 6 NH_4 Cl + N_2 \)
Reaction of chlorine with limited amount of ammonia produces products formed are nitrogen trichloride and HC1.
\( NH_3 + 3 Cl_2 \ \rightarrow \ NCl_3 + 3 HCl \)
On reaction with \( H_2S \) , chlorine oxidizes \( H_2S \) to sulphur. Yellow coloured sulphur powder is formed.
\( H_2 S + Cl_2 \ \rightarrow \ S + 2 HCl \)

Reaction with Turpentine

Chlorine gas is passed over a filter paper dipped in hot turpentine.
It burns with a black sooty flame with the formation of black soot of carbon.
\( C_{10} H_{16} + 8 Cl_2 \ \rightarrow \ 10 C + 16 HCl \)

Uses

  1. In the manufacture of bleaching power \( (CaOCl_2) \) .

  2. For bleaching wood pulp, cotton, paper, silk, rayon etc.,

  3. For preparing poisonous gases like mustard gas \( (ClC_2 H_4 - S - C_2 H_4 Cl) \) , Phosgene \( (COCl_2) \) , tear gas \( (CCl_3N_2O) \) .

  4. In the preparation of hydrochloric acid.

  5. In the extraction of metals like platinum, gold, titanium etc.,

  6. In the manufacture of chloroform \( (CHCl_3) \) , carbon tetrachloride \( (CCl_4) \) , dichloro diphenyl trichloroethane (DDT) etc., and also in rubber, plastic and paint industries.

  7. In the purification of drinking water.

Solved examples

Example 1:

Which property of chlorine helps in using it for sterilizing water?
Solution:
The oxidising nature of chlorine helps the chlorine in sterilizing water, as it can destroy bacteria.

Example 2:

What is the reason why chlorine gas turns colourless potassium bromide solution to coloured?
Solution:
Chlorine gas is more electro negative than bromine and hence can displace bromine. On passing chlorine gas through colourless potassium bromide solution, due to displacement bromide is displaced by chlorine forming products potassium chloride and bromine (which is red in colour).

Example 3:

The bleaching action of chlorine is an oxidation reaction. Why?
Solution:
Chlorine dissolves in moisture forming hydrochloric acid and hypochlorous acid. Hypochlorous acid liberates oxygen. This oxygen [O] combines with the colouring matter changing them to colourless compounds.
\( \begin{align} Cl_2 + H_2 O \ \rightarrow \ HCl + HClO \\\\[3pt] HClO \rightarrow HCl + [O] \\\\[3pt] \text{Coloured \ matter} + [O] \ \rightarrow \ \text{Colourless matter}. \end{align} \)

Example 4:

Chlorine is called as a saviour as well as killer. Explain.
Solution:
Chlorine gas when passed through water sterilizes water. But the same chlorine when inhaled causes problem in mucous membrane and prolonged inhaling causes death. In first case it acts as a saviour of life from bacteria. In second case it acts as a killer.

Example 5:

Chlorine does not exist in free state. Give reason.
Solution:
Chlorine does not occur in the free state due to its highly reactive nature, high electro negativity and salt forming tendency.

Example 6:

Name the products formed when chlorine is mixed with

  1. A little ammonia

  2. excess ammonia

Solution:

  1. \( NCl_3 \) (nitrogen trichloride)

  2. ammonium chloride

Example 7:

Chlorine water is usually stored in dark - coloured bottles why?
Solution:
Chlorine dissolved in water is called chlorine water consisting of HCl and HC1O
\( Cl_2 + H_2 O \ \rightarrow \ HCl + HOCl \)
n standing or explosure to sunlight the unstable hypochlorous acid decomposes into hydrochloric acid with liberation of oxygen. Hence chlorine water is usually stored in dark coloured bottles, so that light does not enter it.

Example 8:

State three tests by which you could identify a gas as chlorine.
Solution:

  1. Greenish yellow gas with pungent and irritating odour.

  2. Turns wet blue litmus red and then bleaches it.

  3. Bleaches coloured petals and leaves of plants

Example 9:

With the help of equations briefly explain the bleaching action of chlorine.
Solution:

  1. \( \underset{(Chlorine \ water)}{Cl_2 + H_2 O} \ \rightarrow \ HCl + HOCl \)

  2. \( \underset{Hypochlorous \ acid}{HOCl} \ \rightarrow \ HCl + \underset{nascent \ oxygen}{[O]} \)

  3. \( Colouring \ matter + [O] \ \rightarrow \ colourless \ product \)

Example 10:

A greenish yellow gas burns in a rotten egg smelling gas giving an acidic gas and a yellow coloured non metal. Identify the gas and the non metal. Write the balanced chemical equation of the reaction.
Solution:
\( \underset{Greenish \ yellow}{Cl_2} + \underset{rotten \ egg \ smell}{H_2 S} \ \rightarrow \ \underset{acidic \ gas}{2HCl} + \underset{Yellow \ coloured}{S} \) .
