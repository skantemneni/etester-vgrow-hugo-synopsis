Common misconception Fact
1 Ammonium chloride is formed when rod dipped in NH,DH solution is brought near colourless, irritating odour gas HO and \( NH_3 \) . It is fact that both HCI and \( NH_3 \) gases forms ammonium chloride. But in case of HCI gas, a rod dipped in \( NH_4 OH \) is brought near it while in case of \( NH_3 \) gas, a rod dipped in conc. HO solution is brought near it as HCI is an acidic gas where as \( NH_3 \) is a basic gas.
2 All transition metal ions are detected by their coloured pericipitates formed on addition of sodium hydroxide or ammonium hydroxide solution. Zinc does not give any characteristic colour on addition of sodium hydroxide or ammonium hydroxide solution even though it belongs to transition metal ions group.

Introduction

Chemistry deals with the study of numerous of chemical compounds .to understand the various application of each compound in industry, their uses need to be known. In this process not only their use but their process of development and their identification is very important. In the identification process of a chemical substance chemical analysis need to be done. The relevant subject that deals with systematic study in identification of chemical substances comes under topic analytical chemistry.

Analysis

The chemical compounds can be basically analyzed in two ways - qualitatively as well as quantitatively. In the qualitative analysis the compound is analyzed based on its chemical nature . The compound is identified as a gas, a salt or an ion. In the quantitative analysis. the compound with respect to its quantity of a substance is analyzed.

Solved examples

Example 1:

How could you distinguish \( Fe^{+2} \) and \( Cu^{+2} \) using ammonium hydroxide solution?

Solution:
\( Solution + NH_4OH \ \rightarrow \ Blue \ ppt \) ., soluble in excess ammonium hydroxide. (Dark blue solution).

  1. Insoluble \( Cu(OH)_2 \) formed,

  2. Soluble complex salt, tetra-amine copper sulphate \( [Cu(NH_3)_4]SO_4 \) formed
    \( \therefore \ Cu^{2+} \) present
    \( Solution + NH_4OH \ \rightarrow \ dirty \ green \ ppt \) . insoluble in excess ammonium hydroxide \( Fe(OH)_2 \) formed,
    \( Fe^{2+} \) present

Example 2:

How would you distinguish between \( Zn^{2+} \) and \( Pb^{2+} \) using ammonium hydroxide solution?
Solution:
\( Solution + NH_4OH \ \rightarrow \ gelatinous \ white \ ppt \) ., soluble in excess ammonium hydroxide

  1. Insoluble \( Zn(OH)_2 \) formed,

  2. Soluble complex salt, zinc amino hydroxide \( Zn(NH_3)_4 (OH)_2 \) formed
    \( \therefore \ Zn^{2+} \) present
    \( Solution + NH_4OH \ \rightarrow \ white \ ppt \) ., insoluble in excess ammonium hydroxide. \( Pb(OH)_2 \) formed,
    \( \therefore \ Pb^{2+} \) present

Example 3:

Four gases A, B, C, D are to be identified. It is observed that gas A is a reducing agent which contains oxygen. Gas B turns bleaches moist litmus paper with characteristic colour. When Gas C is mixed with gas D, it forms white fumes with no formation of other product. Identify the gases A, B, C and D.
Solution:
Gas A being reducing agent containing oxygen it is sulphur dioxide gas. Gas B bleaches moist litmus paper with characteristic colour, it is chlorine gas. Gas C and D are HC1 and \( NH_3 \) , respectively as they react to form \( NH_4C1 \) (white dense fumes).

Example 4:

Can sodium hydroxide solution be used to distinguish between ferrous sulphate and ferric sulphate solution. If so, how.
Solution:
Yes, on addition of sodium hydroxide solution to ferrous sulphate it form a dirty green precipitate of ferrous hydroxide. On addition of sodium hydroxide solution to ferric sulphate it forms a reddish brown precipitate ferric hydroxide.

Example 5:

What are the observations observed when excess of \( NH_4OH \) is added to a substance obtained by adding hydrochloric acid in silver nitrate solution.
Solution:
\( HCl + AgNO_3 \ \rightarrow \ AGCl + HNO_3 \)
On addition of excess of \( NH_4OH \) precipitate AgC1 is soluble.
