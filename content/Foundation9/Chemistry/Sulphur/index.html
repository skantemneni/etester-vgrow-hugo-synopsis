S.No Common Misconception Fact
1 On burning, one gram of rhombic sulphur produces more amount of sulphur dioxide than monoclinic sulphur. Rhombic and monoclinic sulphur are allotropicforms of sulphur. Hence they are chemically identical and produce same amount of SO, on burning same quantity of either rhombic or monoclinic sulphur.
2 On heating sulphur continuously, the liquid sulphur becomes more and more viscous at about \( 160^{\circ}C \) and above so that it cannot be poured out of the test tube. No, sulphur becomes more and more viscous above \( 160^{\circ}C \) but only till \( 200^{\circ}C \) above that, the liquid again becomes mobile.

Introduction

Sulphur is a word derived from Sanskrit word "Sulvari" meaning "enemy of copper". This is because it destroys the metallic properties of copper.
Symbol : S
Atomic number : 16
Mass number : 32
Electronic configuration : 2, 8, 6 (K, L, M)

Occurance of sulphur

In nature sulphur occurs both in the free state as well as in the combined state.

Free State :

Sulphur in the free state is found in the earth's crust (0.03 % of earth crust is elemental sulphur). In the volcanic regions like Sicily and Japan, it is present near the earth's crust and in the non-volcanic regions of U.S.A like with Texas and Louisiana it is found much below the earth's crust (300 to 400 mts below the earth's crust)

Combined State :

Sulphur in the combined state is found in the form of

  1. Sulphates : Gypsum ( \( CaSO_4. \ 2H_2O \) ), Epsom salt ( \( MgSO_4. \ 7H_2O \) ), Glauber's salt ( \( Na_2SO_4. \ 10H_2O \) ).

  2. Sulphides : Cinnabar (HgS), Galena (PbS), Iron pyrites (FeS), Copper pyrites (CuS), Zinc blend (ZnS)

  3. Organic matter : In insulin, glucosionates of plants and animals, nature, gas petroleum, crude oil, coal gas, water springs.

Extraction of sulphur

Sulphur has a wide variety of uses in the industries. It is extracted from the earth's crust by the following processes.

  1. The Sicilian process

  2. The Frasch process

Sicilian Process

The process is used to extract sulphur which is near to the earth's crust (deposits in sicily)
This process is carried out in brick kilns built on sloping hill sides.

Process

Large amounts of clay and stone are mixed with crude sulphur and is stacked inside the brick kiln leaving small spaces in between this is covered with a layer of powdered sulphur which is then subjected to burning. The heat evolved during the burning of this sulphur leads to melting of the remaining sulphur that lies in the bottom layers. The molten sulphur that lies in the bottom layers. The molten sulphur is allowed to flow down and is collected in wooden moulds.

Purification

The sulphur obtained in the above process contains about 5 % of earthly impurities. Depending on the applications, it is used in the natural form or in the purified form. The purification is carried out by distillation process the liquid sulphur obtained after distillation is collected in the form of solid rolls and hence called roll sulphur.

Frasch Process

The sulphur extracted in this process is 99.5 % pure and doesn't need further purification. The process is used to extract the sulphur which i.e 150 - 400 m below the earth's crust. This is also called Louisiana process as charge deposits of this are found in Louisiana. A direct mixing is not possible as the sulphur is present in layers of quick sand and gravel.

Process

In the process three concentric pipes of diameters 25 mm, 76 mm and 152 mm are drilled through the ground to the sulphur deposits. Super heated steam ( \( 170^{\circ}C \) ) under pressure is sent through the outer most pipe. This melts the sulphur below (m.p of sulphur = \( 112 ^{\circ}C \) ). Hot compressed air (up to 35 atm) is sent through the inner most pipe which foots up the molten sulphur below. The sulphur foam formed rises up through the middle pipe and is collected.

Allotropes of sulphur

The phenomenon of existence of an element in more than one physical form is called allotropy ; and the other forms of the same element are called allotropes. Sulphur exists in different allotropic forms. Allotropes have same chemical properties but different physical properties.
The three main allotropic forms of sulphur are

  1. Rhombic sulphur

  2. Monoclinic sulphur

  3. Plastic sulphur

Structure of Allotropic forms of sulphur

Both Rhombic and monoclinic sulphur exist in the form of \( S_8 \) molecules. These \( S_8 \) molecules are in the form of a ring. It forms a crown shaped molecule with four atoms on the top and four atoms at the bottom. The rhombic form and the monoclinic form differ in the arrangement of these \( S_8 \) packed rings. In rhombic sulphur, these rings fit snuggly into each other while in monochlinic sulphur the rings are stacked one on top of the other.

Chemical Properties

Reaction of sulphur with non metals

Oxygen

Sulphur burns in air or oxygen at \( 250 - 300 ^{\circ}C \) with a pale blue flame. \( SO_2 \) and traces of \( SO_3 \) are formed.
\( S + O_2 \rightarrow SO_2 \)
\( 2S + 3O_2 \rightarrow 2SO_3 \)

Hydrogen

Rotten egg smelling hydrogen sulphide is formed when hydrogen is bubbled through boiling sulphur.
\( H_2 + S \rightarrow H_2S \)

Carbon

When sulphur is passed over red hot charcoal, a colourless liquid \( CS_2 \) is formed.
\( C + 2S \rightarrow CS_2 \)

Chlorine

When chlorine is bubbled through boiling sulphur, a red liquid of sulphur mono chloride is formed.
\( 2S + Cl \rightarrow S_2{Cl}_2 \)

Phosphorus

When sulphur is warmed with red phosphorus in the absence of air, it forms a mixture of phosphorus trisulphide and phosphorus pentasulphide.
[Heating is generally done in an atmosphere of \( CO_2 \) ]
\( P_4 + 3S \xrightarrow{\triangle} P_4S_3 \)
\( P_4 + 5S \xrightarrow{\triangle} P_4S_5 \)

Reaction of sulphur with metals

Zinc

A mixture of zinc and sulphur on heating forms a greyish residue of zinc sulphide.
\( Zn + S \rightarrow ZnS \)

Iron

When sulphur is heated with iron fillings, black iron sulphide is formed.
\( Fe + S \rightarrow FeS \)

Copper

Copper reacts with sulphur below \( 400^{\circ}C \) to form copper sulphide (cupric sulphide) and at a higher (cuprous sulphide)
\( Cu + S \xrightarrow{ < 400} CuS \)
\( 2Cu + \xrightarrow{ > 400} Cu_2S \)

Mercury

When sulphur vapour is passed over metallic mercury, mercury (II) sulphide (mercuric sulphide) is formed.
\( Hg + S \rightarrow HgS \)

Reaction with acids alkalies

With acid

Sulphur reacts with hot and conc. \( H_2SO_4 \) and \( HNO_3 \) to get \( SO_2 \) and \( NO_2 \) respectively.
\( S + 2H_2SO_4 \rightarrow 3SO_2 + 2H_2O \)
\( S + 6HNO_3 \rightarrow H_2SO_4 + 6NO_2 + 2H_20 \)

With alkali

When sulphur is boiled with sodium or potassium hydroxide, it reacts to form a mixture of their respective polysulphides, thiosulphates and water.
\( 6NaOH + 12S \rightarrow \underset{(Sodium \ poly \ sulphide)} {2Na_2S_5} + \underset{(Sodium \ thiosulphate)}{Na_2S_2O_3} + 3H_2O \)

Uses

  1. As calcium bi-sulphite \( Ca{(HSO_3)}_2 \) for bleaching.

  2. Burning S gives \( SO_2 \) which further oxidized to \( SO_3 \) , which converts to \( H_2SO_4 \) with water.

  3. Converts soft rubber into hard rubber by heating with sulphur at a definite temperature.

  4. Fungicidal preparation.

  5. Dermal ointments for skin ailments and Sulphur drugs to prevent bacterial growth.

  6. Sodium thiosulphate \( (Na_2S_2O_3) \) in photography for fixing and developing.

  7. Ordinary match contains phosphorous disulphide \( P_4S_3 \) + antimony tri sulphide \( Sb_2S_3 \) . Safety match contains sulphur and potassium chlorate.

  8. In gun powder manufacture (S+ charcoal + nitre) are used. Sulphur helps in ignition as it has low ignition temperature.

Solved examples

Example 1:

Question:
What happens if rhombic sulphur is heated rapidly ?

Solution:
On heating rhombic sulphur rapidly, it fails to convert into monoclinic sulphur and melts at \( 113^{\circ}C \) .

Example 2:

Question:
In Frasch process, how does \( H_2S \) changes to sulphur.

Solution:
In one process to remove \( H_2S \) from a gaseous mixture, the gas stream is split into two parts. One part (consisting of about one-third of the stream) is burnt to convert \( H_2S \) to \( SO_2 \) . The streams are rejoined in a catalytic converter at 200 to \( 300^{\circ}C \) , where this reaction occurs.
\( 2H_2S(g) + SO_2(g) \rightarrow 3S(g) + 2H_2O(g) \)

Example 3:

Question:
Does sulphur catenate.
Solution:
Sulphur has the most tendency to catenate compared to elements of its group. It forms chains containing upto 8 atoms.

Example 4:

Question:
When \( H_2S \) gas is passed into a mixture solution acidified with dil HC1, yellowish turbidity appears explain.
Solution:

  1. It is due to presence of some oxidising agents (acidic radicals like \( {NO}_2^-, \ NO_3^- \) ) which oxidise H2S to collodial sulphur (yellowish turbidity).
    \( H_2S + O \rightarrow H_2O + S \) (collodial yellowish turbidity)

  2. \( FeCl_3 + NH_4SCN \rightarrow \underset{red}{[(Fe(SCN)]Cl_2} + NH_4Cl \)
    \( FeCl_3 \) changes to \( FeCl_2 \) on passing \( H_2S \) hence, no colour with \( NH_4SCN \) .

  3. \( {MnO}_4^- + \xrightarrow{H_2O} S + Mn^{2-}, \ H_2S \) is a reducing agent.
    \( H_2S + H_2O_2 \rightarrow H_2O + H_2SO_4, \ H_2S \) is a reducing agent.
    \( H_2S + NaOH \rightarrow NaHS \rightarrow Na_2S, \ H_2S \) is a diprotic acid.

Example 5:

Question:
What is the oxidation state of sulphur in the following :

  1. \( SO_2 \)

  2. \( PbS \)

Solution:

  1. Let oxidation number of S in \( SO_2 \) be x.
    x + 2 (-2) = 0
    x = 4
    Therefore oxidation number of S in \( SO_2 \) is +4

  2. Let oxidation number of S in PbS be x.
    2 + x = 0
    x = - 2
    Therefore number of S in PbS is -2

Question:

When dry chlorine gas is bubbled through molten Sulphur, sulphur monochloride red liquid] is formed.

Example 7:

Question:
An element X when heated in oxygen, above \( 300^{\circ}C \) , burns with a pale blue flame to give a colourless gas Y and traces of gas Z. Identify X, Y and Z.
Solution:
On heating sulphur in air (Burns when heated above \( 300^{\circ}C \) with a pale blue flame) Sulphur dioxide, sulphur trioxide (traces) are formed.
\( \underset{(2S+3O_2 \rightarrow 2SO_3)}{S + O_2 \xrightarrow{> 300^{\circ}C} SO_2} \)

Example 8:

Question:
X with conc.nitric acid gives reddish brown gas and \( H_2SO_4 \) . Identify X, the gas and the acid.
Solution:
When sulphur is boiled with conc. nitric acid, it is oxidised to sulphuric acid.
\( S+6HNO_3 \rightarrow H_2SO_4 +6NO_2 + 2H_2O \)
