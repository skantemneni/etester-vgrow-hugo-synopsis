Common misconception Fact
1 Phosphorus glows in dark. No. Only white phosphorus glows in dark red phosphorus cannot glow in dark.
2 Chemical formula of phosphorus pentoxide is \( P_2 O_5 \) . Formula of phosphorus pentoxide is written as \( P_4 O_{10} \) as molecule of phosphorus pentoxide exists as dimer.

Introduction

Phosphorus is greek word meaning phos = light and phero = carry. This implies that phosphorus is an element that glows in darkness. Phosphorus was discovered by Brand. In 1669, Scheele isolated it from bone ash and Lavoisier in 1777 proved that phosphorus is an element.
Symbol: P
Atomic Number: 15
Atomic Weight: 31
Electronic Configuration: 2, 8, 5 (K, L, M)

Occurace

Phosphorus is an active element, it is not found free in nature. It is widely distributed in nature in the combined state. It occurs as phosphates in the rocks and in the soil and as phosphoproteins in all living beings. It is an essential constituent of bones, teeth, blood and nervous tissues. It is necessary for the growth of plants. Bone ash contains about 80% calcium phosphate, it is present in milk, eggs and guano.
The principal minerals of phosphorus are

  1. Phosphorite \( Ca_3 (PO_4)_2 \)

  2. Flourapatite \( 3 Ca_3 (PO_4)_2 . Ca F_2 \)

  3. Chlorapatite \( 3 Ca_3 (PO_4)_2. CaCl_2 \)

Extraction

Phosphorus is extracted either from phosphorite or bone ash by the application of follow two processes.

Retort process or old process

In this process, the phosphorite mineral or bone ash is digested with concentrated sulphuric acid (about 60%). Products formed are : Insoluble calcium sulphate and orthophosphoric acid.
\( Ca(PO_4)_2 + 3 H_2SO_4 \rightarrow \ 3 CaSO_4 + 2 H_3 PO_4 \)
The syrupy liquid is separated from insoluble residue by filtration. The liquid is evaporate = then it changes into metaphosphoric acid with evolution of water.
\( H_3 PO_4 \rightarrow \ HPO_3 + H_2 O \)
The metaphosphoric acid is mixed with powdered coke and distilled in fireclay retorts a bright red heat. The acid is reduced to phosphorus by carbon which comes in vapouris: form. The vapours are condensed below water
\( H_3 PO_4 + 10 C \rightarrow \ P_4 + 10 CO + 2H_2 O \)

Electrothermal process or modern process

In these days phosphorus is obtained by the direct reduction of mineral phosphorite by carbon in presence of silica. The mixture of phosphorite, carbon and silica is dried and then introduced into the electric furnace. The furnace is an iron tank lined inside with refractory bricks. Carbon electrodes are fitted on either side of furnace. The furnace has two exits, one for removal of vapours in the upper part of the furnace and other for removal of slag in the lower part of the furnace. The charge is introduced through the closed hopper arrangement. The mixture is heated at \( 1400^{\circ} \ C - 1500^{\circ} \ C \) by the discharge of an alternating current between carbon electrodes. Silica combines with calcium phosphate and forms phosphorus pentoxide which is reduced by carbon into phosphorus.
\( 2 Ca(PO_4)_2 + 6 Si O_2 + 10 C \rightarrow \ 6 Ca SiO_3 + P_4 + 10 CO \)
Vapours of phosphorus and carbon monoxide leave the furnace through the upper exit and are condensed under water. The liquid slag is tapped out periodically through an exit in the base.

Purification

Phosphorus obtained is further purified by melting under acidified potassium dichromate solution. When the impurities are oxidized. It is redistilled.

Allotropic forms of Phosphorus

Phosphorus exists in many allotropic forms. Of these three main allotropic forms are

  1. White phosphorus

  2. Red phosphorus

  3. Black phosphorus

  1. It is used in the preparation of HBr and HI.

  4. It is used in the manufacture of incenditary bombs and smoke screens.

Phosphorus Pentoxide

Preparation

It is prepared by heating phosphorus in a free supply of air or oxygen is
\( P_4 + 5O_2 \ \rightarrow \ P_4 O_{10} \)
It is further purified by sublimation. Reaction of phosphorus with \( CO_2 \) also produces phosphorus pentoxide is
\( 2P + 5CO_2 \ \rightarrow \ P_2 O_5 + 5 CO \)

Physical Properties

  1. It is a white crystalline solid.

  2. It is odourless in its pure form. The usual garlic odour is due to presence of small amount of \( P_4 O_6 \) as impurity

  3. It undergoes sublimation on heating.

  4. It has great affinity for water. The final product is orthophosphoric acid. It is therefore, termed as phosphoric anhydride.
    \( P_4 O_{10} \ \xrightarrow{ 2 H_2 O} \ 4 HPO_3 \ \xrightarrow{ 2 H_2 O} \ 2 H_4 P_2 O_7 \ \rightarrow \ 2 H_2 O \ \rightarrow \ 4 H_3 PO_4 \)

Chemical Properties of Phosphorus Pentoxide

  1. When heated strongly with carbon, it forms red phosphorus
    \( P_4 O_{10} + 10 C \ \rightarrow \ 10 CO + 4 P \)

  2. When fused with basic oxides, it forms phosphates
    \( 6 Ca O + P_4 O{10} \ \rightarrow \ 2 Ca_3 (PO_4)_2 \)

  3. Moisture of \( P_2 O_5 \) and \( O_2 \) in vapour state combine in presence of electric discharge to form \( P_2 O_6 \) called phosphorus peroxide.
    \( P_2 O_5 + \frac{1}{2} O_2 \ \rightarrow \ P_2 O_6 \)

Uses

It is the most effective drying or dehydrating agent below \( 100^{\circ} \ C \) .

Phosphoric Acid

Phosphoric acid is also called as orthophosphoric acid.

  1. Reaction of phosphorus pentoxide with boiled water
    \( P_2 O_5 + 3 H_2 O \ \rightarrow \ 2 H_3 PO_4 \)

  2. Reaction of \( PCl_5 \) with boiled water :
    \( PCl_5 + 4 H_2 O \ \rightarrow \ H_3 PO_4 + 5 HCl \)

  3. Reaction of \( Pb(PO_4)_2 \) with hydrogen sulphideand
    \( Pb(PO_4)_2 + 3 H_2 S \ \rightarrow \ 3 Pb S + 2 H_3 PO_4 \)

  4. Reaction of calcium phosphate with silicon dioxide
    \( 2 Ca_3 (PO_4)_2 + 6 SiO_2 \ \rightarrow \ 6 Ca SiO_3 + P_4 O_{10} \)

Laboratory preparation of phosphoric acid

Heat red phosphorus with conc. nitric acid in a flask with condenser.
\( P + 5 HNO_3 \ \rightarrow \ H_3 PO_4 + H_2 O + 5 O_2 \)
It is also manufactured on large scale from bone ash or phosphorite mineral by decomposing calcium phosphate present in bone ash or phosphate mineral with conc.
\( Ca_3 (PO_4)_2 + 3 H_2 SO_4 \ \rightarrow \ 3 CaSO_4 + 2 H_3 PO_4 \)

Physical Properties

  1. It is a crystalline transparent solid.

  2. It is highly soluble in water.

  3. Melting point of it is \( 44^{\circ} \ C \) .

Chemical Properties

  1. When phosphoric acid is heated is heated at \( 250^{\circ} \ C \) it is converted into pyro phosphoric acid.
    \( 2 H_3 PO_4 \ \rightarrow \ H_2 P_2 O_7 + H_2 O \)
    If further heated it is converted to meta phosphoric acid.
    \( H_3 PO_4 \ \xrightarrow [600^{\circ} \ C] \ H PO_3 + H_2 O \)

  2. It is a tribasic acid.

  3. On reaction with silver nitrate it forms an yellow precipitate silver phosphate.

  4. On reaction with bromide and iodide with hydro bromic and hydro iodic acid respectively.

  5. On reaction with NaOH it forms following compounds
    \( \begin{align} NaOH + H_3 P O_4 \ \rightarrow \ H_2O + Na H_2 P O_4 \\\\[3pt] 2 NaOH + H_3 P O_4 \ \rightarrow \ 2 H_2O + Na_2 H P O_4 \\\\[3pt] 3 NaOH + H_3 P O_4 \ \rightarrow \ 3 H_2O + Na_3PO_4 \end{align} \)

Uses

  1. It is used for making meta phosphoric acid and pyrophosphoric acid .

  2. For preparation of HBr and HI in laboratory.

  3. It is used to stabilize \( H_2 O_2 \) .

  4. In preparation of pharmaceutical industry.

Super Phosphate of Lime

It is a mixture of calcium dihydrogen phosphate and gypsum. It is manufactured by the action of 70% concentrated sulphuric acid on powdered phosphorite or bone ash.
\( Ca_2(PO_4)_2 + 2H_2 SO_4 + 5H_2 O \ \rightarrow \ Ca (H_2 PO_4)_2 + 2 CaSO_4.2H_2 O + heat \) .

Uses

It is used as a fertilizer. It is soluble in water ,so plants easily absorbs this fertilizer. Used in match industry. Radio active phosphorus is used in the treatment of leukemia and other blood disorders.

Solved examples

Example 1:

\( PCl_5 \) is known bur \( PI_5 \) is not known. Why?
Solution:
Due to small size of Cl atom, five Cl atoms can be accommodated around P atom. But I is of large size and therefore, five I atoms cannot be accommodated around P atom. As a result, P - I bonds are weak and prefer to form \( PI_3 \) rather than \( PI_5 \) .

Example 2:

Give one example each of oxyacid of P having the oxidation state

  1. + 4

  2. + 3

Solution:

  1. Hypophosphoric

  2. Phosphorus acid

Example 3:

What is the oxidation state of phosphorus in the following :

  1. \( H_3 PO_3 \)

  2. \( PCl_3 \)

  3. \( Ca_3 P_2 \)

  4. \( Na_3 PO_4 \)

  5. \( POF_3 \)

Solution:

  1. Let the oxidation state of phosphorus in \( H_3PO_3 \) be x.
    \( \therefore \) 3(+1) + x + 3(-2) = 0
    x = 3.
    \( \therefore \) Oxidation state of phosphorus in \( H_3PO_3 \) is + 3.

  2. Let the oxidation state of phosphorus in \( PCl_3 \) be x.
    \( \therefore \) x + 3(-1) = 0
    x = 3.
    \( \therefore \) Oxidation state of phosphorus in \( PCl_3 \) is + 3.

  3. Let the oxidation state of phosphorus in \( Ca_3 P_2 \) be x.
    \( \therefore \) 3(2) + 2(x) = 0
    2x = - 6 = c = - 3.
    \( \therefore \) Oxidation state of phosphorus in \( Ca_3 P_2 \) is - 3.

  4. Let the oxidation state of phosphorus in \( Na_3 PO_4 \) be x.
    \( \therefore \) 3(+1) + x + 4(-2) = 0
    x = + 5.
    \( \therefore \) Oxidation state of phosphorus in \( Na_3 PO_4 \) is + 5.

  5. Let the oxidation state of phosphorus in \( POF_3 \) be x.
    \( \therefore \) x - 3(-1) = 0
    x = 5.
    \( \therefore \) Oxidation state of phosphorus in \( POF_3 \) is + 5.

Example 4:

Name the oxide of nitrogen obtained in the following reactions:

  1. \( Pb(NO_3)_2 \ \xrightarrow {Heat}[673 K] \)

  2. \( NH_2 NO_3 \ \xrightarrow {Heat} \)

  3. \( N_2 O_4 + NO \ \xrightarrow {250 K} \)

Solution:

  1. Nitrogen dioxide

  2. Nitrous oxide

  3. Dinitrogen trioxide

Example 5:

How many valence shell electrons are utilized by each phosphorus atom in \( P_4 \) molecule?
Solution:
From the structural representation of \( P_4 \) , we can infer that each P shares three of its valence electrons with other phosphorus atoms. A lone pair of e's is seen on each P.

Example 6:

What happens when \( AgNO_3 \) solution is added to a solution of \( H_3PO_3 \) ?
Solution:
Ag is precipitated from the solution. The reaction is
\( H_3 PO_3 + 2 Ag NO_3 + H_2 O \ \rightarrow \ H_3 PO_4 + 2 Ag + 2 HNO_3 \) .
