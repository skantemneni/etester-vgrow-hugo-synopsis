Volumes of Solids

Cube

If 'a' be the edge of a cube, then
Volume = \( a^3 \)
Total surface area = \( 6 a^2 \)
Diagonal of a cube = \( \sqrt{3} \times edge = \sqrt{3} a \)
Edge of a cube = \( \sqrt[3]{volume} \)

Cuboid

If 'l' be the length, 'b' be the breadth and 'h' be the height or depth of a cuboid, then

  1. Volume = length \( \times \) breadth \( \times \) height
    Length (l) = \( \frac{volume}{b \times h} \)
    Breadth (b) = \( \frac{volume}{l \times h} \)
    Height (h) = \( \frac{volume}{l \times b} \)

  2. Total surface area = 2(lb + bh + hl)

  3. Diagonal of a cuboid = \( \sqrt{l^2 + b^2 + h^2} \)

Cylinder

If 'r' is the radius of the base
'h' is the height of the cylinder
'd' is the diameter of the base

  1. Area of curved surface = \( 2 \pi r h = \pi d h \)

  2. Total surface area = \( 2 \pi r (h + r) = \pi d \left ( \frac{d}{2} + h \right ) \)

  3. Volume of the cylinder = \( \pi r^2 h \)

Hollow Cylinder

  1. Volume of the hallow cylinder = \( ( \pi R^2 - \pi r^2)h = \pi(R^2 - r^2)h \)

  2. Area of the curved surface = \( 2 \pi R h + 2 \pi r h = 2 \pi(R + r)h \)

  3. Total surface area = area of curved surface + 2 (area of base)
    = \( 2 \pi(R + r)h + 2 \pi(R^2 - r^2) \)
    = \( 2 \pi(R + r)h + 2 \pi(R + r)(R - r) \)
    = \( 2 \pi(R + r)(h + R - r) \)

Cone

If 's' is the slant height, 'r' is the radius of the base and 'h' is the vertical height.

  1. Area of curved surface = \( \pi \ rs \)
    \( = \pi r ( \sqrt{h^2} + r^2}) \)

  2. Total surface area = curved surface area + area of the base
    \( \begin{align} = \pi \ rs + \pi r^2 \\\\[3pt] = \pi r (s + r) \end{align} \)

  3. Volume of the cone \( = \frac{1}{3} \pi r^2 h \)
    \( \begin{align} s^2 = h^2 + r^2; \ s = \sqrt{h^2 + r^2}; \\\\[3pt] h = \sqrt{s^2 - r^2} \\\\[3pt] r = \sqrt{s^2 - h^2} \end{align} \)

Sphere

If 'r' is the radius of the sphere and 'd' is the diameter of the sphere, then

  1. Surface Area (A) = \( 4 \pi \ r^2 = \pi d^2 \)

  2. Volume of the sphere (V) = \( = \frac{4}{3} \pi \ r^3 \)
    \( = \frac{ \pi}{6} d^3 \)

  3. Radius (r) \( = \sqrt[3]{ \frac{3V}{4 \pi}} \)

Hollow Sphere

Volume of the Hollow Sphere \( = \frac{4}{3} \pi R^3 - \frac{4}{3} \pi r^3 \)
\( = \frac{4}{3} \pi (R^3 - r^3) = \frac{ \pi}{6}(D^3 - d^3) \)

Triangular Prism

  1. Volume = \( A \times h = \frac{ \sqrt{3}}{4} a^2 \times h \)

  2. Total surface area = lateral surface area + sum of areas of two ends
    \( \begin{align} = ah + ah + ah + \frac{ \sqrt{3}}{4} a^2 \\\\[3pt] = 3 ah + \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4} a^2 \end{align} \)

Pentagonal Prism

  1. Surface area of pentagon \( = \sqrt{3} a^2 \)

  2. Volume = \( \sqrt{3} a^2 \times h \)

  3. Total surface area = \( 5 ah + 2. \sqrt{3} a^2 \)

Hexagonal Prism

  1. Surface area of the Hexagonal \( = 2.5981 a^2 \)

  2. Volume of Hexagonal = \( A \times h \)
    \( = 2.591 . a^2 \times h \)

  3. Total surface area = \( 6 ah + 2 \times 2.5981 a^2 \)

Square Pyramid

  1. Volume \( = \frac{1}{3} h \times \) area of base
    \( = \frac{1}{3} h \times a^2 \)

  2. Lateral surface area of the pyramid
    \( \begin{align} = \frac{1}{2} \times Perimeter \times slant \ height \\\\[3pt] = \frac{1}{2} \times 4a \times s \end{align} \)
    = 2as

  3. Total area of the pyramid = \( \frac{1}{2} \times 4as + a^2 \)
    \( = 2as + a^2 = a(2s + a) \)

Triangular Pyramid

  1. Area of the lateral surface of the pyramid
    \( \begin{align} = \frac{1}{2} \times perimeter \times slant \ height \\\\[3pt] = \frac{1}{2} \times 3a \times s = \frac{3}{2} as \end{align} \)

  2. Volume = \( \frac{1}{3} \times h \times \) area of base
    \( = \frac{1}{3} \times h \times \frac{ \sqrt{3}}{4}.a^2 = \frac{ha^2}{4 \sqrt{3}} \)

  3. Total area of the pyramid = \( \frac{1}{2} \times 3as + \frac{ \sqrt{3}}{4} a^2 \)

Pentagonal Pyramid

  1. Total area of the pyramid = \( \frac{1}{2} \times 5as + \sqrt{3} a^2 \)
    Where 's' is the slant height

  2. Volume = \( \frac{1}{3} \times h \times area \ of \ base \)
    \( = \frac{1}{3} \times h \times \sqrt{3}a^2 = \frac{ha^2}{ \sqrt{3}} \)

Hexagonal Pyramid

  1. Total surface area = \( \frac{1}{2} \times 6as + 2.5981 a^2 \)
    Where 'S' is the slant height

  2. Volume = \( \frac{1}{3} \times h \times area \ of \ base \)
    \( = \frac{1}{3} \times h \times 2.5981 a^2 \)

Area of the four walls of a room

If l, b, h are the length, breadth and height
Total area of the four walls (A) = height of the room \( \times \) perimeter of the floor
= \( h(2l + 2b) = 2h(l + b) \)

Rectangular paths

If a rectangular path of uniform width surrounds a rectangle, then the length 'l' and the breadth 'b' of the outer rectangle are obtained by adding twice the width 'w' of the path to the length and breadth of the inner rectangle.

  1. Area of outer rectangle = (l + 2w)(b + 2w)

  2. Area of path = (l + 2w)(b + 2w) - lb

Tours (Solid Ring)

Tour is a solid of revolution of three dimensions obtained when a circle is rotated about an axis lying in its plane but not inserting the circle.
Example: A cycle tube, rings, bangles, etc.
If the radius of the circle which rotates is 'r' and if 'a' is the distance between the centre of the surface of circle and axis of revolution, then

  1. Volume of the tours = \( \pi r^2 . 2 \pi a \)
    = \( 2 \pi^2 r^2 a \) cubic units.

  2. The curved surface area of tours = \( 2 \pi r. 2 \pi a \) square units.
    The area of the ring around the top of the hemispherical vessel = \( \pi (R^2 - r^2) \) units.
    Total surface area of a hemispherical vessel = \( 3 \pi(R^2 + r^2) (unit)^2. \)
    Where 'R' is the outer radius.
    and 'r' is the inner radius.

Frustum of cone

  1. Volume of frustum of the cone = \( \frac{ \pi h}{3} [ R^2 + Rr + r^2 ] \) cubic units

  2. Lateral surface of frustum of the cone = \( \pi l (R + r) \) sq. units

  3. Total surface area of frustum = lateral surface area + area of base + area of top
    = \( \pi [ R^2 + r^2 + l(R + r)] \) sq units.

Solid Sphere

A solid formed by rotating semi circle having its diameter as an axis.

Hemisphere

A plane through the centre of a sphere divides it into two equal parts, each of which is called a hemi - sphere.

Solved examples

Example 1:

Calculate the surface area of the following prism.

Solution:
The given prism has 2 triangles and 3 rectangles on its surfaces.
Area of the two triangles = \( 2 \left [ \frac{1}{2} \times 8 \times 5 \right ] \)
Area of rectangle type P = \( 2(10 \times 6) \)

Area of rectangle type Q = \( 8 \times 10 \)
Total surface area
= \( 2 \left [ \frac{1}{2} \times 8 \times 5 \right ] + 2(10 \times 6) + 8 \times 10 \)
= 40 + 120 + 80
= 240 \( cm^2 \)

Example 2:

Calculate the surface area of the solid cylinder with diameter 14 cm and height 10 cm. \( \left (Assume \ \pi = \frac{22}{7} \right ) \)

Solution:
The net of the cylinder shows two circles and a rectangle.
Area of the two circles = \( 2( \pi \times 7^2) = 2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times 7 \times 7 \)

Area of the rectangle = \( 2 \pi(7)(10) \)
= \( 2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times 7 \times 10 \)
Total surface area of the cylinder
= \( 2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times 7 \times 7 + 2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times 7 \times 10 \)
= 308 + 440
= \( 748 \ cm^2 \)

Example 3:

Find the surface area of the square pyramid shown below.

Solution:
The given pyramid has one square and four equal triangles for its surface.

Area of the 4 triangles
= \( 4 \left ( \frac{1}{2} \times 10 \times 12 \right ) \)
Area of the square = 10 \( \times \) 10
Total surface area of the pyramid
= \( 4 \left ( \frac{1}{2} \times 10 \times 12 \right ) + (10 \times 10) \)
= 240 + 100
= 340 \( cm^2 \)

Example 4:

The diagram shows a cone. The radius of its base is 2.8 cm and its slant height is 8 cm. Find the area of its curved surface. \( \left ( \pi = \frac{22}{7} \right ) \)

Solution:
The net of the cone is a sector of a bigger circle and a circle.

Area of the curved surface
= \( \pi \times \) radius of base \( \times \) slant height
= \( \pi r s = \frac{22}{7} \times 2.8 \times 8 \)
= \( 70.4 \ cm^2 \)

Example 5:

Sides of two cubes are in the ratio of 2 : 3. Find the ratio of their surface areas.

Solution:
Ratio of surface areas
= \( (Ratio \ of \ sides)^2 = (2 : 3)^2 = 4 : 9 \)

Example 6:

If the heights and the radii of two right circular cylinders are in the ratio 2 : 3 and 4 : 5 respectively. Find the ratio of their curved surface areas.

Solution:
Ratio of curved surface areas
= (Ratio of radii) (ratio of heights)
= \( \frac{4}{5} \times \frac{2}{3} = 8 : 15 \)

Example 7

Two right circular cones of equal curved surface areas have their slant heights in the ratio of 3 : 5. Find the ratio of their radii.

Solution:
Ratio of radii = inverse ratio of slant heights = \( \frac{1}{3} : \frac{1}{5} = 5 : 3 \)

Example 8

The diagram shows the solid cone. The radius of its base is 3.5 cm and its surface area is \( 148.5 \ cm^2 \) . What is its slant height ? \( \left ( \pi = \frac{22}{7} \right ) \)

Solution:
surface area of the cone = \( \pi r^2 + \pi r s \)
\( 148.5 = \frac{22}{7} \times 3.5 \times 3.5 + \frac{22}{7} \times 3.5 \times s \)
148.5 - 38.5 = \( \frac{22}{7} \times 3.5 \times s \)
\( 110 = \frac{22}{7} \times 3.5 \times s \ or \ \frac{22}{7} \times 3.5 \times = 110 \)
\( s = \frac{110 \times 7}{22 \times 3.5} = 10 \ cm \)

Example 9

Find the slant height, curved surface area and the whole surface area of a cone of radius 21 cm and the height 28 cm.

Solution:
Here, r = 21 cm and h = 28 cm.
\( \therefore \) slant height, l = \( \sqrt{r^2 + h^2} = \sqrt{(21)^2 + (28)^2} = \sqrt{1225} = 35 \ cm \) .
Curved surface area = \( \pi r l = \left ( \frac{22}{7} \times 21 \times 35 \right ) \ cm^2 = 2310 \ cm^2 \)
Total surface area = \( ( \pi r l + \pi r^2) = \left (2310 + \frac{22}{7} \times 21 \times 21 \right ) \ cm^2 = 3696 \ cm^2 \)

Example 10

Calculate the surface area of a sphere of radius 2.8 cm.

Solution:
Surface area of the sphere = \( 4 \pi r^2 \)
= \( 4 \times \frac{22}{7} \times 2.8 \times 2.8 \)
= \( 98.56 \ cm^2 \)

Example 11

A ball in the shape of a sphere has a surface area of \( 221.76 \ cm^2 \) . Calculate its diameter.

Solution:
Surface area of the sphere = \( 4 \pi r^2 \)
\( \begin{align} 4 \pi r^2 = 221.76 \\\\[3pt] 4 \times \frac{22}{7} \times r^2 = 221.76 \\\\[3pt] r^2 = \frac{221.76 \times 7}{4 \times 22} \\\\[3pt] r^2 = 17.64 \\\\[3pt] r = \sqrt{17.64} \\\\[3pt] = 4.2 \ cm \end{align} \)
Diameter = 2r = \( 2 \times 4.2 \)
= 8.4 cm.

Example 12

Diameter of a sphere is 28 cm. Find its surface area.

Solution:
Radius of the sphere (r) = \( \frac{28}{2} = 14 \ cm. \)
surface area = \( 4 \pi r^2 = 4 \times \frac{22}{7} \times 14 \times 14 \)
= 2464 sq.cm.

Example 13

Calculate the total surface area of each of the following combined solids. \( \left ( \pi = \frac{22}{7} \right ) \)

Solution:

  1. The solid is made up of a triangular prism and a cuboid.
    surface area of prism
    = \( 2 \left ( \frac{1}{2} \times 6 \times 4 \right ) + 2(8 \times 5) \)
    = 24 + 80
    = 104 \( cm^2 \)
    surface area of cuboid
    = \( 2(6 \times 7) + 2(8 \times 7) + (6 \times 8) \)
    = 84 + 112 + 48
    = 244 \( cm^2 \)
    The total surface area of the combined solid
    = 104 + 244
    = 348 \( cm^2 \)

  2. The solid is made up of a cone and a cylinder.
    surface area of the cone
    = \( \pi r s \) (curved surface area of the cone)
    = \( \frac{22}{7} \times 3.5 \times 7 \)
    = \( 77 \ cm^2 \)
    surface area of the cylinder = (area of curved surface) + (area of base)
    = \( 2 \pi r h + \pi r^2 \)
    = \( 2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times 3.5 \times 10 + \frac{22}{7} \times 3.5 \times 3.5 \)
    = 220 + 38.5
    = \( 258.5 \ cm^2 \)
    The total surface area of the combined solid = 77 + 258.5 = \( 335.5 \ cm^2 \)

Example 14

Find the volumes of these right prisms.

Solution:

  1. Volume of prism
    = Base area \( \times \) Height
    = 26 \( \times \) 8
    = 208 \( cm^3 \)

  2. Volume of prism

Example 15

The cross section of a prism 12 cm long, is a trapezium with the measurements shown. If the volume of the prism is \( 300 \ cm^3 \) , calculate the value of h.

Solution:
Volume of prism = Area of cross section \( \times \) length
\( \begin{align} 300 = \frac{1}{2}(4 + 6)(h) \times 12 \\\\[3pt] = \frac{1}{2}(10) h \times 12 \end{align} \)
= 60 h
\( \therefore h = \frac{300}{60} \)
= 5 cm

Example 16

Calculate the volume of the following cylinders.

  1. Area of cross section = \( 135 \ mm^2 \) ;
    Height = 30 mm

  2. Base radius = 5 cm; Length = 8 cm
    \( ( \pi = 3.142) \)

Solution:

  1. Volume of cylinder
    = Area of cross section \( \times \) Height
    = 135 \( \times \) 30
    = \( 4050 \ mm^3 \)

  2. Volume of cylinder
    \( \begin{align} = \pi r^2 h \\\\[3pt] = 3.142 \times 5^2 \times 8 \\\\[3pt] = 628.4 \ cm^3 \end{align} \)

Example 17

The volume of a cylindrical oil can is \( 3080 \ cm^3 \) . If the base radius is 10.5 cm, find the height of the can. ( \( \pi = 3.142 \) )

Solution:

Volume of can = \( \pi r^2h \)
\( \begin{align} 3080 = 3.142 \times 10.5^2 \times h \\\\[3pt] h = \frac{3080}{3.142 \times 10.5^2} \\\\[3pt] \therefore \ h = 8.89 \ cm \end{align} \)

Example 18

The volume of a cylinder is \( 330 \ cm^3 \) . If the length of the cylinder is 4 cm, calculate the radius of the cross section. ( \( \pi = 3.142 \) )

Solution:

Volume of cylinder = \( \pi r^2h \)
\( \begin{align} 330 = 3.142 \times r^2 \times 4 \\\\[3pt] r^2 = \frac{330}{3.142 \times 4} \\\\[3pt] r = \sqrt{26.26} = 5.12 \ cm \end{align} \)
\( \therefore \) Radius of cross section = 5.12 cm.

Example 19

The diagram shows the net of a right cylinder. Find the volume of the cylinder, in \( cm^3 \)

  1. \( \frac{20}{\pi} \)

  2. \( \frac{50}{\pi} \)

  3. \( \frac{25}{\pi} \)

  4. \( 40 \pi \)

Solution:

Circumference of circular base 10 cm
\( \begin{align} 2 \pi r = 10 \\\\[3pt] r = \frac{10}{2 \pi} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{5}{\pi} \end{align} \)
Volume of cylinder = \( \pi r^2 h \)
\( \begin{align} = \pi \left ( \frac{5}{\pi} \right)^2 \times 2 \\\\[3pt] = \pi \times \frac{25}{ \pi^2} \times 2 \\\\[3pt] = \frac{50}{ \pi} \ cm^2 \end{align} \)

Example 20

In the right cylindrical container shown, the base radius is 8 cm. If the height of the water level is 20 cm, find the volume of the water in the container. ( \( \pi = 3.142 \) )

Solution:
Volume of water = \( \pi r^2h \)
\( \begin{align} = 3.142 \times 8^2 \times 20 \\\\[3pt] = 4021.8 \ cm^3 \\\\[3pt] = 4021.8 \ ml \\\\[3pt] = 4.0218 \ l \end{align} \)
