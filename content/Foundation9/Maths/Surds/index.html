Surds

Let "a" be a rational number and "n" be a positive integer such that \( \sqrt[n]{a} \) is irrational then \( \sqrt[n]{a} \) is called a surd of the order "n".
Example : \( \sqrt[3]{9}, \ \sqrt[6]{4}, \ \sqrt[5]{24} \) .........

Types of Surds

Quadratic Surd

A surd of order 2 is called a "quadratic surd".
Example : \( \sqrt{7}, \ \sqrt{13}, \ \sqrt{14} \) .........

Cubic surd

A surd of order 3 is called a "Cubic Surd".
Example : \( \sqrt[3]{4}, \ \sqrt[3]{7}, \ \sqrt[3]{11} \) ........

Biquadratic Surd

A surd of order 4 is called a "Biquadratic Surd"
Example : \( \sqrt[4]{6}, \ \sqrt[4]{15}, \ \sqrt[4]{35} \) ........

Pure Surds

A surd which has unity only as rational factor, the other factor being irrational, is called a pure surd.
Example : \( \sqrt{2}, \ \sqrt[3]{3}, \ \sqrt[4]{5} \) ..........

Mixed Surds

A surd which has a rational factor other than unity, the other factor being irrational, is called a mixed surd.
Example : \( \sqrt[2]{3}, \ \7 \sqrt[3]{5}, \ \frac{3}{5} \sqrt[4]{5}, \) ........

Compound Surds

An expression consisting of the sum or difference of two or more simple surds or the sum of or difference of a rational and a surd is called a compound surd.
Example : \( \sqrt{3} + \sqrt{2}, \ 2 - \sqrt{3}, \) ......... etc

Binomial Surds

The compound surd consisting of only two terms are called binomial surds.
Example : \( \sqrt{2} + \sqrt[3]{3}, \ \sqrt{3} - \sqrt{7} \) ........etc

Trinomial Surds

Compound surds consisting of three terms are said to be trinomial surds.
Example : \( \sqrt{7} + \sqrt{5} - \sqrt{3}, \ 3 \sqrt{5} - 4 \sqrt{2} - 2 \sqrt{11} \) ...... etc

Polynomial Surds

A compound surd consisting of more than two terms are said to be polynomial surds.
Example : \( 2 \sqrt{3} + 4 \sqrt{5} - 7 \sqrt{11} + 6 \sqrt{3} \) ...... etc
Note

  1. \( \sqrt{ \pi} \) is not a surd, since \( \pi \) is irrational

  2. \( \sqrt[3]{8} \) is not a surd, since \( \sqrt[3]{8} = 2 \) , which is a rational number

  3. Every surd is an irrational number, but every irrational number need not be a surd

Comparison of two Surds

If two surds are of the same order then the one whose radical is larger is the larger of the two.
Example : \( \sqrt[3]{19} > \sqrt[3]{13}, \ \sqrt[7]{80} < \sqrt[7]{93} \) ,........

Like and Unliked surds

Two or more surds having the same irrational factor are known as "similar or like surds". Otherwise, they are called "dissimilar or unliked surds".
Exampe :

  1. \( \sqrt{5}, \ 8 \sqrt{5}, \ \frac{2}{3} \sqrt{5} \) are like surds

  2. \( \sqrt{2}, \ \sqrt{5}, \ \sqrt[3]{7} \) are unlike surds

Operations on Surds

Addition and Subtraction of like surds

Like surds can be added or subtracted by using the distributive laws.
Example : \( 4 \sqrt{5} + 6 \sqrt{5} + \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{5} = \left( 4 + 6 + \frac{1}{2} \right) \sqrt{5} = \frac{21}{2} \sqrt{5} \)

Multiplication and Division of surds

The surds of the same order can be multiplied or divided by using the rules,
Example :

  1. \( \sqrt[n]{a} \times \sqrt[n]{b} = \sqrt[n]{ab} \)

  2. \( \frac{ \sqrt[n]{a}}{ \sqrt[n]{b}} = \sqrt[n]{ \frac{a}{b}} \)

If the given surds are not of the same order then they are converted to the lowest common order and then multiplied or divided as required.
Example :

  1. \( \sqrt[3]{5} \times \sqrt[3]{7} = \sqrt[3]{35} \)

  2. \( \sqrt[5]{7} \div \sqrt[5]{9} = \sqrt[5]{ \frac{7}{9}} \)

Rationalisation of Surds

Rationalising Factor (R.F.)

When the product of two surds is a rational number, then each surd is called a 'rationalising factor' of the other.
Example : \( ( \sqrt{3} + \sqrt{2}) ( \sqrt{3} - \sqrt{2}) = 3 - 2 = 1 \) , which is rational
\( \therefore ( \sqrt{3} + \sqrt{2}) \) and \( ( \sqrt{3} - \sqrt{2}) \) are rationalising factors of each other.

Rationalisation of surds

The process of converting a surd to a rational number by multiplying it with a suitable rationalising factor is called the rationalisation of the surd.

Monomial surds

A surd consisting of only one term is known as a monomial surd.
Example: \( \sqrt[3]{5}, \ \sqrt[5]{7}, \ \sqrt{3} \) ,..........

Rationalising factor of a monomial surd

One of the rationalising factor of \( a^{ \frac{1}{n}} \) is \( a^{ \left( 1 - \frac{1}{n} \right)} \)
Example : \( 5^{ \frac{3}{5}} \) and \( 5^{ \frac{2}{5}} \) are rationalising factor of each other

Rationalising factor of a binomial surd

  1. Rationalising factor of \( (a + \sqrt{b}) \) is \( (a - \sqrt{b}) \)

  2. Rationalising factor of \( ( \sqrt{a} - \sqrt{b}) \) is \( ( \sqrt{a} + \sqrt{b}) \)

  3. Rationalising factor of \( (a - \sqrt{b}) \) is \( (a + \sqrt{b}) \)

  4. Rationalising factor of \( ( \sqrt{a} + \sqrt{b}) \) is \( ( \sqrt{a} - \sqrt{b}) \)

Rationalising factor of trinomial surd

To find the rationalising factor of a trinomial surd, we group two of its terms together.
Thus rationalising factor of \( \left [( \sqrt{a} + \sqrt{b}) - \sqrt{c} \right ] \) is \( \left [( \sqrt{a} + \sqrt{b}) - \sqrt{c} \right ] \)

Conjugate Surds

Two binomials surds which differ only in the signs ( + / -) between them are called conjugate surds.
Example :

  1. \( (a + \sqrt{b}) \) and \( (a - \sqrt{b}) \) are conjugate binomial surds.

  2. \( ( \sqrt{a} + \sqrt{b}) \) and \( ( \sqrt{a} - \sqrt{b}) \) are conjugate binomial surds.

Some important results

  1. If \( a + \sqrt{b} = c + \sqrt{d} \) where a, c rational numbers and \( \sqrt{b}, \ \sqrt{d} \) are surds, then a = c and b = d.

  2. If a, b, \( \sqrt{a^2 - b} \) are positive rational numbers and \( \sqrt{b} \) is a surd, then
    \( \sqrt{a + \sqrt{b}} = \sqrt{ \frac{a + \sqrt{a^2 - b}}{2}} + \sqrt{ \frac{a - \sqrt{a^2 - b}}{2}} \)

  3. If a, b, c, d are positive rational numbers and \( \sqrt{b}, \ \sqrt{c}, \ \sqrt{d} \) are surds, then \( \sqrt{ a + \sqrt{b} + \sqrt{c} + \sqrt{d}} = \sqrt[4]{ \frac{bd}{4c}} + \sqrt[4]{ \frac{bc}{4d}} + \sqrt[4]{ \frac{cd}{4b}} \)

  4. \( \sqrt[5]{a + k \sqrt{b}} = \sqrt{ \sqrt{ \frac{4b^2 + k}{5}} - b} + \sqrt{b} \)

  5. \( \sqrt[3]{a + b \sqrt{c}} = \sqrt{ \frac{b - c}{3}} + \sqrt{c} \)
    If necessary adjust "b" so that it will be less than half of "a"

  6. \( \sqrt[3]{a} + \sqrt[3]{b} \) is a rationalising factor of \( a^{ \frac{2}{3}} - a^{ \frac{1}{3}}. b^{ \frac{1}{3}} + b^{ \frac{2}{3}} \) and vice versa.

  7. \( \sqrt[3]{a} - \sqrt[3]{b} \) is a rationalising factor of \( a^{ \frac{2}{3}} + a^{ \frac{1}{3}}. b^{ \frac{1}{3}} + b^{ \frac{2}{3}} \) and vice versa.

  8. \( (a + \sqrt{b})^{x^2 - K} + (a - \sqrt{b})^{x^2 - K} = 2a, \ x^2 - k = 1 \Rightarrow x = \pm \sqrt{K \pm 1} \)

Solved examples

Example 1:

Question:
Find the value of "x" if \( \sqrt[3]{4x - 5} - 5 = 0 \)

Solution:
Cubing on both sides.
4x - 5 = 125
4x = 130 or x = 32.5

Example 2:

Question:
\( x = 1 - \sqrt{2}, \) then find the value of \( \left( x - \frac{1}{x} \right)^3 \) .

Solution:
\( \begin{align} \frac{1}{x} = \frac{1}{1 - \sqrt{2}} = \frac{1}{1 - \sqrt{2}} \times \frac{1 + \sqrt{2}}{1 + \sqrt{2}} = -(1 + \sqrt{2}) \\\\[3pt] \left(x - \frac{1}{x} \right)^3 = (1 - \sqrt{2} + 1 + \sqrt{2})^3 = (2)^3 = 8 \end{align} \)

Example 3:

Question:
If \( x = \frac{ \sqrt{3} + \sqrt{2}}{ \sqrt{3} - \sqrt{2}} \) and \( y = \frac{ \sqrt{3} - \sqrt{2}}{ \sqrt{3} + \sqrt{2}} \) then find \( x^2 + y^2 \) .

Solution:
\( \begin{align} x = \frac{( \sqrt{3} + \sqrt{2})^2}{1} = 5 + 2 \sqrt{6} \\\\[3pt] y = \frac{( \sqrt{3} - \sqrt{2})^2}{1} = 5 - 2 \sqrt{6} \\\\[3pt] \therefore (x^2 + y^2) = (5 + 2 \sqrt{6})^2 + (5 - 2 \sqrt{6})^2 \\\\[3pt] = 2(25 + 24) = 98 \end{align} \)

Example 4:

Question:
If \( \sqrt{x} + \sqrt{x - \sqrt{1 - x}} = 1, \) then show that \( x = \frac{16}{25} \) .

Solution:
\( \sqrt{x - \sqrt{1 - x}} = 1 - \sqrt{x} \)
Squaring on both sides,
\( x - \sqrt{1 - x} = 1 + x - 2 \sqrt{x} \Rightarrow - \sqrt{1 - x} = 1 - 2 \sqrt{x} \)
Squaring on both sides, \( 1 - x = 1 + 4x - 4 \sqrt{x} \)
\( \Rightarrow 5x = 4 \sqrt{x} \Rightarrow \sqrt{x} = \frac{4}{5} \ or \ x = \frac{16}{25} \)

Example 5:

Question:
If \( x = \frac{1}{2} \left( \sqrt{a} + \frac{1}{ \sqrt{a}} \right), \) then show that \( \frac{ \sqrt{x^2 - 1}}{x - \sqrt{x^2 - 1}} = \frac{a - 1}{2} \)

Solution:
\( \begin{align} x = \frac{1}{2} \left( \sqrt{a} + \frac{1}{ \sqrt{a}} \right) \Rightarrow x = \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{a + 1}{ \sqrt{a}} \right) \Rightarrow x^2 - 1 = \frac{(a + 1)^2}{4 a} - 1 \\\\[3pt] = \frac{(a + 1)^2 - 4a}{4a} = \frac{(a - 1)^2}{4a} \\\\[3pt] \sqrt{x^2 - 1} = \sqrt{\frac{(a - 1)^2}{4a}} = \frac{1}{2} \frac{(a - 1)}{\sqrt{2}} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow x - \sqrt{x^2 - 1} = \frac{a + 1}{2 \sqrt{a}} - \frac{(a - 1)}{2 \sqrt{a}} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{2}{2 \sqrt{a}} = \frac{1}{ \sqrt{a}} \\\\[3pt] \therefore \frac{ \sqrt{x^2 - 1}}{x - \sqrt{x^2 - 1}} = \frac{ \frac{(a - 1)}{2 \sqrt{a}}}{ \frac{1}{ \sqrt{a}}} = \frac{a - 1}{2} \end{align} \)

Example 6:

Question:
If \( x = 3 + 3^{ \frac{1}{3}} + 3^{ \frac{2}{3}} \) then show that \( x^3 - 9x^2 + 18x - 12 = 0 \)

Solution:
\( \begin{align} x = 3 + 3^{ \frac{1}{3}} + 3^{ \frac{2}{3}} \\\\[3pt] x - 3 = 3^{ \frac{1}{3}} + 3^{ \frac{2}{3}} \end{align} \)
Cubing on both sides,
\( \begin{align} (x - 3)^3 = (3^{ \frac{1}{3}} + 3^{ \frac{2}{3}})^3 \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow x^3 - 9x^2 + 27 x - 27 = 12 + 3(3) (x - 3) \\\\[3pt] \left [ since, \ 3^{ \frac{1}{3} + 3^{ \frac{2}{3}}} = x - 3 \right] \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow x^3 - 9x^2 + 18x - 12 = 0 \end{align} \)

Example 7:

Question:
If \( x = (4 + \sqrt{15})^{ \frac{1}{3}} + (4 - \sqrt{15})^{ \frac{1}{3}} \) , then show that \( x^3 - 3x - 8 = 0 \)

Solution:
\( \begin{align} \Rightarrow x^3 = (4 + \sqrt{15}) + (4 - \sqrt{15}) + 3 (4 + \sqrt{15})^{ \frac{1}{3}} (4 - \sqrt{15})^{ \frac{1}{3}} \times \\\\[3pt] \left[ (4 + \sqrt{15})^{ \frac{1}{3}} + (4 - \sqrt{15})^{ \frac{1}{3}} \right] \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow x^3 = 8 + 3x \\\\[3pt] \therefore x^3 - 3x - 8 = 0 \end{align} \)

Example 8:

Question:
If \( \sqrt{x} - \sqrt{12} = \sqrt{4} - \sqrt{x}, \) then find x

Solution:
\( \begin{align} 2 \sqrt{x} = 2 + 2 \sqrt{3} \\\\[3pt] \sqrt{x} = 1 + \sqrt{3} \\\\[3pt] x = 4 + 2 \sqrt{3} \end{align} \)

Example 9

Question:
If \( a^{ \frac{1}{3}} + b^{ \frac{1}{3}} + c^{ \frac{1}{3}} = 0, \) then show that \( (a + b + c)^3 = 27 \ abc \)

Solution:
Given that \( a^{ \frac{1}{3}} + b^{ \frac{1}{3}} + c^{ \frac{1}{3}} = 0 \)
\( a^{ \frac{1}{3}} + b^{ \frac{1}{3}} = - c^{ \frac{1}{3}} \)
Cubing on both sides,
\( \begin{align} \left( a^{ \frac{1}{3}} + b^{ \frac{1}{3}} \right )^3 = \left( - c^{ \frac{1}{3}} \right)^3 \\\\[3pt] a + b + 3a^{ \frac{1}{3}} b^{ \frac{1}{3}} \left(a^{ \frac{1}{3}} + b^{ \frac{1}{3}} \right) = - c \\\\[3pt] (a + b + c) + 3 a^{ \frac{1}{3}} b^{ \frac{1}{3}} (- c^{ \frac{1}{3}}) = 0 \\\\[3pt] a + b + c = 3 a^{ \frac{1}{3}} b^{ \frac{1}{3}} c^{ \frac{1}{3}} \\\\[3pt] (a + b + c)^3 = 27 abc \end{align} \)

Example 10

Question:
Show that \( \frac{1}{ \sqrt{2} + \sqrt{3}} - \frac{2}{ \sqrt{5} - \sqrt{3}} + \frac{3}{ \sqrt{5} - \sqrt{2}} = 0 \)

Solution:
\( \begin{align} ( \sqrt{3} - \sqrt{2}) - \frac{2 ( \sqrt{5} + \sqrt{3})}{2} + \frac{3 ( \sqrt{5} + \sqrt{2})}{3} \\\\[3pt] = \sqrt{3} - \sqrt{2} - \sqrt{5} - \sqrt{3} + \sqrt{5} + \sqrt{2} = 0 \end{align} \)

Example 11

Question:
If \( \sqrt{15 - x \sqrt{14}} = \sqrt{8} - \sqrt{7}, \) then find the value of "x".

Solution:
\( \begin{align} 15 - x \sqrt{14} = 8 + 7 - 2 \sqrt{8 \times 7} \\\\[3pt] x \sqrt{14} = 4 \sqrt{14} \\\\[3pt] x = 4 \end{align} \)

Example 12

Question:
Prove that \( \left( \frac{2 + \sqrt{3}}{ \sqrt{2} + \sqrt{2 + \sqrt{3}}} + \frac{2 - \sqrt{3}}{ \sqrt{2} - \sqrt{2 - \sqrt{3}}} \right)^2 = 2 \)

Solution:
\( \begin{align} \sqrt{2 + \sqrt{3}} = \sqrt{ \frac{3}{2}} + \sqrt{ \frac{1}{2}} \\\\[3pt] \frac{2 + \sqrt{3}}{ \sqrt{2} + \sqrt{2 + \sqrt{3}}} = \frac{ \left( \sqrt{ \frac{3}{2} } + \sqrt{ \frac{1}{2}} \right)^2}{ \sqrt{2} + \left( \sqrt{ \frac{3}{2} } + \sqrt{ \frac{1}{2}} \right)} = = \frac{(1 + \sqrt{3})^2}{ \sqrt{2}(\sqrt{3} + 3)} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{(1 + \sqrt{3})^2}{ \sqrt{2} \sqrt{3}(1 + \sqrt{3})} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{1 + \sqrt{3}}{ \sqrt{6}} \end{align} \)
\( \begin{align} \frac{2 - \sqrt{3}}{ \sqrt{2} - \sqrt{2 - \sqrt{3}}} = \frac{ \left( \sqrt{ \frac{3}{2} } - \sqrt{ \frac{1}{2}} \right)^2}{ \sqrt{2} - \left( \sqrt{ \frac{3}{2} } - \sqrt{ \frac{1}{2}} \right)} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{(1 - \sqrt{3})^2 - \sqrt{2}}{2 (3 - \sqrt{3})} = \frac{ \sqrt{3} - 1}{ \sqrt{6}} \\\\[3pt] \therefore \left( \frac{2 + \sqrt{3}}{ \sqrt{2} + \sqrt{2 + \sqrt{3}}} + \frac{2 - \sqrt{3}}{ \sqrt{2} - \sqrt{2 - \sqrt{3}}} \right)^2 \\\\[3pt] = \left( \frac{1 + \sqrt{3}}{ \sqrt{6}} + \frac{ \sqrt{3} - 1}{ \sqrt{6}} \right)^2 = \left( \frac{2 \sqrt{3}}{ \sqrt{6}} \right)^2 \\\\[3pt] = \frac{ \not{4}^2 \times \not{3}}{ \not{6}_2} = 2 \end{align} \)

Example 13

Question:
solve: \( \sqrt{9 + 2x} - \sqrt{2x} = \frac{5}{ \sqrt{9 + 2x}} \)

Solution:
\( \begin{align} 9 + 2x - \sqrt{2x (9 + 2x)} = 5 \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow 4 + 2x = \sqrt{2x (9 + 2x)} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow (4 + 2x)^2 = 2x (9 + 2x) \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow 16 + 4x^2 + 16 x = 18 x + 4x^2 \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow 16 = 2x \\\\[3pt] x = 8 \end{align} \)

Example 14

Question:
Let \( \frac{A}{a} = \frac{B}{b} = \frac{C}{c} = \frac{D}{d} \)
Prove that \( \sqrt{Aa} + \sqrt{Bb} + \sqrt{Cc} + \sqrt{Dd} = \sqrt{(a + b + c + d) (A + B + C + D)} \)

Solution:
\( \begin{align} \frac{A}{a} = \frac{B}{b} = \frac{C}{c} = \frac{D}{d} = k \\\\[3pt] A = ak, \ B = bk, \ C = ck, \ D = dk \\\\[3pt] A + B + C + D = k(a + b + c + d) \Rightarrow k = \frac{A + B + C + D}{a + b + c + d} \\\\[3pt] or \ \sqrt{k} = \frac{ \sqrt{A + B + C + D}}{ \sqrt{a + b + c + d}} \\\\[3pt] \sqrt{Aa} + \sqrt{Bb} + \sqrt{Cc} + \sqrt{Dd} = \sqrt{k} (a + b + c + d) \\\\[3pt] \sqrt{Aa} + \sqrt{Bb} + \sqrt{Cc} + \sqrt{Dd} = \sqrt{A + B + C + D} \times \sqrt{a + b + c + d} \\\\[3pt] i.e, = \sqrt{(A + B + C + D) (a + b + c + d)} \end{align} \)

Example 15

Question:
Show that \( \sqrt[3]{ax^2 + by^2 + cz^2} = \sqrt[3]{a} + \sqrt[3]{b} + \sqrt[3]{c} \)
if \( ax^3 = by^3 = cz^3 \) and \( \frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{y} + \frac{1}{z} = 1 \)

Solution:
\( \begin{align} \sqrt[3]{ax^2 + by^2 + cz^2} = \sqrt[3]{ \frac{ax^3}{x} + \frac{by^3}{y} + \frac{cz^3}{z}} = \sqrt[3]{ax^3 \left( \frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{y} + \frac{1}{z} \right)} \\\\[3pt] = x^3 \sqrt{a}........(1) \\\\[3pt] \sqrt[3]{ax^2 + by^2 + cz^2} = \sqrt[3]{ \frac{ax^3}{x} + \frac{by^3}{y} + \frac{cz^3}{z}} = \sqrt[3]{ax^3 \left( \frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{y} + \frac{1}{z} \right)} \\\\[3pt] = y^3 \sqrt{b}........(2) \\\\[3pt] \sqrt[3]{ax^2 + by^2 + cz^2} = \sqrt[3]{ \frac{ax^3}{x} + \frac{by^3}{y} + \frac{cz^3}{z}} = \sqrt[3]{ax^3 \left( \frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{y} + \frac{1}{z} \right)} \\\\[3pt] = z^3 \sqrt{c}........(3) \end{align} \)
Adding equations (1), (2), (3),
\( \begin{align} \frac{ \sqrt[3]{ax^2 + by^2 + cz^2}}{x} + \frac{ \sqrt[3]{ax^2 + by^2 + cz^2}}{y} + \frac{ \sqrt[3]{ax^2 + by^2 + cz^2}}{z} \\\\[3pt] = \sqrt[3]{a} + \sqrt[3]{b} + \sqrt[3]{c} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow \sqrt[3]{ax^2 + by^2 + cz^2} \left[ \frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{y} + \frac{1}{z} \right] = \sqrt[3]{a} + \sqrt[3]{b} + \sqrt[3]{c} \\\\[3pt] \therefore \sqrt[3]{ax^2 + by^2 + cz^2} = \sqrt[3]{a} + \sqrt[3]{b} + \sqrt[3]{c} \end{align} \)

Example 16

Question:
Prove that for \( x \geq; 1 \)
\( \sqrt{ x + 2 \sqrt{x - 1}} + \sqrt{x - 2 \sqrt{x - 1}} \) is equal to if \( x \leq 2 \) , and is equal to \( 2 \sqrt{x - 1} \) if x > 2.

Solution:
We have
\( \sqrt{A + \sqrt{B}} + \sqrt{A - \sqrt{B}} = 2 \sqrt{ \frac{A + \sqrt{A^2 - B}}{2}} \)
Here, A = x, B = 4x - 4, \( A^2 - B = x^2 - 4x + 4 \)
\( \begin{align} \sqrt{A^2 - B} = \sqrt{(x - 2)^2} = \left\lbrace \begin{array}{ccc} x - 2 & if & x > 2 \\\\ 2 - x & if & x < 2 \end{array}\right. \end{align} \)
If \( x > 2 \) then
\( \sqrt{x + 2 \sqrt{x - 1}} + \sqrt{x - 2 \sqrt{x - 1}} = 2 \sqrt{ \frac{x + x - 2}{2}} = 2 \sqrt{x - 1} \)
If \( x \leq 2 \) then
\( \sqrt{x + 2 \sqrt{x - 1}} + \sqrt{x - 2 \sqrt{x - 1}} = 2 \sqrt{ \frac{x + 2 - x}{2}} = 2 \)

Example 17

Question:
Find the value of \( \sqrt{ \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{ \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{ \frac{1}{2}..... \infty}}} \)

Solution:
Let \( x = \sqrt{ \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{ \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{ \frac{1}{2}..... \infty}}} \)
\( \begin{align} x = \sqrt{ \frac{1}{2} x} \\\\[3pt] x^2 = \frac{x}{2} \Rightarrow x^2 - \frac{x}{2} = 0 \Rightarrow x \left(x - \frac{1}{2} \right) = 0 \Rightarrow x = 0, \ \frac{1}{2} \\\\[3pt] But \ x \neq 0 \ \therefore x = \frac{1}{2} \end{align} \)

Example 18

Question:
Find the value of \( \sqrt{12 + \sqrt{12 + \sqrt{12 + ...... \infty}}} \)

Solution:
Let,
\( \begin{align} x = \sqrt{12 + \sqrt{12 + \sqrt{12 + ...... \infty}}} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow x = \sqrt{12 + x} \Rightarrow x^2 = 12 + x \\\\[3pt] x^2 - x - 12 = 0 \Rightarrow (x - 4) (x + 3) = 0 \\\\[3pt] x = 4, \ - 3 \\\\[3pt] \therefore \sqrt{12 + \sqrt{12 + ...... \infty}} = 4 \ only \end{align} \) .

Example 19

Question:
Find the square root of \( 8 + 2 \sqrt{15} \)

Solution:
\( \begin{align} 8 + 2 \sqrt{15} = 5 + 3 + 2 \sqrt{5 \times 3} \\\\[3pt] = ( \sqrt{5})^2 + ( \sqrt{3})^2 + 2 \sqrt{5} \sqrt{3} \\\\[3pt] = ( \sqrt{5} + \sqrt{3})^2 \\\\[3pt] \therefore \sqrt{8 + 2 \sqrt{15}} = \sqrt{5} + \sqrt{3} \end{align} \)

Example 20

Question:
Find the square root of \( \sqrt{50} + \sqrt{48} \)

Solution:
\( \begin{align} \sqrt{50} + \sqrt{48} = 5 \sqrt{2} + 4 \sqrt{3} \\\\[3pt] = \sqrt{2} \left [ 5 + 2 \sqrt{2} \times \sqrt{3} \right ] = \sqrt{2} [3 + 2 + 2 \sqrt{3} \times \sqrt{2}] \\\\[3pt] = \sqrt{2} ( \sqrt{3} + \sqrt{2})^2 \\\\[3pt] \therefore \sqrt{50 + \sqrt{48}} = 2^{ \frac{1}{4}} ( \sqrt{3} + \sqrt{2}) \end{align} \)

Example 21

Question:
Find fourth root of \( 124 - 32 \sqrt{15} \)

Solution:

Example 27

Question:
If \( x = \frac{ \sqrt{a + 2b} + \sqrt{a - 2b}}{ \sqrt{a + 2b} - \sqrt{a - 2b}} \) , then show that \( bx^2 - ax + b = 0 \) .

Solution:
\( \begin{align} x = \frac{ \sqrt{a + 2b} + \sqrt{a - 2b}}{ \sqrt{a + 2b} - \sqrt{a - 2b}} \times \frac{ \sqrt{a + 2b} + \sqrt{a - 2b}}{ \sqrt{a + 2b} + \sqrt{a - 2b}} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{( \sqrt{a + 2b} + \sqrt{a - 2b})^2}{(a + 2b) - (a - 2b)} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{a + 2b + a - 2b + 2 \sqrt{(a + 2b) (a - 2b)}}{4b} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{2(a + \sqrt{a^2 - 4b^2})}{2 \times 2b} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow 2bx = a + \sqrt{a^2 - 4b^2} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow 2bx - a = \sqrt{a^2 - 4b^2} \end{align} \)
On squaring both sides, we get
\( \begin{align} 4b^2 x^2 + a^2 - 4abx = a^2 - 4b^2 \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow 4b^2 x^2 - 4 abx + 4b^2 = 0 \end{align} \)
Dividing by 4b, we, get, \( bx^2 - ax + b = 0 \)
Hence the result.

Example 28

Question:
If \( a = \frac{ \sqrt{5} + 1}{ \sqrt{5} - 1}, \) and \( b = \frac{ \sqrt{5} - 1}{ \sqrt{5} + 1} \) , then find the value of \( \frac{a^2 + ab + b^2}{a^2 - ab + b^2} \) :

Solution:
\( \begin{align} a + b = \frac{ \sqrt{5} + 1}{ \sqrt{5} - 1} + \frac{ \sqrt{5} - 1}{ \sqrt{5} + 1} \\\\[3pt] a + b = \frac{( \sqrt{5} + 1)^2 + ( \sqrt{5} - 1)^2}{( \sqrt{5} - 1)( \sqrt{5} + 1)} = \frac{5 + 1 + 2 \sqrt{5} + 5 + 1 - 2 \sqrt{5}}{5 - 1} = \frac{12}{4} = 3 \\\\[3pt] ab = \frac{ \sqrt{5} + 1}{ \sqrt{5} - 1} \times \frac{ \sqrt{5} - 1}{ \sqrt{5} + 1} = 1 \\\\[3pt] \therefore a^2 + b^2 + ab = (a + b)^2 - 2ab + ab = (3)^2 - 1 = 8 .........(1) \end{align} \)
and \( a^2 + b^2 - ab = (a + b)^2 - 2ab - ab = 9 - 3 = 6.........(2) \)
Dividing (1) by (2), we get
\( \frac{a^2 + ab + b^2}{a^2 - ab + b^2} = \frac{8}{6} = \frac{4}{3} \)

Example 29

Example 30

Question:
Show that \( \sqrt{2} \) is not a rational number.

Solution:
Let us assume that \( \sqrt{2} \) is a rational number, then
\( \sqrt{2} = \frac{p}{q} \Rightarrow 2q^2 = p^2 \) \( \left [ \frac{p}{q} \ \text{ is in simplest form} \right ] \)
\( p^2 \) is even \Rightarrow p is even \Rightarrow p = 2k
\( 2q^2 = 4k^2 \Rightarrow q^2 = 2k^2 \Rightarrow q^2 \) is even
\Rightarrow q is even.
\Rightarrow p, q have 2 as a common factor which is contradiction to assumption.
\( \therefore \sqrt{2} = \frac{p}{q} \) is a false \( \Rightarrow \ \sqrt{2} \) is not a rational.
