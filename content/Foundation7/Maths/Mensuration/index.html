Circles

A circle is a plane figure bounded by one line (ABC) such that the distance of this line from a fixed point within it (point O), remains constant throughout.
That is OB = OC = OA = constant.
This line is known as the circumference of a circle (C). The fixed point (O) is the centre of a circle.
The constant distance from the centre to the circumference is known as the radius of the circle. (OA = OB = OC = radius) (r)

Any line through the centre and terminaled both ways by the circumference is called the diameter of a circle (AC = diameter)

Circumference and area

Circumference \( \rightarrow \) C \( \rightarrow \) the perimeter of the circle.
Area \( \rightarrow \) A \( \rightarrow \) the space enclosed by the circle.

Measurement for Circumference (C) Area(A)
OB = radius = r
AD diameter = d
d = 2r
length ABDA = Circumference = C
Formula
\( C = 2 \pi r = \pi d \)
\( A = \pi r^2 \)
Correlation Formula \( A = \frac{C^2}{4 \pi} \)

Perimeters and Areas of Composite Plane Figures

There are many shapes around us that are formed by joining two or more basic geometric shapes. Their perimeters and areas can be found by applying the formulae fot the basic shapes.

Solved examples

Example 1:

What is the side of that equilateral triangle whose area costs as much to pave at Rs. 10 per square metre as it would cost to fence the three sides at Rs. 25 a metre?

Solution:
Assume that the side of equilateral triangle is a metre
\( Area(A) = \frac{ \sqrt{3}}{4} a^2 m^2 \)
\( \rightarrow \) Cost of paving = \( 10 \times \frac{ \sqrt{3}}{4} a^2 \) rupees
Similarly, perimeter (P) = 3a m
\( \Rightarrow \) Cost of fencing = \( 25 \times 3a \) rupees
According to the equation.
\( 10 \times \frac{ \sqrt{3}}{4} a^2 = 25 \times 3a \) \( \Rightarrow a = 10 \sqrt{3} m \)
\( \Rightarrow \) a = 17.32 m
Hence, the side of the equilateral triangle is 17.32 cm

Example 2:

The perimeter of an isosceles triangle is 306 in, and each of the equal side is of the \( \frac{5}{8} \) of the base. Find the area.

Solution:
Let the equal side of the isosceles triangle = a the base of the isosceles triangle = b
then \( a = \frac{5}{8} \)
Now, perimeter of isosceles triangle = 2a + b
\( \Rightarrow 308 = 2 \times \frac{5}{8} b + b \)
\( \Rightarrow b = 136 \)
then \( a = \frac{5}{8} \times 136 = 85 \)
Using the formula
Area of isosceles triangle = \( \frac{b}{4} \sqrt{4a^2 - b^2} \)
\( \Rightarrow A = \frac{136}{4} \sqrt{4 \times (85)^2 - (136)^2} m^2 \)
\( = 34 \times 102 m^2 \)
\( = 3468 \ m^2 \)
Hence, the area of the isosceles triangle is \( 3468 \ m^2 \)

Example 3:

Two sides of a triangle are 85 m and 154 m, and the perimeter is 324 m. Find the area of the triangle.

Solution:
Assuming the sides of the triangle as a, b and c
a + b + c = perimeter of triangle = 324
\( \Rightarrow \) 85 + 154 + c = 324
\( \Rightarrow \) c = 85
Here, a = c = 85, b = 154, so, it is an isosceles triangle.
Using the formula, Area \( = \frac{b}{4} \sqrt{4a^2 - b^2} \)
\( \Rightarrow A = \frac{154}{4} \sqrt{4 \times (85)^2 - (154)^2} m^2 \)
\( \Rightarrow A = 2772 \ m^2 \)
Hence, the area of the given triangle is \( 2772 \ m^2 \)

Example 15:

A rectangular grass plot 80 m \( \times \) 60 m has two roads, each 10 m wide, running in the middle of it, one parallel to length and the other parallel to breadth. Find the cost of gravelling the roads at Rs. \( 2 \ per \ m^2 \)

Solution:
Let ABCD be a rectangular grass plot
whose length = l = 80 m
breadth = b = 60 m
Two roads of width W = 10 m (shaded part) are crossing each other at the middle of plot.
Area of roads = W (l + b - W)
\( = 10(80 + 60 - 10) m^2 \)
\( = 1300 m^2 \)
Cost of gravelling the roads
= rate of gravelling/ \( m^2 \times \) area of roads
= Rs. \( 2 \times 1300 \)
= Rs. 2600
Hence, the cost of one gravelling the roads is Rs. 2600.
