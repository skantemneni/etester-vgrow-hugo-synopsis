Synopsis

Simple Interest

  1. \( I = \frac{PTR}{100} \)
    I = simple interest, P = principle, T = time
    R = rate percent per annum

  2. Amount(A) = Principle + Interest
    \( = P + \frac{PTR}{100} = P \left ( 1 + \frac{TR}{100} \right ) \)

  3. R \( \times \) T = 100(N - 1)
    R = rate percent, T = time
    N = number of times the sum gets multiplied (i.e., doubled, trebled ....etc.)
    E.g.: A sum of money at SI doubles itself in 4 years. Find the rate percent per annum.
    \( R \times T = 100(N - 1), R \times 4 = 100(2 - 1) \)
    \( R = \frac{100}{4} = 25 \% \)

  4. SI is calculated uniformly on the original principle through out the time period.

  5. If a certain sum of money becomes \( N_1 \) times at a rate \( R_1 \) in \( T_1 \) years and becomes \( N_2 \) times at a rate \( R_2 \) in \( T_2 \) years, then
    \( \frac{R_1 \times T_1}{R_2 \times T_2} = \frac{N_1 - 1}{N_2 - 1} \)

  6. If the value of P and R are fixed, time (T) is changed from \( T_1 \) to \( T_2 \) and amount (A) is changed from \( A_1 \) to \( A_2 \) , then \( \frac{A_1}{A_2} = \frac{100 + RT_1}{100 + RT_2} \)

  7. If the value of P and T are fixed, rate (R) is changed from \( R_1 \) to \( R_2 \) and amount (A) is changed from \( A_1 \) to \( A_2 \) , then \( \frac{A_1}{A_2} = \frac{100 + R_1T}{100 + R_2T} \)

  8. If the value of P is fixed, time (T) is changed from \( T_1 \) to \( T_2 \) , rate (R) is changed from \( R_1 \) to \( R_2 \) and amount (A) is changed from \( A_1 \) to \( A_2 \) , then \( \frac{A_1}{A_2} = \frac{100 + R_1T_1}{100 + R_2T_2} \) and \( A_1 - A_2 = \frac{P}{100} [R_1T_1 - R_2T_2] \)

  9. A man wants to repay a debt of Rs.M in 'n' number of installments and if the value of each instalment is Rs. a, then
    \( M = na + \frac{ra}{100 \times y} \times \frac{n(n - 1)}{2} \)
    r = rate percent and
    y = number of installments per year, i.e., if y = 1, then each instalment is paid annually and
    y = 2, then if each instalment is paid half-yearly.

Solved examples

Example 1:

If principal is Rs. 250 and rate of interest is 4% per annum, find the simple interest for 6 years.

Solution:
P = Rs. 250
R = 4% per annum
T = 6 years
\( \begin{align} Simple \ Interest \ (I) = \frac{P \times T \times R}{100} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{250 \times 6 \times 4}{100} \end{align} \)
= Rs. 60

Example 2:

Calculate the simple interest and the amount on Rs. 1000 for 2 years at 10% per annum.

Solution:
P = Rs. 1000
T = 2 years
R = 10% per annum
Now,
\( \begin{align} I = \frac{P \times T \times R}{100} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{1000 \times 2 \times 10}{100} \end{align} \)
= Rs. 200
Amount = Principal + Simple interest
or A = P + I
= Rs. 1000 + Rs. 200
= Rs. 1200

Example 3:

A man borrowed Rs. 8000 from a bank at 8% per annum. Find the amount he has to pay after \( 4 \frac{1}{2} \) years ?

Solution:
P = Rs. 8000
\( T = 4 \frac{1}{2} \ years = \frac{9}{2} \ years \)
R = 8% per annum
Now,
\( \begin{align} I = \frac{P \times T \times R}{100} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{8000 \times 9 \times 8}{2 \times 100} \end{align} \)
I = Rs. 2880
Now, amount = Principal + S.I.
= Rs. 8000 + Rs. 2880
= Rs. 10880
\( \therefore \) He has to pay Rs.10880 after \( 4 \frac{1}{2} \) years.

Example 4:

Neena borrowed Rs. 900 from her friend at 10% per annum. She returned the amount after six months. How much amount did she pay ?

Solution:
P = Rs. 900
T = 6 months = \( \frac{6}{12} = \frac{1}{2} \) year
R = 10% per annum
Now,
\( \begin{align} I = \frac{P \times T \times R}{100} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{900 \times 1 \times 10}{2 \times 100} \end{align} \)
= Rs. 45
Also, A = P + S.I.
= Rs. 900 + Rs. 45
= Rs. 945
Total amount paid by Neena is Rs. 945.

Example 5:

A person deposits Rs. 25000 in a firm who pays an interest at the rate of 20% per annum. Calculate the income he gets, from it annually.

Solution:
P = Rs. 25,000
R = 20% per annum
T = 1 year
Now,
\( \begin{align} I = \frac{P \times T \times R}{100} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{25000 \times 1 \times 20}{100} \end{align} \)
= Rs. 5000
\( \therefore \) His annual income is Rs. 5000.

Example 6:

Find the interest on Rs. 2400 at 6% per annum for 146 days.

Solution:
P = Rs. 2400
R = 6% per annum
T = 146 days = \( \frac{146}{365} \) years
Now,
\( \begin{align} I = \frac{P \times T \times R}{100} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{2400 \times 146 \times 6}{365 \times 100} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{288}{5} \end{align} \)
= Rs. 57.60
Interest Rs. 57.60

Example 7:

Calculate the simple interest on Rs. 7200 at \( 12 \frac{3}{4} \) % per annum for 9 months.

Solution:
\( SI = \frac{P \times T \times R}{100} = \frac{7200 \times 51 \times 9}{4 \times 12 \times 100} \ \ \ \ \left ( R = \frac{51}{4} \%; \ T = \frac{9}{12} \ years \right ) \)
= Rs. 688.50

Example 8:

At what rate per cent per annum will a sum of money double in 8 years ?

Solution:
Let P = x, then A = 2x SI = (2x - x) = x, T = 8 years
\( R = \frac{SI \times 100}{P \times T} = \frac{x \times 100}{x \times 8} = 12.5 \% \)

Example 9:

The simple interest on a sum of money is 25% of the principal and the rate per annum is equal to number of years. Find the rate per cent.

Solution:
Let the principal be Rs. x then S.I. = \( Rs. \frac{x}{4} \)
T = r, if R = rate per annum
\( \therefore \frac{x}{4} = \frac{x \times R \times R}{100}, \ R^2 = 25 \ or \ R = 5 \% \)

Example 10:

A certain sum of money amounts to Rs. 1560 in 2 years and Rs. 2100 in 5 years. Find the rate percentage per annum.

Solution:
Amount (Principal + Interest) for 2 years = Rs. 1560
Amount (Principal + Interest) for 5 years = Rs. 2100
Hence, interest for 3 years = (2100 - 1560) = Rs. 540
Simple interest for 2 years = Rs. 360
\( \therefore \) Principal = (1560 - 360) = Rs. 1200
\( R = \frac{360 \times 100}{1200 \times 2} = 15 \% \)

Example 11:

A certain sum of money amounts to Rs. 1008 in 2 years and to Rs. 1164 in \( 3 \frac{1}{2} \) years. Find the sum and the rate of interest.

Solution:
SI for \( \left ( 3 \frac{1}{2} - 2 \right ) \) i.e., \( 1 \frac{1}{2} \) years = Rs. (1164 - 1008) = Rs. 156
SI for 2 years = Rs. 208
\( \therefore \) Principal = Rs. (1008 - 208) = Rs. 800
Now, P = 800, T = 2 years and SI = Rs. 208
\( \therefore Rate = \frac{100 \times SI}{P \times T} = \frac{100 \times 208}{800 \times 2} = 13 \% \)

Example 12:

A man borrowed Rs. 24000 from two money lenders. For one loan he paid 15% annum and for the other 18% per annum. At the end of one year, he paid simple interest of Rs. 4050. How much did he borrow at each rate?

Solution:
Let the sum borrowed at 15% be Rs. x then sum borrowed at 18% will be Rs. (24000 - x).
\( therefore, \frac{x \times 15 \times 1}{100} + \frac{(24000 - x) \times 18 \times 1}{100} = 4050 \)
or, 15x + 432000 - 18x = 405000 or x = 9000
\( \therefore \) and money borrowed at 18% = Rs.(24000 - 9000) = Rs. 15000

Example 13:

Rs. 800 amounts to Rs. 920 in 3 years at simple interest. If the interest is increased by 3%, it would amount to how much ?

Solution:
SI = (920 - 800) = Rs. 120
\( 120 = \frac{800 \times R \times 3}{100} \ \ \therefore R = 5\% \)
Interest at 8% interest = \( \frac{800 \times 8 \times 3}{100} \) = Rs. 192
\( \therefore \) Amount = (800 + 192) = Rs. 992

Example 14:

What annual instalment will discharge a debt of Rs. 1092 due in 3 years at 12% simple interest?

Solution:
Let each instalment be Rs. x, then first instalment paid after 1 year will earn a interest for 2 years at 12% and second instalment paid after 2 year will earn an interest for 1 year at the same rate as the debt has to be free off in 3 years.
\( \begin{align} \therefore \left [ x + \frac{x \times 12 \times 1}{100} \right ] + \left [ x + \frac{x \times 12 \times 2}{100} \right ] + x = Rs. 1092 \\\\[3pt] \frac{28x}{25} + \frac{31x}{25} + x = 1092 \end{align} \)
or 28x + 31x + 25x = 10921 \( \times \) 25
\( \therefore \) x = Rs. 325

Example 15:

A sum was put at simple interest at a certain rate for 2 years. Had it been put 3% higher rate, it would have fetched Rs. 72 more. Calculate the sum.

Solution:
Let the sum be Rs. x and rate by r
\( then, \ \frac{x \times (r + 3) \times 2}{100} - \frac{x \times r \times 2}{100} = 72 \)
or, 2rx + 6x - 2rx = 7200 \( \Rightarrow \) x = Rs. 1200

Example 16:

The rate of interest on a sum of money is 4% per annum for the first 2 years, 6% per annum for the next 3 years and 8% per annum for the period beyond 5 years. If the simple interest accrued by the sum for a total period of 8 years is Rs. 1280. What is the sum ?

Solution:
Rate of interest for first 2 years = 4 \( \times \) 2 = 8%
Rate of interest for next 3 years = 6 \( \times \) 3 = 18%
Rate of interest for last 3 years = 8 \( \times \) 3 = 24%
Total rate of interest for 8 years = 50%
then \( 1280 = \frac{P \times 50}{100} \ \ \therefore P = Rs. 2560 \)
[then of 8 years is already adjusted in the total rate of interest calculation].
