Geometric Solids

  1. Geometric solids are three-dimensional figures that have length, breadth and height.

  2. Every geometric soild has a fixed number of edges, vertices and face.

  3. the face of a solid is a flat or curved surface that is two-dimensional in shape

  4. Every soild has flat surfaces or curved surfaces or both types of surfaces (flat and curved).

  5. A soild with all its surface flat is a polyhedron. Each surface of a polyhedron is called a face of the soid.

  6. If a soild has a curved surface, it has no faces.

  7. An edge from when two faces meet.

  8. The vertex is the point where three or more edges meet.

Geometric properties

Prisms

A prisms is a soild with

  1. two parallel opposite faces in the shape of congruent polygons. These congruent polygons are called bases of the prism.

  2. all other faces in the shape of parallelograms are called lateral face.

    1. the perpendicular distance between the two bases is the height of the prism.

    2. A right prism is a prism in which the bases of the prism are perpendicular to the lateral faces.

    3. the cross section of a prism is the section that is parallel to its base. It is the same shape and as its base.

    4. Prisms are named after the shape of their bases.

Cuboid

A cuboid is a solid with six rectangular faces. Opposite faces have the same shapes and sizes.

Cube

A cuboid is a special type of cuboid with six square faces of the same size. All the edges are equal in length.

Pyramids

A pyramid is a soild with

  1. A polygon as its base, and

  2. lateral triangular faces

    1. The lateral triangular faces meet at a common vertex.

    2. The perpendicular distance from the common vertex to the base is the height of the pyramid.

    3. A right pyramid is a pyramid in which its vertex is vertically above the centre of the base.

    4. Pyramids are named after the shape of their bases.

Cylinders

A cylinder is a soild with

  1. two parallel opposite faces, called bases, in the shape of congruent circles at both its ends.

  2. a curved surface.

  3. an axis that joins the centers of two bases at both ends

    1. The perpendicular distance between the two bases is the height of the cylinder.

    2. A right cylinder is a cylinder in which the axis is perpendicular to the bases.

Cones

A cone is a soild with

  1. a base which is a circle.

  2. a curved surface which meets at a common vertex.

  3. an axis that joins the vertex to the centre of the circular base.

    1. The length from the vertex to the base is the slant height of the cone

    2. The perpendicular distance from the vertex to the base is the height of the cone.

    3. A right cone is a cone in which the vertex is vertically above the centre of the base.

Cones

A sphere is a solid in which all points on its curved surface are equidistant from a fixed point which is the center of the sphere.

The fixed distance from the center to the surface is its radius.

Nets

A net is a flat diagram on one plane that is folded to make a hollow soild. Alternatively if a hollow solid is opened out, the result is its net.

  1. A nnet is also known as a layout.

  2. A net of a solid may have different shapes.
