Important Facts and Formulae

Concept of percentage

The word percentage means per hundred. By certain percent, we mean that many hundredths. Thus, x percent means x hundredths, written as x%. Information conveyed in percentage does not give the exact but gives an approximation.
To express x% as a fraction: We have, x% = \( \frac{x}{100}. \)
Thus, \( 20 \% = \frac{20}{100} = \frac{1}{5}; \ 48 \% = \frac{48}{100} = \frac{12}{25} \) , etc.
To express \( \frac{a}{b} \) as a percent: We have, \( \frac{a}{b} = \left( \frac{a}{b} \times 100 \right) \% \)
Thus, \( \frac{1}{4} = \left( \frac{1}{4} \times 100 \% \right) = 25 \% ; \ 0.6 = \frac{6}{10} = \frac{3}{5} = \left( \frac{3}{5} \times 1000 \right) \% = 60 \% \)

If the price of a commodity increases by R%, then the reduction in consumption so as not to increase the expenditure is \( \left[ \frac{R}{(100 + R)} \times 100 \% \right] \% \)

If the price of a commodity decreases by R%, then the increase in consumption so as not to decrease the expenditure is \( \left[ \frac{R}{(100 - R)} \times 100 \% \right] \% \)

Results on population

Let the population of town be P now and suppose it increases at the rate of R% per annum, then:

  1. Population after n years = \( P \left(1 + \frac{R}{100} \right)^n \)

  2. Population n years ago = \( \frac{P}{ \left( 1 + \frac{R}{100} \right)^n} \)

Let the present value of a machine be P. Suppose it depreciates at the rate of R% per annum. Then:

  1. Value of the machine after n years = \( P \left(1 - \frac{R}{100} \right)^n \)

  2. Value of the machine n years ago = \( \frac{P}{ \left(1 - \frac{R}{100} \right)^n} \)

If A is R% more than B, then B is less than A by \( \left[ \frac{R}{(100 + R)} \times 100 \right] \% \)
If A is R% less than B, then B is more than A by \( \left[ \frac{R}{(100 - R)} \times 100 \right] \% \)

Percentage increase/Percentage decrease

Increase or decrease is always in absolute terms whereas percentage increase/decrease is expressed in percentage terms. Percentage increase/decrease is calculated with respect to the base value unless mentioned otherwise
Percentage increase/decrese = \( \frac{Increase/Decrease}{Base \ value} \times 100 \)

  1. If a quantity is increased by r% then final quantity (after increase) is obtained by multiplying the original quantity by \( \left( \frac{100 + r}{100} \right) \)

  2. Likewise, if quantity is decreased by r%, the final quantity (after decrease) is obtained by multiplying the original qunatity by \( \left( \frac{100 - r}{100} \right) \)

Successive increase/decrease

The successive application of the percentage to a given (original) value. All successive change in % (increase or decrease) can be represented as a single percentage. which is given by \( \left[ a + b + \frac{ab}{100} \right ] \% \) , where and a and b show the first and second percentage changes.

Solved examples

Example 1:

Charan has a saving account with an interest rate of 5% p.a. and a fixed deposit scheme with an interest rate of 10.5% p.a. in Central Bank. After he deposite Rs. 17,000 in both, he received a total interest of Rs. 1,510, after one year. How much money is deposited in the fixed deposit account?

Solution:
Let the amount deposited by Charan in fixed deposit be Rs. x.
So money in savings account = (17,000 - x)
Interest got from fixed deposit = 10.5% of x = \( \frac{10.5}{100} \times x \)
Interest got from savings account = 5% of (17,000 - x) = \( \frac{5}{100} (17, 000 - x) \)
Total interest received = Rs. 1,510
\( \begin{align} i.e., \frac{10.5 x}{100} + \frac{5(17000 - x)}{100} = 1,510 \\\\[3pt] \frac{10.5x + 85000 - 5x}{100} = 1,510 \\\\[3pt] 5.5x + 85,000 = 1,510 \times 100 \\\\[3pt] 5.5x = 1,51,000 - 85,000 = 66,000 \\\\[3pt] x = \frac{66000 \times 10}{55 \times 10} = Rs. 12,000 \end{align} \)

Example 2:

In a certain office 80% of the workers prefer tea and 40% prefer coffee. If each of them prefers tea or coffee and 80 like both. Find the total number of workers in the office.

Solution:
Since each of the workers prefers tea or coffee.
Then number of workers who prefer both (considering the total strength to be 100) = 20
(80 + 40 = 120 \( \therefore \) 120 - 100 = 20)
Now, if 20 workers prefer both then total strength = 100
if 80 workers prefer both then total strength = \( \frac{100}{20} \times 80 = 400 \)

Example 3:

An inspector rejects 0.08% of the metres as defective. How many will be examine to reject 2?

Solution:
Let the number of metres to be examined be x.
Then, 0.08% of x = \( 2 \Leftrightarrow \left( \frac{8}{100} \times \frac{1}{100} \times x \right)= 2 \Leftrightarrow x = \left( \frac{2 \times 100 \times 100}{8} \right) = 2500 \)

Example 4:

Ravi's health insurance premium for last year was Rs. 1,440. If he paid Rs. 1,512 this year, what is the percent increase on his health insurance premium?

Solution:
Premium paid last year = Rs. 1,440.
Premium for this year = Rs. 1,512
Increase in premium = 1512 - 1440 = Rs. 72
Percent of increase = \( \frac{72}{1440} \times 100 = 5 \% \)

Example 5:

The price of a Maruti car rises by 30%, while the sales of the car goes down by 20%. What is the percentage change in the total revenue?

Solution:
Revenue = Price \( \times \) Sale
Hence percentage change in revenue = \( \left(30 - 20 - \frac{30 \times 20}{100} \right) \% \) = 4%
Therefore, revenue will increase by 4%

Example 6:

Sixty five percent of a number is 21 less than four fifth of that number. What is the number?

Solution:
Let the number be x.
Then, \( \frac{4}{5} x - (65 \% of x) = 21 \Leftrightarrow \frac{4}{5} x - \frac{65}{100} x = 21 \Leftrightarrow 15x = 2100 \Leftrightarrow x = 140 \)

Example 7:

From a man's salary, 10% is deducted on tax, 20% of the rest is spent on education and 25% of the rest is spent on food. After all these expenditures, he is left with Rs. 2,700. Find his salary.

Solution:
Let the salary of the man be Rs. 100 Then,
after all the expenses he is left with
\( 100 \times 0.9 \times 0.8 \times 0.75 \) = Rs. 54
If he is left withRs. 54 his salary = Rs. 100
It he is left with Rs. 2,700, his salary would be = \( \frac{100}{54} \times 2700 = Rs. 5000 \)

Example 8:

Original price of a sofa is Rs. 7,000. It is now on sale for Rs. 5,500. What was the percent of decrease?

Solution:
Original price of sofa = Rs. 7,000. Selling price = Rs. 5,500
Decrease in price = 7,000 - 5,500 = Rs. 1,500
Percent of decrease \( \frac{1500}{7000} \times 100 = \frac{150}{7} \) = 21.4%

Example 9:

A salesman's commission is 5% on all sales upto Rs. 10,000 and 4% on all sales exceeding this. He remits Rs. 31,100 to his parent company after deducting his commission. Find the total sales.

Solution:
Let his total sales be Rs. x. Now,(Total Sales) - (Commission) = Rs. 31,100
\( \therefore \) x - [5% of 10000 + 4% of (x - 10000)] = 31100 \( \Leftrightarrow x - \left[\frac{5}{100} \times 10000 + \frac{4}{100}(x - 10000) \right] = 31100 \Leftrightarrow x - 500 - \frac{(x - 10000)}{25} \) = 31100 \( \Leftrightarrow x - \frac{x}{25} = 31200 \Leftrightarrow \frac{24x}{25} \) = 31200 \( \Leftrightarrow x = \left(\frac{31200 \times 25}{24} \right) \) = 32500
\( \therefore \) Total sales = Rs. 32,500

Example 10:

After spending 82% of his monthly income, Ramana has Rs. 423 left with him. Find his monthly income.

Solution:
Let his monthly income be 100.
He spent = 82
Money left = 100 - 82 = 18
18% of x = 423
\( \frac{18}{100} \times \) x = 423
x = 423 \( \times \frac{100}{18} \) = Rs. 2350

Example 11:

The salary of a person was reduced by 10%. By what percent should his reduced salary be raised so as to bring it at par with his original salary?

Solution:
Let the original salary be Rs. 100. New salary = Rs. 90
Increase on 90 = 10. Increase on 100 = \( \left(\frac{10}{90} \times 100 \right) \% = 11 \frac{1}{9} \% \)

Example 12:

A car is purchased for Rs. 80,000. Its value depreciates every year by 20%. Find the value of the car at the end of 2 years.

Solution:
Cost of the car = Rs. 80,000
Decrease at the end of one year = 20% of 80,000
= \( \frac{20}{100} \times 80,000 \) = Rs. 16,000
Value of the car at the end of one year = 80,000 - 16,000 = Rs. 64,000
Decrease at the end of 2 years = 20% of Rs. 64,000
= \( \frac{20}{100} \times 64,000 \) = Rs. 12,800
Value of the car at the end of 2 years = 64,000 - 12,800 = Rs. 51,200.

Example 13:

If the numerator of a fraction be increased by 15% and its denominator be diminished by 8%, the value of the fraction is \( \frac{15}{16} \) . Find the original fraction.

Solution:
Let the original fraction be \( \frac{x}{y} \) .
Then, \( \frac{115 \% \ of \ x}{92 \% \ of \ y} = \frac{15}{16} \Rightarrow \frac{x}{y} = \left(\frac{15}{16} \times \frac{92}{115} \right) = \frac{3}{4} \)

Example 14:

A sofa set is sold at a profit of 20%. If it had been sold at a profit of 25%, it would have fetched Rs. 750 more. Find the cost price of the sofa set.

Solution:
Let the cost price of the sofa set be Rs. 100.
Profit = 20% = Rs. 20
Selling price of sofa in the first case = 100 + 20 = Rs. 120
In the second case, profit = 25% = Rs. 25
\( \therefore \) Selling price in the second case = 100 + 25 = Rs. 125
Difference in two selling prices = Rs. 125 - Rs. 120 = Rs. 5
\( \begin{align} \begin{array}{ccc} \text{Difference in selling price} & Costprice \\\\ Rs. 5 & 100 \\\\ Rs. 750 & ? \end{array} \end{align} \)
Direct variation.
Cost price = \( \frac{750}{5} \times 100 \) = Rs. 15,000

Example 15:

The population of a town is 1,76,400. If it increases at the rate of 5% per annum, what will be its population 2 years hence ? What was it 2 years ago?

Solution:
Population after 2 years = 176400 \( \left(1 + \frac{5}{100} \right)^2 = \left(176400 \times \frac{20}{21} \times \frac{20}{21} \right) \) = 194481
Population 2years ago \( \frac{176400}{\left(1 + \frac{5}{100} \right)^2} = \left(176400 \times \frac{20}{21} \times \frac{20}{21} \right) = 160000 \)

Example 16:

If the price of petrol increases successfully by 20% and then by 10%, what is the net change in percentage terms?

Solution:
Net change in % = \( \left[20 + 10 + \frac{20 \times 10}{100} \right] \) % = 32%

Example 17:

Bharath borrowed Rs. 7,500 on 26th March 1996 from a cooperative bank at the rate of 8% p.a. simple interest. If he wants to clear the account on 7th June 1996, then what amount will he have to pay?

Solution:
Principal = Rs. 7,500
Calculation of time from \( 26^{th} \) March to \( 7^{th} \) June
March (31 - 26) = 5
April = 30
May = 31
June = 7
Total no. of day = 73 days
SI = \( \frac{PTR}{100} =7500\times \frac{73}{365} \times \frac{8}{100} \) = 120
The amount he have to pay = 7500 + 120 = Rs. 7620.

Example 18:

If A earns \( 33 \frac{1}{3} \% \) more than B, how much percent does B earn less than A?

Solution:
Required percentage =
\( \left[\frac{\left(\frac{100}{3} \right)}{\left(100 + \frac{100}{3} \right)} \times 100 \right] \% \) 25%

Example 19:

If A's income is 20% more than that of B, then how much percent is B's income less than that of A?

Solution:
Let the income of B be Rs. 100,
then income of A = Rs. 120
In the question B's income is being compared with that of A and hence base value to find the % decrease will be the income of A.
% decrease = \( \frac{Decrease}{Base value} \times 100 = \frac{(120 - 100)}{120} \times 100 = \frac{20}{120} \times 100 = \frac{50}{3} \% \ or \ 16 \frac{2}{3} \% \)

Example 20:

In an examination, 35% of total students failed in Hindi, 45% failed in English, and 20% in both. Find the percentage of those who passed in both the subjects.

Solution:
Let A and B be the sets of students who failed in Hindi and English respectively.
Then, n(A) = 35, n(B) = 45, n(A \( \cap \) B) = 20
So, n(A \( \cap \) B) = n(A) + n(B) - n(A \( \cap \) B) = (35 + 45 - 20) = 60.
\( \therefore \) Percentage failed on Hindi or English or both = 60% Hence, percentage passed = (100 - 60)% = 40%

Example 21:

If the price of petrol is increased by 20% and subsequently by 40%. What is final price per litre, if the original price was Rs. 25 per litre?

Solution:
Final price = \( 25 \times 1.2 \times 1.4 = 25 \times \frac{6}{5} \times \frac{7}{5} \) = Rs. 42

Example 22:

The sale of a particular item has decreased by 20%. By what percent price of the item should be increased so that there is no change in the revenue collection?

Solution:
% increase in price = \( \frac{20}{100 + (-20)} \times 100 = \frac{20}{80} \times 100 \) = 25%

Example 23:

The length of a rectangle is increased by 20% and breadth is decreased by 10%. Calculate the percentage change in the area.

Solution:
Percentage change in area = \( a + b + \frac{ab}{100} = 20 - 10 - \frac{200}{100} \) = 8%
