Ratio

The ratio of two like quantities a and b is the fraction \( \frac{a}{b} \) which indicates how many times of b is the quantity a. In other words, their ratio indicates their relative sizes.
Let the weights of two persons be 10 kg and 20 kg. Clearly, the weight of the second person is double the weight of the first person because \( 10 \ kg = 2 \times 10 \ kg \)
\( \therefore \ \frac{\text{weight of the first person }}{\text{weight of the second person}} = \frac{10 kg}{20 kg} = \frac{1}{2} \)
We say, the ratio of the weight of the first person to the weight of the second person is \( \frac{1}{2} \) or 1 : 2.The ratio of two like quantities a and b is the quotient \( a \div b \) , and it is written as a : b (read a is to b).
In the ratio a : b, a and b are called terms of the ratio, a is called the antecedent or first term, and b is called the consequent or second term.
Ex:
ratio of the heights of two person A and B whose heights are 6 ft and 5 ft is \( \frac{6 \ ft}{5 \ ft}, \ i.e., \ \frac{6}{5} \) or 6 : 5. Hence, 6 is the antecedent and 5 is the consequent.
Note:
The ratio of two unlike quantities is not defined. For example, the ratio between 5 kg and 15 metres cannot be found.
Ratio has no unit.
The ratio of a and b is the same as the ratio of the quantities ma and mb, or \( \frac{a}{m} \) and \( \frac{b}{m} \) , where \( m \neq 0 \) .
If two quantities are in the ratio m : n then the quantities will be of the form m.k and n.k, where k is any number, \( k \neq 0 \) . Thus, if the ratio of two quantities x andis 3 : 4, x and y can be 6 and 8 (k = 2), 9 and 12 (k = 3), and so on.
If a is k % of b then the ratio a : b = k : 100. Also, if a : b = p : q then \( a = \frac{p}{q} \times 100 \ \% \) of b.

Types of Ratios

  1. Compounded ratio:
    The compounded ratio of the two ratios a : b and c : d is the ratio ac : bd, and that of a : b, c : d and e : f is the ratio ace : bdf

  2. Duplicate ratio :
    The duplicate ratio of the ratio a : b is the ratio \( a^2 : b^2 \)

  3. Triplicate ratio :
    The triplicate ratio of the ratio a : b is the ratio \( a^3 : b^3 \)

  4. Subduplicate ratio :
    The subduplicate ratio of the ratio a : b is the ratio \( \sqrt{a} : \sqrt{b} \) . So the subduplicate ratio of \( a^2 : b^2 \) is the ratio a : b.

  5. Subtriplicate ratio :
    The Subtriplicate ratio of the ratio a : b is the ratio \( \sqrt[3]{a} : \sqrt[3]{b} \) . So the Subtriplicate ratio of \( a^3 : b^3 \) is the ratio a : b.

  6. Reciprocal ratio:
    The reciprocal ratio of the ratio a : b \( ( a \neq 0, \ b \neq 0) \) is the ratio \( \frac{1}{a} : \frac{1}{b} \) .

Comparison of Ratio

To compare two ratios, follow these steps:

  1. Make the second terms of both the ratios equal.
    For this, determine the LCM of the second terms of the ratios. Divide the LCM by the second term of each ratio. Multiply the numerator and the denominator of each ratio by the quotient.

  2. Compare the first terms (numerators) of the new ratios.
    Ex:
    Compare the ratios 3 : 5 and 2 : 11.
    LCM of the second terms, i.e., 5 and 11. = 55.
    Now, dividing the LCM by the second term of each ratio, we get \( 55 \div 5 = 11 \) , and \( 55 \div 11 \) = 5.
    \( \begin{align} \therefore \ \frac{3}{5} = \frac{3 \times 11}{5 \times 11} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{33}{55}, \ \frac{2}{11} = \frac{2 \times 5}{11 \times 5} = \frac{10}{55} \\\\[3pt] As \ 33 > 10, \ \frac{3}{5} > \frac{2}{11}, \ i.e., \ 3 : 5 > 11 \end{align} \) .

To divide a number into two parts in a given ratio

Let the number be p. It is to be divided into two parts in the ratio m : n.
The two parts are a and b if a + b = p ....... (1)
and \( \frac{a}{b} = \frac{m}{n} \) ...... (2)
From (2), \( \frac{a}{m} = \frac{b}{n} \) = k (say), They, a = mk , b = nk.
Substituting in (1), mk + nk = p \( \Rightarrow \ (m + n)k = p \ \ \ \Rightarrow \ k = \frac{p}{m + n} \) .
\( \therefore \ a = mk = \frac{m}{m + n} \) p and b = nk \( \frac{n}{m + n} p \) .
Two parts of p in the ratio m : n are \( \frac{mp}{m + n} \) and \( \frac{np}{m + n} \) .

Example:
Divide 75 into two parts in the ratio 8 : 7.
Solution:
The two parts are \( \frac{8}{8 + 7} \times 75 \) and \( \frac{8}{8 + 7} \times 75 \) , i.e., 40 and 35.

Ratio a : b : c

Three quantities are said to be in the ratio a : b : c if the quantities are a.k, b.k and c.k respectively, where k is any number.
Similarly, four quantities are in the ratio a : b : c : d if the quantities are ak, bk, ck and io dk respectively.

To divide a number into three parts in a given ratio

Let the number be p. It is to be divided into three parts in the ratio a : b : c. Let the parts be x, y and z. Then, x + y + z = p ...... (1)
and x = ak, y = bk, z = ck. ..... (2)
Substituting in (1), ak + bk + ck = p \( \Rightarrow \ k(a + b + c) = p \ \therefore \ k = \frac{p}{a + b + c} \)
\( \therefore \ x = ak = \frac{ap}{a + b + c}, \ y = bk = \frac{bp}{a + b + c} , \ z = ck = \frac{cp}{a + b + c} \)
The three parts of p in the ratio a : b : c are
\( \frac{ap}{a + b + c} + \frac{bp}{a + b + c} + \frac{cp}{a + b + c} \)

Proportion

If four quantities a, b, c d are such that the ratio a : b is equal to the ratio c : d then we say a, b, c and d are in proportion or a, b, c and d are proportional. We express it by writing a : b :: c : d.
If a, b, c and d are in proportional then the last term d is also called the fourth proportional.
For example, 3 : 5 :: 12 : 20 because \( \frac{3}{5} = \frac{12}{20} \) .
Note :
If a, b, c and d are in proportion then ad = bc, i.e., the product of the extreme terms = the product of the middle terms.
Three quantities a, b and c are said to be in continued proportion if a : b = b : c, i.e., \( \frac{a}{b} = \frac{b}{c} \) .
Similarly, if a, b, c and d are fouk quantities such that a : b = b : c = c : d, i.e., \( \frac{a}{b} = \frac{b}{c} = \frac{c}{d} \) ,they are said to be in continued proportion.

  1. The numbers 4, 6 and 9 are in continued proportion because \( \frac{4}{6} = \frac{6}{9} \ (or \ 6^2 = 4 \times 9) \)

  2. The numbers 2, 4, 6 are not in continued proportion because \( \frac{2}{4} \neq \frac{4}{6} \) .

  3. The numbers 2, 4, 8 and 16 are in continued proportion because \( \frac{2}{4} = \frac{4}{8} = \frac{8}{16} \) .

Mean proportional and third proportional

If a, b, c and c are in continued proportion then b is called the mean proportional (or:0 geometric mean) of a and c.
If b is the mean proportional of a and c, \( b^2 \) = ac, i.e., b = \( + \sqrt{ab} \) .
For example, the mean proportional of 4 and 6 \( = + \sqrt{4 \times 16} = + \sqrt{64} = 8 \) .
If a, b and c are in continued proportional then c is called the third proportional.
For example, the third proportional of 4, 8 is 16.

Solved examples

Example 1:

The price of a train ticket has a direct relationship to the length of the journey. If Rs. 504 is charged for a journey 4x km long, then how much is charged for a journey \( \frac{y}{2} \ km \) long?

Solution:
Since the price of a train ticket is directly related to the length of the journey,
so, \( \frac{\text{Prince of ticket}}{\text{length of journey}} \) = constant ratio
\( \begin{align} = \frac{504}{4x} = \frac{\text{504 price of ticket for y /2 km}}{\frac{y}{2}} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow \ Required \ change = \frac{504}{4x} \times \frac{y}{2} = \frac{63 y}{x} \ rupees. \end{align} \)

Example 2:

The ratio between two numbers is 3 : 5. If each number is increased by 4, the ratio becomes 2 : 3. Find the number.

Solution:
Let the numbers be 3x and 5x.
\( \Rightarrow \ \frac{3xx + 4}{5x + 4} = \frac{2}{3} \Rightarrow \ x = 4 \)
\( \therefore \) Numbers are 12 and 20.

Example 3:

P, Q and R have 100, x and y color pencils respectively. If Q gives P, 20 color pencils he, is left with half as many as color pencils as R. If they together had 60 more color pencils, each of them would have had 100 color pencils on an average. Find \( \frac{x}{y} \)

Solution:
If Q gives P, 20 color pencils, Q has x - 20 color pencils and P has (100 + 20) = 120.
Given that,
\( x - 20 = \frac{y}{2} \ \Rightarrow \ y = 2x - 40 \) ....... (i)
Also given that,
100 + x + y + 60 = 300
x + y = 140
x + 2x - 40 = 140 [by (i)]
3x = 180
x = 60
Substituting the value of x in (i) we get
y = 120 - 40 = 80
\( \therefore \ \frac{x}{y} = \frac{60}{80} = \frac{3}{4} \)

Example 4:

A bag contains an equal number of one rupees, 50 paise and 25 paise coins respectively. If the total value is Rs. 35, how many coins of each type are there?

Solution:
Here number of each type of coins is same , hence, we may write,
Number of each type of coin = \( \frac{\text{Total \ amount}}{\text{Sum of value of each coin}} \)
\( \therefore \) Number of eaach type of coin = \( \frac{35}{1 + 0.5 + 0.25} \) = 20 coins of each type.

Example 5:

The sum of the present age of A, B and C is 90 years. Six years ago, their ages are in th ratio 1 : 2 : 3. What is the present age of C?

Solution:
(A + B + C)'s age 6 years age = \( 90 - 6 \times 3 = 72 \)
\( \therefore \) C's age 6 years ago = \( \frac{3}{(1 + 2 + 3)} \times 72 \) = 36
\( \therefore \) C's present age = 36 + 6 = 42 years.

Example 6:

The sum of two numbers is 'c' and their quotient is \( \frac{p}{q} \) .Find the numbers.

Solution:
Let the numbers be x, y.
\( \therefore \) x + y = c (given)
\( \frac{x}{y} = \frac{p}{q} (given) \)
\( \begin{align} \therefore \ \frac{x}{x + y} = \frac{p}{p + q} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow \ \frac{x}{c} = \frac{p}{p + q} \ \Rightarrow \ x = \frac{pc}{p + q} \\\\[3pt] and \ y = \frac{qc}{q + p} \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \left [ Since \ \frac{x}{y} = \frac{p}{q} \right ] \end{align} \)

Example 7:

A bag contains rupees, fifty paise, twenty five paise and ten paise coins in the proportion 1 : 3 : 5 : 7. If the total amount is Rs. 22.25, find the number of coins of each kind.

Solution:
Let the number of coins be x, 3x, 5x, 7x respectively as rupees, fifty paise, twenty five paise and ten paise. Since
Number of coin \( \times \) Value of coin in rupee = Amount in rupees
Now,
value of 50 paise coin in rupee = \( \frac{1}{2} \)
value of 25 paise coin in rupee = \( \frac{1}{4} \)
value of 10 paise coin in rupee = \( \frac{1}{10} \)
\( \begin{align} \therefore \ \left [ (x \times 1) + \left ( 3x + \frac{1}{2} \right ) + \left ( 5x \times \frac{1}{4} \right ) + \left ( 7x \times \frac{1}{10} \right ) \right ] = 22.25 \\\\[12pt] \Rightarrow \ \frac{89 x}{20} = 22.5 \ \Rightarrow \ x = 5 \end{align} \) .
\( \therefore \) Number of rupee coins = 5 \( \times \) 1 = 5
Number of 50 paise coins = 3x = 15
Number of 25 paise coins = 5x = 25
Number of 10 paise coins = 7x = 35.

Example 8:

If \( \frac{a}{b} = \frac{3}{5}, \ \frac{b}{c} = \frac{4}{7} \) . Find a : b : c

Solution:
\( a : b : c = 3 \times 4 : 5 \times 4 : 5 \times 7 = 12 : 20 : 35 \)

Example 9:

In a mixture of 60 litres, the ratio of milk and water is 2 : 1. What amount of water must be added to make the ratio 1 : 2?

Solution:
Quantity of milk = \( \frac{2}{3} \times 60 = 40 \) litres and that of water = 20 litres.
Let x litre of water be added to make the ratio 1 : 2.
\( \frac{40}{20 + x} = \frac{1}{2} \ \Rightarrow \ x = 60 \ litres \) .

Example 10:

Two vessels contain mixtures of water and milk in the ratio 1 : 2 and 2 : 5. These mixtures of two vessels are mixed in the ratio 1 : 4. Find the ratio of water and milk in the resulting mixture.

Solution:
In vessel I quantity of water = \( \frac{1}{3} \) and that of milk = \( \frac{2}{3} \)
In vessel II quantity of water = \( \frac{2}{7} \) and that of milk = \( \frac{5}{7} \)
From vessel I, \( \frac{1}{5} \) is taken and from vessel II, \( \frac{4}{5} \) is taken.
Therefore, the ratio of water to milk in the new vessel
\( \begin{align} = \left ( \frac{1}{3} \times \frac{1}{5} + \frac{2}{7} \times \frac{4}{5} \right ) : \left ( \frac{2}{3} \times \frac{1}{5} + \frac{5}{7} \times \frac{4}{5} \right ) \\\\[12pt] = \left ( \frac{1}{15} + \frac{8}{35} \right ) : \left ( \frac{2}{15} + \frac{20}{35} \right ) = 31 : 74 \end{align} \) .

Example 11:

A and B are two alloys of gold and copper prepared by mixing metals in proportional 7 : 2 and 7 : 11 respectively. If equal quantities of alloys are melted to form a third alloy C, then find the proportion of gold and copper in C.

Solution:
In alloy C, gold = \( \left ( \frac{7}{9} + \frac{7}{18} \right) = \frac{21}{18} \) and copper \( \left ( \frac{2}{9} + \frac{11}{18} \right) = \frac{15}{18} \)
\( \therefore \) Ratio of gold and copper = \( \frac{21}{18} : \frac{15}{18} = 7 : 5 \)

Example 12:

If a : b = 5 : 9 and b : c = 4 : 7, find a : b : c.

Solution:
a : b = 5 : 9 and b : c = 4 : 7 = \( \left ( 4 \times \frac{9}{4} \right ) : \left ( 7 \times \frac{9}{4} \right ) = 9 : \frac{63}{4} \)
\( \Rightarrow \) a : b : c = 5 : 9 : \( \frac{63}{4} \) = 20 : 36 : 63.

Example 14:

Divide Rs. 1162 among A, B, C in the ratio 35 : 28 : 20.

Solution:
Sum of ratio terms = (35 + 28 + 20) = 83.
A's share = Rs. \( \left ( 1162 \times \frac{35}{83} \right ) \) = Rs. 490 ; B's share = Rs. \( \left ( 1162 \times \frac{28}{83} \right ) \) = Rs. 392; C's share = Rs. \( \left ( 1162 \times \frac{20}{83} \right ) \) = Rs. 280.

Example 15:

A bag contains 50 p, 25 p and 10 p coins in the ratio 5 : 9 : 4, amounting to Rs. 206. Find the number of coins of each type.

Solution:
Let the number of 50 p, 25 p and 10 p coins be 5x, 9x and 4x respectively.
\( \frac{5x}{2} + \frac{9x}{4} + \frac{4x}{10} = 206 \)
\( \Leftrightarrow \) 50x + 45x + 8x = 4120 \( \Leftrightarrow \) 103x = 4120 \( \Leftrightarrow \) x = 40.
Number of 50 p coins = (5 \( \times \) 40) = 200; number of 25 p coins = (9 x 40) = 360;
Number of 10 p coins = (4 \( \times \) 40) = 160.

Example 16:

If x, 8, 16 are in continued proportion then find x.

Solution:
By definition, x 8 = 8 : 16
\( \Rightarrow \ x \times 18 = 8^2 \)
\( \therefore \ x = \frac{8^2}{16} = \frac{64}{16} = 4 \)

Example 17:

If x, 5, 10, y are in continued proportion then find x and y.

Solution:
x, 5, 10, y are in continued proportion
\( \Rightarrow \ \frac{x}{5} = \frac{5}{10} = \frac{10}{y} \)
Now, \( \Rightarrow \ \frac{x}{5} = \frac{5}{10} \ \ \ \ \ \Rightarrow \ x = \frac{25}{10} = \frac{5}{2} \)
Also, \( \frac{5}{10} = \frac{10}{y} \ \ \ \ \Rightarrow \ 5y = 100 \)
\( \Rightarrow y = \frac{100}{20} = 1000 \)

Example 18:

Three sons of a man have Rs. 12, Rs. 15 and Rs. 19 with them. The father asks his sons to give him equal amount so that the money held by the sons now are in continued proportion. Find the amount taken from each son.

Solution:
Let the amount taken from each son is Rs. x. Then,
12 - x, 15 - x and 19 - x are in continued proportion.
\( \begin{align} \therefore \ \frac{12 - x}{15 - x} = \frac{15 - x}{19 - x} \ \Rightarrow \ (12 - x)(19 - x) = (15 - x)^2 \\\\[12pt] or \ 228 - 12x - 19x + x^2 = 225 - 30x + x^2 \ \Rightarrow \ 228 - 31x = 225 - 30x \end{align} \)
or 228 - 225 = 31x - 30x
\( \therefore \) The required amount is Rs.3.

Example 19:

A bag contains Rs. 60 of which some are 50 paisa coins, some coins are 1-rupee coins and the rest are 2-rupee coins. The ratio of the number of respective coins is 8 : 6 : 5. Find the total number of coins in the bag.

Solution:
Let the number of coins be m, n and p respectively.
Then, m : n p is equal to 8 : 6 : 5.
\( \therefore \) m = 8k, n = 6k, p = 5k.
\( \therefore \) total sum = 8k \( \time \) 50 paise + 6k \( \time \) 1 rupee + 5k \( \time \) 2 rupees
\( = Rs. \left ( 8k \times \frac{1}{2} + 6k \times 1 + 5k \times 2 \right ) \) Rs.(4k + 6k + 10k) = Rs.20k.
\( \therefore \) Rs. 60 = Rs.20k. \( \therefore \) 20k = 60.
\( \therefore \ k = \frac{60}{20} = 3 \)
\( \therefore \) from (1), the number of 50-paise coins = 8k = \( 8 \times 3 \) = 24 the number of 1-rupee coins = 6k = \( 6 \times 3 \) = 18.
the number of 2-rupee coins = 5k = \( 5 \times 3 \) = 15.
\( \therefore \) The total number of coins = 24 + 18 + 15 = 57.

Example 20:

In an examination the ratio of the number of successful candidates and unsuccessful candidates is 4 : 1. Had 20 more appeared and 2 more passed, the ratio of successful candidates to unsuccessful candidates would have been 2 :1. Find the number of candidates who appeared in the examination originally.

Solution:
Let the number of candidates appeared be n.
Dividing n in two parts in the ratio 4 : 1, the number of successful candidates = \( \frac{4}{5}n \) and the number unsuccessful candidates \( \frac{1}{5} n \) .
In the second case, the number of successful candidates \( \frac{4}{5}n + 2 \)
and the number of unsuccessful candidates \( = \frac{1}{5}n + (20 - 2) = \frac{1}{5}n + 18 \)
From the question, \( \left ( \frac{4}{5}n + 2 \right ) : \left ( \frac{1}{5}n + 18 \right ) = 2 : 1 \)
\( \begin{align} or \ \frac{\frac{4}{5}n + 2}{\frac{1}{5}n + 18} = \frac{2}{1} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow \ \frac{4}{5} n + 2 = \frac{2}{5} n + 36 \\\\[3pt] or \ \left ( \frac{4}{5} - \frac{2}{5} \right ) n = 34 \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow \ \frac{2}{5} n = 34 \ \Rightarrow \ n = 34 \times \frac{5}{2} = 85 \end{align} \)
\( \therefore \) The required number of candidates is 85.
