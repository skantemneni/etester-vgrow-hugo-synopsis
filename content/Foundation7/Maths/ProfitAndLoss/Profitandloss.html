Synopsis

Some Important Formulae

  1. Gain = Selling Price (S.P.) - Cost Price (C.P.)

  2. Loss = C.P. - S.P.

  3. Gain % = \( \frac{gain}{CP} \times 100 \)

  4. Loss % = \( \frac{loss}{CP} \times 100 \)

  5. \( S.P. = \left ( \frac{100 + gain \%}{100} \right ) \times C.P. = \left ( \frac{100 - loss \%}{100} \right ) \times C.P. \)

  6. \( C.P. = \left ( \frac{100 + gain \%}{100} \right ) \times S.P. = \left ( \frac{100}{100 - loss \%} \right ) \times S.P. \)

  7. An article purchased at a certain price is sold at two different selling prices \( SP_1 \) and \( SP_2 \) thereby gaining or losing \( x_1 \% \) and \( x_2 \% \) respectively. Then,
    \( \frac{SP_1}{100 + x_1} = \frac{SP_2}{100 + x_2} \)
    Note : In this formula,
    Put \( - x_1 \) and \( - x_2 \) in case of loss.

  8. S.P. = Marked Price - Discount

  9. If "M" is the marked price and "d%" is the discount percent, then \( SP = \frac{M(100 - d)}{100} \)

  10. If one article incurs a gain of x% and other a loss of x%, then overall % loss = \( \frac{x^2}{100} \% \) (both articles sold at same price).

  11. If SP of some articles is equal to the CP of some articles, then % loss or % gain = \( \frac{No \ of \ articles \ on \ CP - No. \ of \ articles \ on \ SP}{No. \ of \ articles \ on \ SP} \times 100 \)
    Note : If it is +ve, then there is a gain.
    If it is -ve, then there is a loss.

  12. Using false scale a merchant has a overall gain percent(G%) if he sells goods at x% loss or gain. Then
    \( \frac{100 + G}{100 + x} = \frac{True \ scale}{False \ scale} \)
    Note : If the merchant is selling the goods at loss then put -x in place of x.

  13. When the merchant sells y articles with gain or loss of x, then
    % gain or % loss = \( \frac{x}{y - x} \times 100 \% \)
    Note : If the merchant loses, then put -x in place of x.

  14. A shopkeeper uses a false scale for selling and purchasing an article. On purchasing he deceives the seller by x% and on selling he deceives the customer by y%. Then overall gain %
    \( = \left [ \frac{(100 + x)(100 + y)}{100} - 100 \right ] \% \)

  15. If \( \frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d} \) , then

    1. \( \frac{a + b}{b} = \frac{c + d}{d} \)

    2. \( \frac{a}{a - b} = \frac{c}{c - d} \)

    3. \( \frac{a + b}{a - b} = \frac{c + d}{c - d} \) ....... etc. rules are applicable.

Solved examples

Example 1:

Meena buys an article for Rs. 20 and sells it to Neena for Rs. 25. Find

  1. Meena's profit

  2. Meena's profit %.

Solution:
Cost price of an article (C.P.) = Rs. 20
Selling price of an article (S.P.) = Rs. 25
Since S.P. > C.P. \( \therefore \) Meena will gain.

  1. Meena's gain = S.P. - C.P.
    = Rs. 25 - Rs. 20 = Rs. 5

  2. Meena's gain % = \( \left ( \frac{Gain}{C.P.} \times 100 \right ) \% \)
    \( = \left ( \frac{5}{20} \times 100 \right ) \% = 25 \% \) Alternative

Example 2:

A man purchased a bicycle for Rs. 600 and sold it for Rs. 550. Find his

  1. Loss

  2. Loss %.

Solution:
Cost price = Rs. 600
Selling price = Rs. 550
Since S.P. < C.P. \( \therefore \) there will be loss.

  1. Loss = C.P. - S.P.
    Rs. 600 - Rs. 550 = Rs. 50

  2. Also, Loss % = \( \frac{loss}{C.P.} \times 100 \)
    \( = \frac{50}{600} \times 100 \)
    \( = \frac{25}{3} = 8 \frac{1}{3} \)

Alternative:

Example 3:

By selling a book for Rs. 95 a man gained Rs. 5. Find

  1. the cost price of the book and

  2. his gain %.

Solution:
S.P. = Rs. 95
Gain = Rs. 5
C.P. = ?

  1. Now, S.P. - C.P. = Gain
    or C.P. = S.P. - Gain
    = 95 - 5 = Rs. 90

  2. And, gain % = \( \frac{gain}{C.P.} \times 100 \)
    \( = \frac{5}{90} \times 100 = \frac{50}{9} = 5 \frac{5}{9} \% \)

Alternative:

Example 4:

A man buys 6 pens, at Rs.10 per pen and sells 4 pens at Rs. 12 per pen and the remaining 2 pens at Rs.9 per pen. Find his gain or loss %.

Solution:
C.P. of 1 pen = Rs. 10
C.P. of 6 pens = 10 \( \times \) 6 = Rs. 60
Also, S.P. of 1 pen = Rs. 12
S.P. of 4 pens = 12 \( \times \) 4 = Rs. 48
Remaining pens:
S.P. of 1 pen = Rs. 9
S.P. of 2 pens = 9 \( \times \) 2
= Rs. 18
Total S.P. of 6 pens = Rs. 48 + Rs. 18
Total S.P. of 6 pens = Rs. 66
Now, C.P. = Rs. 60
S.P. = Rs. 66
Since S.P. > C.P. \( \therefore \) there will be gain.
or, gain = S.P. - C.P.
= Rs. 66 - Rs. 60
= Rs. 6.
And, gain % = \( \frac{gain}{C.P.} \times 100 \)
\( = \frac{6}{60} \times 100 = 10 \% \)

Example 5:

At what price must Anil sell his cycle, which costs him Rs. 600, so as to make a profit of 25%.

Solution:
Method 1 :
C.P. of the cycle = Rs. 660
Profit = 25%
\( \therefore Profit = \frac{25}{100} \times 600 \)
= Rs. 150
Now, S.P. of the cycle = C.P. + gain
= Rs. 600 + Rs. 150
= Rs. 750

Example 6:

A man sells his scooter for Rs. 3000 making a profit of 20%. How much did the scooter cost him ?

Solution:
Desired profit = 20%
It means, if
C.P. = Rs. 100, then
Profit (P) = Rs. 20
\( \therefore \) S.P. = C.P. + profit
= Rs. 100 + Rs. 20 = Rs. 120
Now,

\( C.P. = \frac{3000 \times 100}{120} = Rs. 2500 \)

Example 7:

Shyam bought a T.V. for Rs. 4000 and spent Rs. 500 on its repairs. He later sold it for Rs. 5000. Find his gain %.

Solution:
Price of T.V. = Rs. 4000
Money spent on repairs = Rs. 500
\( \therefore \) Total C.P. = Rs. 4000 + Rs. 500
= Rs. 4500
S.P. of the T.V. = Rs. 5000
Since S.P. > C.P. \( \therefore \) there is a gain and,
Gain = S.P. - C.P.
= Rs. 5000 - Rs. 4500
Gain = Rs. 500
Gain % = \( \frac{gain}{C.P.} \times 100 \)
\( = \frac{500}{4500} \times 100 = 11 \frac{1}{9} \% \)
Alternative:

Example 8:

A person incurs 5% loss by selling a watch for Rs. 1140. At what price should the watch be sold to earn 5% profit ?

Solution:
Let the new S.P. be Rs. x then,
\( \begin{align} (100 - loss \ \%) : (1st S.P.) = (100 + gain \ \%) : (2nd S.P.) \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow \left ( \frac{100 - 5}{1140} \right ) = \left ( \frac{100 + 5}{x} \right ) \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow x = \left ( \frac{105 \times 1140}{95} \right ) = 1260 \end{align} \)
\( \therefore \) New S.P. = Rs. 1260.

Example 9:

A book was sold for Rs. 27.50 with a profit of 10%. It it were sold for Rs. 25.75, then what would have been the percentage of profit or loss ?

Solution:
S.P. = Rs. 27.50, Profit = 10%
So, \( C.P. = Rs. \left ( \frac{100}{110} \times 27.50 \right ) \) = Rs. 25
When S.P. = Rs. 25.75, profit = Rs.(25.75 - 25) = Re. 0.75
\( Profit \ \% = \left ( \frac{0.75}{25} \times 100 \right ) \% = 3 \% \)

Example 10:

By selling 33 metres of cloth, one gains the selling price of 11 metres. Find the gain percent.

Solution:
(S.P. of 33 m) - (C.P. of 33 m) = Gain = S.P. of 11 m
\( \therefore \) S.P. of 22 m = C.P. of 33 m.
Let C.P. of each metre be Re. 1. Then, C.P. of 22 m = Rs. 22, S.P. of 22 m = Rs. 33
\( \therefore Gain \ \% = \left ( \frac{11}{22} \times 100 \right ) \% = 50 \% \)

Example 11:

A man sells an article at a profit of 25%. If he had bought it at 20% less and sold it for Rs. 10.50 less, he would have gained 30%. Find the cost price of the article.

Solution:
Let the C.P. be Rs. x.
1st S.P. = 125% of x = \( \frac{125}{100}x = \frac{5x}{4}; \)
2nd C.P. = 80% of x = \( \frac{80}{100}x = \frac{4x}{5} \)
2nd S.P. = \( 130 \% \ of \ \frac{4x}{5} = \left ( \frac{130}{100} \times \frac{4x}{5} \right ) = \frac{26x}{25} \)
\( \therefore \frac{5x}{4} - \frac{26x}{25} = 10.50 \Leftrightarrow \frac{21x}{100} = 10.50 \Leftrightarrow x = \left ( \frac{10.50 \times 100}{21} \right ) = 50 \)
Hence, C.P. = Rs. 50

Example 12:

A person bought an article and sold it at a loss of 10%. If he had bought it for 20% less and sold it for Rs. 55 more, he would have had a profit of 40%. Find the CP of the article.

Solution:
Let the CP of an article be Rs. x
then SP = Rs. 0.9x(at a loss of 10%)
New CP = Rs. 0.8x (at 20% less than original)
New SP = Rs. 1.4 \( \times \) 0.8x(at 40% profit) = 1.12 x
Given the difference between new SP and old SP is Rs. 55
\( \therefore \) 1.12x - 0.9x = Rs. 55
\( \Rightarrow \) 0.22x = Rs. 55 or x = Rs. 250
\( \therefore \) Cost price of the article be Rs. 250.

Example 13:

If a commission of 20% is given on retail price, the profit is 60%. Find the profit percentage when the commission is increased by 5% of the retail price.

Solution:
Let the retail price be Rs. 100
then SP = Rs. 80 (after a discount of 20%)
Since profit = 60%
\( \therefore CP = \frac{SP}{1.6} = \frac{80}{1.6} = Rs. 50 \)
New SP = Rs. 75 (after a commission of 25%)
\( \therefore \) Profit = Rs. (75 - 50) = Rs. 25,
Hence profit % = \( \frac{25}{50} \times 100 = 50 \% \)

Example 14:

The cost price of an article increases by Rs. 100. The selling price increases by 10%. If the new profit decreases from 15% to 10%, what is the original cost price ?

Solution:
Let the original cost price be Rs. x
SP at an initial profit of 15% = Rs. 1.15x
New cost price = Rs. (x + 100)
New SP at a profit of 10% = Rs. 1.1(x + 100)
Given that New SP = 1.1 \( \times \) 1.15x
or 0.15x = 100 \( \Rightarrow \) x = 666.66

Example 15:

The profit earned when an article is sold for Rs. 800 is 20 times the loss incurred when it is sold for Rs. 275. At what price should the article be sold if it is desired to make a profit of 25% ?

Solution:
Let the CP of the article be x
then gain = (800 - x)
and loss = (x - 275)
Given (800 - x) = 20(x - 275)
\( \Rightarrow \) 21x = 6300 \( \Rightarrow \) x = 300
\( \therefore \) CP = Rs. 300
Now SP at a gain of 25% = 125% of 300 = Rs. 375

Example 16:

Sunanda buys rice at Rs. 10 kg and puts a price tag on it to earn a profit of 20%. However, his faulty balance shows 1000 gm when it is actually 800 g. What is the actual gain percentage ?

Solution:
CP = Rs. 10/kg
Marked price = Rs. 12/kg (20% above CP)
Since she uses a false weight of 800 g in place of 1 kg. Therefore selling price of 800 g of rice would be Rs. 12
CP of 800 g of rice is Rs. 8
\( \therefore \) Profit = (12 - 8) = Rs. 4
Hence,
\( profit \% = \frac{gain}{CP} \times 100 = \frac{4}{8} \times 100 = 50 \% \)

Example 17:

An orange seller makes a profit of 20% by selling oranges at a certain price. If he charges Rs. 1.2 higher per orange he would gain 40%. Find the original price at which he sold an orange.

Solution:
Let the CP of the orange be Rs. x
Then, SP = 1.2x
New SP = (1.2x + 1.2)
\( \therefore CP = \frac{1.2x + 1.2}{1.4} \) [since the gain is 40%]
Since the CP in both the cases is same
\( \therefore x = \frac{1.2x + 1.2}{1.4} \)
or, 1.4x = 1.2x + 1.2 \( \Rightarrow \) 0.2x = 1.2
\( \Rightarrow \) x = Rs. 6
Therefore, original SP = Rs. 1.2 \( \times \) 6 = Rs. 7.20

Example 18:

A man bought a horse and a carriage for Rs. 3000. He sold the horse at a gain of 20% and the carriage at aloss of 10%, thereby gaining 2% on the whole. Find the cost of the horse.

Solution:
Let the C.P. of the horse be Rs. x
Then, C.P. of the carriage = Rs. (3000 - x)
\( \therefore \) 20% of x - 10% of (3000 - x) = 2% of 3000
\( \Rightarrow \frac{x}{5} - \frac{(3000 - x)}{10} = 60 \Rightarrow 2x - 3000 + x = 600 \Rightarrow 3x = 3600 \Rightarrow x = 1200 \)
Hence, C.P. of the horse = Rs. 1200.

Example 19:

An uneducated retailer marks all his goods at 50% above the cost price and thinking that he will still make 25% profit, offers a discount of 25% on the marked price. What is his actual profit on the sales ?

Solution:
Let C.P. = Rs = 100. Then, marked price = Rs. 150
S.P. = 75% of Rs. 150 = Rs. 112.50
\( \therefore \) gain % = 12.50%

Example 20:

A retailer buys 40 pens at the marked price of 36 pens from a wholesaler. If he sells these pens giving a discount of 1%, what is the profit percent ?

Solution:
Let the marked price of each pen be Re. 1.
Then, C.P. of 40 pens = Rs. 36 S.P. of 40 pens = 99% of Rs. 40 = Rs. 39.60
\( Profit \% = \left ( \frac{3.60}{36} \times 100 \right ) \% = 10 \% \)

Example 21:

At what percentage above the C.P. must an article be marked so as to gain 33% after allowing a customer a discount of 5% ?

Solution:
Let C.P. = Rs. 100. Then, S.P. = Rs. 133.
Let marked price be Rs. x.
Then, 95% of x = 133
\( \frac{95}{100} x = 133 \Rightarrow x = \left ( 133 \times \frac{100}{95} \right ) = 140. \)
\( \therefore \) Marked price = 40% above C.P.

Example 22:

When a producer allows 36% commission on the retail price of his product, he earns a profit of 8.8%. What would be his profit percent if the commission is reduced by 24% ?

Solution:
Let retail price = Rs. 100. Then, commission - Rs. 36
\( \therefore \) S.P. = Rs.(100 - 36) = Rs. 64
But, profit = 8.8%
\( \therefore C.P. = Rs. \left ( \frac{100}{108.8} \times 64 \right ) = Rs. \frac{1000}{17} \)
New commission = Rs. 12. New S.P. = Rs.(100 - 12) = Rs. 88.
\( Gain = Rs. \left ( 88 - \frac{1000}{17} \right ) = Rs. \frac{496}{17} \)
\( Gain \% = \left ( \frac{496}{17} \times \frac{17}{1000} \times 100 \right ) \% = 49.6 \% \)
