Cudes

Cube of numbers

  1. The cube of number is the product of the number multiplied by itself thrice.
    Ex: The cube of 2 = \( 2 \times 2 \times 2 \) = 8
    The cube of \( (-3) = (-3) \times (-3) \times (-3) = - 27 \)

  2. Write the cube of a number using the cube symbol or notation.
    Ex: The cube of 7 is written as \( 7^3 \)
    \( \therefore \ 7^3 = 7 \times 7 \times 7 \)
    The cube of (-12) is written as \( (-12)^3 \)
    \( \therefore \ (-12)^3 = (-12) \times (-12) \times (-12) \)

  3. \( 8^3 \) read as 'eight cubed' or 'the cube of eight', or 'eight to the power of three'.

    1. The cube of any positive number is positive
      Ex: \( 4^3 = 4 \times 4 \times 4 = 64 \)

    2. The cube of any negative number is negative.
      Ex: \( (-5)^3 = (-5) \times (-5) \times (-5) = - 125 \)

    3. The cube of zero is zero.
      Ex: \( 0^3 = 0 \times 0 \times 0 = 0 \)

    4. The cube of one is one.
      Ex: \( 1^3 = 1 \times 1 \times 1 = 1 \)

Cubes Roots

  1. The cube root of a number is a number which, when multiplied by it self twice, equals the given number.
    Ex: What is the cube root of 64?
    The cube root of 64 is 4 because when 4 is multiplied by itself thrice, it gives 64.
    \( 4 \times 4 \times 4 = 64 \) .
    Therefore, the cube root of 64 is 4.

  2. The symbol used for cube root is \( \sqrt[3] \)
    Ex: The cube root 64 can be written as \( \sqrt[3]{64} \) , that is \( \sqrt[3]{64} = 4 \)
    \( \sqrt[3]{64} \) is read as 'cube root of sixty four'.

Solved examples

Example 1:

Write the following using the cube notation.

  1. The cube of 0.06

  2. \( \left ( 1 \frac{2}{5} \right ) \times \left ( 1 \frac{2}{5} \right ) \times \left ( 1 \frac{2}{5} \right ) \)

Solution:

  1. The cube of 0.06 = \( (0.06)^3 \)

  2. \( \left ( 1 \frac{2}{5} \right ) \times \left ( 1 \frac{2}{5} \right ) \times \left ( 1 \frac{2}{5} \right ) = \left ( 1 \frac{2}{5} \right )^3 \)

Example 2:

Calculate the following:

  1. \( (-0.4)^3 \)

  2. \( \left ( 1 \frac{1}{4} \right )^3 \)

Solution:

  1. \( (- 0.4)^3 = (- 0.4) \times (- 0.4) \times (- 0.4) = 0.16 \times (-0.4) \)
    = - 0.064

  2. \( \begin{align} \left ( 1 \frac{1}{4} \right )^3 = \left ( \frac{5}{4} \right )^3 \\\\[3pt] = \frac{5}{4} \times \frac{5}{4} \times \frac{5}{4} = \frac{125}{64} \end{align} \)

Example 3:

Estimate the cube of 10.6 by determining the range in which its value lies.

Solution:
10 < 10.6 < 11 \( \leftarrow \) (Determine the range)
\( 10^3 < (10.6)^3 < 11^3 \leftarrow \) (Cube the range)
\( 1,000 < (10.6)^3 < 1,331 \leftarrow \) (Estimated answer)
\( \therefore \ (10.6)^3 \) is between 1,000 and 1, 331

Example 4:

Find the value of

  1. \( 5^3 \)

  2. \( (-3.8)^3 \)

  3. \( \left ( \frac{4}{3} \right )^3 \)

Solution:

  1. \( 5^3 = 125 \)

  2. \( (-3.8)^3 = -54.872 \)

  3. \( \left ( \frac{4}{3} \right )^3 = \frac{64}{343} \)

Example 5:

Calculate the value of \( 1 - (-0.3)^3 \)

Solution:
\( 1 - (-0.3)^3 = 1 - [(-0.3) \times (-0.3) \times (-0.3)] \)
= 1 - (-9.027) = 1 + 0.027 = 1.027

Example 6:

Estimate the cube root of 320.

Solution:
216 < 320 < 343
\( \begin{align} \sqrt[3]{216} < \sqrt[3]{320} < \sqrt[3]{343} \\\\[3pt] 6 < \sqrt[3]{320} < 7 \end{align} \)

Example 7:

Find the value of

  1. \( \sqrt[3]{-125} \)

  2. \( \sqrt[3]{512} \)

Solution:

  1. \( \begin{align} \sqrt[3]{-125} = \sqrt[3]{(-5) \times (-5) \times (-5)} \\\\[3pt] = \sqrt[3]{(-5)^3} = - 5 \end{align} \)

  2. \( \sqrt[3]{512} = \sqrt[3]{8 \times 8 \times 8} = \sqrt[3]{8^3} = 8 \)

Example 8:

Calculate the value of the following:

  1. \( \sqrt[3]{\frac{16}{250}} \)

  2. \( \sqrt[3]{- \frac{4}{256}} \)

Solution:

  1. \( \sqrt[3]{\frac{16}{250}} = \sqrt[3]{\frac{8}{125}} = \frac{2}{5} \)

  2. \( \sqrt[3]{- \frac{4}{256}} = \sqrt[3]{ - \frac{1}{64}} = - \frac{1}{4} \)

Example 9:

Calculate the value of the following:

  1. \( \sqrt[3]{0.008} \)

  2. \( \sqrt[3]{- 0.729} \)

Solution:

  1. \( \begin{align} \sqrt[3]{0.008} = \sqrt[3]{\frac{8}{1,000}} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{2}{10} = 0.2 \end{align} \)

  2. \( \sqrt[3]{- 0.729} = \sqrt[3]{\frac{-729}{1,000}} = \frac{-9}{10} = - 0.9 \)

Example 10:

Find the value of \( \sqrt[3]{-125} + 2 \) .

Solution:
\( \sqrt[3]{-125} + 2 = - 5 + 2 = -3 \)

Example 11:

Find the value of \( \sqrt[3]{-0.216} \) .

Solution:
\( \sqrt[3]{-0.216} = \sqrt[3]{\frac{-216}{1,000}} \leftarrow \) (Convert decimal number into a fraction).
\( = \frac{\sqrt[3]{-216}}{\sqrt[3]{1000}} \leftarrow \) (Work out the cube root of the numerator and denominator separately)
\( = \frac{-6}{10} \)
= 0. 6

Example 12:

Find the value of \( - 6 + \sqrt[3]{-64} \) .

Solution:
\( - 6 + \sqrt[3]{-64} = - 6 + (-4) \)
= - 6 - 4 = - 10
