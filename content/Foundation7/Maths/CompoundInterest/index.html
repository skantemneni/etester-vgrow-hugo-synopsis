Synopsis

Compound Interest

  1. \( A = P \left ( 1 + \frac{R}{100} \right )^n \)
    A - amount, - principle, R - rate of interest, n - time.

  2. Compound interest = A - P.
    In case of growth A = \( A = P \left ( 1 + \frac{R}{100} \right )^n \)

  3. In case of depreciation (or) decay, \( A = P \left ( 1 - \frac{R}{100} \right )^n \)

  4. If the rates be p%, q% and r% during 1st, 2nd and 3rd year respectively, then the population after 3 years
    \( = P \left ( 1 + \frac{p}{100} \right ) \left ( 1 + \frac{q}{100} \right ) \left ( 1 + \frac{r}{100} \right ) \)

  5. If principle = Rs.P, rate = R% per annum and time = n years then,

    1. amount after n years (compounded annually) \( A = P \left ( 1 + \frac{R}{100} \right )^n \)

    2. Amount after n years (compounded half-yearly) \( A = P \left ( 1 + \frac{R}{2 \times 100} \right )^{2n} \)

    3. Amount after n years (compounded quarterly) \( A = P \left ( 1 + \frac{R}{4 \times 100} \right )^{4n} \)

  6. When T = 2 years and n = 1, then CI - SI = \( \frac{R \times S.I}{2 \times 100} = P \left ( \frac{R}{100} \right )^2 \)

  7. When T = 3 years and n = 1, then CI - SI = \( \frac{SI}{3} \left [ \left ( \frac{R}{100} \right )^2 + 3 \left ( \frac{R}{100} \right ) \right ] \)

  8. If a certain amount becomes N times in T years, then same amount will be \( N^2 \) times in \( T \times 2 \) years, IsT3 times in T x 3 years and \( N^3 \) times in \( T \times 3 \) Years and \( N \times \) times in \( T \times x \) years.

Solved examples

Example 1:

Find the compound interest for the principal Rs. 3,000 at the rate of 8% per annum for 2 years, interest being compounded annually.

Solution:
Principal = Rs.3,000, r = 8%, n = 2 years.
Amount on C.I.= \( P \left ( 1 + \frac{r}{100} \right )^n \) (annual interest)
\( \begin{align} 3,000 \left ( 1 + \frac{8}{100} \right )^2 \\\\[3pt] = 3,000 \left ( 1 + \frac{108}{100} \right )^2 \\\\[12pt] = 3,000 \times \frac{108}{100} \times \frac{108}{100} = Rs. 3,499.20 \end{align} \)
Compound interest = A - P = 3,499.20 - 3,000 = Rs.499.20

Example 2:

Rohit deposited Rs.7,000 with a finance company for 3 years at an interest of 15% per annum. What is the compound interest and the amount that Rohit gets after 3 years?

Solution:
Principal = Rs.7,000, r = 15 %, n = 3 years.
Amount on C.I.= \( P \left ( 1 + \frac{r}{100} \right )^n \)
\( \begin{align} 7,000 \left ( 1 + \frac{15}{100} \right )^3 \\\\[3pt] = 7,000 \left ( 1 + \frac{115}{100} \right )^3 \\\\[3pt] = 7,000 \times \frac{115}{100} \times \frac{115}{100} \times \frac{115}{100} = Rs. 10.646.125 \end{align} \)
= Rs.10,646.13
= Rs.10,646 (approx.)
Compound interest = A - P = 10,646 - 7,000 = Rs. 3,646

Example 3:

Swathi borrowed Rs. 25,000 from a bank for 1 year at the rate of 10.5% per annum. Compute the amount she has to pay to the bank to clear her loan amount, interest is compounded half-yearly.

Solution:
Principal = Rs.25,000, r = 10.5 %, n = 1 years.
Amount on C.I.= \( P \left ( 1 + \frac{r}{100} \right )^{2n} \) (interest is compounded half-yearly)
\( \begin{align} 25,000 \left ( 1 + \frac{10.5}{200} \right )^{2 \times 1} \\\\[3pt] = 25,000 \left ( 1 + \frac{10 \frac{1}{2}}{200} \right )^2 \\\\[3pt] = 25,000 \left ( 1 + \frac{21}{400} \right )^2 \\\\[3pt] = 25,000 \left ( \frac{421}{400} \right )^2 \\\\[3pt] = 25,000 \times \frac{421}{400} \times \frac{421}{400} = Rs.27,693.91 \end{align} \)

Example 4:

Find the compound interest at the rate of 10% per annum for 2 years on the principal which in four years at the rate of 4% p.a. gives Rs.2,000 as simple interest.

Solution:
Let Rs. P be the principal. S.I. = Rs. 2,000, r = 4 %, n = 5 years.
\( P = \frac{100 \times S.I}{n \times r} = \frac{100 \times 2000}{4 \times 4} \) = Rs.12,500
We have to calculate C.I. for the principal of Rs.12,500 for 2 years at 10% p.a.
\( \therefore \) Amount on C.I.= \( P \left ( 1 + \frac{r}{100} \right )^n \) (Annual interest)
\( \begin{align} = 12,500 \left ( 1 + \frac{10}{100} \right )^2 \\\\[3pt] = 12,500 \left ( \frac{110}{100} \right )^2 \\\\[3pt] = 12,500 \times \frac{110}{100} \times \frac{110}{100} = Rs.15,125 \end{align} \)
Compound interest = A - P = 15,125 - 12,500 = Rs. 2,625

Example 5:

Meera borrowed Rs. 16,000 at \( \17 \frac{1}{2} \ \% \) p.a. simple interest. On the same day she lent it to Asha at 18% p.a but compounded annually. What does she gain at the end of 2 years?

Solution:
P = Rs.16,000, r = \( \17 \frac{1}{2} \ \% \) , n = 2 years.
Simple interest = \( \frac{p \times n \times r}{100} = 16,000 \times 2 \times \frac{17 \frac{1}{2}}{100} \)
\( 16,000 \times 2 \times \frac{35}{2 \times 100} \)
Rs.5,600
We have to calculate compound interest for the same principal.
P = Rs. 16,000, r = 18%, n = 2 years.
\( \therefore \) Amount on C.I.= \( P \left ( 1 + \frac{r}{100} \right )^n \)
\( \begin{align} = 16,000 \left ( 1 + \frac{18}{100} \right )^2 \\\\[3pt] = 16,000 \left ( \frac{118}{100} \right )^2 \\\\[3pt] = 16,000 \times \frac{118}{100} \times \frac{118}{100} = Rs.22,270.40 \end{align} \)
Compound interest = A - P = 22,278.40 - 16,000 = Rs. 6,278.40
Amount gained by Meera = 6,278.40 - 5,600 = Rs.678.40

Example 6:

A certain sum invested at 4% per annum compounded semi-annually amounts to Rs. 7,803 at the end of one year. Find the sum.

Solution:
Amount = Rs. 7,803, r = 4%, n = 1 year interest calculated semi-annually
Amount on C.I.= \( P \left ( 1 + \frac{r}{200} \right )^{2n} \)
\( \begin{align} = 7,803 = p \left ( 1 + \frac{4}{200} \right )^{2 \times 1} \\\\[3pt] = P \left ( \frac{204}{200} \right )^2 \\\\[3pt] = \left ( \frac{51}{50} \right )^2 \\\\[3pt] p = 7,803 \frac{50}{51} \times \frac{50}{51} = Rs.7,500 \end{align} \)

Example 7:

Find the difference between C.I. and S.I. on Rs. 3,000 for 2 years at 7% p.a.

Solution:
P = Rs. 3,000, n = 2 years, r = 7% p.a. Simple interest = \( \frac{p \times n \times r}{100} = \frac{3000 \times 2 \times 7}{100} = 420 \)
Amount on C.I.= \( P \left ( 1 + \frac{r}{100} \right )^n \)
\( \begin{align} = 3,000 \left ( 1 + \frac{7}{100} \right )^2 \\\\[3pt] = 3,000 \times \frac{107}{100} \times \frac{107}{100} = Rs.3,434.70 \end{align} \)
Compound interest = A - P = 3,434.70 - 3,000
= Rs.434.70
Difference between C.I. and S.I. = 434.70 - 420
= Rs.14.70

Example 8:

Find the compound interest on Rs.30,500 at 15% per annum for 2 years compounded annually.

Solution:
\( \begin{align} A = 30,500 \left ( 1 + \frac{15}{100} \right )^2 \\\\[3pt] = 3,000 \time 1.15 \times 1.15 = 40336.25 \end{align} \)
\( \therefore \) Compound interest = (40336.25 - 30,500)
= Rs.9836.25

Example 9:

Find the compound interest on Rs. 12,000 at 12% per annum for 1 year, compounded quarterly.

Solution:
\( R = \frac{12}{4} \) 3% per quarterly P = 12,000
\( \therefore \ Amount = 12,000 \left ( 1 + \frac{3}{100} \right )^4 = 12000 (1.04)^4 \)
= Rs.13506.11
CI = (13506.11 - 12000) = Rs.1506.11

Example 10:

Find the compound interest on Rs. 16000 at 20% per annum for 9 months compounded quarterly.

Solution:
R = 5% per quarter; T = 9 months or 3 quarterly
\( \therefore \ Amount = 16,000 \left ( 1 + \frac{5}{100} \right )^3 = 16000 \times \frac{21}{20} \times \frac{21}{20} \times \frac{21}{20} = Rs.18522 \)
\( \therefore \) CI = (18522 - 16000) = Rs.2522

Example 11:

The difference between the compound interest and simple interest on a certain sum at 10% per annum for 2 years is Rs.631. Find the sum.

Solution:
Let the sum be Rs.x.
\( \begin{align} CI = x \left ( 1 + \frac{10}{100} \right )^2 - x = \frac{21x}{100} \\\\[3pt] SI = \frac{x \times 10 \times 2}{100} = \frac{x}{5} = Rs.18522 \end{align} \)
given, \( \frac{21x}{100} - \frac{x}{5} = 631 \ \Rightarrow \ x = Rs.63100 \)

Solution:
\( \therefore \ Amount = 25000 \times 1.03 \times 1.04 \times 1.05 = Rs.28119 \)

Example 13:

At what rate percent per annum will Rs. 1000 amount to Rs. 1331 in 3 years? The interest is compounded yearly.

Solution:
\( \begin{align} \frac{1331}{1000} = \left ( 1 + \frac{R}{100} \right )^3 \\\\[3pt] \left ( 1 + \frac{11}{10} \right ) = \left ( 1 + \frac{R}{100} \right )^3 \\\\[3pt] \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow \ \frac{11}{10} = 1 + \frac{R}{100} \ or \ R = 10 \% \end{align} \)

Example 14:

A sum of money placed at compound interest doubles itself in 4 years. In how many years will it amount to eight times it self.

Solution:
\( \begin{align} Given \ 2p = p \left ( 1 + \frac{r}{100} \right )^4 \ or , \ \left ( 1 + \frac{r}{100} \right )^4 = 3, \\\\[3pt] or , \ \left [ \left ( 1 + \frac{r}{100} \right )^4 \right]^3 = (2)^3 = 8 \\\\[3pt] or, \ \left ( 1 + \frac{r}{100} \right )^{12} = 8p \end{align} \)
Hence required time is 12 years.

Example 15:

If the compound interest on a certain sum for 2 years at 3% be Rs.101.50, what would be the simple interest?

Solution:
CI on 1 rupee \( = \left ( 1 + \frac{3}{100} \right )^3 - 1 = \frac{609}{10000} \)
SI on 1 rupee = \( \frac{2 \times 3}{100} = \frac{6}{100} \)
\( \begin{align} \frac{SI}{CI} = \frac{6}{100} \times \frac{10000}{609} = \frac{200}{203} \\\\[3pt] SI = \frac{200}{203} \times CI = \frac{200}{203} \times 101.5 \end{align} \)
Rs.100

Example 16:

On a certain sum of money, the simple interest for 2 years is Rs. 50 at the rate of 5% per annum. Find the difference in CI and SI.

Solution:
Difference between C.I and S.I for 2 years = \( \frac{R \times S.I}{200} \) Difference in CI and SI = \( \frac{50 \times 5}{200} = Rs.1.25 \)
Concept: \( 50 = \frac{p \times 2 \times 5}{100} \ \therefore \ p = Rs.500 \)
\( A = 500 \times \frac{105 \times 105}{100 \times 100} = Rs.551.25 \)
\( \therefore \) CI = (551.25 - 500) = Rs.51.25 hence CI - SI = 51.25 - 50 = Rs. 1.25

Example 17:

The difference between the compound interest and the simple interest on a certain sum of money at 5% per annum for 2 years is Rs.1.50. Find the sum.

Solution:
Difference between C.I. and S.I. for 2 years = \( p \left ( \frac{R}{100} \right)^2 \)
So, sum = \( 1.5 \left ( \frac{100}{5} \right )^2 = 1.5 \times 400 = Rs.600 \)

Example 18:

Find the difference between the compound interest and simple interest for Rs. 2500 at 10% per annum for 2 years.

Solution:
Difference between C.I and S > I. for 2 years = \( p \left ( \frac{R}{100} \right)^2 \)
Difference = sum \( \left ( \frac{R}{100} \right)^2 = 2500 \times \left ( \frac{10}{100} \right)^2 = Rs.25 \)

Example 19:

If the difference between CI and SI on a certain sum of money for 3 years at 5% Per annum is Rs. 122, find the sum.

Solution:
Difference between C.I. and S.I for 3 years
\( \begin{align} = \frac{SI}{3} \left [ \left ( \frac{R}{100} \right )^2 + 3 \left ( \frac{R}{100} \right )^2 \right ] \\\\[3pt] Sum = \frac{122 \times 100 \times 100}{5^2(300 + 5)} = Rs.16,000 \end{align} \)

Example 20:

Find the difference between CI and SI on Rs. 8000 for 3 years at 2.5% per annum.

Solution:
Difference between C.I. and S.I for 3 years

= Rs. 15.125
