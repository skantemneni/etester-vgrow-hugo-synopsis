Introduction, Important Compounds of Oxygen

16 Group Elements

This group contains 5 elements ie, oxygen, sulphur, selenium, tellurium and polonium and called oxygen family . The first four members are non-metals and collectively known as chalcogens ( meaning ore forming ) as metal ores normally occur in the form of oxides, sulphides etc.

Occurrence

There are three naturally occurring isotopes of oxygen ie, \( _8O^{16}(99.762 \%), \ _8O^{17}(0.038 \%), \ _8O^{18}(0.200 \%) \) .Oxygen is most abundant element on earth and constitutes 50% by weight of earth crust. Water contains 88.8% oxygen by weight. Dioxygen ( \( O_2 \) ) makes up about 23.2% by weight of atmosphere. Sulphur occurs in the earth crust to the extent of 0.05% mostly as metal sulphides and sulphates. It also occurs in nature in radium minerals such as pitch blend to the extent of \( 5 \times 10^{-9} \% \) . It is known since, 1898.

Preparation and Properties of some Elements of 16 Group

Oxygen

It was discovered by Priestley and Scheele, but named by A.L. Lavoisier.
Methods of preparation : Oxygen is prepared by the following methods :
In lab , \( O_2 \) is obtained by the thermal decomposition of oxygen rich compounds like \( KMnO_4, \ KClO_3 \) etc.eg
\( 2KClO_3(s) \xrightarrow[or \ MnO_2/420K]{670-720 K} 3O_2(g) + 2 KCl(s) \)

Important Compounds of 16 Group Elements

Physical and chemical properties

The important properties of oxygen include

  1. \( O_2 \) is a colourless, odourless and tasteless gas.

  2. It is slightly soluble in water and slightly heavier than air.

  3. It can be liquified by the application of pressure below its critical temperature. The liquid is pale blue in colour and strongly magnetic.

  4. It boils at \( - 183^{\circ} C \) and freezes to bluish white solid at \( - 219^{\circ} C \) .

  5. It is highly reactive and combines with metals and non-metals to from binary compounds, called oxides.

Uses

Oxygen is used

  1. in special occupations.

  2. for artificial respiration.

  3. in welding and cutting as oxyhydrogen or oxy acetylene torch.

Ozone

Among the allotropes of oxygen, \( O_3 \) (ozone) has its special position.

Method of preparation

\( O_3 \) is formed when energy is supplied to \( O_2 \) in the form of UV radiations or silent electric discharge, in an apparatus, called ozoniser. \( 3O_2 \rightleftharpoons 2O_3 \)

Physical properties

It is a pale blue gas with a characteristic pungent odour. It condenses to a deep blue liquid (bp 161.2 K) and to a violet black solid (mp. 80.6 K). It is diamagnetic in nature and acts as a powerful oxidising agent due to liberation of \( O_2 \) . The colour of \( O_3 \) is due to intense absorption of red light.

Chemical properties

Chemical properties of \( O_3 \) involves :

  1. \( O_3 \) is thermodynamically unstable and decomposes to \( O_2 \)
    \( 2O_3 \rightarrow 3O_2; \ \triangle G = -163 \ KJ \ mol^{-1} \)

  2. \( O_3 \) is an extremely powerful oxidising agent.
    \( \begin{align} O_3 + 2H^{+} \rightleftharpoons O_2 + H_2O \\\\[3pt] O_3 + H_2O + 2e^{-} \rightleftharpoons O_2 + 2OH^{-} \end{align} \)

Important Compounds of Sulphur

Sulphur

Method of extraction

S is extracted from underground deposits by pumping superheated steam (- 440 K) down the beds to melt the element and then blowing out molten sulphur with compressed air ( Frasch process ). Sulphur is also obtained from natural gas by separating \( H_2S \) firstly through absorption in ethanolamine and then converting \( H_2S \) into S as :
\( \begin{align} H_2S + \frac{3}{2} O_2 \rightarrow SO_2 + H_2O \\\\[3pt] 2H_2S(g) + SO_2(g) \xrightarrow[673 \ K]{Fe_2O_3 \ catalyst} \frac{3}{8} S_8(g) + 2H_2O(g) \end{align} \)

Hydrogen Sulphide

Methods of preparation

\( H_2S \) is prepared

  1. By passing hydrogen gas through boiling sulphur.
    \( H_2 + S \rightarrow H_2S \)

  2. By the action of dilute HC1 or \( H_2SO_4 \) on iron sulphide.
    \( FeS + H_2SO_4 \rightarrow FeSO_4 + H_2S \)
    Since, hydrogen sulphide is frequently required in the laboratory for qualitative analysis, it is prepared in a Kipres apparatus.

  3. By heating powdered antimony sulphide with pure concentrated HCl
    \( Sb_2 S_3 + 6HCl \rightarrow 2SbCl_3 + 3H_2S \)
    (The \( H_2S \) obtained by this method is pure)

  4. By the action of water an aluminium sulphide
    \( Al_2S_3 + 6H_2O \rightarrow 2Al(OH)_3 + 3H_2S \)
    (This method also gives pure \( H_2S \) .

Sulphur Dioxide

Methods of preparation

It is prepared

  1. By burning sulphur in air as
    \( S + O_2 \rightarrow SO_2 \)

  2. By heating sulphur or Cu or Ag with concentrated \( H_2SO \) as
    \( \begin{align} S + 2H_2SO_4 \xrightarrow{Heat} 2H_2O + 3SO_2 \\\\[3pt] Cu + 2H_2SO_4 \rightarrow CuSO_4 + 2H_2O + SO_2 \end{align} \)

  3. By the decomposition of sulphites or bisulphites, eg
    \( Na_2SO_3 + H_2SO_4 \rightarrow Na_2SO_4 + SO_2 + H_2O \)
    (The \( SO_2 \) obtained by this method is pure).

Physical properties

It is a colourless gas with a pungent, suffocating odour. It is 2.2 times heavier than air and highly soluble in water. It can be easily liquefied to a colourless liquid at \( 10^{\circ} C \) and into a snow like solid at \( 76^{\circ} C \) .

Chemical properties

Important chemical properties exhibited by \( SO_2 \) are as follows :
It is incombustible and non-supporter of combustion. However, burning Mg and K continue to burn in its atmosphere, eg,
\( 3Mg + SO_2 \rightarrow 2MgO + MgS \)

Sulphur Trioxide

Methods of preparation

It is prepared

  1. By heating ferric sulphate strongly
    \( Fe_2(SO_4)_3 \rightarrow Fe_2O_3 + 3SO_3 \)

  2. By oxidation of \( SO_2 \) with oxygen in presence of a catalyst ( commercial method ).
    \( 2SO_2 + O_3 \rightleftharpoons{Pt} 2SO_3 \)
    For this method both \( SO_2 \) and \( O_2 \) should be completely dry. The mixture is passed over platinised asbestos at \( 400^{\circ} C \) , when \( SO_3 \) is formed.

Physical properties

\( SO_3 \) exists in three forms:

  1. \( \alpha - SO_3 \) is a colourless liquid, with m.p. \( 17^{\circ} C \) and b.p. \( 45^{\circ} C \) . Its specific gravity is 1.9228 at \( 25^{\circ} C \) . It solidifies in the form of prismatic needle like crystals. This forms is obtained by repeated fractionation of \( SO_3 \) .

  2. \( \beta - SO_3 \) is formed when \( \alpha \) - form allowed to stand at \( 16^{\circ} C \) for some time. It corresponds to the formula \( S_2O_6 \) .

  3. \( \gamma - SO_3 \) is obtained from \( \beta \) - form on complete drying. It sublimes without melting under ordinary pressure.

Chemical properties

\( SO_3 \) gives following chemical reactions :

  1. \( SO_3 \) is an acidic oxide and dissolves in water forming sulphuric acid with the evolution of heat.
    \( SO_3 + H_2O \rightarrow H_2SO_4 + heat \)

  2. \( SO_3 \) when heated decomposes into sulphur dioxide and oxygen as :
    \( 2SO_3 \rightleftharpoons 2SO_2 + O_2 \)

  3. It dissolves in concentrated \( H_2SO_4 \) forming oleum (fuming sulphuric acid).
    \( H_2SO_4 + SO_3 \rightarrow \underset{oleum}{H_2S_2O_7} \)

Summary

  1. The Group 16 elements have general electronic configuration \( ns^2np^4 \) . They show maximum oxidation state, + 6. Gradation in physical and chemical properties is observed in the group 16 elements. In laboratory, dioxygen is prepared by heating \( KClO_3 \) in presence of \( MnO_2 \) . It forms a number of oxides with metals. Allotropic form of oxygen is \( O_3 \) which is a highly oxidising agent. Sulphur forms a number of allotropes.

  2. Of these, \( \alpha - \) and \( \beta - \) forms of sulphur are the most important. Sulphur combines with oxygen to give oxides such as \( SO_2 \) and \( SO_3 \) . \( SO_2 \) is prepared by the direct union of sulphur with oxygen. \( SO_2 \) is used in the manufacture of \( H_2SO_4 \) . Sulphur forms a number of oxoacids.

  3. Amongst them, most important is \( H_2SO_4 \) . It is prepared by contact process. It is a dehydrating and oxidising agent. It is used in the manufacture of several compounds.
