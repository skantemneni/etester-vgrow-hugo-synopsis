Different Periodic Classification, Modern Periodic law, Nomenclature of elements and the periodic table

Modern Periodic Law

Moseley, while working in Rutherford's team in 1913, studied the wavelength of the characteristic X-rays by using different metals as anticathode and showed that the square root of the frequency of a line (of X-ray spectrum) is related to the atomic number, Z of the target material as
\( \sqrt{v} = a(Z - b) \)
where, a = proportionality constant
b = another constant (It has same value for all the lines of X-rays spectrum)
Z = atomic number.
Thus, Moseley proposed a modified periodic law, according to which, it is the atomic number, and not the atomic weight on which the properties of elements depend. Or in other words, "The physical and chemical properties of the elements are periodic function of their atomic numbers."

Long Form Of Periodic Table

The Modern Periodic Table was further modified to extended form or long form of Periodic Table. This modification was not done by a single person, but it incorporates the modifications by many scientists especially Rang, Werner, ohr, Bury etc.
This table is just graphical representation of aufbau principle . The electronic configuration based classification of elements was given by Bohr that's why the table is also called Bohr's Periodic Table .

Nomenclature Of The Elements

CNIC (Commission on Nomenclature of Inorganic Chemistry) appointed by IUPAC in 1994, approved official names for elements with atomic number 104 to 109. For the elements having Z > 109, IUPAC recommended a nomenclature to be followed for naming these elements until their names are officially recognised. This nomenclature is based on the Latin words for the atomic numbers of the elements.
Very recently, on August 16, 2003, IUPAC approved the name for the element of atomic number 110, as Darmstadtium, with symbol Ds.
The names are derived by using roots for the three digits in the atomic number of the elements and adding the ending -ium. The roots for the numbers are as

Digit Name Abbrevation
0 nil n
1 un u
2 bi b
3 tri t
4 quad q
5 pent p
6 hex h
7 sept s
8 oct o
9 enn e

eg, the element with atomic number 109 will be named as un-nil-ennium and symbolised as une (u for 1, n for 0 and e for 9).

Periodicity and s,p,d,f- blocks, Periodic properties and chemical reactivity

s - block elements

The s-block elements are present on the extreme left in the Periodic Table. All of these are highly reactive metals except hydrogen.

  1. Their general electronic configuration is:
    [inert gas] \( ns^x \) (x = 1, 2)
    If x = 1, elements constitute a groilp of alkali metals placed in group 1 (IA) and if x = 2, elements constitute a group of alkaline earth metals placed in group 2 (IIA).

  2. Alkali metals form monovalent cations while alkaline earth metals form divalent cations.

p-block elements

The p-block elements are present in the right portion of Periodic Table. A clear demarcation line separates the metals and non-metals here. Semi-metals are present along the sides of line. This line begins from B, runs in a zig-zag manner and terminates at \( P_o \) . Thus, this is the only block that contains metals, semi-metals and non-metals.

  1. Metals are solids generally but Hg is liquid at room temperature and Cs and Ga have very low melting point. Non-metals may be solid, liquid or gas.

  2. General electronic configuration of p-block elements is \( ns^2np^x \) (where, x = 1 to 6).

  3. Group number of an element of this block is (2 + x) A based on old system like IIIA, IVA, ... etc, and (12 + x) based on new IUPAC system like, 13, 14, 15, ..., etc.

Transitional elements

The elements having incompletely filled d- orbitals in the ground state or in excited or chemically combined state are called transitional elements (T-families) or d-block elements. All the elements of d-block are present in the middle portion-of Periodic Table, in between the s and p-block elements and are all metals.

Inner-Transition elements

The inner-transition elements or f-block elements have their (n - 2)f orbitals incompletely filled. There are 2 series of inner transition elements, namely, 4f and 5f series. The 4f series begins with cerium (Z = 58) and ends on lutetium (Z = 71) and the 5f series contains elements from thorium (Z = 90) to lawrencium (Z = 103).

Periodic Properties

The properties which are repeated at regular intervals are called periodic properties. The main cause of periodicity in properties is the repetition of similar electronic configuration at regular intervals.

Chemical Reactivity

It is highest at the two extremes of a period and is lowest at the centre. This is because, the elements present at left extreme of a period have the lowest ionisation enthalpy and that present at the right extreme have the highest electron affinity.

  1. Highly reactive elements are always found in combined state.

  2. In the groups, the reactivity of former elements (that present at left extreme) increases while that of latter decreases.
