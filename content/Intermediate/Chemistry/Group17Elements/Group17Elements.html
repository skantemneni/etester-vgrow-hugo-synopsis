Introduction, Important Compounds of Chlorine

Group - 17 Elements

This group consists of five elements namely fluorine (F), chlorine (C1), bromine (Br), dine (I) and astatine (At) . These elements are also called halogen (means sea salts), ie, their alts are abundantly present in sea water.

Occurrence

They do not occur free in nature but in combined state these occur abundantly. Astatine is Idioactive and occur very sparsely. These are mainly found as metal halides although iodine also occurs as iodate ( \( IO_{3^{-}} \) ) . Chlorine is most abundant among halogen and its commercial source is NaCl (common salt). Hypochlorous Acid, HClO

Hypochlorous acid, HClO is known only in solution.
Methods of preparation It is prepared by following methods.
By passing \( Cl_2 \) into \( H_2O \) or into a suspension of \( CaCO_3 \) in water or into an aqueous solution of potassium hypochlorite (KCIO) or bleaching powder \( (CaOCl_2) \) .
\( Cl_2 + H_2O \rightarrow HCl + HClO \)
In this reaction, \( Cl_2 \) undergoes disproportionation into HCl and HClO. Other reactions are
\( \begin{align} CaCO_3 + H_2O + 2Cl_2 \rightarrow CaCl_2 + CO_2 + 2HClO \\\\[3pt] KClO + Cl_2 + H_2O \rightarrow KCl + 2HClO \\\\[3pt] CaCOl_2 + Cl_2 + H_2O \rightarrow CaCl_2 + 2HClO \end{align} \)

Chloric Acid, \( HClO_3 \)

This acid is known only in solution.
Methods of preparation \( HClO_3 \) is prepared by following methods.
By the action of dil \( H_2SO_4 \) on \( (Ba(ClO_3)_2) \) .
\( (Ba(ClO_3)_2) + H_2SO_4 \rightarrow BaSO_4(ppt.) + 2HClO_3 \)
The precipitate of \( BaSO_4 \) is obtained by filtration. The unused \( H_2SO_4 \) is precipitated with baryta water. The filtrate is evaporated in a vacuum desicator ovet concentrated \( H_2SO_4 \) until a 4% solution of \( HClO_3 \) is obtained.
(If the solution containing \( HClO_3 \) is evaporated further more, it gets decomposed into perchloric acid, \( HClO_4 \) .
\( 2KClO_3 + H_2SiF_6 \rightarrow K_2 SiF_6(ppt.) + 2HClO_3 \) .

Perchloric Acid, \( HClO_4 \)

Methods of preparation Perchloric acid, \( HClO_4 \) is prepared as

  1. By heating \( HClO_3 \)
    \( 3HClO_3 \xrightarrow{\triangle} HClO_4 + Cl_2 + 2O_2 + H_2O \)

  2. By treating \( Ba(C1O_4)_2 \) with calculated quantity of dil \( H_2SO_4 \) and then removing the insoluble \( BaSO_4 \) by filtration.
    \( Ba(ClO_4)_2 + H_2SO_4 \rightarrow 2HClO_4 + BaSO_4 \downarrow \)

Metaperiodic Acid, \( HIO_4 \)

Methods of preparation

  1. \( HIO_4 \) is obtained by heating paraperiodic acid, \( H_5IO_6 \) .
    \( H_5IO_6 \xrightarrow{100^{\circ}C} HIO_4 + 2H_2O \)

  2. Its salts which are called metaperiodates is prepared by the thermal decomposition of iodates, eg,
    \( 4KIO_3 \xrightarrow{\triangle} H^{+} + IO{_4^{-}} \)

Paraperiodic Acid, \( HIO_4.2H_2O \ or \ H_5IO_6 \)

Its chemical formula is \( HIO_4.2H_2O \ or \ H_5IO_6 \)
Methods of preparation It is obtained

  1. By the electrolytic oxidation of 50% iodic acid, \( HIO_3 \) on a \( PbO_2 \) anode.

  2. By decomposing silver mesoperiodate, \( Ag_3IO_5 \) with \( Cl_2 \) .
    \( 4Ag_3IO_5 + 6Cl_2 + 10 H_2O \rightarrow 4H_5IO_6 + 12AgCl + 3O_2 \)

Bleaching Powder, \( CaOCl_2.H_2O \)

Its chemical formula is \( CaOCl_2.H_2O \ or \ CaCl(OCl).H_2O \) .
Hence, its correct chemical name is calcium chlorohypochlorite or calcium oxychloride .

Manufacture

It is manufactured by the action of \( Cl_2 \) on dry slaked lime, \( Ca(OH)_2 \) .
\( Ca(OH)_2 + Cl_2 \rightarrow \underset{bleaching \ powder}{Ca(ClO)Cl} + H_2O \)

Physical properties

Bleaching powder is a pale yellow powder. It has strong smell of \( Cl_2 \) . It is soluble in water but the solution is never clear due to the presence of impurities in it.

Oxoacids

Because of its high electronegativity and small size, fluorine forms only one oxoacid, HOF (known as fluoric acid or hypofluorous acid ). Other halogens form many oxoacids. These are stable in aqueous solution or in the form of their salts. These are as follows

Some of the general properties of oxoacids are as follows

  1. Thermal stability of these oxoacids depends on the oxidation number and electronegativity of the central halogen atom. Greater is the oxidation number or electronegativity of halogen atom, greater will be the thermal stability of the acid and vice-versa. Thus, the following two cases may be studied

    1. In case of oxo-acids of a given halogen atom (eg, \( HCIO, \ HClO_2, \ HClO_3, \ HClO_4 \) ), since the electronegativity of the central halogen atom viz, Cl-atom in this case remains the same. the thermal stability depends only upon the oxidation number of the central atom. As the oxidation number of the central atom increases, X-0 bond in the acids becomes more and more covalent and hence, thermal stability of the acids increases. ie. thermal stability of the given acids is in the order : \( HClO < HClO_2 < HClO_3 < HClO_4 \) .

    2. In case of oxoacids, having the same formula and different halogen atom (eg, HClO, HBrO, HIO) , since, the oxidation number of the halogen atoms remains the same, the thermal stability depends only upon the electronegativity of the halogen atom. Thermal stability decreases with the decrease in electronegativity of halogen atom. Thus, thermal stability of the given acids is in the order:
      \( HClO > HBrO > HIO \) .

  2. Acidity (or acid strength) of oxoacids also follows same trend as thermal stability.

  3. Oxidising power also depends upon the oxidation, number and electronegativity of the central halogen atom. Here also following two cases may be possible

    1. Oxidising power of oxoacids of a given halogen atom (eg, \( HCIO, \ HClO_2, \ HClO_3, \ HClO_4 \) ) decreases with the increase in the oxidation number of the central halogen atom. This is because the X-0 bond becomes more and more covalent as the oxidation number of the central atom increases. Thus, the oxidising power of the given acids is in the order: \( HClO > HClO_2 > HClO_3 > HClO_4 \) .

    2. In case of oxoacids, having the same formula and different halogen atom (eg, HClO, HBrO, HIO) , oxidising power decreases with the decrease in the electronegativity of the halogen atom. Thus, oxidising power of the given acids is in the order:
      \( HClO_3 > HBrO_3 > HIO_3 \) .

  4. Stability and basicity of the oxo-anions of a given halogen atom \( (eg, \ ClO^{-} , \ ClO{_2^{-}}, \ ClO{_3^{-}} \ and \ ClO{_4^{-}} \) have been found to be in the following order
    Stability : \( ClO{_4^{-}} > ClO{_3^{-}} > ClO{_2^{-}} > ClO^{-} \)
    Basicity : \( ClO{_4^{-}} < ClO{_3^{-}} < ClO{_2^{-}} < ClO^{-} \)

Inter halogen compounds

Halogens react with each other to produce a number of interhalogen compounds ( \( XX_n' \) where, n = 1, 3, 5 or 7). An interhalogen compound, infact, is regarded as the halide of more electropositive halogen, with a halogen with less electropositive character (electropositive character increases down the group). Thus, as far as this trend is concerned, F cannot form any interhalogen compound as central atom, while I has the maximum tendency to form interhalogen compounds.

  1. Interhalogens can be grouped into four categories namely XX' (eg, CIF, BrF etc) \( XX_3' \) (eg, \( CIF_3 , \ BrF_3 \) etc.) \( XX_5' \) (eg, \( CIF_s, \ IF_5 \) etc) and \( XX_7' \) (eg, \( IF_7 \) ) The oxidation states of X atom in these are +1, +3, +5 and +7 respectively.

  3. The stability of interhalogen compounds increases as the size of central atom increases. Out of two halogens the one with smaller size and higher electronegativity assigned negative oxidation state. XX' interhalogen compounds have linear structure, \( XX_3' \) compounds have bent T structure \( XX_3' \) compounds have square pyramidal structure and \( IF_7 \) have pentagonal bipyramidal structure.

Summary

  1. Group 17 of the periodic table consists of the following elements F, Cl, Br, I and At.These elements are extremely reactive and as such they are found in the combined state only.

  2. The common oxidation state of these elements is -1. However, highest oxidation state can be +7. They show regular gradation in physical and chemical properties. They form oxides, hydrogen halides, interhalogen compounds and oxoacids. Chlorine is conveniently obtained by the reaction of HCl with \( KMnO_4 \)

  3. HCl is prepared by heating NaCl with concentrated \( H_2SO_4 \) . Halogens combine with one another to form interhalogen compounds of the type \( X \ {X^1}_n \) (n = 1, 3, 5, 7) where \( X^1 \) is lighter than X. A number of oxoacids of halogens are known.

  4. In the structures of these oxoacids, halogen is the central atom which is bonded in each case with one OH bond as X-OH. In some cases X = 0 bonds are also found.
