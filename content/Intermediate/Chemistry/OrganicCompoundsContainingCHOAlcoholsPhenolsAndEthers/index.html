Preparation and Properties of Carbon Hydrogen, Oxygen containing Compounds

Physical Properties

  1. Lower alcohols are colourless toxic liquids. They have characteristic odour and burning taste. Higher members are colourless, odourless, wax like solids.

  2. The boiling point of an alcohol is always much higher as compared to hydrocarbon, ether and haloalkane of correspondence masses. This is because of the presence of hydrogen bonding (intermolecular) in alcohols.

  3. Boiling point of alcohols increases as the number of carbon atoms increases. Among the isomeric alcohols, the boiling points are in the order \( 1^{ \circ} > 2^{ \circ} > 3^{ \circ}. \)
    This is because, as the branching increases, van der Waals' forces, and thus, the surface area decreases.

  4. The lower members are miscible with water (due to intermolecular H-bonding between alcohol and water molecules). However, solubility falls as the length of hydrocarbon chain in the alcohol increases.

Chemical Properties

The chemical properties of alcohols are attributed to the polar \( - \underset{ |} {\overset{|} C} - OH \) . Their reactivity is different in different reactions, eg

  1. The reaction in which O-H bond cleaves, the reactivity order of alcohols is \( 1^{ \circ} > 2^{ \circ} > 3^{ \circ}. \)

  2. The reaction in which C-O bond cleaves, the reactivity order of alcohols is \( 3^{ \circ} > 2^{ \circ} > 1^{ \circ}. \)
