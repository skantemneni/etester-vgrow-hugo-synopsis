Introduction, Important Compounds of Nitrogen

Group VA contains 5 elements, namely, nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), arsenic (As), antimony (Sb) and bismuth (Bi). These elements are collectively called pnicogens (taken from Greek word 'pniomigs' meaning suffocating) and their compounds are called pniconides.

Occurrence

Nitrogen constitutes about \( 78\% \) (by volume) of atmosphere, and is therefore, the most common gas. Phosphorus is the only element that does not occur in free state. It is usually found as phosphate. Nitrogen in combined state, is found as nitrates or in proteins and amino acids. The rest elements occur as sulphides or oxides. P is the eleventh most abundant element in the earth, crust.

Some other Properties of Oxides of Nitrogen

  1. \( N_2O \) (nitrous oxide) is a colourless gas fairly soluble in cold water but practically insoluble in hot water, easily liquifiable at room temperature by applying pressure (critical temperature \( 35^{\circ}C) \) . It has a faint odour. When inhaled in moderate quantities, it produces hysterical laughter (that's why called laughing gas). Its higher quantity doses may make a person unconcious. Chemically, it is relatively inert and does not reacts with halogens, alkali metals and even ozone at room temperature. However, it decomposes to \( N_2 \) and \( O_2 \) at \( 600^{\circ}C \) and form metal azides with molten alkali metals as :
    \( NaNH_2 + N_2O \xrightarrow{200^{\circ}C} NaN_3 + H_2O. \)

Phosphorus

Phosphorus does not occur free in nature as it is an extremely active element. In combined form, it occurs as phosphates in rocks and soil, as phospho-proteins in all living cells. The principal minerals containing phosphorus are :
Phosphorite \( Ca_3(PO_4)_2 \) ; apatite \( 3Ca_3(PO_4)_2 CaF_2 \) and chlorapatite \( 3Ca_3(PO_4)_2 . CaCl_2. \)
Besides it is an essential constituent of bones (bone ash contains about \( 80\% \) calcium phosphate) and is also present in blood, brain and other parts of the body. It is necessary for the growth of plants.
Method of preparation It is manufactured by heating bone ash or phosphorite with sand \( (SiO_2) \) and coke (C) in an electric furnace as :
\( 2Ca_3(PO_4)_2 + 6SiO_2 + 10C \rightarrow 6CaSiO_3 + 10CO + P_4 \)

Oxides of Phosphorus

Phosphorus trioxide exists as a dimer and is formulated as \( P_4O_6 \)
Methods of preparation When white P is burn in the limited supply of air both \( P_4O_6 \) and \( P_4O_{10} \) are formed. The vapour of the mixture of oxides are passed through a condensor in which water at \( 50-60^{\circ}C \) is circulated, \( P_4O_{10} \) being solid at \( 60^{\circ}C \) separates out easily.
\( P_4O_{10} \) is obtained by burning P in a free supply of air or oxygen. The pentoxide formed is purified further by sublimation at \( 360^{\circ}C \) .
Properties and uses of oxides of phosphorus Among the various pentoxides, \( P_2O_5 \) has the strongest affinity for water. It can extract water from several inorganic and organic compounds, thus acts as a strong dehydrating agent. It dehydrates \( H_2SO_4 \ to \ SO_3, HNO_3 \ to \ N_2O_5, \) cellulose to carbon and amides to nitriles.

Summary

  1. Groups 13 to 18 of the periodic table consist of p-block elements with their valence shell electronic configuration \( ns^2np^{1 - 6} \) . Groups 13 and 14 were dealt with in Class XI. In this Unit remaining groups of the p-block have been discussed.

  2. Group 15 consists of five elements namely, N, P, As, Sb and Bi which have general electronic configuration \( ns^2np^3 \) . Nitrogen differs from other elements of this group due to small size, formation of \( p \pi - p \pi \) multiple bonds with itself and with highly electronegative atom like O or C and non-availability of d orbitals to expand its valence shell. Elements of group 15 show gradation in properties. They react with oxygen, hydrogen and halogens.

  3. They exhibit two important oxidation states, + 3 and + 5 but +3 oxidation is favoured by heavier elements due to 'inert pair effect'.

  4. Dinitrogen can be prepared in laboratory as well as on industrial scale. It forms oxides in various oxidation states as \( N_2O, \ NO, \ N_2O_3, \ NO_2, \ N_2O_4 \ and \ N_2O_5 \)

  5. These oxides have resonating structures and have multiple bonds. Ammonia can be prepared on large scale by Haber's process . \( HNO_3 \) is an important industrial chemical. It is a strong monobasic acid and is a powerful oxidising agent. Metals and non-metals react with \( HNO_3 \) under different conditions to give NO or \( NO_2 \)

  6. Phosphorus exists as \( P_4 \) in elemental form. It exists in several allotropic forms. It forms hydride, \( PH_3 \) which is a highly poisonous gas. It forms two types of halides as \( PX_3 \) and \( PX_5 \) . \( PCl_3 \) is prepared by the reaction of white phosphorus with dry chlorine while \( PCl_5 \) is prepared by the reaction of phosphorus with \( SO_2Cl_2 \) . Phosphorus forms a number of oxoacids. Depending upon the number of P-OH groups, their basicity varies. The oxoacids which have P-H bonds are good reducing agents.
