Properties of 18th Group Elements, Compounds of Xenon

18 Group Elements

This group of Periodic Table contains He, Ne, Ar, Kr, Xe and Rn. These were called inert gases due to their inert nature. However, these are now called noble gases because some of these elements from compounds under specific conditions

Occurence

All these gases except radon (Rn) are present in atmosphere. Rn results from disintegration of radium is itself radioactive. The total abundance of these elements in dry air (except Rn) is \( \sim \) 1% by volume, out of which Ar is the major component.

General and Physical Properties

  1. Electronic configuration These gases have highly stable \( ns^2 np^6 \) configuration thus,
    have very little tendency to form chemical compounds with other elements (inert gases).

  2. Atomic and ionic radii The atomic radii of 18th group elements correspond to the van der Waals' radii and increases on moving down the group.

  3. Boiling points They have low boiling points in comparison to other elements and their boiling point increases with increases in atomic sizes.

  4. Ionisation energy and electron affinity Noble gases have stable \( ns^2 np^6 \) (fully filled) configuration, thus, have no tendency to add or lose electron.
    Therefore, their ionisation energy is very high. On the other hand, their electron affinity is zero.

  5. Heat of vaporisation and polarisability They possess very low values of heat of vaporisation due to the presence of very weak vander Waals' forces of attraction between their monatomic molecules.
    However, the value of heat of vaporisation increases with atomic number and this shows that there is an increasing polarizability of the larger electronic clouds of the elements with higher atomic number.
    In other words, the polarizability increases down the group as He < Ne < Ar < Kr < Xe.

  6. Solubility They are slightly soluble in water and their solubility generally increases on moving down the group.

  7. Adsorption Except He, all the noble gases are adsorbed by coconut charcoal and the ease of adsorption increases down the group.

  8. Conductivity They have high electrical conductivity at low pressures.

  9. Spectra All of them give characteristic spectra, by which they can be identified.

  10. Liquification Due to the presence of weak van der Waals' forces of attraction, it is difficult to liquify noble gases.
    Ease of liquification increases down the group from He to Rn due to increase in intermolecular forces.

Compounds of Elements of 18 Group

No true compounds of He, Ne and Ar are known.

Xenon Fluorides

Xe forms three binary fluorides, ie, \( XeF_2, \ XeF_4 \) and \( XeF_6 \) by the direct union of elements under appropriate experimental conditions.
In these fluorides, Xe is in +2, +4 and +6 oxidation states respectively

Xenon Trioxide, \( XeO_3 \)

Method of preparation Xenon trioxide ( \( XeO_3 \) ) is prepared by complete hydrolysis of \( XeF_4 \) and \( XeF_6 \) as
\( \begin{align} 6 XeF_4 + 12 H_2 \rightarrow 2 XeO_3 + 4 Xe + 3 O_2 + 24 HF \\\\[3pt] XeF_6 + 3 H_2O \rightarrow XeO_3 + 6 HF \end{align} \)
Method of preparation It is a colourless solid, highly explosive and powerful oxidising agent.
Structure \( XeO_3 \) \( sp^3 \) hybridisation trigonal pyramidal geometry because of the presence of one lone pair of electrons over Xe.

Xenon Tetraoxide, \( XeO_4 \)

Method of preparation Xenon Tetraoxide, ( \( XeO_4 \) ) is prepared by the action of cone \( H_2SO_4 \) on sodium or barium xenate ( \( Na_4XeO_6; \ Ba_2XeO_6 \) ) at room temperature.
\( \begin{align} Na_4 XeO_6 + 2 H_2SO_4 \rightarrow XeO_4 + 2 Na_2SO_4 + 2 H_2O \\\\[3pt] Ba_2 XeO_6 + 2 H_2SO_4 \rightarrow XeO_4 + 2 BaSO_4 + 2 H_2O \end{align} \)
\( XeO_4 \) is purified by vacuum sublimation at 195 K.
Properties It is quite unstable gas and decomposes to xenon and oxygen,
\( XeO_4 \rightarrow Xe + O_2 \)
Structure \( XeO_4 \) has tetrahedral structure due to \( sp^3 \) hybridization of Xe. There are four Xe - O double bonds containing \( p \pi - d \pi \) overlapping.

Summary

  1. Group 18 of the periodic table consists of noble gases. They have \( ns^2 np^6 \) valence shell electronic configuration except He which has \( 1s^2 \) . All the gases except Rn occur in atmosphere. Rn is obtained as the decay product of \( ^{226}Rn \)

  2. Due to complete octet of outermost shell, they have less tendency to form compounds. The best characterised compounds are those of xenon with fluorine and oxygen only under certain conditions.

  3. These gases have several uses. Argon is used to provide inert atmosphere, helium is used in filling balloons for meteorological observations, neon is used in discharge tubes and fluorescent bulbs.
