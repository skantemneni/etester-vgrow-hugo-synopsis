Structure of cyanides and isocyanides

Cyanides and isocyanides are isomeric compounds. Both of these are derivatives of HCN which is known to exist in two isomeri forms, eg,
\( H - C \equiv N \leftrightharpoons H - N \equiv C \)
Thus, the general formula for cyanide and isocyanides are
\( R - C \equiv N \ and \ R - N \equiv C \)
(where, R = alkyl or aryl group)

Physical Properties

  1. Lower members are colourless liquids but higher member; are cystalline solids.

  2. Alkyl cyanides have generally pleasant odours but alky isocyanides have very unpleasant odours.

  3. Their boiling points are higher than corresponding alkyl halides, however, if we compare in between themselves, isocyanides have lower boiling points than corresponding cyanides.

  4. Lower alkyl cyanides are soluble in water, isocyanides on the other hand, are insoluble in water.
