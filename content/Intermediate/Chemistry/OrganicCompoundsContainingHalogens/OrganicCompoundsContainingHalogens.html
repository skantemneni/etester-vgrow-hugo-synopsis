Sbstitution Reactions

A substitution reaction is that which involves the direct replacement (displacement or substitution) of an atom or a group of atoms in an organic molecule by another atom or group of atoms without any change in the remaining part of the molecule. The product obtained as a result of substitution is called the substitution product and the new atom or group of atoms which enters the molecule is called a substituent, eg,

  1. Substitution reactions can further be classified into three categories on the basis of intermediate species which begins the reaction. These are
    Free radical substitution Substitution reaction brought about by free radicals are called free radical substitutions . The best example of this kind of reactions is halogenation of alkanes in the presence of sunlight or heat ie,
    \( \underset{substrate -1}{R-CH_3} + \underset{substrate -2 \ or \ reagent}{X_2} \xrightarrow[heat]{Sunlight} \underset{Product-1}{RCH_2X} + \underset{Product-2}{HX} \)
    Halogenation reactions are mainly given by those hydrocarbons which have at least one hydrogen on spa hybrid carbon.

  2. Free radical reactions run under following three steps (These reactions are infact chain reactions)
    Step I :Chain initiation step It involves the formation of free radicals from the reagent ie,
    \( Reagent \xrightarrow[Heat \ or]{hv \ or \ free \ radical \ initiators} two \ free \ radicals \)
    eg, for the above halogenation reaction
    \( X_2 \xrightarrow[Heat \ or \ hv \ or]{free \ radical \ initiators} X^{\cdot} + X^{\cdot} \)
    This step can be recognised by the fact that free radicals are present on the product side ie, after the arrow.
    Step II : Chain propagation step It Involves attack of adicals formed in step I on the substrate ie,

    eg, in the above reaction.
    \( \underset{substrate -1}{R-CH_3} + \underset{free \ radical}{X^{\cdot}} \rightarrow \underset{free \ radical}{R \overset{\cdot}CH_2} + \underset{Product-2}{HX} \)
    The free radical we got above continues the process as follows
    \( \underset{free \ radical}{R- \overset{\cdot}CH_2} + \underset{substrate -2 \ or \ reagent}{X_2} \rightarrow \underset{Product-1}{RCH_2X} + \underset{free \ radical}{X^{\cdot}} \)
    This free radical formed further attacks on substrate-1
    Thus, the steps continue repeating till one of the substrates is finished.
    (This step can be recognised by the presence of free radicals on both the sides of arrows.)
    Step III : Chain termination step This step comes into action only when one of the substrates is finished. Here the free radicals which are present start joining at random, resulting to various kinds of products in traces ie,
    For the above reaction :
    \( \begin{align} \overset{\cdot}X + \overset{\cdot}X \rightarrow X_2 \\\\[3pt] R - \overset{\cdot}CH_2 + R - \overset{\cdot}CH_2 \rightarrow R - CH_2CH_2 - R \\\\[3pt] R-\overset{\cdot}CH_2 + \overset{\cdot}X \rightarrow R - CH_2X \end{align} \)
    (This step can be recognised by the presence of free radicals before the arrow only)
