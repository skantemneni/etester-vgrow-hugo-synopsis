Pair Of Straight Lines

  1. Pair of straight lines through the origin. A homogeneous equation of second degree
    \( ax_2 + 2hxy + by_2 = 0 \ ...... \ (1) \)
    The slopes \( m_1 \) and \( m_2 \) of these straight lines are given by the relations
    \( \begin{align} m_1 + m_2 = - \frac{2h}{b} \ ...... (2) \\\\[3pt] m_1 m_2 = \frac{a}{b} \ .......(3) \end{align} \)

  2. Angle between the lines represented by \( ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 = 0 \) . If \( \theta \) is an angle, between the lines represented by (1), then
    \( \begin{align} tan \ \theta = \frac{m_1 - m_2}{1 + m_1m_2} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{\pm \sqrt{(m_1 - m_2)^2 - 4 m_1 m_2 - 4 m_1 m_2}}{1 + m_1 m_2} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{\pm \sqrt{4h^2 - 4ab}}{a + b} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow \theta = tan^{-1} \left ( \frac{\pm 2 \sqrt{h^2 - ab}}{a + b} \right ) \end{align} \)
    If a + b = 0, the lines are perpendicular, and if \( h_2 = ab \) , the lines are coincident.

  3. Equation of the bisectors of the angles between the lines
    \( ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 = 0 \)
    Let \( y = m_x \) and \( y = m_2x \) be the equations of the straight lines represented by the given equation.
    The joint equation of these bisectors can be written as
    \( \begin{align} \left ( \frac{y + m_x}{\sqrt{1 +{m_1}^2}} + \frac{y - m_2 x}{{\sqrt{1 +{m_2}^2}}} \right ) \left ( \frac{y + m_x}{\sqrt{1 +{m_1}^2}} - \frac{y - m_2 x}{{\sqrt{1 +{m_2}^2}}} \right ) \\\\[3pt] \frac{x^2 - y^2}{a - b} = \frac{xy}{h} \end{align} \)

  4. The general equation of second degree
    \( ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 + 2gx + 2fy + c = 0 \) represents a pair of straight lines if and only if \( abc + 2fgh - af^2 - bg^2 - ch^2 = 0 \ i.e., \) \( \begin{align} \left| \begin{array}{ccc}a & h & g \\\\ h & b & f \\\\ g & f & c \end{array} \right| = 0 \end{align} \)
    If \( h_2 \neq ab \) , the coordinates \( (x_1, \ y_1) \) of the point of intersection of these lines are obtained by solving the equations
    \( ax_1 + hy_1 + g = 0 \) and \( hx_1 + by_1 + f = 0 \)
    \( \Rightarrow x_1 = \frac{hf - bg}{ab - h^2} \) and \( \frac{gh - af}{ab - h^2} \)

  5. Equation a \( x^2 + 2hxy + by^2 = 0 \) represents a pair of straight lines through the origin parallel to the lines given by the general equation of second degree given in (4) and hence the angle between the lines given in (4) is same as in (2), i.e.,
    \( \theta = tan^{-1} \frac{\pm 2 \sqrt{h^2 - ab}}{a + b} \)
    so that if a + b = 0, the lines are perpendicular and if \( h^2 = ab \) , the lines are parallel.

  6. Equation of the lines joining the origin to the points of intersection of a line and a conic.
    Let L = lx + my + n = 0 and \( S = ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 + 2gx + 2fy + c = 0 \)
    be the equations of a line and a conic, respectively. Writing the equation of the line as \( \frac{lx + my}{-n} = 1 \) and making S = 0 homogeneous with its help, we get \( S = ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 + 2(gx + fy) \left ( \frac{lx + my}{-n} \right ) + c \left ( \frac{lx + my}{-n} \right )^2 = 0 \)
    which being a homogeneous equation of second degree, represents a pair of straight lines through the origin and passing through the points common to S = 0 and L = 0.

Some important results regarding pair of lines

  1. Equation of the pair of lines through the origin perpendicular to the pair of lines \( ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 = 0 \) is \( bx^2 - 2hxy + ay^2 = 0 \) .

  2. The product of the perpendiculars drawn from the point \( (x_1, \ y_1) \) on the lines \( ax_2 + 2hxy + by_2 = 0 \) is
    \( \left | \frac{a{x_1}^2 + 2h x_1 y_1 + b{y_1}^2}{\sqrt{(a - b)^2 + 4h^2}} \right | \)

  3. The product of the perpendiculars drawn from the origin to the lines \( ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 + 2gx + 2fy + c = 0 \) is
    \( \frac{c}{\sqrt{(a - b)^2 + 4h^2}} \)

  4. Equations of the bisectors of the angles between the lines represented by \( ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 + 2gx + 2fy + c = 0 \) are given by
    \( \frac{(x - x_1)^2 - (y - y_1)^2}{a - b} = \frac{(x - x_1)(y - y_1)}{h} \)
    where \( (x_1, \ y_1) \) is the point of intersection of the lines represented by the given equation.

  5. If \( ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 + 2gx + 2fy + c = 0 \) represents two parallel straight lines, then the distance between them is \( 2 \sqrt{\frac{g^2 - ab}{a(a + b)}} \) .
