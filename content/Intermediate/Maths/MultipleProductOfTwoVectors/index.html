Scalar Triple Product

The scalar triple product of the three vectors a, b and c is denoted by [a, b, c], and is defined as \( a \times b.c \)

Properties of the scalar triple product

  1. \( (a \times b). c = a. (b \times c) \)

  2. [a b c] = [b c a] = [c a b] = - [b a c] = - [c b a] = - [a c b]

  3. If \( a = a_1 i + a_2 j + a_3 k \ and \ b = b_1 i + b_2 j + b_3 k, \) then

  4. \( \begin{align} a \times b. c = [a b c] = \left|\begin{array}{ccc} a_1 & a_2 & a_3 \\\\ b_1 & b_2 & b_3 \\\\ c_1 & c_2 & c_3 \end{array} \right| \end{align} \) thus
    [qp rc] = pqr[a b c], where p, q, r are scalars

  5. The volume of the parallelopiped whose adjacent sides are represented by the vectors a, b and c, is \( a \times b. c \) In particulars,

  6. The volume of a tetrahedron ABCD is equal to \( \frac{1}{6} |AB \times AC. AD| \)

  7. Any three vectors a, b and c are coplanar if and only if [a b c] = 0

  8. [a + bcd] = [a c d] + [b c d]

  9. Three vectors a, b and c form a right - handed or left - handed system, according as [a b c] > or < 0

  10. \( \begin{align} (a \times b). (c \times d) = \left| \begin{array}{cc} a.c & b.c \\\\ a.d & b.d \end{array} \right| \end{align} \)

  11. \( \begin{align} [a b c] [u v w] = \left| \begin{array}{ccc} a.u & b.c & c.u \\\\ a.v & b.v & c.v \\\\ a.w & b.w & c.w \end{array} \right| \end{align} \)

  12. Four points with position vectors a, b, c and d will be coplanar if
    [b d c] + [d c a] + [d a b] = [a b c] or equivalently
    [b - a c - a d - a] = 0

Vector Triple Product

The vector product of three vectors a, b and c is the vector
\( \begin{align} a \times (b \times c), \\\\[3pt] a \times (b \times c) = (a. c)b - (a. b)c \ and \\\\[3pt] (a \times b) \times c = (a. c)b - (b. c) - a \end{align} \)
Clearly, \( a \times (b \times c) \neq (a \times b) \times c \) in general. In fact \( (a \times b) \times c = a \times (b \times c) \) if and only if the vectors a and c are collinear.

Scalar products of four vectors

The scalar quadruple product is defined as the dot product of two cross products:
\( (a \times b) \cdot(c \times d), \)
where a, b, c, d are vectors in three-dimensional Euclidean space. It can be evaluated using the identity:
\( (a \times b) \cdot(c \times d) = (a \cdot c})(b \cdot d}) - (a \cdot d})(b \cdot c}). \)
or using the determinant:
\( \begin{align} (a \times b) \cdot(c \times d) = \left | \begin{array}{ccc} a \cdot c & a \cdot d \\\\ b \cdot c & b \cdot d \end{array} \right | \end{align} \)

Vector products of four vectors

The vector quadruple product is defined as the cross product of two cross products:
\( (a \times b) \times (c \times d), \)
where a, b, c, d are vectors in three-dimensional Euclidean space. It can be evaluated using the identity:
\( (a \times b) \times (c \times d) = [a, \ b, \ d] c - [a, \ b, \ c] d, \)
This identity can also be written using tensor notation and the Einstein summation convention as follows:
\( (a \times b) \times (c \times d)= \varepsilon_{ijk} a^i c^j d^k b^l - \varepsilon_{ijk} b^i c^j d^k a^l=\varepsilon_{ijk} a^i b^j d^k c^l - \varepsilon_{ijk} a^i b^j c^k d^l \)
using the notation for the triple product:
\( \begin{align} [a,\ b, \ d] = (a \times b) \cdot d = \begin{vmatrix} a \cdot \hat{i} & b \cdot \hat{i} & d \cdot \hat{i} \\\\ a \cdot \hat{j} & b \cdot \hat{j} & d \cdot \hat{j} \\\\ a \cdot} \hat{k} & b \cdot \hat{k} & d \cdot \hat{k} \end{vmatrix} = \begin{vmatrix} a \cdot \hat{i} & a \cdot \hat{j} & a \cdot \hat{k} \\\\ b\cdot \hat{i} & b \cdot \hat{j} & b\cdot \hat{k} \\\\ d \cdot \hat{i} & d \cdot \hat{j} & d \cdot \hat{k} \end{vmatrix}, \end{align} \)
where the last two forms are determinants with \( \hat{i}, \ \hat{j}, \ \hat{k} \) denoting unit vectors along three mutually orthogonal directions.
Equivalent forms can be obtained using the identity:
\( [b, \ c, \ d] a - [c, \ d, \ a]b+[d,\ a, \ b]c} -[a,\ b, \ c] d = 0 \) .
