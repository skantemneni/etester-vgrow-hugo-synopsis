Angle between the vectors

The scalar product of two vectors a and b is given by |a| |b| \( cos \theta \) where \( \theta(0 \leq \theta \leq \pi) \) is the angle between the vectors a and b. It is denoted by a. b.

Properties of the scalar product

  1. \( \begin{align} a. a = |a|^2 = a^2 \\[3pt] a. b = b. a \end{align} \)

  2. a. (b + c) = a. b + a. c

  3. Two non-zero vectors a and b make an acute angle if a. b > 0, an obtuse angle if a. b < 0 and are inclined at a right angle if a. b = 0

  4. a. b = (projection of a on b) |b|. \( OL = \frac{a.b}{|a|} \)
    = Projection of b on a
    \( OM = \frac{a. b}{|b|} \) = projection of a on b. Component of a in the direction of b is the vector \( OM = \frac{a. b}{|b|^2} a \)

  5. \( \begin{align} (a + b). (a - b) = a^2 - b^2 \\\\[3pt] (a + b)^2 = a^2 + b^2 + 2a. b \\\\[13pt] (a - b)^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2a. b \end{align} \)

  6. If i, j and k are three unit vectors along three mutually perpendicular lines, then
    i.i = j.j = k.k = 1 and
    i.j = j.k = k. i = 0

  7. If
    \( \begin{align} a = a_1 i + a_2 j + a_3 k \ and \ b = b_1 i + b_2 j + b_3 k \ then \\\\[3pt] a.b = a_1 b + a_2 b_2 + a_3 b_3 \\\\[3pt] |a| = \sqrt{a^2_1 + a^2_2 + a^2_3} \ and \ cos \theta = \frac{a_1 b_1 + a_2 b_2 + a_3 b_3}{ \sqrt{a^2_1 + a^2_2 + a^2_3}{ \sqrt{b^2_1 + b^2_2 + b^2_3}}} \end{align} \)

  8. Component of a vector r in the direction of a given vector a in the plane of r and a is \( \frac{r.a}{|a|^2} a. \)

  9. Component of a vector r perpendicular to a vector a in the plane of r and a is \( r - \left( \frac{r.a}{|a|^2} \right) a. \)

Geometrical Applications

Bisector of an angle If a and b are unit vectors along the sides of an angle, then a + b and a - b are the vectors along the internal and external bisectors of the angle, respectively. The bisectors of the angles between the lines r = xa and r = yb are given by

\( r = \lambda \left( \frac{a}{|a|} + \frac{b}{|b|} \right) \ ( \lambda \epsilon R) \)

Reciprocal systems of vectors Let a, b and c be a system of three non-coplanar vectors. Then the system a', b' and c', which satisfies
a.a' = b.b' = c.c' = 1
and a.b' = a.c' = b'.a = b.c' = c.a' = c.b' = 0
is called the reciprocal system to the vectors a, b and c.

Vectorial equation of a line Let a be the position vector of any point P on the line, and let b be a vector parallel to a given line. Then an equation of this line is r = a + tb, t being a parameter.

Vector equation of a line passing through points with position vectors a and b is r = a + t(b - a), t is a parameter.
Shortest distance between two non-intersecting lines passing through the points whose position vector are a and b and are parallel to the vectors c and d is \( \frac{(a - b).(c \times d)}{|c \times d|} \) and the vector determined by the short distance is \( \frac{(a - b).(c \times d)}{|c \times d|^2} c \times d \)

Equation of a plane. The equation of a plane passing through the point with position vector a and parallel to the plane containing b and c, is
\( r = a + \lambda b + \mu c \ or \ [r - a, b, c] = 0 \)
\( \lambda \ and \ \mu \) being parameters. The equation of a plane through three points a, b and c is
\( \begin{align} r = (1 - \lambda - \mu)a + \lambda b + \mu c \\\\[3pt] or \ r.(b \times c + c \times a + a \times b) = [a b c] \\\\[3pt] \lambda \ and \ \mu \end{align} \) being parameters.

Equation of a plane which is at a distance d from the origin having a unit normal n is r. n = d.

Equation of a plane passing through a point with position vector a having a unit normal n is (r - a).n = 0.

Work done. If a force F acts at a point A and displaces it to the point B, then the work done by the force F is F.AB. The moment of a force F applied at B about the point A is the vector \( AB \times F \)

Solved examples

Example 1:

Find the angle between two vectors \( \overrightarrow{a} \) and \( \overrightarrow{b} \) with magnitudes 1 and 2 respectively and when \( \overrightarrow{a}. \overrightarrow{b} = 1. \)
Solution
Given \( \overrightarrow{a}.\overrightarrow{b} = 1, \ |\overrightarrow{a}| = 1 \ and \ |\overrightarrow{b}| = 2. \)
we have
\( \begin{align} \theta = cos^{-1} \left ( \frac{\overrightarrow{a}.\overrightarrow{b}}{|\overrightarrow{a}||\overrightarrow{b}|} \right ) \\\\[3pt] = cos^{-1} \left ( \frac{1}{2} \right ) = \frac{\pi}{3} \end{align} \)

Example 2:

Find angle \( '\theta' \) between the vectors \( \overrightarrow{a} = \hat{i} + \hat{j} - \hat{k} \) and \( \overrightarrow{b} = \hat{i} - \hat{j} + \hat{k} \)
Solution
The angle \( '\theta' \) between two vectors \( \overrightarrow{a} \ and \ \overrightarrow{b} \) is given by
we have
\( \begin{align} cos \ \theta = \frac{\overrightarrow{a}.\overrightarrow{b}}{|\overrightarrow{a}|.|\overrightarrow{b}|} \\\\[3pt] Now \ \overrightarrow{a}.\overrightarrow{b} = (\hat{i} + \hat{j} - \hat{k})(\hat{i} - \hat{j} + \hat{k}) = 1 - 1 - 1 = - 1. \\\\[3pt] Therefore, \ we \ have \ cos \ \theta = - \frac{1}{3} \\\\[3pt] \text{hence the required angle is} \ \theta = cos^{-1} \left ( \frac{1}{3} \right ) \end{align} \)

Example 3:

If \( \overrightarrow{a} = 5 \hat{i} - \hat{j} - 3 \hat{k} \ and \ \overrightarrow{b} = \hat{i} + 3 \hat{j} - 5 \hat{k} \) then show that the vectors \( \overrightarrow{a} + \overrightarrow{b} \) and \( \overrightarrow{a} - \overrightarrow{b} \) are perpendicular.
Solution
We know that two nonzero vectors are perpendicular if their scalar product is zero.
\( \begin{align} Here \ \overrightarrow{a} + \overrightarrow{b} = (5 \hat{i} - \hat{j} - 3 \hat{k}) + (\hat{i} + 3 \hat{j} - 5 \hat{k}) = 6 \hat{i} + 2 \hat{j} - 8 \hat{k} \\\\[3pt] and \ \overrightarrow{a} - \overrightarrow{b} = (5 \hat{i} - \hat{j} - 3 \hat{k}) - (\hat{i} + 3 \hat{j} - 5 \hat{k}) = 4 \hat{i} - 4 \hat{j} + 2 \hat{k} \\\\[3pt] So \ \overrightarrow{a} + \overrightarrow{b}. \overrightarrow{a} - \overrightarrow{b} = = (6 \hat{i} + 2 \hat{j} - 8 \hat{k}).(4 \hat{i} - 4 \hat{j} + 2 \hat{k}) \\\\[3pt] = 24 - 8 - 16 = 0 \\\\[3pt] Hence \ \ \overrightarrow{a} + \overrightarrow{b} \ and \ \overrightarrow{a} - \overrightarrow{b} \ \text{are perpendicular vectors.} \end{align} \)

Example 4:

Find the projection of the vector \( \overrightarrow{a} = 2 \hat{i} + 3 \hat{j} + 2 \hat{k} \) on the vector \( \overrightarrow{b} = \hat{i} + 2 \hat{j} + \hat{k} \)
\( Solution \)
the projection of the vector \( \overrightarrow{a} \) on the vector \( \overrightarrow{b} \) is given by
\( \begin{align} \frac{1}{|\overrightarrow{b}|}(\overrightarrow{a}. \overrightarrow{b}) = \frac{ (2 \times 1 + 3 \times 2 + 2 \times 1)}{\sqrt{(1)^2 + (2)^2 + (1)^2}} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{10}{\sqrt{6}} = \frac{5}{3} \sqrt{5} \end{align} \)

Example 5:

Find \( |\overrightarrow{a}. \overrightarrow{b}| \) , if two vectors \( \overrightarrow{a} \ and \ \overrightarrow{b} \) are such that \( |\overrightarrow{a}| = 2, \ |\overrightarrow{b}| = 3 \ and \overrightarrow{a} . \overrightarrow{b} = 4 \) .
Solution
We have
\( \begin{align} |\overrightarrow{a} - \overrightarrow{b} |^2 = (\overrightarrow{a} - \overrightarrow{b}).(\overrightarrow{a} - \overrightarrow{b}) \\\\[3pt] = \overrightarrow{a} \overrightarrow{a} - \overrightarrow{a} . \overrightarrow{b} \\\\ - \overrightarrow{b} . \overrightarrow{a} + \overrightarrow{b}.\overrightarrow{b} \\\\[3pt] = |\overrightarrow{a}|^2 - 2(\overrightarrow{a}.\overrightarrow{b}) |\overrightarrow{b}|^2 \\\\[3pt] = (2)^2 - 2(4) + (3)^2 \\\\[3pt] Therefore \ |\overrightarrow{a} - \overrightarrow{b}| = \sqrt{5} \end{align} \)

Example 6:

If \( \overrightarrow{a} \) , is a unit vector and \( (\overrightarrow{x} - \overrightarrow{a}). (\overrightarrow{x} + \overrightarrow{a}) = 8 \) , then find \( |\overrightarrow{x}| \) .
Solution
Since \( \overrightarrow{a} \) is a unit vector, \( \begin{align} |\overrightarrow{a}| = 1. \ Also, \\\\[3pt] ( \overrightarrow{x} - \overrightarrow{a}).( \overrightarrow{x} + \overrightarrow{a}) = 8 \\\\[3pt] or \ \overrightarrow{x}. \overrightarrow{x} \\\\ + \overrightarrow{x}. \overrightarrow{a} - \overrightarrow{a}. \overrightarrow{x} - \overrightarrow{a}. \overrightarrow{a} = 8 \\\\[3pt] or \ |\overrightarrow{x}|^2 - 1 = 8 \ i.e. \ |\overrightarrow{x}|^2 - 1 = 9 \end{align} \)
Therefore \ \( |\overrightarrow{x}| = 3 \) (as magnitude of a vector is non negative).

Example 7:

For any two vectors \( \overrightarrow{a} \) and \( \overrightarrow{b} \) , we always have \( |\overrightarrow{a} - \overrightarrow{b}| \leq \ |\overrightarrow{x}||\overrightarrow{a}| \) , (Cauchy - Schwartz inequality).
Solution
The inequality holds trivially when either \( \overrightarrow{a} = \overrightarrow{0} \) or \( \overrightarrow{b} = \overrightarrow{0} \) . Actually, in such a situation we have \( |\overrightarrow{a}. \overrightarrow{b}| = 0 |\overrightarrow{a}| |\overrightarrow{0}| \) . So, let us assume that \( |\overrightarrow{a}| \neq 0 \ |\overrightarrow{b}| \) .
\( \begin{align} \frac{|\overrightarrow{a}. \overrightarrow{b}|}{|\overrightarrow{a}||\overrightarrow{b}|} \\\\ = | cos \ \theta| \ \leq \ 1 \\\\[3pt] Therefore \ |\overrightarrow{a} . \overrightarrow{b}| \ \leq \ |\overrightarrow{a}||\overrightarrow{b}| \end{align} \)

Example 8:

For any two vectors \( \overrightarrow{a} \) and \( \overrightarrow{b} \) , we always have \( |\overrightarrow{a} + \overrightarrow{b}| \leq \ |\overrightarrow{x}||\overrightarrow{a}| \) , (triangle inequality).
Solution

The inequality holds trivially in case either
\( \overrightarrow{a} = \overrightarrow{0} \) or \( \overrightarrow{b} = \overrightarrow{0} \) .(How ?). So, let \( |\overrightarrow{a}| \neq 0 \ |\overrightarrow{b}| \) . Then,
\( \begin{align} |\overrightarrow{a} + \overrightarrow{b}|^2 = (\overrightarrow{a} + \overrightarrow{b})^2 = (\overrightarrow{a} + \overrightarrow{b}).(\overrightarrow{a} + \overrightarrow{b}) \\\\[3pt] = \overrightarrow{a} . \overrightarrow{a} + \overrightarrow{a}.\overrightarrow{b} + \overrightarrow{b}.\overrightarrow{a} + \overrightarrow{b}.\overrightarrow{b} \\\\[3pt] = |\overrightarrow{a}|^2 + 2 \overrightarrow{a} \overrightarrow{b}+ |\overrightarrow{b} \ \text{(scalar product is commutative)} \\\\[3pt] \leq |\overrightarrow{a}|^2 + 2 \overrightarrow{a} . \overrightarrow{b} + \ |\overrightarrow{b}|^2 \ (since \ x \ \leq \ |x| \ \forall \ x \ \epsilon \ R) \\\\[3pt] \leq |\overrightarrow{a}|^2 + 2 |\overrightarrow{a}| . |\overrightarrow{b}| + \ |\overrightarrow{b}|^2 \\\\[3pt] = (|\overrightarrow{a}||\overrightarrow{b}|)^2 \end{align} \)

Example 9:

Show that the points \( A = ( - 2 \hat{i} + 3 \hat{j} + 5 \hat{k}), \ B = ( \hat{i} + 2 \hat{j} + 3 \hat{k}) \) and \( A = ( 7 \hat{i} - \hat{k}) \) are collinear.
Solution
We have
\( \begin{align} \overline{AB} = (1 - 2) \hat{i} + (2 - 3) \hat{j} + (3 - 5) \hat{k} = 3 \hat{i} - \hat{j} - 2 \hat{k}, \\\\[3pt] \overline{BC} = (7 - 1) \hat{i} + (0 - 2) \hat{j} + (-1 - 3) \hat{k} = 6 \hat{i} - 2 \hat{j} - 4 \hat{k}, \\\\[3pt] \overline{AC} = (7 + 2) \hat{i} + (0 - 3) \hat{j} + (- 1 - 5) \hat{k} = 9 \hat{i} - 3 \hat{j} - 6 \hat{k}, \\\\[3pt] |\overline{AB}| = \sqrt{14}, \ |BC| = 2 \sqrt{14} \ and \ |\overline{AC}| = 3 \sqrt{14} \\\\[3pt] Therefore \ |\overline{AC}| = |\overline{AB}| + |\overline{BC}| \end{align} \)
Hence the points A, B and C are collinear
