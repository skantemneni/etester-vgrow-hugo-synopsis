Results Regarding Points in a Plane

Distance Formula

The distance between two points \( P(x_1, \ y_1) \) and \( Q(x_2, \ y_2) \) is given by \( PQ = \sqrt{(x_1 - x_2 )^2 + (y_1 - y_2 )^2} \) . The distance from the origin O(0, 0) to the point \( P(x_1, \ y_1) \) is \( OP = \sqrt{{x_1}^2 + {y_1}^2} \)

Section Formula

If R(x, y) divides the join of \( P(x_1, \ y_1) \) and \( Q(x_2, \ y_2) \) in the ratio \( m : n (m, \ n > 0, \ m \neq n) \) then
\( x = \frac{mx_2 \pm nx_1}{m \pm n} \) and \( y = \frac{my_2 \pm ny_1}{m \pm n} \)

The positive sign is taken for internal division and the negative sign for external division. The mid-point of \( P(x_1, \ y_1) \) and \( Q(x_2, \ y_2) \) is \( \left ( \frac{x_1 + x_2}{2}, \ \frac{y_1 + y_2}{2} \right ) \) which corresponds to internal division when m = n. Note that for external division \( m \neq n \) .

Centroid of a Triangle

If G(x, y) is the centroid of the triangle with vertices \( A(x_1, \ y_1), \ B(x_2, \ y_2) \) and \( C(x_3, \ y_3) \) , then
\( x = \frac{x_1 + x_2 + x_3}{3} \) and \( y = \frac{y_1 + y_2 + y_3}{3} \)

Incentre of a Triangle

If I(x, y) is the incentre of the triangle with vertices \( A(x_1, \ y_1), \ B(x_2, \ y_2) \) and \( C(x_3, \ y_3) \) , then
\( x = \frac{ax_1 + bx_2 + cx_3}{3} \) and \( y = \frac{ay_1 + by_2 + cy_3}{3} \)
a, b and c being the lengths of the sides BC, CA and AB, respectively of the triangle ABC.

Note:

When A, B, C are taken as vertices of a triangle, it assumed that they are not collinear.

Area of Triangle

ABC with vertices \( A(x_1, \ y_1), \ B(x_2, \ y_2) \) and \( C(x_3, \ y_3) \) is
\( \begin{align} \frac{1}{2} \left| \begin{array}{ccc}x_1 & y_1 & 1 \\\\ x_2 & y_2 & 1 \\\\ x_3 & y_3 & 1 \end{array} \right| \\\\[3pt] = \frac{1}{2} |x_1(y_2 - y_3) + x_2(y_3 - y_2) + x_3(y_1 - y_2)| \end{align} \)
and is generally denoted by \( \bigtriangleup \) . Note that if one of the vertex \( (x_3, \ y_3) \) is at O(0, 0), then \( \bigtriangleup = \frac{1}{2}|x_1 y_2 - x_2 y_1|. \)

Condition of Collinearity

Three points \( A(x_1, \ y_1), \ B(x_2, \ y_2) \ and \ C(x_3, \ y_3) \) are collinear if and only if
\( \begin{align} \left| \begin{array}{ccc}x_1 & y_1 & 1 \\\\ x_2 & y_2 & 1 \\\\ x_3 & y_3 & 1 \end{array} \right| = 0 \end{align} \)

Some Useful Points

To show that A, B, C, D are the vertices of a

Parallelogram

Show that the diagonals AC and BD bisect each other.

Rhombus

Show that the diagonals AC and BD bisect each other an a pair of adjacent sides, say, AB and BC are equal.

Square

Show that the diagonals AC and BD are equal and bisect each other, a pair of adjacent sides say AB and BC an equal.

Rectangle

Show that the diagonals AC and BD are equal and bisect each other.

Locus of a Point

To obtain the equation of a set of points satisfying some given condition(s) called locus, proceed as follows.

  1. Let P(h, k) be any point on the locus.

  2. Write the given condition involving h and k and simplify.

  3. Eliminate the unknowns, if any.

  4. Replace h by x and k by y and obtain an equation in terms of (x, y) and the known quantities. This is the required locus.
    (P(x, y) can also be taken as a point on the locus)
