Definite integral as the limit of a Sum

Let f(x) be a continuous function defined on the closed interval [a, b]. Then
\( \int \limits_{a}^{b} f(x) dx = \underset{ \underset{nh = b - a} {n \rightarrow \infty, \ h \rightarrow 0}}{lim} h \sum \limits_{r = 1}^{n} f(a + rh). \) In particular, \( \lim \limits_{n \rightarrow \infty} \sum \limits_{r = 1}^{r = n} \frac{1}{n} f \left ( \frac{r}{n} \right ) = \int \limits_{0}^{1} f(x) dx \)
