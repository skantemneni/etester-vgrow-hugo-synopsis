Cross product

The vector product of two vectors a and b , denote \( a \times b \) , is the vector c with \( |c| = |a| |b| |sin \theta| \) , where \( \theta \) the angle between a and b , with \( 0 \leq \theta \leq \pi \) c is supported by the line perpendicular to a and b , and the direction is such that a, b and c form a right-handed system.

Properties of the vector product

  1. \( a \times b = - b \times a \)

  2. \( a \times a = 0 \)

  3. \( a \times (b + c) = a \times b + a \times c \)

  4. \( (a \times b)^2 = a^2 b^2 - (a. b)^2 \)

  5. \( i \times i = j \times j = k \times k = 0 \ and \ i \times j = k, \ j \times k \ i = j \)

  6. Two non - zero vectors a and b are collinear only if \( a \times b = 0 \)

  7. If \( \begin{align} a = a_1 i + a_2 j + a_3 k \ and \ b = b_1 i + b_2 j + b_3 k, \ then \\\\[3pt] a \times b = \left| \begin{array}{ccc} i & j & k \\\\ a_1 & a_2 & a_3 \\\\ b_1 & b_2 & b_3 \end{array} \right| = (a_2 b_3 - a_3 b_2)i + (a_3 b_1 - a_1 b_3)j + (a_1 b_2 - a_2 b_1)k \end{align} \)

  8. The area of the parallelogram whose adjacent sides are represented by the vectors OA = a and OB = b is \( |a \times b| \) , and the area of the triangle OAB is \( \frac{1}{2} |a \times b| \) . The vector area of the above parallelogram is \( a \times b \)

  9. A unit vector perpendicular to the place of a and b is \( \frac{a \times b}{|a \times b|} \)
    and a vector of magnitude \( \lambda \) perpendicular to the plane of a and b is
    \( \pm \frac{ \lambda (a \times b)}{|a \times b|} \)

  10. If \( a = a_1 i + a_2 j + a_3 k, \ b = b_1 i + b_2 j + b_3 k \ and \ \theta \) is the angle between a and b then
    \( sin^2 \theta = \frac{(a_2 b_3 - a_3 b_2)^2 + (a_3 b_1 - a_1 b_3)^2 + (a_1 b_2 - a_2 b_1)^2}{( \sum a_i^2)( \sum b_i^2)} \)

  11. Two non zero vector a and b are parallel if and only if \( a \times b = 0 \) or equivalently \( \frac{a_1}{b_1} = \frac{a_2}{b_2} = \frac{a_3}{b_3} \)

  12. Perpendicular distance of a point P( p ) from line l which is passing through a point A(a) and is parallel to a unit vector b is \( |(p - a) \times \overrightarrow{b}| \)

Solved examples

Example 1:

Find \( | \overrightarrow{a} \times \overrightarrow{b}|, \ if \ \overrightarrow{a} = 2 \hat{i} + \hat{j} + 3 \hat{k} \ and \ \overrightarrow{b} = 3 \hat{i} + 5 \hat{j} - 2 \hat{k} \)
Solution
We have
\( \begin{align} \left | \begin{array}{ccc} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\\\ 2 & 1 & 3 \\\\ 3 & 5 & -2 \end{array} \right | \\\\[3pt] = \hat{i} (-2 - 15) - (-4 - 9) \hat{j} + (10 - 3) \hat{k} \\\\[3pt] = - 17 \hat{i} + 13 \hat{j} + 7 \hat{k} \\\\[3pt] Here \ | \overrightarrow{a} \times \overrightarrow{b}| =\sqrt{(-17)^2 + (13)^2 + (7)^2} = \sqrt{507} \end{align} \)

Example 2:

Find a unit vector perpendicular to each of the vectors
\( |\overrightarrow{a} + \overrightarrow{b}| \) and \( |\overrightarrow{a} - \overrightarrow{b}| \) , where \( |\overrightarrow{a} = \hat{i} + \hat{j} + \hat{k}, \ \overrightarrow{b} = \hat{i} + 2 \hat{j} + 3 \hat{k} \)
Solution
We have
\( |\overrightarrow{a} + \overrightarrow{b} = \hat{i} + \hat{j} + \hat{k}, \ \overrightarrow{a} - \overrightarrow{b} = - \hat{j} - 2 \hat{k} \)
A vector which is perpendicular to both \( |\overrightarrow{a} + \overrightarrow{b}| \) and \( |\overrightarrow{a} - \overrightarrow{b}| \) is given by
\( \begin{align} (\overrightarrow{a} - \overrightarrow{b}) \times (\overrightarrow{a} - \overrightarrow{b}) = \left | \begin{array}{ccc} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\\\ 2 & 3 & 4 \\\\ 0 & -1 & - 2 \end{array} \right | \\\\[3pt] Now \ | \overrightarrow{c}| = \sqrt{4 + 16 + 4} = \sqrt{24} = 2 \sqrt{4} \end{align} \)
Therefore, the required unit vector is
\( \frac{\overrightarrow{c}}{|\overrightarrow{c}|} = \frac{- 1}{\sqrt{6}} \hat{i} + \frac{- 2}{\sqrt{6}} \hat{j} - \frac{1}{\sqrt{6}} \hat{k} \)

Example 3:

Find the area of a triangle having the points A(1, 1, 1), B(1, 2, 3) and C(2, 3, 1) as its vertices.
Solution
We have
\( \overline{AB} = \hat{j} + 2 \hat{k} \) and \( \overline{AC} = \hat{i} + 2 \hat{j} \) The area of the given triangle is \( \begin{align} \frac{1}{2} | \overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AC} | \\\\[3pt] Now, \ \overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AC} = \left | \begin{array}{ccc} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\\\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\\\ 1 & 2 & 0 \end{array} \right | = - 4 \hat{i} + 2 \hat{j} - \hat{k} \\\\[3pt] Therefore \ | \overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AC} | = \sqrt{16 + 4 + 1} = \sqrt{21} \end{align} \)
Thus, the required area is \( \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{21} \)

Example 4:

Find the area of a parallelogram whose adjacent sides are given by the vectors \( \overrightarrow{a} = 3 \hat{i} + \hat{j} + 4 \hat{k} \ and \ \overrightarrow{b} = \hat{i} - \hat{j} + \hat{k} \)
Solution
The area of a parallelogram with \( \overrightarrow{a} \) and \( \overrightarrow{b} \) as its adjacent sides is given by
\( \begin{align} | \overrightarrow{a} \times \overrightarrow{b} | \\\\[3pt] \left | \begin{array}{ccc} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\\\ 3 & 1 & 4 \\\\ 1 & -1 & 1 \end{array} \right | = 5 \hat{i} + \hat{j} - 4 \hat{k} \\\\[3pt] Therefore \ | \overrightarrow{a} \times \overrightarrow{b} | = \sqrt{25 + 1 + 16} = \sqrt{42} \end{align} \)
and hence, the required area is \( \sqrt{42} \)
