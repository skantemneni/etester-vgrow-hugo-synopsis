Hyperbola

A hyperbola is the locus of a point which moves in a plane such that its distance from a fixed point a focus is always e times its distance from a fixed line a directrix where e > 1 is the eccentricity of the hyperbola
Let a focus be S (ae, 0), and the directrix be \( x = \frac{a}{e} \) . If P(x, y) is any point on the locus, then \( (x - ae)^2 + y^2 = e^2 \left( \frac{a}{e} - x \right)^2 \)
or \( (1 - e^2)x^2 + y^2 = a^2(1 - e^2) \)
or \( \frac{x^2}{a^2} + \frac{y^2}{a^2(1 - e^2)} = 1 \)
If e > 1, i.e. \( 1 - e^2 < 0 \) , then writing \( a^2(1 - e^2) = - b^2 \) we get the equation of the hyperbola in the standard form as
\( \frac{x^2}{a^2} - \frac{y^2}{b^2} = 1 \)

The following terms are used in context of a hyperbola.

  1. The point C(0, 0) is the centre of the hyperbola

  2. The lines A' A and B' B e called the principal axes. A' A is called the transverse axis and its length is 2 a. B' B is called the conjugate axis and its length is 2b.

  3. The points A(a, 0) and A'(-a, 0) are the two vertices of the hyperbola

  4. A line through the focus S of the hyperbola and perpendicular to its transverse axis, is called its latus rectum. If we denote it by LSL', its equation is x = ae and its length is \( \frac{2b^2}{a} \) .
    By symmetry of the curve, or directly, we can see that if the point S' (-ae, 0) is taken as a focus and the line \( x = - \frac{a}{e} \) is taken as a directrix, we get the same equation of the hyperbola
    Thus the hyperbola has two foci, S(ae, 0) and S'(-ae, 0), and also two directrices, \( x = \frac{a}{e} \) and \( x = - \frac{a}{e} \) , respectively. Therefore it has two lateral recta whose equations are, \( x = \pm a e \)

The following are some standard results for a hyperbola:

  1. The parametric equations of a hyperbola or the coordinates of any point on the hyperbola are \( x = a cos \theta, \ y = b sin \theta (x = a sec \theta, \ y = b tan \theta) \) . The point is denoted " \( \theta \) "

  2. An equation of the tangent at the above point " \( \theta \) " is
    \( \frac{x}{a} cos \theta + \frac{y}{b} sin \theta = 1 \left ( \frac{x}{a} sec \theta - \frac{y}{b} tan \theta = 1 \right ) \)

  3. An equation of the normal at the same point " \( \theta \) " is
    \( \frac{ax}{cos \theta} - \frac{by}{sin \theta} = a^2 - b^2 \left ( \frac{ax}{sec \theta} + \frac{by}{tan \theta} = a^2 + b^2 \right ) \)

  4. An equation of the tangent at the point P(x', y') on the hyperbola is
    \( \frac{xx'}{a^2} + \frac{yy'}{b^2} = 1 \)

  5. An asymptote to a curve is a line which touches the curve at infinity. Equation of the asymptotes of the hyperbola \( \frac{x^2}{a^2} - \frac{y^2}{b^2} = 1 \) is
    \( \frac{x^2}{a^2} - \frac{y^2}{b^2} = 0 \) or \( \frac{x}{a} \pm \frac{y}{b} = 0 \)

  6. A hyperbola whose asymptotes are perpendicular to each other is called a rectangular hyperbola and its equation is \( x^2 - y^2 = a^2 \)
    By taking the asymptotes of the rectangular hyperbola as the coordinate axes, its equation can be written as \( x y = c^2 \) (where \( c^2 = \frac{a^2}{2} \) ) and the parametric equation of this rectangular hyperbola is x = c t, \( y = \frac{c}{t} \) , t being the parameter
    A hyperbola whose transverse and conjugate axes are respectively the conjugate and transverse of a given hyperbola is called conjugate hyperbola, of given hyperbola
    Equation of the conjugate hyperbola of the hyperbola \( \frac{x^2}{a^2} - \frac{y^2}{b^2} = 1 \) is \( \frac{x^2}{a^2} - \frac{y^2}{b^2} = - 1 \)

Properties of hyperbola

  1. If PN be the ordinate of a point P on the hyperbola and the tangent at P meets the tranverse axis in T, then ON . \( OT = a^2 \) , O being the origin.

  2. If PM be drawn perpendiculars to the conjugate axis from a point P on the hyperbola and the tangent at P meets the conjugate axis in T.then
    \( OM.OT = - b^2 \) ; O, being the origin

  3. If the normal at P on the hyperbola meets the transverse axis in G, then SG = eSP; S being a foci and e be the eccentricity of the hyperbola

  4. The tangent and normal at any point of a hyperbola bisect the angle between the focal radii to that point

  5. The locus of the feet of the perpendiculars from the foci on a tangent to a hyperbola is the auxiliary circle.

  6. The product of the perpendiculars from the foci on any tangent to a hyperbola is constant and equal to \( b^2 \)

Solved examples

Example 1:

Find the coordinates of the foci and the vertices, the eccentricity,the length of the latus rectum of the hyperbolas:
\( \frac{x^2}{9} - \frac{y^2}{16} = 1 \)
Solution
Comparing the equation
\( \frac{x^2}{9} - \frac{y^2}{16} = 1 \) with the standard equation \( \frac{x^2}{a^2} - \frac{y^2}{b^2} = 1 \)
Here, a = 3, b = 4 and \( c = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2} = \sqrt{9 + 16} = 5 \)
Therefore, the coordinates of the foci are \( (\pm 5, \ 0) \) and that of vertices are \( (\pm 3, \ 0) \) .Also,
The eccentricity \( e = \frac{c}{a} = \frac{5}{3} \) . The rectum = \( \frac{2b^2}{a} = \frac{32}{3} \)

Example 2:

Find the coordinates of the foci and the vertices, the eccentricity,the length of the latus rectum of the hyperbolas:
\( y^2 - 16x = 1 \)
Solution
Dividing the equation by 16 on both sides, we have
\( \frac{y^2}{a^2} - \frac{x^2}{1} = 1 \)
Comparing the equation with the standard equation \( \frac{y^2}{a^2} - \frac{x^2}{b^2} = 1 \) , we find that
a = 4, b = 1 and \( c = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2} = \sqrt{16 + 1} = \sqrt{17} \) .
Therefore, the coordinates of the foci are \( (0, \ \pm \sqrt{17}) \) and that of the vertices are \( (0, \ \pm 4) \) . Also,
The eccentricity \( e = \frac{c}{a} = \frac{\sqrt{17}}{4} \) . The latus rectum \( = \frac{2b^2}{a^2} = \frac{1}{2} \) .

Example 3:

Find the equation of the hyperbola with foci \( (0, \ \pm 3) \) and vertices \( \left ( 0, \ \pm \frac{\sqrt{11}}{22} \right ) \)
\( y^2 - 16x = 1 \)
Solution
Since the foci is on y-axis, the equation of the hyperbola is of the form
\( \frac{y^2}{a^2} - \frac{x^2}{b^2} = 1 \)
Since vertices are \( \left ( 0, \ \pm \frac{\sqrt{11}}{22} \right ), \ a = \frac{\sqrt{11}}{2} \)
Also, since foci are \( (0, \ \pm 3) \) ; c = 3 and \( b^2 = c^2 - a^2 = \frac{25}{4} \) .
Therefore, the equation of the hyperbola is
\( \frac{y^2}{\frac{11}{4}} - \frac{x^2}{\frac{25}{4}} = 1, \ i.e., \ 100y^2 - 44 x^2 = 275. \)

Example 4:

Find the equation of the hyperbola where foci are \( (0, \ \pm 12) \) and the length of the latus rectum is 36.
\( y^2 - 16x = 1 \)
Solution
Since foci are \( (o, \ \pm 12) \) , if follows that c = 12.
Length of the latus rectum \( \frac{2b^2}{1} = 36 \ or \ b^2 = 18a \) \( \begin{align} Therefore \ c^2 = a62 + b^2; \ give \\\\[3pt] 144 = a^2 + 18a \\\\[3pt] i.e., \ a^2 + 18a - 144 = 0. \end{align} \)
So a = -24, 6 .
Since a cannot be negative, we take a = 6 and so \( b62 = 108 \) .
Therefore, the equation of the required hyperbola is \( \frac{y^2}{36} - \frac{x^2}{108} = 1 \) , i.e., \( 3y^2 - x^2 = 108 \)
