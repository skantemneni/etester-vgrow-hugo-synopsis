Direct and indirect

CONTENT :

  1. Direct speech or quoted speech.

  2. Indirect speech or reported speech.

  3. Changing Direct Speech into Indirect Speech.

  4. Changes made when converting Direct to Indirect Speech.

  5. Reporting a question.

There are two ways to report what a person says :

  1. Direct speech and

  2. Indirect speech.

In Direct speech, we use the exact words that someone says with the use of quotation marks. In Indirect speech, we report what someone says without using their exact words. We don't use quotation marks with indirect speech. but we have to change the pronouns and the tense of the verb.

Direct Speech


Direct Speech Direct speech is a sentence that repeats or quotes the exact words spoken by someone. When we use direct speech, we use quotation marks to enclose the words spoken without changing any of them.
Examples : He said to me, "Be carefull" This is the Direct Speech. We merely repeat the exact words used by the speaker and keep the exact words within quotation marks.
Examples : Quoting exactly what someone said: He said, "I'm going to hunt a tiger."

Indirect or Reported Speech


Indirect or Reported Speech Indirect speech refers to repeating what another person says. When we report what someone says, we do not repeat exactly every word that was said. We normally change the tense of the words spoken as what has been said is already in the past. We also make changes to pronouns and other changes where necessary. Quotation marks are not used.
By using our own words to repeat what the speaker said:
Examples : He said to me, "Stop singing the same old song!" (Direct speech)
He told me to stop singing the same old song. (Indirect speech).
This is the Indirect or Reported Speech.
We use the past tense when the main verb used by the speaker is in the past tense.
Here, we do not use th quotation marks.
Instead, we use an infinitive (to stop) and we repeat the pronouns (He).

  3. Present Perfect Tense changes into Past Perfect Tense.

  4. Present Perfect Continuous Tense changes into Past Perfect Continuous Tense.

Change in Pronouns
Rule 1 : If there is I person pronoun in reported speech, it is changed according to speaker.
Rule 2 : If there is II person pronoun in reported speech, it is changed according to object.
Rule 3 : If there is III person pronoun in reported speech, no change is made.

Examples -

  1. Jai says, "Ravi is a good boy".
    Jai says that Ravi is a good boy.

  2. Nitin says to Tushar, "Hari is playing chess".
    Nitin tells Tushar that Hari is playing chess.

  3. Govind will say, "The boys will play cricket".
    Govind will say that the boys will play cricket.

  4. Lata will say to Suuchita, "Kamla will write an essay".
    Lata will tell Suchita that Kamla will write an essay.

  5. He said to me, "Gautam will row the boat now ".
    He told me that Gautam would row the boat then.

  6. He said, "The sun rises in the east".
    He said that the sun rises in the east.

  7. Janak said to me, "Harsh is writing a letter to his father".
    Janak told me that Harsh was writing a letter to his father.

  8. Vinay said, "Rekha has sung a sweet song".
    Vinay said that Rekha had sung a sweet song.

  9. Harish said to Vimal, "Govind has been washing the clothes".
    Harish told Vimal that Govind had been washing the clothes.

  10. He said. "The dyer had dyed the clothes in orange colour".
    He said that the dyer had dyer had clothes in orange colour.

Interrogative sentences

Rule 1 : Reporting verb (say, say, said) changes into "asks, ask, asked.
Rule 2 : If/whelther is used in place of comma and inverted commas.
Rule 3 : If/whether is not used if Reported speech starts with question word.

  1. He said to me, "Do you teach Urdu?"
    He asked me if I taught Urdu.

  2. He said to his friend, "Are the students playing in the field?"
    He asked his friend if the students were playing in the field.

  3. He said to the stranger, "What is your name?"
    He asked the stranger what his name was.

  4. She said to him, "What is mother cooking?"
    She asked him what mother was cooking.

  5. Ravi said to Reena, "Will you accompany me?"
    Ravi asked Reena if she would accompany him.

  6. He said to me, "How is your mother?"
    He asked me how my mother was.

  7. The teacher said to the student, "Why do you disturb the class?"
    The teacher asked the student why he disturbed the class.

  8. Rita said to me, "Will you lend me this classic?"
    Rita asked me if I would lend her that classic."

  9. i said to him, "How do you know this?"
    I asked him how he knew that.

  10. My friend said to me, "Do you want some more sweets?"
    My friend asked me if/whether I wanted some more sweets.

Imperative Sentences

  1. The captain said to the army, "March 1. forward now".
    The captain ordered the army to march forward then.

  2. The students said to the teacher, "Please explain the sentence again".
    The student requested the teacher explain the sentence again.

  3. The doctor said to the patient, "Get up early in the morning".
    The doctor advised the patient to get up early in the morning.

  4. The doctor said to the patient, "Do not smoke again".
    The doctor advised the patient not to smoke again.

Use of let :

  1. He said, "Let me do my work".
    He requested to let him do his work

  2. Mala said, "Let us play chess".
    Mala proposed that they should play chess.

Note :
Speaker - "Let us" changes into

  1. Person Pronoun (I, we) - We should/We might

  2. Person Pronoun (You) - You should/You might

  3. Person Pronoun (He, She, It, They) - They should/ Might

Optative Sentences

  1. He said, "May God bless you, happy journey!"
    He wished that God might bless him happy journey.

  2. The Hermit said, "May God bless you with a son!" .
    The Hermit prayed that God might bless him with a son.

  3. She said,"May the killer of my son go to hell!"
    She cursed that the killer of her son might go to hell.

  4. He said to Manoj, "May you prosper".
    He wished that Manoj might prosper.

Example :

  1. He said, "How beautiful the rain is!"
    He exclaimed that the rain was very beautiful.

  2. Bharat said, "Alas! I have lost my father".
    Bharat exclaimed with sorrow that he had lost his father.

  3. Brijesh said, "Hurray! I topped the class".
    Brijesh exclaimed with joy that he had topped the class.

  4. The boy said, "Bravo! you have done well".
    The boy applauded him saying that he had done well.

  5. He said, "Oh my god! I have done a mistake".
    He exclaimed with regret/sorrow that he had done a mistake.

  6. She said, "God heavens! I have been appointed".
    She exclaimed with surprise that she had been appointed.
