Idioms and phrase

In the given questions below four alternatives are given for the idiom/phrase ubderlined in the sentence. Choose the alternative which best expresses the meaning of the idion/phrase

Solved examples

Example 1:

He didn't tell me directly, but reading between the lines. I think he is not happy with them.

  1. reading slowly and haltingly.

  2. understanding the sense rather than the actual words.

  3. understanding the meaning of words and not the sense.

  4. reading superï¬?cially

Solution:

(b) understanding the sense rather than the actual words.

Example 2:

Gopi works by fits and starts

  1. consistently

  2. irregularly

  3. in high spirits

  4. enthusiastically

Solution:

(b) irregularly

Example 3:

I cannot put up with your misconduct any longer

  1. excuse

  2. refuse

  3. accept

  4. tolerate

Solution:

(d) tolerate

Example 4:

I did not mind what he was saying, he was only talking through his hat.

  1. talking nonsense.

  2. talking ignorantly

  3. talking irresponsibly

  4. talking insultingly

Solution:

(a) talking nonsense

Example 5:

He is so furious that he would go through fire and water to revenge himself on his foe.

  1. approach everybody for help

  2. avail himself of any opportunity

  3. use any conceivable method.

  4. undergo any risk

Solution:

(d) undergo any risk

Example 6:

The watchdogs were asleep when the bulls ran riot.

  1. behaved cleverly

  2. acted without restraint

  3. wandered aimlessly

  4. had the best of time

Solution:

(b) acted without restraint

Example 7:

Inspite of the immense pressure exerted by the millitants, the government decided not to give in

  1. accede

  2. yield

  3. obiige

  4. Conform

Solution:

(b) yield

Example 8:

The young and the old sat cheek by jowl in the large audience

  1. very near

  2. very far

  3. tongue tied

  4. irritated

Solution:

(a) very near

Example 9:

We wanted to keep the gifts as a surprise for mother but my sister gave the game away

  1. lost the game

  2. gave out the secret

  3. played badly

  4. withdrew from the game

Solution:

(b) gave out the secret
