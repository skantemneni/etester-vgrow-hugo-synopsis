Active And Passive Voice

What do we learn by " voice " ?
Voice is the form of verb that shows whether the subject of a sentence does the action (the active voice) or is affected by it (passive voice).
Examples :
In the sentence "James hit the ball". The verb "hit" is in the active voice. In other words, the form "hit" shows that the subject (James) did the action. The sentence "James hit the ball" is an active sentence. In the sentence "The ball was hit by James", the verb "was hit" is the passive voice. In other words, the form "was hit" shows that the subject (The ball) was affected by the action. The sentence "The ball was hit by James" is a passive sentence.
Here are some more examples.
Active : The boy played cricket.
Passive : Cricket was played by the boy.
Active : He wrote a letter.
Passive : The mail was carried by the mailman.
Active : Karan took a picture.
Passive : A picture was taken by Karan.
Active : They took the kids for a walk.
Passive : The kids were taken for a walk by them.
Active or Passive ?
Most of the sentences in English are active sentences.
And if you think about it, they tend to be much more simple than passive sentences. Have a look at these sentences and see for yourself:

  1. We ate lunch/The lunch was eaten.

  2. I have written a book/A book has been written by me.

Imperative Sentences

Definition :
A type of sentence that gives advise or instruction or that expressess request or command Rules :
Active : I from of verb + object
Passive : Let + Object + be + III from of verb.
Active : Do not + I from of verb + object.
Passive : Let + object + not + be III form of verb.
Rules :
Please - Your are requested to
Order - You are ordered to
Advice - You are advised to
Example :
Active :
Please do you work seriously.
Passive : You are requested to do your work seriously.
By using Modal Auxiliaries - :
(Can, could, may might, should, would, must, ought)

Affirmative Sentences

Definition : A sentence that has a positive meaning is called afirmative sentence.
Rules :
Active : Sun. + M.A. + I from of verb + object.
Passive : Object + M.A. + be + III from of verb + by + agent.

Negative Sentences

Definition : A sentence that lacks a positive meaning is a negative sentence.
Rules :
Active : Sub. + M.A. + not + I form of verb + object

Interrogative Sentence

Definition : A type of sentence that asks a question.
Rules :
Active : M.A. + Sub. + I form of verb + object ?
Passive : M.A. + object + be + III of verb + by + agent ?
