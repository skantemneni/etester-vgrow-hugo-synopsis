Article

A, An and the are called articles and are used before nouns to tell there singularity or their uniqueness in general.

Types

  1. Indefinite : A and An

  2. Definite : The

Eg. This is a book.
Note.
Article A is used before a singular countable noun when it is being mentioned for the first time. but when the first letter of the word just after the article 'A' begins with a vowel sound article "An" is used.
Eg.
A book, a table, A boy, A girl, An X-ray, An employee, An apple, An earth quake, An eye, An ear etc.
Some more example: A European, An hour, A year, A hotel, An idea, A young man, A useful book, A university, A unique Idea, An MBA, An MP, An MLA, An SDO, An IAS etc.
2. Definite article.
" The " is Used before a noun to make it specific or to mention it for the second time.for reference.
Eg.
This is a chair, The chair is mine.
Article "The" is used before :
USA, Himalayas, Musical Instruments, UK, Directions (EAST, WEST etc) Rivers (Ganga, Nile) Imaginary lines dividing the Axis (longitudes and latitudes), Island groups (West Indies), Gulf and Bays (Bengal, Mexico), Historical Monuments ( Taj / Char Minar), Oceans (Pacific, Indian), Revolutions (French/Freedom struggle), Holy Books (Ramayana, Gita, etc.), Superlative Adjectives (Best, most intelligent etc.), Heavenly Bodies (Earth, Moon, Stars, Sun).
Article "The" is not used before certain word :
Proper nouns, Abstract nouns and Material nouns in general.
Eg.
Summer, Winter, Mt Everest, Names of Diseases, Iron, Gold, Diamond, Continents (Asia, Africa etc.) Languages (English, French, Hindi), Universal facts (e.g. Man is mortal)
Akabar is a great King.
Lucknow is a Unique city.
