Verb Forms, Syntax, Modals and Tenses

Verbs :
Actions verbs are called verb.
Example: Go, play, sleep, do etc.

Types

  1. Helping verbs: Do, Does, is, am, are, was, were, have, has, had etc.

  2. Modal verbs : Can, could, shall, should, will, would, must

  3. Transitive verbs : play, help, call, enjoy etc.(followed by an abject)

  4. Intransitive verbs : sleep, go, say etc. (never followed by an abject)

  5. Infinitives : to help, to go, to swim etc.

  6. Gerunds : swimming, playing, exercising etc.

Tenses

  1. Present simple : he helps me a lot, I have a book.

  2. Present continuous : He is working in his office.

  3. Present Perfect: He has done his work.

  4. Present Perfect continuous : He has been working since morning.

  5. Past Indefinite : He has absent, He saw a movie.

  6. Present continuous: He was sleeping here.

  7. Past perfect: He has done his work.

  8. Past perfect continuous: He has been doing his work for the last five hours.

  9. Future continuous: He will be helping others.

  10. Future perfect: He will have arrived.

  11. Future perfect continuous: You will have been working on this project.

Rules

  1. Rule 1 : Two subjects joined by as well as, besides, with, together with, along with etc. Take singular or plural verbs according to the 1st subject.
    Eg : He as well as his friends is lucky.

  2. Rule 2 : In a sentence expressing imaginary wish, supposition, the verb used is plural.
    Eg : I wish I were the king.

  3. Rule 2 : In a sentence expressing imaginary wish, supposition, the verb used is plural.
    Eg : I wish I were the king.

  4. Rule 3 : Two nouns joined by AND if refer to the same person/thing, take singular verb otherwise a plural verb.
    Eg : Bread and Better is a good breakfast.

  5. Rule 4 : When two subject are connected by either - or, neither - nor, not only - but also take singular or plural verbs according to the second subject.
    Eg : Either he or his brothers were busy.

  6. Rule 5 : One of, either of, neither of takes a plural noun after them but followed by a singular verb.
    One of his books is stolen.
    Neither of his books is stolen.

  7. Rule 6 : A number of + Plural Noun + Plural verb. and The number of + plural noun + singular verb.
    Eg : A number of books were there.
    The number of people was quite large.

  8. Rule 7 : Universal facts, Idioms and Phrase and habitual actions come under present tense.
    Eg : The sun rises in the east and sets in the west.
    Honesty is the best policy.

  9. Rule 8 : Present perfect tense is used to mention an activity that just happened.
    Eg : He has just finished his work.

  10. Rule 9 : Past perfect tense represents two different activities of past. Thus takes verb 3rd form in one part and verb 2nd form in another part of the sentence.
    Eg : The train had arrived before you came.

  11. Rule 10 : Present perfect continuous tense is used to represent an action started in past but is still in continuation.
    Eg : He has been working in his room since morning.
