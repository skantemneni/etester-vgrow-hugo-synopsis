One Word Substitution

One word substitutions (Substitutes) may be deï¬?ned as single words that are usedin place of group of words to denote a person, an object, a place, a state of mind a profession etc. In common parlance they are termed as single words used to make an expression brief and pertinent. Polonius in "Hamlet" a play by shakespeare, aptly remarks
"Therefore since brevity in the soul of wit"
The tediousness the limbs and outward flourishes I will be brief.
As such the use of one word substitutions tend to obviate the repetition of unnecessary words avoiding verbosity and ambiguity. Rambling style speaks of a rambling state of mind whereas brevity conveys telling effect.
