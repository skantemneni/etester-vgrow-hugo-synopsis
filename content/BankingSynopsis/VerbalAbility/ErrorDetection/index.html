Directions

Some of the sentences are grammatically correct and some are incorrect. Find out Which part Of the sentence has an error and mark that part. If there is no error. mark that pan as your answer

Solved examples

Example 1:

\( \begin{align} \underset{a}{ \underline{ \text{Another baffling change}}} \\\\ \underset{b} { \underline{ \text{that I notice in him now -a -days}}} \\\\ \underset{c}{ \underline{ \text{is that he avoids to speak to me}}} \\\\ \underset{d}{ \underline{ \text{No error}}} \end{align} \)

Solution:

(c) say 'speaking' for 'to speak'

Example 2:

\( \begin{align} \underset{a}{ \underline{ \text{I asked him}}} \\\\ \underset{b} { \underline{ \text{how could he go out}}} \\\\ \underset{c}{ \underline{ \text{if it started raining}}} \\\\ \underset{d}{ \underline{ \text{No error}}} \end{align} \)

Solution:

(b) say 'he could go'

Example 3:

\( \begin{align} \underset{a}{ \underline{ \text{One of the state in which}}} \\\\ \underset{b} { \underline{ \text{Satyagraha was offered was Rajkot}}} \\\\ \underset{c}{ \underline{ \text{where he had spent his youth}}} \\\\ \underset{d}{ \underline{ \text{No error}}} \end{align} \)

Solution:

(a) say 'one of the states'

Example 4:

\( \begin{align} \underset{a}{ \underline{ \text{Hardly the inspector had arrived there}}} \\\\ \underset{b} { \underline{ \text{to investigate the crime}}} \\\\ \underset{c}{ \underline{ \text{when the house was set ablaze}}} \\\\ \underset{d}{ \underline{ \text{No error}}} \end{align} \)

Solution:

(a) Say 'had the insepctor'

Example 5:

\( \begin{align} \underset{a}{ \underline{ \text{Since his arrival at his native town}}} \\\\ \underset{b} { \underline{ \text{he is trying to the best of his power}}} \\\\ \underset{c}{ \underline{ \text{to spread education among the poor masses}}} \\\\ \underset{d}{ \underline{ \text{No error}}} \end{align} \)

(b) say 'has been trying'

Example 6:

\( \begin{align} \underset{a}{ \underline{ \text{In various parts of the country}}} \\\\ \underset{b} { \underline{ \text{ponds just dry down}}} \\\\ \underset{c}{ \underline{ \text{in the scorching heat of summer}}} \\\\ \underset{d}{ \underline{ \text{No error}}} \end{align} \)

Example 7:

\( \begin{align} \underset{a}{ \underline{ \text{He lay the watch on the table}}} \\\\ \underset{b} { \underline{ \text{and then forgot all about it}}} \\\\ \underset{c}{ \underline{ \text{when he went out}}} \\\\ \underset{d}{ \underline{ \text{No error}}} \end{align} \)

Example 8:

\( \begin{align} \underset{a}{ \underline{ \text{Although he draws a reasonably good salary}}} \\\\ \underset{b} { \underline{ \text{he has a large family to support}}} \\\\ \underset{c}{ \underline{ \text{and he finds it difficult to make both ends meet}}} \\\\ \underset{d}{ \underline{ \text{No error}}} \end{align} \)

Example 9:

\( \begin{align} \underset{a}{ \underline{ \text{Being a sunny day}}} \\\\ \underset{b} { \underline{ \text{I decide to skip}}} \\\\ \underset{c}{ \underline{ \text{work and stay at home}}} \\\\ \underset{d}{ \underline{ \text{No error}}} \end{align} \)
