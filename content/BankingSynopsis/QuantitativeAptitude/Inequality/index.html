Inequality

In this chapter, we will study about the problems based on equations and methods of comparing their values. Inequality basically deals with few signs. (<) "Less then"; (>) "greater than"; (=) equal to; \( (\leq) \) "less than or equal to"; \( (\geq) \) "greater than or equal to". Examination pattern of asking questions is as follows :-
In the following example two equations are given. You have to solve both equations and Give the answer

  1. x > y

  2. \( x \geq y \)

  3. x < y

  4. \( x \leq y \)

  5. x = y or the relation can't be established.

Variable

Variables are the values which do not remain fixed and thus are denoted by English alphabets Ex. (a, b, c, m, n, o, p, q, r, x, y, z, etc.)

Constant

Constant are the values which remain fixed. Ex. (1,2,3,4....... and so on ).
The power of any constant unknown variable is always one and the variable has one or more than one is known as linear equation.
