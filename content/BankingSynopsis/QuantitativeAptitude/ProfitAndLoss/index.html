Cost Price (C.P):

It is the price at which an article is purchased. Profit and loss both are are marked at cost price.

Selling Price (S.P):

It is the price at which the article is sold.

Profit or Gain :

If S.P. is greater than the C.P. there is profit or gain.
Profit = S.P - C.P

Loss:

If S.P. is less than the C.P there is loss.
Loss = C.P. - S.P.
If S.P. = C.P. then there is no loss or gain.
Gain on Rs.100 is gain percent and loss on Rs.100 is loss percent

General Formulae:

  1. \( Gain \ \% = \left ( \frac{Gain}{C.P.} \times 100 \right ) \)

  2. \( Loss \ \% = \left ( \frac{Loss}{C.P.} \times 100 \right ) \)

  3. \( S.P = \left ( \frac{100 + Gain \%}{100} \times C.P \right ) \)

  4. \( S.P = \left ( \frac{100 - Loss \%}{100} \times C.P \right ) \)

  5. \( C.P = \left ( \frac{100}{100 + Gain \%} \times S.P \right ) \)

  6. \( C.P = \left ( \frac{100}{100 - Loss \%} \times S.P \right ) \)

Type 1:

Conditional Trick If cost price of x good = S.P. of goods then

  1. \( Gain \ \% = \frac{x - y}{y} \times 100 \) (In case of x > y)

  2. \( Loss \ \% = \frac{y - x}{y} \times 100 \) (In case of x > y)

Type 2:

  1. If \( X_1 \) and \( X_2 \) both are the rates of gain or both are the rates of loss, then, C.P.
    \( = \left ( \frac{100}{x_1 - X_2} \right ) \times \text{Amount of difference} \)
    between selling prices.

  2. If \( X_1 \) and \( X_2 \) one is the rate of gain and another is the rate of loss.
    then, C.P = \( \left ( \frac{100}{X_1 + X_2} \right ) \times \) Amount of difference between selling prices

Miscellaneous Trick:

  1. When a man buys two things on equal price each and in those things one is sold on the profit of x% and another is sold on the loss of x% then there is no loss or gain percent.

  2. When a man sells two things at the same price each and in this pro-cess his loss on first things is x% and gain on second things is x% then in such a type of question, there is always a loss.
    Loss % = x % of x
    \( \frac{x^2}{100} = \left ( \frac{x}{10} \right )^2 \)

  3. Dishonest dealer and less weight gain = \( \frac{Error}{\text{True value - Error}} \times 100 \)
    = 100 gm - Used weight of goods.

  4. If A sells a thing to B at a gain of B sells it to C at a gain of \( R_2 \% \) and C sells is to D at a gain of \( R_3 \% \)
    Then, cost price of D
    = Cost price of \( A \left ( 1 + \frac{R_1}{100} \right ) \left ( 1 + \frac{R_2}{100} \right ) \left ( 1 + \frac{R_3}{100} \right ) \)

  5. If A sells a thing to B at a loss of \( R_1 \% \) ,B sells it to C at loss of \( R_2 \% \) and C sells it to D at a loss of \( R_3 \% \) then
    Cost Price of D
    = Cost price of \( A \left ( 1 - \frac{R_1}{100} \right ) \left ( 1 - \frac{R_2}{100} \right ) \left ( 1 - \frac{R_3}{100} \right ) \)

  6. An article is sold at a profit of x%. Had it sold for Rs.a more, y% would have been gained. Then,
    \( C.P. = \frac{More \ gain \ \times 100}{\text{Difference in percentage profit}} \)

  7. If C.P. of x articles is equal to the selling price of y articles, then profit percentage = \( \frac{x - y}{y} \times 100 \% \)

  8. A dealer purchases a certain number of articles at x a rupee and the same number at y a rupee. He mixes them together and sells them at z a rupee. Then his gain or loss%
    \( = \left [ \frac{2xy}{z(c + y) - 1} \right ] \times 100 \)
    according to + ve or - ve sign.

  9. If a shopkeeper marks his good at x% above his C.P. and allows buyers a discount of y% for cash payment, then there is
    \( \left ( x - y - \frac{xy}{100} \right ) \% \) profit or loss according to + ve or - ve sign.

Solved examples

Example 1:

A Sunglasses is sold at the price of Rs. 770 and gained profit 10%. If it sold at the price of 680 then Find the profit and loss.

Answer:
\( Cost \ price = \frac{100 \times 770}{110} = 700 \)
C.P = Rs.700
Loss = 700 - 680 = Rs.20

Example 2:

A girls purchase a book for Rs. 200 and sell it for Rs 180. What would be her loss percent?

Answer:
loss \ \% = \frac{loss \times 100}{C.P}
(200 - 180) = 20
\( \frac{20 \times 100}{200} = 10 \% \)

Example 3:

Rajan bought a bike for Rs.60,000 and spent Rs. 4000 on repair and Rs. 1000 on transport and sold it with 25% profit. What price did he sell the car?

Answer:
Cost price = Rs. 60,000 Spent on repair = Rs. 4000 transport = Rs. 1000 and profit 25%
So, Cost price = Rs.( 60,000 + 4000 + 1000 ) = Rs. 65,000
So, Selling price = \( \frac{Rs.65,000 \times 125}{100} = 81250. \)
