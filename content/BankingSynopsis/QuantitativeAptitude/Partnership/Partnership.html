Partnership

Persons Two or more than two persons when start and manage the new business in company with of there own choice, the persons start they are called Partners and the trade is done between the partners is known as Partnership.

Ratio of Division of Gains

  1. The amount investment of all the partners are for the same time period, the gain or loss amount is distributed among the partners in the ratio of their investments amounts.

  2. Suppose, Mukesh and Anil invest some money \( Rs.M_1 \) and \( M_2 \) respectively for a year in a business, at the end of year profit will be distributed among them ( Mukesh share of profit ) : ( Anil share of profit ) = Mukesh : Anil.
    Suppose, Mukesh invest \( Rs.M_1 \) for \( T_1 \) months and Anil invest \( Rs.M_2 \) for \( T_2 \) months, then (Mukesh share of profit ) : ( Anil share of profit ) = Mukesh T1 : Anil \( T_2 \) .

  3. When investments are for different time periods, then equivalent capital are calculated a unit of time by taking ( Capital x number of unit of time ).

Working and Sleeping Partners:

A partner who manages the business is known as a working partners and the one who simply invest the money is a sleeping partner.

Solved examples

Example 1:

Rahim invest money into a new business Rs. 7000/- After eight months Ram joined him with Rs. 9000. What should be profit they earn and distributed at the end of the year?

Answer:
\( 7000 \times 12 : 9000 \times 4 = 84000 : 36000 = 7 : 3 \) .

Example 2:

A and B started a buisness with partnership. A joined Rs.6000. How much amount would B invest so that profit after one year be divided in the ratio of 2 : 3?

Answer:
\( \begin{align} \frac{6000 \times 12}{X} \times 12 = \frac{2}{3} \\\\[3pt] 24X = 21600 = X = 9000. \end{align} \)

Example 3:

Started a buisness and he invest in it Rs.45,000. After 4 month Naren joined him with capital of Rs. 75,000. End of the year they gained profit was Rs.47500, Find the Suresh share of profit?

Answer:
Share of profit ratio = Suresh : Naren =
\( \begin{align} 45,000 \times 12 : 75,000 \times 8 \\\\[3pt] = 54,0000 : 60,0000 = 9 : 10 \end{align} \)
Share of profit of Suresh is \( \frac{47500 \times 9}{19} = 22500. \)

Example 4:

Goutam started a retail shop with Rs. 6000, After four months Naren joined and invested Rs. 4000. End of the year they gained Rs. 2400, Find the share of profit of Naren was.

Answer:
\( \begin{align} 6000 \times 12 : 4000 \times 8 = 72000 : 32000 \\\\[3pt] = 72 : 32 = 9 : 4 \end{align} \)
Share of Naren is \( \frac{24000 \times 4}{12} = 800. \)
