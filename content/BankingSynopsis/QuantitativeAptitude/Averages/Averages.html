Averages

The result obtained by adding several quantities together and then dividing this total by the number of quantities is called Average. The main term of average is equal sharing of a value among all, where it may share persons or things. We obtain the average of a number using formula that is sum of observations divide by Number of observations.
Formula 1:
\( Average = \frac{\text{Sum of observations}}{\text{Number of observations}}. \)
Ex: There are Five Numbers, we are going to calculate the average of  29, 31, 33, 37, 49.
\( \begin{align} Average = \frac{(29 + 31 + 33 + 37 + 49 )}{5} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{179}{5} =  35.8 \end{align} \) .
Formula 2:
Average of first n natural numbers = \( \frac{n + 1}{2}. \)
Formula 3: Average of first n square natural numbers = \( \frac{(n + 1)(2n + 1)}{6}. \)
Formula 4: Average of first n cube natural numbers = \( \frac{(n)(n + 1)^2}{4}. \)
Formula 5: Average of first n even numbers = (n + 1)
Formula 6: Average of first n odd numbers = n

Solved examples

Example 1:

average of six numbers is 18, If average of four number is 12. What would be the average of remaining two number ?

Answer:
average of six nos = \( \frac{\text{Sum of observations}}{\text{Number of observations}} = \) \( \frac{\text{sum of 6 nos}}{6} \) = 18
sum of 6 nos = \( (18 \times 6) \)
similarly, the sum of four nos = \( (12 \times 4) \)
the sum of remaining two nos = \( (18 \times 6)-(12 \times 4) = 108 - 48 =60 \)
the average of remaining two number = \( \frac{\text{Sum of observations}}{\text{Number of observations}} = \) \( \frac{60}{2}= 30 \) .

Example 2:

What would be the average of first 20 natural numbers ?

Answer:
Sum of first n natural numbers \( \frac{n(n + 1)}{2} \)
So, we can find easily average of first 20 natural numbers \( 20 \times \frac{21}{2}= 210. \)
So, then Required average is = \( \frac{210}{20} = 10.5. \)

Example 3:

In a college out of 30 students, 16 girls has the average age is 18 years and 14 boys has the average age 17 years. What would be the average age of entire college ?

Answer:
the average age of 16 girls is 18 years. So,the sum of their ages = \( (16 \times 18) = 288 \)
4 boys has the average age 17 years . The sum of their ages = \( ( 14 \times 17 ) = 238 \)
Average age is = \( \frac {288 + 238}{30} = \frac{526}{30} \) = 17.54 years

Example 4:

The average of five numbers is 64.6. The average of first and second numbers is 66 and the average of 4th and 5th number is 86. Find the third number.

Answer:
The average of five numbers is 64.6.the sum of 5 nos = \( (64.6 \times 5) = 323 \)
The average of first two numbers is 66.their sum = \( (66 \times 2) = 132 \)
The average of last two nos is 86.their sum = \( (86 \times 2) = 172 \)
3rd number is = 323 - (132 + 172) = 323 - 304 = 19.

Example 7:

The average marks of 13 students is 40, the first six students is 30 and that of the last six students is 32. Find the value of the 7th number.

Answer:
7th student marks number = Total of 13 students result - ( Total of first six students + Total of last six students results )
\( \begin{align} = 13 \times 40 - ( 6 \times 30 + 6 \times 32 ) \\\\[3pt] = 520 - 180 + 192 = 148. \end{align} \)

Example 8:

Answer:
formula when num excluded and avg decreases is : previous avg + [(decrease in avg) \( \times \) (num of nos excluding the excluded num)]

\( = 29 + (2 \times 4) = 29 + 8 = 37. \)
