Pipes and Cisterns

The method of solving the questions on pipes and cistern is almost same as that of solving the questions of Time and Work, but with a bit difference as negative work, comes into play due to leakage of pipes and cistern.

Important Points :

Inlet : A pipe connected with a cistern or a tank or a reservoir that fills it is known as inlet.
Outlet : A pipe connected with a tank or a cistern or a reservoir to empty it, is known as an outlet.

Important Formuale :

  1. If a pipe can fill a tank in x hours then part of tank filled in 1 hour \( = \frac{1}{x} \)

  2. If a pipe can empty a full tank in y hours, then part of tank emptied in 1 hour \( = \frac{1}{y} \)

  3. If a pipe can fill a tank in x hours and another pipe can empty the full tank in y hours (where y > x), then on opening both the pipes at the same time, the net part filled in 1 hour \( = \left( \frac{1}{x} - \frac{1}{y} \right) \)

Solved examples

Example 1:

Question:
A water reservoir can fill by a tap in 12 hours and leakage can vacant that water reservoir by tap in 18 hours. if the tap of a water reservoir fill and opened simultaneously, then what time will it takes to be filled.

Solution:
\( \frac{1}{12} - \frac{1}{18} = \frac{1}{36} \)
In \( \frac{1}{36} \) time will takes to be filled.

Example 2:

Question:
Two pipes A and B can fill a water reservoir in 12 hours and 14 hours respectively while a third pipe C empties the full water reservoir in 30 hours. If all the A, B, C operate simultaneously, How much time will take be filled the water reservoir ?

Solution:
Step 1: Net part filled by 1 hour = \( \left( \frac{1}{12} + \frac{1}{14} \right) - \frac{1}{30} = \frac{51}{40} \)
Step 2: The time taken will fill the water reservoir is \( \frac{420}{51} \) = 8 hr 12 minutes.
So, time taken to fill the water reservoir 8 hr 12 minutes.

Example 3:

Question:
Two pipes that pipe 1 and pipe 2 can fill a water reservoir in 36 hours and 45 hours respectively. If both the pipes are simultaneously, how much time will be taken to fill the water reservoir ?

Solution:
Here both pipes are filled so we can easily say it is a positive.
Step 1: So at first of we calculate fill pipe in 1 hours time taken.
pipe 1 + pipe 2 together filled a water reservoir in 1 hour = \( \left( \frac{1}{36} + \frac{1}{45} \right) = \frac{9}{180} = \frac{1}{20} \)
Step 2: So in 1 hour it fill with \( \frac{1}{20} \)
hence time taken both the pipe 1 and pipe 2 will fill the water reservoir in 20 hours.

Example 4:

Question:
Two pipes that pipe 1 and pipe 2 can fill a water reservoir in 3 hours and 6 hours respectively. Pipe 3 can empty it in 12 hours. If all the three pipes are opened together, then the water reservoir will be filled in:

Solution:
Net part filled in 1 hour = \( \left( \frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{6} - \frac{1}{12} \right) = \frac{5}{12} \)
The water reservoir will be full in \( \frac{12}{5} \) hrs.

Example 5:

Question:
