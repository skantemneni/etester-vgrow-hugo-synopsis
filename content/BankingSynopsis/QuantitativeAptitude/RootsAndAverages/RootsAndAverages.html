Square and Square root

Points to Remember

  1. A number n is a perfect square number, if \( n = m^2 \)

  2. A perfect number never ends in 2, 3, 7, or 8

  3. The number of zeros at the end of a perfect square is even

  4. The square of an even number is even

  5. The square of an odd number is odd

  6. There are no natural number p and q such that \( p^2 = 2q^2 \)

  7. A number m is a square root of n, if \( n = m \times m = m^2 \) Positive square root of n is written as \( \sqrt{n} \)

  8. If p and q are perfect squares \( (q \neq 0) \) , then

    1. \( \sqrt{p \times q} = \sqrt{p} \times \sqrt{q} \)

    2. \( \sqrt{ \frac{p}{q}} = \frac{ \sqrt{p}}{ \sqrt{q}} \)

    3. A perfect squares leaves remainder 0 or 1 on division by 3

Cubes and Cube Roots

Points to Remember

  1. If n is a perfect cube and \( n = m^3 \) then m is a cube root of n. A cube root of n is written as \( \sqrt[n]{n} \ or \ (n)^{ \frac{1}{3}} \)

  2. The cube root of a perfect cube can be obtained by prime factorization of the number.

  3. The cube root of a Quotient of two perfect cubes is the product of the cube roots of the perfect cubes i.e.,
    \( \sqrt[n]{ab} = \sqrt[n]{a}. \sqrt[n]{b} \)

  4. The cube root of a Quotient of two perfect cubes is the quotient of their cube roots i.e.,
    \( \sqrt[3]{ \frac{a}{b}} = \frac{ \sqrt[3]{a}}{ \sqrt[3]{b}}, \ b \neq 0 \) , where a and b are perfect cubes.

  5. The cubes root of a negative perfect cube is nrgative.

Averages

\( Average = \left( \frac{\text{Sum of observations}}{{ \text{Number of observations}}} \right) \)
Suppose a man covers a certain distance at x kmph and an equal distance at y kmph. Then the average speed during the whole journey is \( \left( \frac{2xy}{x y} \right) \ kmph \)

Solved examples

Example 1:

Question:
Find the average of all prime numbers between 30 and 50

Solution:
Theref are five prime numbers between 30 and 50
They are 31, 37, 42, 43 and 47
\( \therefore \) Required average = \( \left( \frac{31 + 37 + 41 + 43 + 47}{5} \right) = \frac{199}{5} = 39.8 \)

Example 2:

Question:
Find the average of first 40 natural numbers

Solution:
Sum of first n natural numbers = \( \frac{n(n + 1)}{2} \)
So, sum of frist 40 natural numbers = \( \frac{40 \times 41}{2} = 820 \)
\( \therefore \) Required average = \( \frac{820}{40} = 20.5 \)

Example 3:

Question:
The average of first four consective even numbers is 27. Find the largest of these numbers.

Solution:
Let the number be x, x + 2, x + 4 and x + 6 = 0. Then,
\( \frac{x + (x + 2) + (x + 4) + (x + 6)}{4} = 27 \)
\( \Rightarrow \frac{4x + 12}{4} = 27 \Rightarrow x + 3 = 27 \)
x = 24
\( \therefore \) Largest number = (x + 6) = 24 + 6 = 30.

Example 4:

Question:
Evaluate \( \sqrt{248 + \sqrt{51 + \sqrt{159}}} \)

Solution:
Given expression
= \( \sqrt{248 + \sqrt{51 + 13}} = \sqrt{248 + \sqrt{64}} \)
= \( \sqrt{248 + 8} = \sqrt{256} = 16 \)

Example 5:

Question:
What is the square root of 0.0009

Solution:
\( \begin{align} \sqrt{0.0009} = \sqrt{ \frac{9}{10000}} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{ \sqrt{9}}{ \sqrt{10000}} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{3}{100} = 0.03 \end{align} \)
