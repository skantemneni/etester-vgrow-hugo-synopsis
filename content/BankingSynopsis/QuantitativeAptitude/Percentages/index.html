What is percentage?

Percentage is most important chapter and its shortcut tricks are need to know why because it uses other chapters for calculation like data interpretation chapter so we first learn percentage shortcut tricks and Percentage is A fraction or a numerical expression or a ration with 100 assumed as denominator. So we can represent 1 percentage as 1%.

Basic fact and Formula

  1. Now 1% we can express it as a 1% = \( \frac{1}{100} \) . Similarly we can write \( 10 \% = \frac{10}{100} \) and \( 50 \% = \frac{50}{100} \) ( 50 part of total 100 part ).

  2. If a student get 40% marks in Math that means student got marks in math is 40 out of 100 of full marks.

  3. To express \( \frac{p}{q} \) as a percentage
    We express it as \( \frac{p}{q} = \left( \frac{p}{q} \times 100 \right) \% \)
    So we can express like \( \frac{1}{4} = \left( \frac{1}{4} \times 100 \right) \% = 25 \% \)
    and Now, \( 0.4 = \frac{4}{10} = \left( \frac{4}{10} \times 100 \right) \% = 40 \% \)

  4. If the price of a commodity increases by R%, then the reduction in consumption as not to increase the expenditure is \( \left[ \frac{R}{(100 + R)} \times 100 \right] \% \)

  5. If the price of a commodity decreases by R%, then the increase in consumption as not to decrease the expenditure is \( \left[ \frac{R}{(100 + R)} \times 100 \right] \% \)

  6. Results on Population :
    Let the population of a town be P now and suppose it increases at the rate of R% per annul, then :

    1. Population after n years = \( P \left(1 + \frac{R}{100} \right)^n \)

    2. Population n years ago = \( \frac{p}{ \left(1 + \frac{R}{100} \right)^n} \)

  7. Result on Depreciation :
    Let the present value of a machine be P. Suppose it depreciates at the rate of R% per annul, then :

    1. Value of the machine after n years = \( P \left(1 - \frac{R}{100} \right)^n \)

    2. Value of the machine n years ago \( \frac{p}{ \left(1 - \frac{R}{100} \right)^n} \)

    3. If A is R% more then B, then B is less than A by \( \left[ \frac{R}{(100 + R)} \times 100 \right] \% \)
      If A is R% less then B, then B is more than A by \( \left[ \frac{R}{(100 - R)} \times 100 \right] \% \)

Solved examples

Example 1:

Question:
In school exam sachin got 610 marks and failed by 30 marks, It is required to get 40% marks to pass. What is the maximum marks in school examination ?

Solution:
610 + 30 = 640
\( 100 \times \frac{640}{40} = 1600 \)
So, maximum marks is 1600.

Example 2:

Question:
In school examination Harish scored a total of 870 mark out of 1250. what is his approximate % in the exam ?

Solution:
\( 870 \times \frac{100}{1250} = 69.6 \% \)

Example 3:

Question:
The difference between 49% of a number and 14% of the same number is 8920. What would be the 70% of that number ?

Solution:
\( 49 \% - 14 \% = 8920 \)
35% = 8920
\( 75 \% = 8920 \times \frac{70}{35} = 17840 \)

Example 4:

Question:
Sahin invest Rs.8640 which is 24% of his monthly income in share market. What is his annual income ?

Solution:
\( \begin{align} 8640 \times \frac{100}{25} = 36000 \\\\[3pt] 36000 \times 12 = 43200 \end{align} \)

Example 5:

Question:
When denominator of a fraction increased by 20% and numerator of a friction is decreased by 40% then resultant fraction becomes \( \frac{4}{6} \) , what would be the original fraction ?

Solution:
\( \begin{align} \frac{60}{120} = \frac{4}{6} \\\\[3pt] 4 \times \frac{120}{6 \times 60} = \frac{4}{3} \end{align} \)

Example 6:

Question:
In examination, it is required to pass 30% marks. Ramesh gets 294 and he failed by 6 marks. What is the maximum marks of this exam ?

Solution:
294 + 6 = 300
\( 300 \times \frac{100}{30} = 1000. \)
So, the maximum marks of this exam is 1000.

Example 7:

Question:
What percent is 4% of 5%?

Solution:
Required percentage is = \( \left(4 \% \times \frac{100}{5 \%} \right) \% \)
\( \begin{align} \left[ \left( \frac{4}{100} \right) \times \frac{100}{ \left( \frac{5}{100} \right)} \right] \\\\[3pt] = \left(4 \times 100 \times \frac{100}{100 \times 5} \right) \% = 80 \% \end{align} \)

Example 8:

Question:
The population of a city is 175000. In the first year it decreases by 6% and in the second year it increases by 8%. What would be the population of the city at end of the years ?

Solution:
\( \frac{175000 \times 94 \times 108}{100 \times 100} = 177660 \)
