Time and Work

Certain general rules are given below. The candidates will find them quite useful while solving problems related to Time and Work.

  1. While solving problems related to Time and Work we have to compare the work of several working agents (men, women on children).
    To solve this purpose, we need find out the amount of work done by each agent in a common unit time. This unit time is generally taken as an hour, one minute or one day depending upon the nature of the problem.

  2. The total work is assumed to be 1.

  3. If one can do a piece of work in 15 days, then it means that he can do \( \frac{1}{15} \) of work in 1 day

  4. Conversely, if one can do \( \frac{1}{15} \) work in 1 day, it implies that he can complete the work in 15 days.

  5. One day's work \( = \frac{1}{ \text{No.of days required to finish the work}} \)

  6. No. of days required to finish the work \( \frac{1}{ \text{One day's work}} \)

  7. If A does \( \frac{1}{10} \) of work in 1 day and B does \( \frac{1}{15} \) of the same work in 1 day then A + B together will do \( \frac{1}{10} + \frac{1}{15} \) i.e., \( \frac{1}{6} \) of that work in 1 day

  8. If A is thrice as good as a workman as B then
    A's work : B's work = 3 : 1
    and ratio of the times taken by A and B to complete the work = 1 : 3 i.e. A. will take \( \frac{1}{3} \) of the time taken by B to complete that work.

  9. The time required to complete a certain piece of work is increased (or decreased) in the same ratio in which the number of men engaged to do that piece of work is decreased (or increased).
    For example, if the number of men engaged to do a piece of work be changed in the ratio 4 : 5, the time required to complete that piece of work will change in the ratio 5:4.

  10. Time and number of men each are always in direct proportion to work. All the above points can be summarised as given below :
    If \( 'M_1' \) persons can do \( 'W1' \) works in \( 'D_1' \) days and \( 'M_2' \) persons can do \( 'W_2' \) works in \( 'D_2' \) days then we have a very general formula in the relationship of
    \( M_1 D_1 W_2 = M_2 D_2 W_1 \)
    It is an all in one formula and basic formula. We can also conclude that

    1. More men less days and conversely more days less men.

    2. More men more work and conversely more work more men.

    3. More days more work and conversely more work more days.

  11. If 'A' can do a piece of work in x days and 'B' can do it in y days then 'A' and 'B' working together can do the same work in \( \frac{xy}{x + y} \) days.

  12. If A, B and C can do a work in x, y and z days respectively then all of them working together can finish the work in \( \frac{xyz}{xy + yz.xz} \) days.

Time and Distance

  1. Speed: Speed is the distance covered by an object per unit time.
    Thus, if a person travels a distance of 25 kilometres in 5 hours, then his speed is \( 5 \frac{km}{hour}. \)

  2. \( Distance = Speed \times Time \)

  3. \( Speed = \frac{Distance}{Time} \)

  4. \( Time = \frac{Distance}{Speed} \)

  5. However, one has to remember the units of expression. Generally if the distance is in km, we measure time in hours and speed in km per hour and is written as km.
    per/hr and if distance is in metre then time is taken in seconds and speed in metre per second and written as mpsec. To bring uniformity in given data, it is required to remember the conversion factor of speed.

    1. To convert the speed given in the kmph to Metre per/sec . We multiply it by a factor \( \frac{5}{18} \)

    2. To convert the speed given in Metre per/sec . to Km. per/hr., we divide it by a factor \( \frac{5}{18} \) or multiply by a factor \( \frac{18}{5} \) .
      More clearly, 1 kmph = \( \frac{1 \ km}{1 \ hour} \)
      \( = \frac{1000 \ m}{60 \times 60 \ sec} = \frac{5}{18} \ m/sec \)
      Thus, a speed of 18 kilometres per hour = A speed of 5 metres per second

  6. The speed of a body relative to the other body is called its relative speed.

  7. If two bodies are moving in the same direction, then their relative velocity will be equal to the difference of their speeds.

  8. If two bodies are moving in the opposite directions then their relative velocity will be equal to sum of their speeds.

  9. If the speed of a body is changed in a certain ratio say a : b then the time taken by it to travel a given distance is changed in the inverse ratio b : a.

  10. If the ratio of the speeds of A and B is a : b, then the ratio of the time taken by them to cover the same distance is b : a.

  11. If a certain distance is covered at \( x \frac{km}{hr} \) . and the same distance is covered at \( y \frac{km}{hr} \) then the average speed during the whole journey.
    \( = \frac{2 xy}{x + y} \frac{km}{hr} \)
    OR
    Suppose a man covers first half of his journey at the rate of \( x \frac{km}{hr} \) and second half at the rate of \( y \frac{km}{hr} \) , then average speed during the whole journey.
    \( = \frac{2 xy}{x + y} \frac{km}{hr} \)

Incase of Problems on Trains

  1. The time taken by a train in passing a telegraph pole, or a signal post or a standing man is the same as the time taken by the train to cover a distance equal to its own length.

  2. Time taken by a train in passing a platform or a bridge or a train (stationary objects of some length) is equal to the time taken by the train to cover a distance equal to its own length plus the length of the platform or bridge or another train.

  3. If two trains of length x km and y km are moving in the same direction at u kmph and v kmph where u > v, then there relative velocity = (u - v) kmph and the time taken by faster train to cross the slower train
    \( = \left( \frac{x + y}{u - v} \right) \) hrs.

  4. If two trains of length x km and y km are moving in opposite directions at u kmph and v kmph then, their relative speed = (u + v) km/hr and time taken by the trains to cross each other
    \( = \frac{x + y}{u + v} \) hours

  5. If two trains start at the same time from two points A and B towards each other and after crossing each other they take a and b hours in reaching B and A respectively, then, A's speed : B's speed \( = \left( \sqrt{b} : \sqrt{a} \right) \)

Solved examples

Example 1:

Question:
Suresh travel on car and covers a 360 km distances in 8 hours and Ramesh travel on car and covers a 405 km distances in 9 hours. What would be the sum of there speed of both cars ?

Solution:
\( \frac{1}{12} - \frac{1}{18} = \frac{1}{36} \)
Speed of Suresh car = \( \frac{360}{8} = 45 \frac{km}{hr} \)
Speed of Ramesh car = \( \frac{405}{9} = 45 \frac{km}{hr} \)
So, sum of both cars speed = (45 + 45) = \( 90 \frac{km}{hrs} \)

Example 2:

Question:
A boy without any stoppage running on a road at an average speed of \( 80 \frac{km}{hr} \) , and with stoppage he covers the same distance at an average speed of \( 60 \frac{km}{hr} \) . How many minutes per hour does boy stop ?

Solution:
Let the total distance cover by boy on road is x km .
Time take to complete at a speed of \( 80 \frac{km}{hours} = \frac{x}{80} \) hours .
Time take to complete at a speed of \( 80 \frac{km}{hours} = \frac{x}{60} \) hours .
hours .
So he stop to rest \( \left( \frac{x}{80} - \frac{x}{60} \right) hours = \frac{20 x}{60 \times 80} = \frac{x}{240} \ hours. \)
So he rest per hour = \( \frac{ \frac{x}{240}}{ \frac{x}{60}} = \frac{x}{240} \times \frac{60}{x} = \frac{1}{4} \ hours = 15 \ minutes \)
Shortcut Tricks :
Time of rest per hour = \( \left( \frac{ \text{Difference of speed}}{ \text{Speed without stopage}} \right) \)
\( \frac{(80 - 60)}{80} = \frac{1}{4} = 15 \ minutes \)

Example 3:

Question:
A girl goes to his college from his house at a speed of \( 3 \frac{km}{hr} \) and return at speed of \( 2 \frac{km}{hr} \) . If he takes 5 hours in going and coming, the distance between his house and college is :

Solution:
Average speed = \( \left( \frac{2 \times 3 \times 2}{2 + 3} \right) \frac{km}{hr} = \frac{12}{5} \frac{km}{hr} \)
Distance between his house and college he travelled in 5 hours: \( \left( \frac{12}{5 \times 5} \right) km \) = 12 km
The distance between his house and college is : \( \left( \frac{12}{2} \right) = 6 \ km \)

Example 4:

Question:
A bus covers the intial first 46 kms in 42 minutes remaining 26 kms covers in 38 minutes, Find the average speed of the car.

Solution:
46 + 26 = 72 (add both distances)
\( \frac{72 \times 60}{80} = 54 \frac{km}{hr} \)

Example 5:

Question:
Ajay passes a car in 16 seconds, the same car passes a lamp post in 6 seconds, find respective ratio between speed of car and speed of the man.

Solution:
\( \frac{1}{16} : \frac{1}{6} = 3 : 8 \)
So, the respective ratio between speed of car and speed of the man is 3:8
