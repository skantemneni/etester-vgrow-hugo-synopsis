Boats and streams

In water, if the direction of the boat is along the direction of the stream is known as downstream. And the direction of the boat is against the direction of stream is called upstream.
If speed of a boat in still water be 'u' \( \frac{km}{hr} \) . and the speed of the stream be 'v' \( \frac{km}{hr} \) then:
Speed of the boat along the stream (downstream) = speed of boat in still water + speed of stream.
i.e; (u + v) \( \frac{km}{hr} \)
and, Speed of the boat against the stream ( upstream) = speed of boat in still water - speed of stream.
= (u - v) \( \frac{km}{hr} \)
If the speed downstream is 'a' \( \frac{km}{hr} \) and the speed upstream is 'b' \( \frac{km}{hr} \) then,
Speed of boat in still water = \( \frac{1}{2} (a + b) \frac{km}{hr}. \)
Speed (Rate) of stream = \( \frac{1}{2} (a - b) \frac{km}{hr}. \)
speed = \( \frac{distance}{time covered} \)
Note:
1 \( \frac{km}{hr} = \frac{5}{18}\frac{m}{sec} \)

Solved examples

Example 1:

A boat man covers a distance of 24 km against the current and 36 km in direction of current, If it takes each time 6 hours then find the speed of current.

Answer:
Speed of boat man upstream = \( \frac{\text{dist covered in upstream}}{\text{time taken}} = \frac{24}{6} = 4 \frac{km}{hr} \)
Speed of boat man downstream = \( \frac{\text{dist covered in downstream}}{\text{time taken}} = \frac{36}{6} = 6 \frac{km}{hr} \)
Speed of stream =
\( \begin{align} \frac{1}{2} (\text{Speed of downstream} - \text{Speed of upstream}) \\\\[3pt] = \frac{1}{2}(6 - 4) = 1 \frac{km}{hr} \end{align} \) .

Example 2:

A boat running downstream and passes a distances of 16 km in 4 hours while covering the same distance upstream, takes 8 hours, Find the speed of the boat in still water?

Answer:
rate of downstream = \( \left ( \frac{16}{4} \right ) kmph = 4 \ kmph \)
Rate of upstream = \( \left ( \frac{16}{8} \right ) kmph = 2 \ kmph \)
Speed of the boat in still water =
\( \begin{align} \frac{1}{2} (\text{speed of downstream} + \text{speed of upstream}) \\\\[3pt] = \frac{1}{2}(4 + 2) kmph = 3 \ kmph \end{align} \)

Example 3:

A fisherman running upstream and covers 16km and running downstream and covers 28km taking 6 hours each time. Find the velocity of water current.

Answer:
fisherman running speed in still water is u km/hr.
Speed of of current is v km/hr
So, Speed of downstream is (u + v)
Speed of upstream is (u - v)
So, 6(u - v) = 16 and 6(u + v) = 28
6u + 6v = 28
6u - 6v = 16
Subtracting we get
12v = 12
v = 1 km/hr.

Example 4:

A boy can row 4 km against the stream in 24 minutes and back in 20 minutes. What would be the rate of current?

Answer:
24 minutes converted into hr = \( \frac{24}{60} \) hr
so, Speed of boy upstream 4 km in 24 minutes = \( \frac{4 \times 60}{24} = 10 km/hr. \)
Speed of boy downstream 4 km in 20 minutes = \( \frac{4 \times 60}{20} = 12 km/hr. \)
So, Rate of current is : \( \frac{1}{2} (downstream - upstream) = \frac{1}{2}(12 - 10) = 1 \frac{km}{hr}. \)

Example 5:

The speed of a boat in still water is 18 km/hr and the rate of current is 6 km/hr. What distance passes downstream in 14 minutes?

Answer:
Speed downstream = (18 + 6) km/hr = 24 km/hr.
Distance passes = \( \frac{24 \times 14}{60} km = 5.6 \ km \)

Example 6:

Speed of a boat in still water is 12 km/hr and the speed of the stream is 4 km/hr. A boy row to place which at a distance of 80 km and then he come return to his starting point.

Answer:
12 - 4 = 8(Speed of upstream)
12 + 4 = 16(speed of downstream)
So, total time taken \( \left ( \frac{80}{8} + \frac{80}{16} \right ) hours \)
= 15 hours.
