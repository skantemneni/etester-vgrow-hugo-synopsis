Ratio

The ratio of two quantities of the same kind and in the same units is a comparison by division of the measure of two quantities.
In other words, ratio of two quantities of the same kind is the relation between their measures and determines how many times the one quantity is greater than or less than the other quantity.
Thus the ratio of a to b is the fraction \( \frac{a}{b} \) and is generally written as a:b.
Ex:
The ratio of Rs.50 to Rs.75 is 50 : 75 or 2 : 3.
Ex:
The ratio of 4m to 160 cm is 400 : 160 or \( \frac{400}{160} \) or 5 : 2.
Ex:
There is no ratio between 3 metres and Rs. 10.
Note:
Since the ratio of two quantities of the same kind determines how many times one quantity is contained by the other, so the ratio of any two quantities of the same kind is an abstract quantity. In other words, ratio has no unit or it is independent of the units used in the quantities compared.
For the ratio a : b, the quantities a and b are called terms of the ratio. The former 'a' is called the first term or antecedent and the latter 'b' is known as the second term or consequent.
The value of ratio remains the same, if both the antecedent and the consequent are multiplied or divided by the same non-zero quantity, because for any non-zero m,
\( \frac{a}{b} = \frac{ma}{mb} \ \Rightarrow \ a : b \) is same as ma : mb and \( \frac{a}{b} = \frac{\frac{a}{m}}{\frac{b}{m}} \ \Rightarrow \ a : b \) is same as \( \frac{a}{m} : \frac{b}{m} \)

Kinds of Ratios :

Ratio of Greater inequality :

A ratio a : b is called a ratio of greater inequality if antecedent 'a' > consequent 'b'.
Ex: 7 : 4, 9 : 5, 3 : 2

Ratio of Less Inequality :

A ratio a : b is said to be a ratio of less inequality if a < b.
Ex: 4 : 7, 5 : 9, 2 : 3.

Ratio of equality :

A ratio a : b is said to be a ratio of equality if a = b.
Ex: 2 : 2, 4 : 4, 5 : 5.

Compounded Ratio

If two or more ratios are multiplied term wise, i.e. the antecedents to that of the consequents, the ratio thus obtained is called their compounded ratio.

  1. The compounded ratio of a: b and c : d is ac : bd.

  2. The compounded ratio of a : b, c : d, and e : f is ace : bdf.

Duplicate Ratio

It is the compounded ratio of two equal ratios.
Thus, the duplicate ratio of a : b is aa : bb, i.e, \( a^2 : b^2 \) .
Ex: \( \frac{4}{9} \) is called the duplicate ratio of \( \frac{2}{3} \)

Triplicate Ratio

It is the compounded ratio of three equal ratios.
Thus, the triplicate ratio of a : b is aaa : bbb, i.e., \( a^2 : b^3 \)
Ex: \( \frac{8}{27} \) is called triplicate ratio of \( \frac{2}{3} \)

Sub-duplicate Ratio

A ratio x : y is the sub-duplicate ratio of a ratio a : b if the duplicate ratio of x : y is a : b.
\( i.e., \ \frac{x^2}{y^2} = \frac{a}{b} \ or , \ \frac{x}{y} = \frac{\sqrt{x}}{\sqrt{b}}. \)
Ex: \( \frac{4}{5} \) is the sub-duplicate ratio of \( \frac{16}{25} \) .

Sub-triplicate Ratio

The ratio between the cube roots of two numbers is called the sub-triplicate ratio of two numbers, Thus the sub-triplicate ratio of a : b is \( \sqrt[3]{a} : \sqrt[3]{b} \)
Ex: Sub-triplicate ratio of 27 : 64 is \( \sqrt[3]{27} : \sqrt[3]{64} \) i.e. 3 : 4.

Reciprocal or Inverse Ratio

the antecedent and consequent of a ratio interchange their places, the new ratio is called the inverse ratio of the first ratio.
In other words, if \( a \neq 0, \ b \neq 0 \) then the reciprocal raatio of a: b is \( \frac{1}{a} : \frac{1}{b}. \)
Clearly, \( \frac{1}{a} : \frac{1}{b}. \) is same as b : a.
Thus the reciprocal ratio of a : b is b : a.
Ex: \( \frac{2}{7} \) is the inverse ratio of \( \frac{7}{2} \) .

Proportion

  1. If two ratios are equal, they make a proportion or the quality of two ratios constitutes a proportion. Thus \( \frac{2}{3} = \frac{4}{6} \) or 2 : 3 = 4 : 6 or 2 : 3 :: 4 : 6 and is read as "2 is to 3 as 4 is to 6".
    Each term of the ratio \( \frac{2}{3} \ and \ \frac{4}{6} \) is called a proportional. They are respectively the first, second, third and fourth proportionals. In the proportion, 2 : 3 : : 4 : 6, the end terms 2 and 6 are called extremes and the middle terms 3 and 4 are called means. Now the above proportionals can be written as \( \frac{2}{3} = \frac{4}{6} \ or \ 2 \times 6 = 3 \times 4 \)
    Hence, the product of the means = the product of the extremes. Take another proportion, say,
    3 : 5 :: 21 : 35
    Since \( 3 \times 35 = 5 \times 21 \) , the result is true that the product of the means = product of the extremes.

  2. Continued Proportion: : Consider the proportion 4, 6, 9. Here \( \frac{4}{6} = \frac{6}{9} \) or the ratio of the first to second is equal to the ratio of the second to the third.
    Thus 4, 6, 9 are in the Continued Proportion.

  3. If a : b :: c : x, x is called the fourth proportion of a, b, c.
    We have \( a \times x = b \times c \ or \ x = \frac{b \times c}{a} \)
    Thus fourth proportion of a, b, c is \( \frac{b \times c }{a} \)

  4. If a : b :: b : x, x is called the third proportion of a, b.
    we have \( a \times x = b \times b \ or \ x = \frac{b^2}{a} \) .
    Thus third proportion of a, b is \( \frac{b^2}{a} \)

  5. If a : x :: x : b, x is called the mean or second proportion of a, b.
    We have x^2
    = a \times b \ or \ x = \sqrt{a \times b}
    \therefore Mean proportion of a and b
    = \sqrt{ab}
    In this cause, a, x, b are also called in continued proportion.

Chain Rule

Direct proportion

Two quantities are said to be directly proportional if on the increase (or decrease) of the one the other increases (or decreases) to the same extent.

  1. Ex.The cost of articles is directly proportional to the number of articles.
    (More articles, More cost) and (Less articles, Less cost.)

  2. Ex. The work done is directly proportional to the number of men working at it.
    (More Men, More work) and (Less Men, Less work).

Indirect Proportion

Two quantities are said to be indirectly proportional if on the increase of the one, the other dereases to the same extent and vice versa.

  1. Ex.Time taken to cover a distance is inversely proportional to the speed of the car.
    (More speed, less is the time taken to cover a distance)

  2. Ex.Time taken to finish a work is inversely proportional to the number of the persons working at it.
    (More persons, less is the time taken to finish a job)

Chain rule

"When a series of quantities are so connected with one another, that we know how much of the first kind is equivalent to a given quantity of second, how much of the second is equivalent to a given quantity of the third and so on, the rule by which we find how much of the last kind is equivalent to a given quantity of the first kind is called the chain rule.
Rule: Beginning with x, place the given relations in the form of equations so that each right hand quantity is of the same kind as the next left hand quantity and the last right hand quantity is the same kind as the first left hand one. Then the product of the numbers on the left side is equal to the product of the numbers on the right side. From this equations x can be found out.

Solved examples

Example 1:

If P : Q : R = 2 : 3 : 4, Then \( \frac{P}{Q} : \frac{Q}{R} : \frac{R}{P} = ? \)

Answer:
P : Q : R = 2 : 3 : 4.
Let P = 2k,
Q = 3k,
R = 4k.
Then,
\( \begin{align} \frac{P}{Q} = \frac{2k}{3k} = \frac{2}{3}, \\\\[3pt] \frac{Q}{R} = \frac{3k}{4k} = \frac{3}{4}, \\\\[3pt] \frac{R}{P} = \frac{4k}{2k} = \frac{2}{1}, \end{align} \)
At first we do LCM of 2, 3, 4 is 12, now we multiplied with ratio numbers ,
Like,
\( \begin{align} \frac{2 \times 12}{3} = 8 \\\\[3pt] \frac{3 \times 12}{4} = 9 \\\\[3pt] \frac{2 \times 12}{1} = 24 \\\\[3pt] So, \ the \ ratio \ of \ \frac{P}{Q} : \frac{Q}{R} : \frac{R}{P} \ is \ 8 : 9 : 24. \end{align} \)

Example 2:

In a town, 20% of the mans are same in numbers as \( \frac{1}{4}th \) of the women. What would be the ratio of mans and woman in that town?

Answer:
20% mans
\( = \frac{1}{4} \ womens \ \Leftrightarrow \ \frac{20 \ man}{100} \)
So, mans =
\( \begin{align} \frac{5}{4} \ womens \\\\[3pt] \frac{mans}{womens} = \frac{5}{4} \end{align} \)
mans : womens = 5 : 4

Example 3:

A money bag contains 50 p, 25 p, and 10 p coins in the ratio 5 : 9 : 4, and the total amounting to Rs.206.
Find the individual number of coins of each type.

Answer:
Let the number of 50 p ,25 p, and 10 p coins be 5x, 9x, 4x respectively.
we can say two 50 paise = 1 rupee that's why we take 2
we can say four 25 paise = 1 rupee that's why we take 4
we can say Ten 10 paise = 1 rupee that's why we take 10
Then, \( \frac{5x}{2} + \frac{9x}{4} + \frac{4x}{10} = 206 \)
50x + 45x + 8x = 4120
103x = 4120
x = 40.
Number of 50 p coins is \( ( 5 \times 40 = 200 ) \) ,
Number of 25 p coins is \( ( 9 \times 40 = 360 ) \) ,
Number of 10 p coins \( (4 \times 40 = 160 ) \)

Example 4:

Rs 75,500/- are divided between A and B in the ratio 1 : 3. what is the difference between thrice the share of A and twice the share of B?

Answer:
Share of \( A = \frac{75,500 \times 1}{1 + 3} = 75,500 \times \frac{1}{4} = 18875 \)
Share of \( B = \frac{75,500 \times 3}{1 + 3} = 75,500 \times \frac{3}{4}  = 56625 \) .
Difference between thrice the share of A and twice the share of B is
= 2B - 3A
= \( 2 \times 56625 - 3 \times 18875 \)
= 113250 - 56625
= 56625.
