Logarithm

Logarithm : If a positive real number, other than 1 and \( a^m = x \) , then we write:
\( m = log_a x \) and we say that the value of log x to the base a is m.
Example:

  1. \( 10^3 = 1000 \Rightarrow log_{10} 1000 = 3 \)

  2. \( 3^4 = 81 \Rightarrow log_3 81 = 4 \)

  3. \( 2^{-3} = \frac{1}{8} \Rightarrow log_2 \frac{1}{8} = - 3 \)

  4. \( (.1)^2 = 0.1 \Rightarrow log_{(-1)} .01 = 2 \)

Properties of Logarithm

  1. \( log_a (xy) = log_a x + log_a y \)

  2. \( log_a \left( \frac{x}{y} \right) = log_a x - log_a y \)

  3. \( log_x x = 1 \)

  4. \( log_a 1 = 0 \)

  5. \( log_a (x^p) = p (log_a x) \)

  6. \( log_a x = \frac{1}{log_x a} \)

  7. \( log_a x = \frac{log_b x}{log_b a} = \frac{log x}{log a} \)

Remember : When base is not mentioned, it is taken as 10.

Common Logarithms :

Logarithms to the base 10 are known as common logarithms.
The logarithm of a number contains two parts, namely characteristic and mantissa.
Characteristic : The integral part of the logarithm of a number is called its characteristic.

  1. Case I: When the number is greater than 1.
    In this case, the characteristic is one less than the number of digits in the left of the decimal point in the given number.

  2. Case II : When the number is less than 1.
    In this case, the characteristic is one more than the number of zeros between the decimal point and the first significant digit of the number and it is negative.
    Instead of -1, -2, etc. we write, \( \overline{1} \) (one bar), \( \overline{2} \) (two bar), etc.

Example:

Number Characteristic Number Characteristic
348.25 2 0.6173 \( \overline{1} \)
46.583 1 0.03125 \( \overline{2} \)
9.2193 0 0.00125 \( \overline{3} \)

Mantissa:

The decimal part of the logarithm of a number is known is its mantissa., For mantissa, we look through log table.

Solved examples

Example 1:

Question:
Evaluate \( log_3 27 \)

Solution:
Let \( log_3 27 = n. \)
Then, \( 3^n = 27 = 3^3 \ or \ n = 3. \)
\( \therefore log_3 27 = 3 \) .

Example 2:

Question:
Evaluate \( log_7 1 = 0 \)

Solution:
We know that \( log_a 1 = 0 \) , so \( log_7 = 1 \) .

Example 3:

Question:
If \( log_{ \sqrt{8}} x = 3 \frac{1}{3} \) , find the value of x.

Solution:
\( \begin{align} log_{ \sqrt{8}} x = \frac{10}{3} \Leftrightarrow x = ( \sqrt{8})^{ \frac{10}{3}} \\\\[3pt] = (2^{ \frac{3}{2}})^{ \frac{10}{3}} = 2^{ \left( \frac{3}{2} \times \frac{10}{3} \right)} \\\\[3pt] = 2^5 \\\\[3pt] = 32. \end{align} \)

Example 4:

Question:
If \( log_2 = 0.3010 \) and log 3 = 0.4771, find the value of log 25

Solution:
\( \begin{align} log 25 = log \left( \frac{100}{4} \right) \\\\[3pt] = log 100 - log 4 \\\\[3pt] = 2 - 2 log 2 \\\\[3pt] = (2 - 2 \times 0.3010) \\\\[3pt] = 1.398 \end{align} \)

Example 5:

Question:
If \( log_2 = 0.30103 \) , find the number of digit in \( 2^{56} \) .

Solution:
\( log(2^{56}) = 56 log 2 = (56 \times 0.30103) = 16.85768 \)
Its characteristic is 16. Hence, the number of digits in \( 2^{56} \) is 17.
