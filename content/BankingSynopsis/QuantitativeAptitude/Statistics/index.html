Statistics

Meaning of 'statistics'. Primary and secondary data. Raw / ungrouped data. Range of data, Grouped data - class intervals, Class marks, Presentation of data - frequency distribution table, Discrete frequency distribution and continuous frequency distribution.

Important Terms:

Range of a Data

The difference between the largest and the smallest observations is known as the range.

Mid - value

The mean of lower limit and upper limit is known as mid - value. It is represented by 'x'.

Frequency

Number of observations falling in a particular class is called the frequency of that class.

Cumulative Frequency

The cumulative frequency of a particular class is the sum of the frequencies of this class and of those prior to it.

Frequency - Distribution:

The distribution of frequency in various classes is known as frequency distribution.

Continuous Form

When 1 - 11, 11 - 21, 21 - 31 etc, the distribution is said to be in continous form.
Note that class 1 - 11 means 1 or more but less than 11. Notethat 1 ;
In class 1 - 11, we have lower limit = 1 and upper = 11.
Class size =(Upper Limit) - (Lower limit).
Class mark = \( \frac{1}{2} \) (Upper limit + Lower limit).

Arithmetic Mean

Some of the all given number are divided by numbers is called arthmetic mean. It Is represented by \( \overline{x} \)

Important Points

  1. For any two numbers a and b. we have:
    Arithmetic Mean (A.M) = \( \frac{1}{2} (a + b) \)
    Geometric Mean (G.M) = \( \sqrt{ab} \)
    Harmonic Mean (H.M) = \( = \frac{2ab}{a + b} \)

  2. For n observations \( x_1, \ x_2 \ ...... \ x_n \) , we have
    \( \begin{align} A.M = \left ( \frac{x_1 + x_2 + .... x_n}{n} \right ) \\\\[3pt] G.M = (x_1 x_2 ..... x_n)^{\frac{1}{n}} \\\\[3pt] H.M = \frac{n}{ \left ( \frac{1}{x_1} + \frac{1}{x_2} + ...... + \frac{1}{x_n} \right )} \end{align} \)

Results on Arithmetic Mean

  1. Let there be 'n' observations \( x_1, \ x_2, ....., x_n \) then:
    \( A.M = \left ( \frac{x_1 + x_2 + ..... + x_n}{n} \right ) \)

  2. When some terms have frequency more than 1, then:
    \( A.M = \frac{\sum(f \times x)}{\sum f} = \frac{\sum ( f \times x)}{n} \)

  3. When data is calssified, then:
    \( A.M = \left [ A + \frac{\sum(f \times d}{\sum f} \right ] \) , where A is the assumed mean, d is deviation of the term from A whose frequency is f.

  4. If observations \( x_1, \ x_2, \ ..... , \ x_n \) have weights \( w_1, \ w_2, \ ..... , \ w_n \) respectively, then:
    Weighted A.M.
    \( = \left ( \frac{w_1 x_1 + w_2 x_2 + ..... w_n x_n}{w_1 + w_2 + ..... + w_n} \right ) \)

  5. Let A.M. of \( x_1, \ x_2, \ ...... \ x_n \) be M, then:

    1. A.M. of \( (x_1 + C), \ (x_2 + C), \ ..... \ (x_n + C) \) is ( M + C)

    2. A.M. of \( Cx_1, \ Cx_2, \ ..... , \ Cx_n \ is \ c \times M \)

    3. A.M. of \( \frac{x_1}{C}, \ \frac{x_2}{C}, \ ...... , \ \frac{x_n}{C} \ is \ \frac{M}{C}, \ where \ C \ \neq \ 0 \) .

Results on Median

  1. In an ungrouped data, the data is arranged in ascending or descending order of magnitude.
    If n is odd, then median = \( \left ( \frac{n + 2}{2} \right )th \) term.
    If n is even, then median
    \( = \frac{1}{2} \left [ \left ( \frac{n}{2} \right ) th \ term + \left ( \frac{n}{2} + 1 \right ) th \ term \right ] \)

  2. \( L_1 + \frac{(L_2 - L_1)}{f} \times \left ( \frac{n}{2} - C \right ) \) , where \( L_1 \ and \ L_2 \) are respectively the lower limit and the upper limit of the median class; f is the frequency of this class; n is the sum of frequencies and C is the cumulative frequency of the class preceding the median class.

Mode

  1. In an ungrouped data. the most occuring term gives the mode,

  2. If some terms have frequenmes more than 1, then the term having largest frequency is the mode.

  3. In a grouped data, we have:
    \( Mode = L_1 + \frac{(f - f_1)}{(2f - f_1 - f_2)} \times (L_2 - L_1) \) , where L is he lower limit of modal class, \( L_2 \) is the upper limit of the modal class f is the frequency of modal class and \( f_1, \ f_2 \) are the respective frequencies of the preceding and succeeding classes of the modal class.

Mean Deviation

  1. Mean deviation = \( \frac{\sum | \delta|}{n}, \ where \ \delta = |x - M| \) , where M is the A.M.

  2. Mean deviation = \( \frac{\sum \ f \times | \delta|}{\sum \ f} \)

Results on standard Deviation

  1. S.D is given by, \( \sigma = \sqrt{ \frac{ \sum | \delta|^2}{n}} \)

  2. \( \sigma = \sqrt{ \frac{\sum f \times \delta^2}{n}{n} - \left ( \frac{\sum f \delta}{n} \right )^2 } \) , where \( \delta = | x - M| \)

  3. \( S.D = \sqrt{variance} \)

Results on Diagrams

Histogram

It is a graphical representation of a frequency distribution in the form of rectangles with class intervals as bases and the corresponding frequencies as heights, there being no gap between any two successive rectangles.

Frequency polygon

When a grouped frequency distribution with equal class intervals is being given, we represent the class - mark on x - axis and the frequencies on y - axis.
we complete the diagram in the form of a potygon by taking two more classes one at the begining and the other at the end each with frequency 0.

Frequency Polygon From Histogram

By joining the mid points of all tops of rectangles of histogram, We obtain the frequency polygon.
