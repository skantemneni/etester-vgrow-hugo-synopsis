Trigonometry

In trigonometry we study the ratio of the different sides of a right angled triangle.

According to Pythagorean theorem \( (Hypotanuse)^2 = (Base)^2 + (Perpendicular)^2 \)

Measurement of Angles

Degree Measurement

(Sexagonal system)
When we divide a right angle into 90 equal small parts one part is equal to one degree.
\( \begin{align} 1^{\circ} = 60' (minutes) \\\\[3pt] 1' = 60" (Seconds) \end{align} \)
(Radian mesurement)
(Circular System)
In a circle, angle subtended by an arc which is equal to the radius, is equal to one radian.
Central angle of a circle

\( \begin{align} 360^{\circ} = (2 \pi)^C \\\\[3pt] 180^{\circ} = \pi^C \\\\[3pt] 1^{\circ} = \left ( \frac{\pi}{180} \right )^{c} \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ 1^c = \left ( \frac{180}{ \pi} \right )^{c} \end{align} \)

Tiritionosnotrical Ratios

The ratio between different sides of right angle triangle w.r.t its accute angle are right called trignometrical ratios.

The trigonometrical ratios, or functions, of angle A are defined as follows :

  1. \( sin \ \theta = \frac{Perpendicular}{Hypotanuse} = \frac{PM}{OP} \)

  2. \( cos \ \theta = \frac{Base}{Hypotanuse} = \frac{OM}{OP} \)

  3. \( tan \ \theta = \frac{Perpendicular}{Base} = \frac{PM}{OM'} \)

  4. \( sec \ \theta = \frac{Hypotanuse}{Base} \)

  5. \( cosec \ \theta = \frac{Hypotanuse}{Perpendicular} \)

  6. \( cot \ \theta = \frac{Base}{Perpendicular} \)

To remember
\( \frac{PBP}{HHB} \)
P - Perpendicular
B - Base
H - Hypotenuse

Quadrants

Let XOX' and YOY' be two mutually perpendicular lines. These lines divide the plane into four parts and each one of them is called a quadrant.

Note :
For \( (90 \pm \theta) \ and \ (270 \pm \theta) \)

Tiritionosnotrical ratios change in following ways:

\( \begin{align} sin \ \theta \ \underset{\leftarrow}{\rightarrow} \ cos \ \theta \\\\[3pt] tan \ \theta \ \underset{\leftarrow}{\rightarrow} \ cot \ \theta \\\\[3pt] sec \ \theta \ \underset{\leftarrow}{\rightarrow} \ cosec \ \theta \end{align} \)
For other angles no change in trigonometrical ratios

Complementary angles

When sum of the two angles is \( 90^{\circ} \) then these angles called complementary angles.

Supplementry angles

When of the sum of the two angles is \( 180^{\circ} \) then these angles are supplementry angles.
