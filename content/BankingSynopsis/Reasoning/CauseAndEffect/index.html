Cause and effect

"cause-effect" relationship is a relationship in which one event (the cause) makes another event happen (the effect). one cause can have several effects.
When one event causes another to happen. The "cause" is "why" it happens. the "effect" is "what" happens. Keywords for cause and effect.

Five Possibilities for Cause and Effect

  1. The fact in the first statement is the effect of that mentioned in the second statement ie, the second statement expresses the cause that leads to the condition mentioned in the first.

  2. The contents of the first statement from the direct cause of the result discussed in the second statement.

  3. Both the statements express generalised results that may not be backed by any specific cause but may influence certain other factors.

  4. Both the statements are independent and may be explained as effects of different independent causes.

  5. The effects in both the given statements may be caused by a third unmentioned event which may they be called the common cause of the given effects.
