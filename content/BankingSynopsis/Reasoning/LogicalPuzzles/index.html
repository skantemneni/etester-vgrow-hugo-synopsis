logical Puzzles

It refers the information which is implied or inferred from a statement, that means the information is never clearly stated.
Inference means a fact that can truly be inferred from the contents of a given statement. Here, candidates are required to approach the questions from a logical angle. In this section of logical reasoning, the question consists one or two statements followed by certain inferences based on it.

Types of Questions

Logical reasoning may be categorised in two types.

Type 1

Statement and Conclusion

A conclusion means a fact that can be truly inferred from the context of a given statement or the passage. Hence, a conclusion is the brief or point of view that is supported or defended with the premises.
In these types of questions, a statement or a set of statements followed by some inferences is given. Candidates are required to analyse the given statement and understand their indirect implications and decide which of the given conclusions logically follows from the given statements.

Type 2

Statement and Assumption

An assumption is the hidden part of an argument and something supposed or taken for grant.
In these types of questions, a statement is followed by two or more assumptions. Candidates are required to access the given statement and decide which of the given assumption implicit directly in the statement.

Solved examples

Example 1:

In the given question, a statement is given followed by two conclusions I and II. You have to consider the statement to be true even if they seem to be at variance from commonly known facts. You have to decide which of the given conclusions, if any follow from the given statement.
Statement:
Uneducated people are exploited by others in the society.
Conclusion:

  1. People take advantage of uneducated people only.

  2. Educated people exploit uneducated persons in society.

  1. Only conclusion I follows

  2. Only conclusion II follows

  3. Both conclusions I and II follow

  4. Neither conclusion I nor II follows

Solution:(d) The word 'only' in conclusion I unfavours the condition while in conclusion II, educated people exploit uneducated persons is not mentioned in the given statement.

Example 2:

Statement:
To keep myself updated, I always listen to 9 o'clock news on radio.
Conclusion:

  1. The person does not read newspaper.

  2. Recent news is available only on radio.

  1. Only conclusion I is implicit

  2. Only conclusion II is implicit

  3. Neither conclusion I nor II is implicit

  4. Both conclusions are implicit

Solution:(C) By the given statement, neither conclusion I nor II is implicit.

Example 3:

Statement:
All wealthy people are happy in life. Conclusions.
Conclusion:

  1. Wealth and happiness are directly related.

  2. Wealth leads to happiness in life.

  1. Only conclusion I follows

  2. Only conclusion II follows

  3. Both conclusions I and II follow

  4. Neither conclusion I nor II follows

Solution:(C) By the given statement, both conclusions are implicit.

Example 4:

In the given question, a statement is followed by two assumption numbered I and II. An assumption is something supposed or taken for granted. You have to decide which of the following assumption is implict in the statement.
Statement:
Statement In city 'Z' people prefer to buy car 'X' instead of car 'Y' as car 'X' has German Technology which is very advanced.
Conclusion:

  1. Cars with German Technology are perceived to be better than other cars in city Z.

  2. Had German Technology been present in car 'Y' also, it's sales would have crossed car 'X's' sales.

  1. If only I is implicit

  2. If only II is implicit

  3. If both I and II are implicit

  4. If neither I nor II is implicit

Solution:(a) It seems that German Technology is better than other technology, so it would increase the sale of car 'X'.. So assumption I is implicit while assumption II indicates the possible result of the sale which cannot be assumed.

Example 5:

One statement is given followed by two assumptions I and II. You have to consider the statements to be true even if it seems to be at variance from commonly known facts. You have to decide which of the given assumptions, if any, follow from the given statements.
Statement:
Politicians become rich by the votes of the people.
Assumption:

  1. People vote to make politicians rich.

  2. Politicians become rich by their virtue.

  1. Only assumption I is implicit

  2. Only assumption II is implicit

  3. Both assumptions I and II are implicit

  4. Both assumptions I and II are not implicit

Solution:(a) In statement, it is strictly said that politicians become rich due to votes of the people which is fully implemented in the assumption I. Assumption II is not directly related to statement. So. only assumption I is implicit.

Example 6:

Two statements are given followed by four assumptions. Select the alternative which is most appropriate.
Statement:
India is becoming industrialised. Pollution is a problem associated with industrialisation.
Assumptions:

  1. All industrial centres are polluted.

  2. India is polluted.

  3. Polluted nations are industrialised.

  4. India may become polluted.

  1. All assumptions are appropriate

  2. None assumption is appropriate

  3. Only assumption IV is appropriate

  4. Only assumption II is appropriate

Solution:(c) Only assumption IV can be drawn from the given statements.
