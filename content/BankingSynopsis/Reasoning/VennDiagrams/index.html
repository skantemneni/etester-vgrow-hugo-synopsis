Venn Diagrams

Venn diagram is a way representing sets pictorially.

Example:
result of a survey of 1000 persons with respect to their knowledge of Hindi (H), English (E.) and Sanskrit (S) is given below.

What is the ratio of these who know all the three languages to those who do not known Sanskrit?

  1. \( \frac{1}{9} \)

  2. \( \frac{1}{10} \)

  3. \( \frac{10}{17} \)

  4. \( \frac{9}{27} \)

Sol:
The persons who know all the three language are represented by the region which is common to all the three circles.
So, number such persons = 100
The persons who do not know Sanskrit are represented by the region outside circle S.
So, number of such persons = (200 + 120 + 220) = 540.
\( \therefore \) Required raatio = 100 : 540 = 5 : 27
