Dance of India
The of India The dance of India can be classified into the following three categories.

  1. Main styles of classical dance

  2. Other classical dance

  3. Folk and Tribal dance

Bhratnatyam:
The earliest exponents of Bharatnatyam were the devadasis (Temple Dancers) in south India. They would perform the dance daily at the of worship or an festive occasions. It came to be patronized by the Cholas, The Pandyas, Nayaks, Vijayanagar rulers and the Marathas. In course of time devadasis started dancing in the royal courts and thus its religious sanctity was lost.
The technique of Bharatnatyam consist of 64 principles of co-ordinated hand, foot, face and body movements which are performed to the accompaniment of dance syllobes popularly known as bols.
The maestros in Bharatnatyam are T. Bala Saraswathi, Shanta Roa, marinlini Sarabhi, Yamini Krishnamurthi, Kamala, Vijayantimala, Sonal Man Singh, Samyukta Panigrahi, Rukmini Devi, Leela Somson, C.V. Chandrasekhar and Padma Subramanyam.
Kathak:
The word katak is derived from katha which means story. Its origin dates back to ancient times when a band of story tellers went obout reciting episodes from the Ramayana and the Mahabharata using music and dance. It soon turned into dance-drama in which actors took part.
It is confined mostly to northern India-Uttar Pradesh, Rajsthan, Madya Pradesh and Pajjab. The main centres are Lucknow. Benaras, jaipur and Delhi.
The maestros in kathak are Sitara Devi, Birju Maharaj, Gopi Krishna, Binda Din Maharaj, Kalkadin, Aachan Maharaj, Uma Sharma, Bharti Gupta, Damyanti Jpshi, Durga Das, Kumudini Lakhia, Sambhu Makaraj and Saswti Sen
Kathakali:
Kathakali is the most refined, the most scientific and elaborately defined dance form of Kerala.
Kathakali combines music, poetry, mime and drama. This dance-drama is usually performed in the open and usually lasts the whole might. The themes are taken from the Ramayana, the Mahabharata and Hindu Mythology.
The maestros in Kathakali are Guru Krishan Kutty Kanak Rele, Raghavan, Kunju Kurup, Karunakaran Nair, Kunj Nair, Rasini Devi, Mrinalini Sarabhai, Rita Ganguly, Gopinathan and Krishnan Nair.
Manipuri :
This dance belongs to the state of Manipur in north-east India.
Manipuri is purely religious dance which is used to invoke divine blessing. Originally only siva and Parvati were propitiated but later on Krishna and Raha, too, were included.
Manipur involves a movement of head, hands and feet in complete harmony. Another feature of this dance is that the men and women dance together.
The credit goes to a ruler, Bhagya Chandra for devising a beautiful mirrored dress known ad kamil, which is invariably worn by the dancers.
The maestros in Manipuri are Guru Bipin Sinha, Jhaveri Sisters, T. Nadia Singh, Cham Malhar, Bipin Singh, Nirmala Mehta and Savita Meta.
Odissi :
It is clasical dance of orissa. It is the considered to be the oldest form of classical dance dating back to pre Christian era.
The dress consists of a bright caloured sari hanging loosely and a tight fitted blouse. The musical instruments accompanying the dance performances are a drum, a pair of cymbals, a flute and a stringed instrumont.
The maestors in odissi are Dhirendra Nath Pattnaik, Indrani Rahman,Debaprasad Das, Kelucharan mahapatra, Priyambada Mohanty, Hare Krishna Behra, Sonal Man Singh, Sanjukta Panegrahk mayadhar Rant, Madhvi Mudgal, Kiran Sehgal and Ravi Karan.
Kuchipudi :
Kuchipudi is a dance-drama of Andhra Pradesh. Kuchipudi derives its name from the name of a village. Kuchelapuram of its origin.
Its closely resembles Bharatnatyam. However, its movements are much faster and style is freer.
The maestros in Kuchipudi are Swapna Sundari Vempathi, Chinna Sathyam, Josyula Seetharamaiah, Yamini Krishnamurthy, Raja and Radha Reddy and Shobha Naidu.
Mohiniattam :
It is classical dance of Kerala. The word 'mohini' literally means a maiden who excites desire or steals the heart of the onlooker.
In format, this is similar to Bharatanatyam. The movements are graceful like odissi and the costumes saber and attractive. It is essentially a salo dance.
The first reference to Mohiniyattam is found in vyavaharamala, composed by Mazhamangalam Narayanan Nambudiri, assined to the 16th centuryA.D.
The macstras in Mohiniattam are Bharathi Sivaji, Vaijantimala and Kanak Rele.
