Appointments/Resignations

Dinesh Sharma

Dinesh Sharma has been elected Board member of Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank (AIIB) on January 17, 2016. Before this appointment, Dinesh Sharma was the Additional Secretary of Ministry of Finance. India, being one of the founding members of the Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank, was elected to the board of directors of the China-sponsored AIIB, which is expected to begin loan approvals before the end of year 2016. Sharma who headed the Indian delegation represented Jaitley at the meeting.

Paul Kaba Thieba

Paul Kaba Thieba is appointed as the Prime Minister of Burkina Faso on January 9, 2016. Thieba, who has little political experience, is charged with forming President Roch Marc Kabore's government as the country continues with a democratic transition after 27 years under leader Blaise Compaore, who was overthrown in October, 2014.
Thieba, worked for many years as an economist for the West African Central bank, BCEAO, in Ivory Coast.

Burkina Faso

It is a landlocked country in West Africa around 274200 sq km in size. Its capital is Ouagadougou. Burkina Faso produces cotton and gold but remains impoverished. Its economy has slowed due to lower global commodity prices and reduced investment during the democratic transition that began after Compaore fell.

Legendary Bharatnatyam dancer, Mrinalini Sarabhai passed away on January 21, 2016. Lt. Gen. JFR Jacob

Lt. Gen. JFR Jacob (Retd.), the war veteran who played a key role in the 1971 war which broke up Pakistan and created Bangladesh, died in New Delhi. Born in the Bengal Presidency under British India, Jacob joined the' army at the age of 19 and also fought in World War II and the Indo-Pakistan War of 1965 before he retired in 1978.

China's Domain '.cn' became World's Largest

China's country code domain '.cn' has become the worlds most commonly used in the internet with 16.36 million users, surpassing Germany's '.de' domain. According to the data released by the China Internet Network Information Centre (CNNIC), which manages the domain, '.cn' had 16.36 million users by the end of 2015.

Economic Survey 2014 - 15

Finance Minister Arun Jaitley tabled the Economic Survey for 2014 - 2015 in Parliament.
Following are the major point of the survey

Growth

Economic Survey has estimated economic growth between 8.1 and 8.5% cent in 2015 - 16. Growth rate in 2014 - 15 is estimated at 7.5%.

Fiscal Deficit

The Survey has suggested that India must meet its medium - term fiscal deficit target of 3% of GDP. Government will adhere to fiscal deficit target of 4.1% of GDP n 2014 - 15.

Inflation

  • Consumer Price Index inflation in 2015 - 16 is likely to range between 5 - 5.5%.
  • Inflation is likely to be below Central Bank target by 0.5 - 1 percentage point.

Current Account Deficit

Survey expects financial Year 2015 current account deficit at 1.3% of GDP. The current account deficit has declined from a peak of 6.7% of GDP (in third quarter, 2012 - 13) to an estimated 1% in the coming fiscal year.

Fiscal Consolidation

The fiscal consolidation plan as enunciated in Budget Estimates (BE) for 2014 - 15 entailed an increase in the tax to GDP ratio and non - debt receipts to GDP ratio to 10.6 per cent and 9.8% respectively and a continuance of the low level of total expenditure to GDP ratio at 13.9%.

  • Agriculture and allied sectors contribute 18% to GDP and grow by 3.7% in 2013 - 14.
  • Foodgrain production for 2014 - 15 estimated at 257.07 million tonnes.

Industry

  • The Index of Industrial Production (IIP) suggests that industrial sector is recovering slowly with a 2.1% in April - December 2014 - 15 over the 0.1 per cent in the same period last year.
  • Mining sector growth has turned positive while manufacturing growth continues to remain tepid, registering growth of 1.2% in April - December 2014 - 15.

Services Sector

India's dynamic Services Sector clocked double digit growth rate of 10.6% as per the Advance Estimate during the current financial year, as compared to 9.1% in the last fiscal year 2013 - 14.
