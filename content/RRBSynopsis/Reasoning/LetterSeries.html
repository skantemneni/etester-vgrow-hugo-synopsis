Introduction

In this chapter, a series of single pairs or groups of letters or combinations of letters are given. Each pair or single element is called the term. The terms of the series form a certain pattern as regards the position of the letters in the English alphabets. You are required to identify this pattern and find the missing or wrong term in the given series which satisfies the pattern.

Types Of Questions

There are mainly three types of questions asked from letter series in various competitive exams. They are as follows.

Type 1

Alphabet Series

In this type, the terms of the series form a particular pattern as regards the position of the letters in the English alphabets.

Type 2

Continuous Pattern Series

In this type, a series of small/capital letters are given which follow a particular pattern. However, some letters are missing from the series. In some of the questions involving alphabetical series, a set of letters is given four or five times with blank spaces or question mark in between. The series follows a specific pattern and you are required to find the letter which should come in place of the blank space or question mark.

Type 3

Conditional Letter Series

In this type, a series of letters are given and candidate is asked to trace out the particular letter.

    Find the next term in the series.
    A, D, G, J, ?

    1. K
    2. L
    3. M
    4. N

    Solution:

    sol: (c) Each next term of the series is next third letter of the alphabets (according to position).

    Z, S, W, O, T, K, Q, G, ?, ?

    1. N, C
    2. N, D
    3. O, C
    4. O, D

    Solution:

    Sol. (a) The given sequence is a combination of two series.

    1. Z, W, T, Q, ?
    2. S, O, K, G, ?

    Now,

    Directions (Q.Nos. 3 and 4) These questions are based on the letter series. In each of these series, some of the letters are missing. Select the correct alternatives.

    Example 3:

    abca_bcaab_ca_bbc_a

    1. ccaa
    2. bbaa
    3. abac
    4. abba

    Solution:

    Sol. (c) The pattern of the series is
    \( abc / a \underline{a} bc / aab \underline{b} c / a \underline{a} b b c \underline{c} / a \Rightarrow abac \)
    In each subsequent block, one new letter is introduced to form a series as aa.... bb ... cc ...

    Example 4:

    _cb_ca_bacb_ca_bac_d

    1. addddb
    2. addbbb
    3. bddddb
    4. bbbddd

    Solution:

    Sol. (a) The pattern of the series is
    \( \underline{a} c b \underline{d} / c a \underline{d} b / a c b \underline{d} / c a \underline{d} b / a c \underline{b} d \Rightarrow addddb \)
    The sequence is made of repetition of two blocks 'acbd' and 'cadb' alternatively.

    Example 5:

    How many T's are there in the following letter series which are preceded by P but not followed by S?
    S T P Q T S P T R P T Q R P S T Q R T R P T M P T S

    1. 1
    2. 2
    3. 3
    4. 4

    Solution:

    Sol. (c) According to the question, three T's are there in the given letter series which are preceded by P but not followed by S.
    \( S T P Q T S P \fbox{T} R P \fbox{T} Q R P S T Q R T R P \fbox{T} M P T S \)

    Example 6:

    How many M's are there in the following letter series which are preceded by D or N?
    F D M L K D M N M F T T L O P O D M T M L F

    1. 1
    2. 2
    3. 3
    4. 4

    Solution:

    Sol. (d) According to the question, four M's are there in the given letter series which are preceded by D or N.
    \( F D \fbox{M} L K D \fbox{M} N \fbox{M} F T T L O P O D \fbox{M} T M L F \)
