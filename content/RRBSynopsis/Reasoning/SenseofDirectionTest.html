Introduction

In this chapter, we deal with the questions which are based on directions and displacements. Direction is a measurement of position of one thing with respect to another thing and displacement is the measurement of distance between the starting and the final point.
A successive follow-up of directions is formulated and the candidate is required to ascertain the final direction or the distance between two points. This test is designed to judge the candidate's ability to trace and follow the logical path correctly and sense the direction correctly.

Main Directions

There are four main directions, viz. East, West, North and South. Sun rises in the East. Just opposite of East is West and South is in the opposite to North.

Abbreviations for these directions are E (East), W (West), N (North) and S (South).

Cardinal Directions

A direction between two main directions is called cardinal direction. Clearly, there are four cardinal directions.
They are N-E (North-East), N-W (North-West), SE (South-East) and S-W (South-West). Students are advised to use of diagram as given in question for the purpose of sensing directions.

Angle formed between two main directions is \( 90^{\circ} \) and angle formed between a cardinal direction and main direction is \( 45^{\circ} \) .

  2. Left turn = Anti-clockwise turn

Shortest Distance

For finding shortest distance, it is necessary to know Pythagoras theorem.

BC = Base, AC = Hypotenuse

Types Of Questions

There are three types of questions are generally asked in various competitive examination.

Type 1

Based on Final Direction

In this type of questions, we have to ascertain the final direction with respect to starting point or the directional relations between two points/persons/things used in questions.

Type 2

Based on Displacement

In this type of questions, we deal with final distance between starting and fmal point or distance between two points/ persons/things.

Type 3

Based on Direction and Displacement

In this type of questions, we deal with the final distance and direction between starting and final point of any person/object or thing.

Example 3:

A person starts from a point A and travels 3 km eastwards B and then turns left and travels thrice that distance to reach C. He again turns left and travels five times the distance he covered between A and B and reaches his destination D. The shortest distance between the starting point and the destination is

  1. 12 km
  2. 15 km
  3. 16 km
  4. 18 km

Solution:

Sol. (b) The movements of the person are as shown in figure below

According to the question,
\( \begin{align} BC = (3 \times 3) = 9 \ km \\\\[6pt] CD = (3 \times 5) = 15 \ km \\\\[6pt] \therefore OD = (15 - 3) = 12 \ km \end{align} \)
Now, required distance,
\( \begin{align} AD = \sqrt{OD^2 + OA^2} = \sqrt{12^2 + 9^2} \\\\[6pt] = \sqrt{144 + 81} = \sqrt{225} = 15 \ km \end{align} \)
