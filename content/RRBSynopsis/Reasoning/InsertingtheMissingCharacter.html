Introduction

In this chapter, we deal with questions which have numbers/blocks/matrices or a simple sequence. All follow a definite pattern. We are given numbers or characters associated with these blocks/sequences including one which is missing. The candidate is required to find this pattern and accordingly find the missing character in the figure. These figure could be circle, triangle, matrices and new shaped figures which having missing character at its place.

Solved Examples

Directions (Q. Nos. 1 - 5) Find the missing number in the following figures.

Example 1:

  1. 4
  2. 5
  3. 6
  4. 8

Solution:

sol: (c) Clearly, pattern associated with given question is \( \Rightarrow e = (b - a) \times (d - c) \) = Difference between left and right numbers \( \times \) Difference between bottom and top numbers

So, missing number = \( (5 - 3) \times (7 - 4) = 2 \times 3 = 6 \)

Example 2:

? 13 49
9 17 69
13 11 59
  1. 9
  2. 5
  3. 10
  4. 21

Solution:

Number in third column = 2 \( \times \) Number in first column + 3 \( \times \) Number in second column.
So, \( (2 \times 9) + (17 \times 3) = 69 \) and \( (13 \times 2) + (11 \times 3) = 59 \)
Hence, \( (? \times 2) + (13 \times 3) = 49 \)
\( \Rightarrow ? \times 2 = 49 - 39 = 10 \Rightarrow ? = \frac{10}{2} = 5 \)
