Cube

The length, breadth and height of a cube is equal that means the figure whose length, breadth and height are equal is called cube.
A cube is three dimensional figure, having 8 corners, 6 surfaces and 12 edges.

Important Facts

If a cube is painted on all of its surfaces with a colour and then divided into smaller cubes of equal size, then after separation, number of smaller cubes, so obtained will be calculated as under

  • Number of smaller cubes with three surfaces painted = 8
  • Number of smaller cubes with two surfaces painted = (n - 2) \( \times \) 12
  • Number of smaller cubes with one surface painted = \( (n - 2)^2 \times 6 \)
  • Number of smaller cubes with no surface painted = \( (n - 2)^3 \)
    Here, n = Number of divisions on the surface of the bigger cube.

Dice

A dice is three dimensional figure with 6 surfaces. It may be in the form of a cube or a cuboid. After observing these figures, we have to find the opposite side of the dice.

Front surface \( \rightarrow \) ABCD
Back surface \( \rightarrow \) EFGH
Top surface \( \rightarrow \) ADHE
Bottom surface \( \rightarrow \) BCGF
Left surface \( \rightarrow \) ABFE
Right surface \( \rightarrow \) DCGH

Dice Formation

A dice is formed by folding a sheet of paper. These forms may be,

  1. 1 lies opposite of 6
    2 lies opposite of 4
    3 lies opposite of 5
  2. 1 lies opposite of 5
    2 lies opposite of 4
    3 lies opposite of 6
  3. 1 lies opposite of 3
    2 lies opposite of 5
    4 lies opposite of 6
  4. 1 lies opposite of 4
    2 lies opposite of 6
    3 lies opposite of 5
  5. 1 lies opposite of 4
    2 lies opposite of 5
    3 lies opposite of 6

Types of Dice

There are two types of dice.

Ordinary Dice

In this type of dice, the sum of opposite sides is not 7. But the sum of two adjacent sides are seven.

Standard Dice

In such type of dice, the sum of opposite sides is 7.

Solved Examples

Example 1:

A 6 cm cube is cut into 2 cm's are small cubes. How many small cubes can be obtained from this?

  1. 27
  2. 64
  3. 32
  4. 28

Solution:(a)
n = \( \frac{6}{2} \) = 3
Hence, number of small cubes
\( = n^3 = (3)^3 \) = 27

Example 2:

A cube painted yellow on all faces is cut into 125 small cubes of equal size. How many small cubes are painted on one face only?

  1. 68
  2. 54
  3. 38
  4. 52

Solution:(b)
n = \( \sqrt[3]{125} \) = 5
\( \therefore \) Number of small cubes with one surface painted
= \( (n - 2)^2 \times 6 = (5 - 2)^2 \times 6 = 9 \times 6 \) = 54

Example 3:

Two positions of a dice are shown below. Identify the number at the bottom when the top is 3?

  1. 2
  2. 4
  3. 5
  4. 16

Solution:(c)
Using both dice, we can say 1, 3, 4 and 5 are adjacent sides of 6. So, 2 is opposite to 6. Here, 4 and 6 are adjacent to 5. So, either 1 or 3 is opposite to 5. But 1 and 3 both are adjacent and when 1 is at top, then 3 is its adjacent side. So, 3 lies opposite of 5. Hence, when the top is 3, then the number at the bottom will be 5.
