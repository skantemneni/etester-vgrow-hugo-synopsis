Introduction

In this chapter, we deal with the questions in which series of numbers is given, numbers are generally called the terms of the series. These terms follow a certain pattern throughout the series. Candidates are asked either to insert a missing, number or to find the one that does not follow the pattern of the series. A number series can be formed by using various characteristics of numbers and by using various type of calculations.

Types Of Questions

There are mainly two types of queStions which are asked in various competitive exams.

Type 1

To Find a Missing Term

Here, we are given a sequence of numbers including one missing number. This series follows a pattern, keeping this pattern in mind, you have to find the missing term.

Type 2

To Find the Number that does not Follow the Pattern

Here, we are given a sequence of number. Whole sequence except one number follow a certain rule. You have to find that number which does not follow the rule.

Methods Of Series

Using the same methods, you can practice with some more questions.

  1. Based on Addition/Subtraction of Consecutive Odd/Even Numbers These questions have a pattern of addition or subtraction of even or odd numbers.
  2. Based on Addition/Subtraction of Prime Numbers These questions have a pattern of addition/subtraction of prime numbers.
  3. Based on Multiplication or Division These questions have a pattern based on multiplication and division

Solved Examples

Directions (Q. Nos. 1 - 7) Find the missing term within the series using the pattern accordingly.

Example 1:

1, 3, 5, 7, 9, ?

  1. 10
  2. 11
  3. 12
  4. 13

Solution:

sol: (b) This series is a sequence of consecutive odd numbers. hence the missing term will be 11.

Directions (Q. Nos. 8 - 9) Find the wrong term within the series.

Example 8:

56, 58, 62, 70, 84, 118, 182

  1. 58
  2. 62
  3. 84
  4. 118

Solution:

Sol. (c) The pattern is \( + 2, \ + 2^2, \ + 2^3, \ + 2^4, \ + 2^5, \ + 2^6 \)
\( \therefore \) Correct number should be = \( 70 + 2^4 \) = 70 + 16 = 86.

Example 9:

2, 3, 12, 37, 86, 166, 288

  1. 2
  2. 3
  3. 166
  4. 86

Solution:

Sol. (c) Series moves with a difference of squares of odd natural numbers, i.e., 1, 9, 25, 49, 81. 121. Hence, the number 166 should be replaced by number 167.
