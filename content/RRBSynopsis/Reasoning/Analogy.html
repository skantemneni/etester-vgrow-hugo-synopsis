Introduction

Analogy means similarity or correspondence i.e., having similar features. In questions based on analogy, a particular relationship is given and another similar relationship has to be identified from the alternatives provided. Questions based on analogy are set upto test a candidate's overall knowledge, power of reasoning and ability to think.
These types of questions cover every types of relationships that one can think. There are many ways of establishing a relationship like quantity and unit, worker and tools, cause and effect, word-synonym, word-antonym, country and capital, state and capital, country and currency, animal and the young ones (kid), male and female, animals and their resting places, games and places of playing.

Types Of Questions

Different types of questions based on anology that are asked in various competitive exams, have been given below.

Type 1

Direct Analogy

In this type of analogy, we deal with questions which have three components. Two have some relationship and you have to choose from the alternatives one which has the same relationship.

Type 2

Completing the Analogous Pair

In this type of questions, two words are given. These words are related to each other in some way. Another word is also given. The candidate is required to find out the relationship between the first two words and choose the word from the given alternatives, which have the same relationship to the third word, as between the first two.

Type 3

Choosing the Analogous Pair

In this type of questions, a pair of words is given, followed by four pairs of words as alternatives. You are required to choose the pair in which the words bear the same relationship.

Type 4

Number Analogy

In this type of analogy, questions have two numbers/ pairs bearing a relationship. There is also third number/pair which have the same relationship from the alternative. You are required to find that alternative.

Type 5

Alphabet Analogy

In this type of analogy, two groups of letters are related to each other in same way as given, you are required to find out this relationship and then choose a letter-group which is related in the same way from the four options

Solved Examples

Example 1:

Major is related to Lieutenant in the same way as Squadron Leader is related to _______

  1. Group Captain
  2. Flying Attendant
  3. Flying Officer
  4. Pilot Officer

Solution:

sol: (c) Major and Squadron Leader are equivalent ranks in the Army and the Air Force, respectively. Same as Lieutenant and Flying Officer are equivalent ranks in the Army and the Air force.

Directions (Q. Nos. 2 - 6) Select the related words/numbers/letters from the given alternatives.

Example 2:

Boat : Ore :: Bicycle : ?

  1. Pedal
  2. Seat
  3. Road
  4. Wheel

Solution:

Sol. (a) Second denotes that part of the first, on which the effort is applied.

Example 3:

Apostate : Religion :: ?

  1. Teacher : Education
  2. Traitor : Country
  3. Potentate : Kingdom
  4. Jailor : Law

Solution:

Apostate is one who renounces religion. Similarly, traitor is one who betrays his country.

Example 4:

5 : 35 :: ?

  1. 7 : 77
  2. 9 : 45
  3. 11 : 45
  4. 3 : 28

Solution:

Sol. (a) The first number is multiplied by the next prime number to obtain the second number.
So, 7 \( \times \) 11 = 77

Example 5:

MUMBAI : LTLAZH :: DELHI : ?

  1. CDKGG
  2. IHLED
  3. CDKGH
  4. BCKGH

Solution:

Sol. (c) Each letter of the first group is moved one step backward to obtain the corresponding letter of the second group.
So, DELHI \( \rightarrow \) CDKGH.
