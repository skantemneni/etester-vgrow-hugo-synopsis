Cost Price (CP)

The cost price of an article is the price at which the article is bought
\( CP = SP \times \frac{100}{100 + P \%} \) [In case of profit]
\( CP = SP \times \frac{100}{100 - L \%} \) [In case of loss]

Selling Price (SP)

The selling price of an article is the price at which the article is sold.
\( SP = CP \times \frac{(100 + P \%)}{100} \) [In case of profit]
\( SP = CP \times \frac{(100 - L \%)}{100} \) [In case of loss]

Profit or Gain

If selling price of an article is more than its cost price, there is profit or gain.
\( Profit \ percent = \frac{SP - CP}{CP} \times 100 \)
Profit = SP - CP

Loss

If selling price of an article is less than its cost price (CP), there is loss.
In fact,
Loss = CP - SP
\( Loss \ percentage = \frac{CP - SP}{CP} \times 100 \)

Marked Price

While selling goods, traders add a certain percentage in the cost price. This addition to the cost price is called the mark up price and this mark up value added to cost price is called the marked price.
Therefore,
Marked price = CP + Mark up price
Now, the seller may sell the product on the marked price.
In such case,
Marked price = Selling price
He may also sell the product after allowing a discount on the marked price.
In such case,
Selling price = Marked price - Discount

Solved Examples

Example 1:

A person sells an article for a price which gives him a profit of 20% on cost price of Rs. 500. Calculate the selling price of the article.

Solution:

\( \begin{align} SP = CP + profit \\\\[6pt] = CP + 20 \% \ of \ CP \\\\[6pt] = 500 \left ( 1 + \frac{20}{100} \right ) \\\\[6pt] = \frac{500 \times 120}{100} = 500 \times 12 \\\\[6pt] = Rs. 600 \end{align} \)
\( \therefore \) Selling price of the article = Rs. 600

Example 2:

A person marks his goods 20% more than the cost price and allows some discount on it. He still makes a profit of 10%. Find the discount percentage.

Solution:

Let the CP of the article be Rs. 100.
Then, MP of the article = Rs. 120
Since, profit = 10%
\( \therefore \) SP = CP + Profit = 100 + 10 = Rs. 110
The difference between marked price and selling price is the discount.
\( \therefore \) Discount = MP - SP = 120 - 110 = Rs. 10
\( Discount \ percentage = \frac{Discount}{Marked \ Price} \times 100 = \frac{10}{120} \times 100 = 8.33 \% \)

Example 3:

By selling two articles for Rs.180 each a shopkeeper gains 20% on one and looses 20% on the other, find the percentage profit / loss

Solution:

Let cost price of two articles are Rs. x and Rs. y, respectively.
\( \begin{align} \therefore 80 = \frac{x \times (100 + 20)}{100} \\\\[6pt] \Rightarrow x = \frac{180 \times 100}{120} = 150 \\\\[6pt] and \ 180 = \frac{y \times (100 - 20)}{100} \\\\[6pt] \Rightarrow y = \frac{180 \times 100}{80} = 225 \end{align} \)
\( \therefore \) Total cost price = 150 + 225 = Rs. 375
and total selling price = 180 + 180 = Rs. 360
Hence, loss = 375 - 360 = Rs. 15
\( \therefore loss \ percent = \frac{15}{375} \times 100 = 4 \% \)

note

In cases, where the selling price of two articles is same and one is sold at the loss of x% and another is sold at a profit of x%. Or in other words, the profit % and loss % is same and selling price is same. This transaction always yields a loss and such loss % = \( \left ( \frac{common \ loss \ or \ gain \%}{10} \right )^2 \)
