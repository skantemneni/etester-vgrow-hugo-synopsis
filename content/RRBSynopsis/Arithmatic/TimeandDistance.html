Time

Time is defined as quantity which governs the order or sequence of an occurence.
Unit of Time Hour (h) and second (s) are mostly taken as the unit of time.

Speed

Speed is the distance covered by an object in unit time. It is obtained by dividing the distance travelled by the time taken.

Distance

When an object is moving with certain speed regardless its direct then the sum of total linear displacement is known as its distance.

  • \( speed = \frac{Distance}{Time} \)
  • \( Distance = Speed \times Time \)
  • \( Time = \frac{Distance}{speed} \)

Conversion

  • To convert speed from \( \frac{km}{h} \) to \( \frac{m}{s} \) multiply the speed by \( \frac{5}{18} \) .
  • To convert speed from \( \frac{m}{s} \) to \( \frac{km}{h} \) multiply the speed by \( \frac{18}{5} \) .

Important Formulae(Rules)

  • \( Average \ speed = \frac{Total \ distance \ travelled}{Total \ time \ taken} \)
  • If a body travels a certain distance at x \( \frac{km}{h} \) and the same distance is covered at y \( \frac{km}{h} \) , then the average speed during whole journey = \( \frac{2xy}{x + y} \ \frac{km}{h} \)
  • If A travels with speed x \( \frac{km}{h} \) for \( t_1 h \) and with speed y \( \frac{km}{h} \) for next \( t_2 h \) , then Average speed = \( \frac{x t_1 + y t_2}{t_1 + t_2} \)

Speed Variation

  • If a man changes his speed in the ratio a : b, then the ratio of time taken becomes b : a.
  • If two bodies are moving in the same direction with speed a and b \( \frac{km}{h} \) , starting from the same point at the same time, then their relative speed is (a - b) \( \frac{km}{h} \) . But if they are moving in opposite directions it is (a + b) \( \frac{km}{h} \) .

Train

  • If a train of length 'l' m passes a pole, it travels a distance equal to its own length, i.e., l m.
  • When a train is passing the other train completely (whether moving in the same direction or in opposite direction), the train, will cover a distance equal to the sum of the lengths of the two trains.
  • If a train of length \( l_1 m \) passes through a bridge, a platform of length \( l_2 m \) , the running train travels a distance \( (l_1 + l_2) m \) .

Boat and Stream

  • If the speed of a boat (or a body) in still water be x \( \frac{km}{h} \) and that of stream be y \( \frac{km}{h} \) , then
    1. Speed of boat downstream = (x + y) \( \frac{km}{h} \)
    2. Speed of boat upstream = (x - y) \( \frac{km}{h} \)
  • Let the speed of boat in downstream = u \( \frac{km}{h} \)
    Speed of boat in upstream = v \( \frac{km}{h} \) , then
    1. Speed of boat in still water = \( \frac{1}{2} (u + v) \frac{km}{h} \)
    2. speed of stream = \( \frac{1}{2} (u - v) \frac{km}{h} \)

Solved Examples

Example 1:

A 100 m long train is moving at a speed of 60 \( \frac{km}{h} \) . Then, the train will cross a signal pole in

Solution:

Here, speed of train = \( 60 \frac{km}{h} \)
\( \begin{align} = 60 \times \frac{5}{18} = \frac{50}{3} \frac{m}{s} \\\\[6pt] Using, \ Time = \frac{distance}{speed} \end{align} \)
\( \therefore \) Time taken to pass the pole = Time taken to cover a distance of 100 m at a speed of \( \frac{50}{3} \frac{m}{s} = 100 \times \frac{3}{50} = 6s \)

Example 2:

Two trains 130 m and 110 m in length are running towards each other on parallel tracks, one at the rate of 32 \( \frac{km}{h} \) and another at 40 \( \frac{km}{h} \) . In what time will they be clear each other from the moment they meet ?

Solution:

As trains are moving towards each other, so relative speed of the train
= 32 + 40 = 72 \( \frac{km}{h} \)
\( = 72 \times \frac{5}{18} = 20 \frac{m}{s} \)
Time taken by train in passing each other
\( = \frac{Distance \ equal \ to \ sum \ of \ length \ of \ times}{Relative \ speed} = \left ( \frac{240}{20} \right ) = 12 s \)

Example 3:

A sailor goes 8 km downstream in 40 min and returns back in 1 h. Then, the speed of the sailor in still water and the speed of the current is,

Solution:

Let the speed of the sailor in still water = x \( \frac{km}{h} \)
and speed of the current (stream) = y \( \frac{km}{h} \)
\( \therefore \) Speed of the sailor downstream = (x + y) \( \frac{km}{h} \)
and speed of the sailor upstream = (x - y) \( \frac{km}{h} \)
\( \therefore \) Time to travel 8 km downstream = 40 min = \( \frac{2}{3} h \)
\( \Rightarrow \frac{8}{x + y} = \frac{2}{3} \Rightarrow x + y = \frac{24}{2} = 12 \)
\( \Rightarrow x + y = 12 ............. (i) \)
Time to return = 1h
\( \Rightarrow \frac{8}{x - y} = 1 \Rightarrow x - y = 8 ...... (ii) \)
On solving Eqs.(i) and (ii), we get
x = 10 and y = 2
Here, speed of sailor = 10 \( \frac{km}{h} \)
Speed of stream = 2 \( \frac{km}{h} \)
