Highest Common Factor (HCF)

The Highest Common Factor (HCF) of two or more algebraic expressions is the factor of the highest degree which divides each of the expressions without a remainder.

Method to Find the HCF of Polynomials

  • Resolve all the polynomials in the complete factor form.
  • Required HCF is the product of the highest powers common to the polynomials.

Least Common Multiple (LCM)

The Least Common Multiple (LCM) of two or more algebraic expressions is the expression of the lowest dimensions which is divisible by each of them without remainder.

Method to Find the LCM of Polynomials

  • Resolve all the polynomials in the complete factor form.
  • Required LCM is the product of factors with their highest power which it has given in any polynomial.

Important Facts

  • LCM of two polynomials = \( \frac{Product \ of \ polynomials}{HCF \ of \ polynomials} \)
  • HCF of two polynomials = \( \frac{Product \ of \ polynomials}{LCM \ of \ polynomials} \)
  • HCF of \( \frac{p(x)}{r(x)} \) and \( \frac{q(x)}{s(x)} = \frac{HCF \ of \ p(x) \ and \ q(x)}{LCM \ of \ r(x) \ and \ s(x)} \)
  • LCM of \( \frac{p(x)}{r(x)} \) and \( \frac{q(x)}{s(x)} = \frac{LCM \ of \ p(x) \ and \ q(x)}{HCF \ of \ r(x) \ and \ s(x)} \)

Solved Examples

Example 1:

The HCF of \( p(x) = 24(6x^4 - x^3 - 2x^2) \) and \( q(x) = 20(2x^6 + 3x^5 + x^4) \) is

Solution:

\( \begin{align} p(x) = 24(6x^4 - x^3 - 2x^2) \\\\[6pt] = 6 \times 4 \times x^2(6x^2 - x - 2) \\\\[6pt] = 6 \times 4 \times x^2(6x^2 - 4x + 3x - 2) \\\\[6pt] = 6 \times 4 \times x^2[ 2x(3x - 2) + 1(3x - 2)] \\\\[6pt] = 6 \times 4 \times x^2(2x + 1)(3x - 2) \end{align} \)
and \( q(x) = 20(2x^6 + 3x^5 + x^4) \)
\( \begin{align} = 5 \times 4 \times x^4 (2x^2 + 3x + 1) \\\\[6pt] = 5 \times 4 \times x^4 (2x^2 + 2x + x + 1) \\\\[6pt] = 5 \times 4 \times x^4 [2x(x + 1) + 1(x + 1)] \\\\[6pt] = 5 \times 4 \times x^4 \times x^2 (2x + 1)(x + 1) \end{align} \)
\( \therefore \) HCF of p(x) and q(x) = \( 4x^2(2x + 1) \)

Example 2:

The HCF of \( 8x^4 - 16x^3 - 40x^2 + 48x \) and \( 16x^5 + 64x^4 + 80x^3 + 32x^2 \) is

Solution:

\( \begin{align} 8x^4 - 16x^3 - 40x^2 + 48x = 8x(x^3 - 2x^2 - 5x + 6) \\\\[6pt] = 8x(x - 1)(x^2 - x - 6) \\\\[6pt] = 8x(x - 1)(x^2 - 3x + 2x - 6) \\\\[6pt] = 8x(x - 1)(x + 2)(x - 3) \end{align} \)
and \( 16x^5 + 64x^4 + 80x^3 + 32x^2 \)
\( \begin{align} = 16x^2(x^3 + 4x^2 + 5x + 2) \\\\[6pt] = 16x^2(x + 1)(x^2 + 3x + 2) \\\\[6pt] = 16x^2(x + 1)(x + 1)(x + 2) \\\\[6pt] = 8x \times 2x(x + 1)^2(x + 2) \end{align} \)
\( \therefore \) HCF of polynomial = 8x(x + 2)

Example 3:

The LCM of \( x^3 - 2x^2 - x + 2 \) and \( x^3 - x^2 - 4x + 4 \) is

Solution:

Here, \( x^3 - 2x^2 - x + 2 = x^2(x - 2) - 1(x - 2) \)
\( = (x - 2)(x^2 - 1) = (x - 2)(x - 1)(x + 1) \)
and \( x^3 - x^2 - 4x + 4 = x^2(x - 1) - 4(x - 1) \)
\( = (x - 1)(x^2 - 4) = (x - 1)(x - 2)(x + 2) \)
\( \begin{align} \therefore Required \ LCM = (x - 1)(x + 1)(x - 2)(x + 2) \\\\[6pt] = (x^2 - 1^2)(x^2 - 2^2)= (x^2 - 1)(x^2 - 4) \end{align} \)

Example 4:

The HCF of two polynomials is x + 3 and their LCM is \( x^3 - 7x + 6 \) . If one of the polynomials is \( x^2 + 2x - 3 \) . Then, the other polynomial is

Solution:

Here, HCF = x + 3 and LCM = \( x^3 - 7x + 6 \)
Also, one polynomial = \( x^2 + 2x - 3 \)
As, LCM \( \times \) HCF = Product of polynomials
other polynomial = \( \frac{LCM \times HCF}{one \ polynomial} \)
\( \begin{align} = \frac{(x + 3) \times (x^3 - 7x + 6)}{x^2 + 2x - 3} \\\\[6pt] = \frac{(x + 3)(x - 1)(x^2 + x - 6)}{(x + 3)(x - 1)} \\\\[6pt] = x^2 + x - 6 \end{align} \)
So, the other polynomial is \( x^2 + x - 6 \)
