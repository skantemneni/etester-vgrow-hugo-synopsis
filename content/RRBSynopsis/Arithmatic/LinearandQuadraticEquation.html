Linear Equation

Any equation which can be put in the form ax + by + c = 0, where a, b and c are real numbers and a and b are not both zero is called linear equation. The solution of a linear equation is not affected when

  1. The same number is added to (or subtracted from) both the sides of the equation.
  2. You multiply or divide both the sides of the equation by the same non-zero number.

Linear Equation in One Variable

An equation of the form ax + b = 0 is called a linear equation in one variable.
Here, a and b are constants and x is variable.

Linear Equations in Two Variables

An equation of the form ax + by + c = 0 is known as linear equation in two variables. Here, x and y are variables.

Simultaneous Linear Equations in Two Variables

A pair of linear equations in two variables is said to form a system of simultaneous linear equations.
e.g., \( a_1 x + b_1 y + c_1 = 0 \ a_2 x + b_2 y + c_2 = 0 \)

Conditions of Solvability

The system of equations, \( a_1 x + b_1 y + c = 0 \) and \( a_2 x + b_2y + c_2 = 0 \)
Case 1. Has no solution, if \( \frac{a_1}{a_2} = \frac{b_1}{b_2} \neq \frac{c_1}{c_2} \)
Case 2. Has an infinite number of solutions, if \( \frac{a_1}{a_2} = \frac{b_1}{b_2} = \frac{c_1}{c_2} \)
Case 3. Has a unique solution, if \( \frac{a_1}{a_2} \neq \frac{b_1}{b_2} \)

Quadratic Equation

An equation of the form \( ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \) . where a, b and c are real number and \( a \neq 0 \) is called a quadratic equation invariable of x.

Methods of Solving Simultaneous Linear Equations

  • Method of eliminating by equating coefficients
  • Method of eliminating by substitution
  • Method of gross multiplication

Methods of Solving Quadratic Equation

  • By factorization method
  • By using formula

Formation of a Quadratic Equation

Let the roots of the equation be \( \alpha \) and \( \beta \) , then
S = Sum of roots = \( \alpha + \beta \)
P = Product of roots = \( \alpha . \beta \)
Then, quadratic equation is
\( x^2 - (\alpha + \beta)x + \alpha \beta = 0 \) or \( x^2 - Sx + P = 0 \)

Important Formulae

  • The general form of a quadratic equation is \( ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \) where a, b, c \( \epsilon \) R and a \( \neq \) 0
  • If \( \alpha, \ \beta \) are the roots of \( ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \) then \( \alpha = \frac{-b + \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} ; \ \beta = \frac{-b - \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \)
  • \( b^2 - 4ac = D(discriminant) \)
  • If D > 0; roots are real and unequal
  • If D = 0; roots are real and equal
  • If D < 0; roots are imaginary
  • Sum of roots \( (\alpha + \beta) = - \frac{b}{a} \)
  • Product of roots \( (\alpha . \beta) = \frac{c}{a} \)
  • A quadratic equation whose roots are \( \alpha \) and \( \beta \) is given by \( x^2 - (\alpha + \beta)x + \alpha \beta = 0 \)
  • If \( \alpha \) and \( \beta \) are the roots of the quadratic equation p(x) = 0 then \( (x - \alpha) \) and \( (x - \beta) \) are factors of p(x).
    \( p(x) = k(x - \alpha)(x - \beta) \) where K is a non-zero constant.

Solved Examples

Example 1:

Sum of two numbers is 35 and their difference is 13. Find the numbers

Solution:

Let the two numbers be x and y.
Then,
x + y = 35 .......(i)
and x - y = 13 ...(ii)
Adding Eqs. (i) and (ii). we get
2x = 48
\( \Rightarrow x = 24 \)
On putting the value of x in Eq. (i), we get
y = 11
Hence, the two numbers are 24 and 11.

Example 2:

In a two digit number, the unit's digit is twice the ten's digit. If 27 is added to the number, the digits interchange their places. Find the number.

Solution:

Let the digit in unit's place be x and digit in the ten's place be y. Then,
x = 2y [Given] ....... (i)
and number = 10y + x
Number obtained by reversing the digit
= 10x + y
According to question,
10y + x + 27 = 10x + y
\( \Rightarrow \) 9x - 9y = 27
\( \Rightarrow \) x - y = 3 ...... (ii)
Putting x = 2y in Eq.(ii), we get
2y - y = 3
\( \Rightarrow \) Y = 3
On putting y = 3 in Eq. (ii), we get
x = 6
Hence, the number is
10 y + x = 10 \( \times \) 3 + 6 = 36

Example 3:

Five years hence, father's age will be three times the age of his son. Five years ago, father was seven times as old as his son. Find their present age of father.

Solution:

Let the present age of father be x yr and that of son be y yr.
According to question,
Five years hence,
Father's age = (x + 5)yr
Son's age = (y + 5)yr
Now using the given information, we have
x + 5 = 3(y + 5)
\( \Rightarrow \) x - 3y = 10 ........ (i)
Five years ago,
Father's age = (x - 5)yr
Son's age = (y - 5)yr
Hence, (x - 5) = 7(y - 5)
\( \Rightarrow \) x - 7y = - 30
subtracting Eqs.(i) and (ii), we get
4y = 40
\( \Rightarrow \) y = 10
therefore x = 40
Hence, present age of father is 40 yr.

Example 4:

The speed of a boat in still water is 15 \( \frac{km}{h} \) . It can go 30 km upstream and return downstream to the original point in 4 h 30 min. Find the speed of the stream.

Solution:

Let the speed of the stream be x \( \frac{km}{h} \) .
Then, speed downstream = (15 + x) \( \frac{km}{h} \)
and speed upstream = (15 - x) \( \frac{km}{h} \)
Time taken by boat to go 30 km upstream = \( \frac{30}{15 - x} h \)
Time taken by boat to return 30 km downstream = \( \frac{30}{15 + x} h \)
Total time taken 4 h 30 min
\( \begin{align} \therefore \frac{30}{15 - x} + \frac{30}{15 + x} = \frac{9}{2} \\\\[6pt] \Rightarrow \frac{900}{225 - X^2} = \frac{9}{2} \\\\[6pt] \Rightarrow 9(225 - x^2) = 900 \times 2 \\\\[6pt] \Rightarrow 225 - x^2 = 200 \\\\[6pt] \Rightarrow x^2 = 25 \\\\[6pt] \Rightarrow x = \pm 5 \end{align} \)
Hence, the speed of the stream = 5 \( \frac{km}{h} \) .

Example 5:

The system of equations is x + 3y = 4 and 2x + 6y = 8

Solution:

From Eq.(i), x = 4 - 3y
On putting the value of x in Eq.(ii), we get
2 (4 - 3y) + 6y = 8 \( \Rightarrow \) 8 - 6y + 6y = 8
\( \therefore \) 8 = 8
Which is always true.
So, the given system of equations is dependent and thus has an infinite number of solutions.

Example 6:

The system of equations has unique solution of when ax + by = c and lx + my = n

Solution:

The system has unique solution, if \( \frac{a_1}{a_2} \neq \frac{b_1}{b_2} \)
i.e., \( \frac{a}{l} = \frac{b}{m} \)
\( \Rightarrow am \neq bl \)
\( \Rightarrow am - bl \neq 0 \)
