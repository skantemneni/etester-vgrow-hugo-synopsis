Factors

A polynomial g(x) is called a factor of polynomial p(x), if g(x) divides p(x) exactly

Factorization

To express polynomial as the product of polynomials of degree less than that of the given polynomial is called as factorization.
e.g., \( x^2 - 7x + 12 = x^2 - 3x - 4x + 12 \)
= x(x - 3) - 4(x - 3)
= (x - 4)(x - 3)

Remainder Theorem

Let p(x) be a polynomial in 'x' of degree not less than one and \( \alpha \) be a real number. If p(x) is divided by \( (x - \alpha) \) , then remainder is \( f(\alpha) \) . Remainder can be evaluated by substituting \( x = \alpha \) in p(x).

Factor Theorem

Let p(x) be a polynomial in x of degree not less than 1 and \( \alpha \) be a real number.
If \( p(\alpha) \) = 0, then \( (x - \alpha) \) is a factor of p(x).
If (x - a) is a factor of p(x), then p(a) = 0

Important Formulae

  • \( (a + b)^2 = a^2 + b^2 + 2ab \) and \( (a - b)^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab \)
  • \( (a + b)^2 - (a - b)^2 = 4ab \) and \( (a + b)^2 + (a - b)^2 = 2(a^2 + b^2) \)
  • \( (a + b)^3 = a^3 + b^3 + 3ab(a + b) \)
  • \( (a - b)^3 = a^3 - b^3 - 3ab(a - b) \) and \( (a^2 - b^2) = (a + b)(a - b) \)
  • \( (a^3 + b^3) = (a + b)(a^2 + b^2 - ab) \)
  • \( (a^3 - b^3) = (a - b)(a^2 + b^2 + ab) \)
  • \( (a + b + c)^2 = [a^2 + b^2 + c^2 + 2(ab + bc + ca)] \)
  • If a + b + c = 0, then \( a^3 + b^3 + c^3 = 3abc \) , while \( (a^3 + b^3 + c^3 - 3abc) = (a + b + c) \)

Solved Examples

Example 1:

The factor form \( 4(3a - 2b)^2 - 5(3a - 2b) \) is

Solution:

\( 4(3a - 2b)^2 - 5(3a - 2b) \)
= (3a - 2b)[4(3a - 2b) - 5] = (3a - 2b)[12a - 8b - 5]

Example 2:

The factor form xy + yz + xa + za is

Solution:

xy + yz + xa + za = y(x + z) + a(x + z) = (x + z)(y + a)

Example 3:

The factor form \( x^4 + x^2 y^2 + y^4 \) is

Solution:

\( \begin{align} x^4 + x^2 y^2 + y^4 = x^4 + 2 x^2 y^2 + y^4 - x^2 y^2 \\\\[6pt] = (x^2 + y^2)^2 - (xy)^2 = (x^2 + y^2 + xy)(x^2 + y^2 - xy) \end{align} \)

Example 4:

The factor form \( (2x + 3y)^3 - (2x - 3y)^3 \) is

Solution:

\( \begin{align} (2x + 3y)^3 - (2x - 3y)^3 \\\\[6pt] put \ 2x + 3y = a \ and \ 2x - 3y = b \\\\[6pt] \therefore (2x + 3y)^3 - (2x - 3y)^3 = a^3 - b^3 \\\\[6pt] = (a - b)(a^2 + ab + b^2) \\\\[6pt] = (2x + 3y - 2x + 3y) \times [(2x + 3y)^2 + (2x + 3y)(2x - 3y) + (2x - 3y)^2] \\\\[6pt] = 6y(4x^2 + 9y^2 + 12xy + 4x^2 - 9y^2 + 4x^2 + 9y^2 - 12xy) \\\\[6pt] = 6y(12x^2 + 9y^2) = 18y(4x^2 + 3y^2) \\\\[6pt] Hence, \ (2x + 3y)^3 - (2x - 3y)^3 = 18y(4x^2 + 3y^2) \end{align} \)
