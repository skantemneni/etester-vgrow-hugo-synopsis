Introduction

To perform complete work or part of the work requires efforts of person(s) over a period of time. Therefore, the number of persons (P), the quantity of work (W) and period of time taken (T) are important variable in the problems of time and work

Some Solving Techniques of Time and Work Problems

Time taken (T) to complete a work depends upon the following variables

  • How many persons are engaged in performing the work?
  • How efficient are they?
  • How many hours per day are the workers working?

Important Facts

  • If a person can do a piece of work in n days, he will do \( \frac{1}{n} \) of the work in one day.
  • If the number of person doing a piece of job is increased or (decreased) in a certain ratio, the time needed to do the same work will be decreased or (increased) in the same ratio.
  • If A is twice as good as a workman as B, then A take one half of the time taken by B to do a certain piece of work.
  • Wages for a piece of work are paid in proportion to the work done by the workers.
    If 'm', men or 'n' women can do a piece of work in 'a' days, then x men and y women can do the same work in \( \frac{1}{ \frac{x}{ma} + \frac{y}{na}} \) days.
  • (Some facts for problems on pipes and cistern) If there are two taps A and B takes a h and b h, respectively to fill a tank, then two taps together fill \( \left ( \frac{1}{a} + \frac{1}{b} \right ) \) part of the tank in an hour and the entire tank is filled in \( \frac{ab}{a + b} h \) .

Solved Examples

Example 1:

Raj can do a piece of work in 20 days and Rohan can do it in 12 days. How long will they take, if both work together?

Solution:

\( \because \) Raj's one day's work = \( \frac{1}{20} \) part
and Rohan's one day's work = \( \frac{1}{12} \) part
\( \therefore \) Raj and Rohan's one day's work = \( \frac{1}{20} + \frac{1}{12} = \frac{3 + 5}{60} = \frac{8}{60} \ part \)
\( \therefore \) Number of days taken by Raj and Rohan together to complete the work = \( \left ( 1 \div \frac{8}{60} \right ) = \frac{60}{8} \) days or \( 7 \frac{1}{2} \) days.

Example 2:

A and B can do a piece of work in 72 days. B and C in 120 days and A and C in 90 days. In what time can A alone do it?

Solution:

Let A, B and C can do a piece of work in x, y and z days respectively.
So A's, B's and C's one day's work are \( \frac{1}{x}, \ \frac{1}{y} \) and \( \frac{1}{z} \) respectively.
\( \begin{align} \therefore \frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{y} = \frac{1}{72} ...... (i) \\\\[6pt] \frac{1}{y} + \frac{1}{z} = \frac{1}{120}......... (ii) \\\\[6pt] and \ \frac{1}{z} + \frac{1}{x} = \frac{1}{90}......... (iii) \end{align} \)
On adding Eqs.(i), (ii) and (iii), we get
\( \begin{align} 2 \left ( \frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{y} + \frac{1}{z} \right ) = \frac{5 + 3 + 4}{360} \\\\[6pt] \frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{y} + \frac{1}{z} = \frac{1}{60} ...... (iv) \end{align} \)
On solving Eqs.(iv) and (ii), we get
\( \frac{1}{x} = \frac{1}{60} - \frac{1}{120} = \frac{1}{120} \)
Hence, A alone will complete the work in 120 days.

Example 3:

If 3 men or 4 women can reap a field in 43 days, how long will 7 men and 5 women take to reap it?

Solution:

Here, 3 men's work = 4 women's work
Since, 3 men can reap \( \frac{1}{43} \) of the field in 1 day
\( \therefore \) 1 man can reap \( \frac{1}{129} \) of the field in 1 day
Also, 4 women can reap \( \frac{1}{43} \) of the field in 1 day
\( \therefore \) 1 woman can reap \( \frac{1}{172} \) of the field in 1 day
\( \therefore \) 7 men and 5 women can reap \( \left ( \frac{7}{129} + \frac{5}{172} \right ) \)
of the field in 1 day or \( \frac{1}{12} \) of the field in 1 day.
Hence, 7 men and 5 women will reap the whole field in 12 days.

Example 4:

Two pipes A and B can fill a tank in 36 min and 4 min respectively. A pipe C can empty the tank in 30 min. If all the three pipes are opened simultaneously, then in how much time the tank will be completely filled?

Solution:

Part filled by pipe A in 1 min = \( \frac{1}{36} \)
and part filled by pipe B in 1 min = \( \frac{1}{45} \)
\( \therefore \) Total water dropped in the tank in 1 min = \( \frac{1}{36} + \frac{1}{45} \)
Water leaked. out in 1 min by C = \( \frac{1}{30} \)
\( \therefore \) Total tank filled in 1 min = \( \frac{1}{36} + \frac{1}{45} - \frac{1}{30} \)
= \( \frac{4 + 5 - 6}{180} = \frac{3}{180} = \frac{1}{60} \)
Hence, the tank will be filled in 60 min or 1 h.
