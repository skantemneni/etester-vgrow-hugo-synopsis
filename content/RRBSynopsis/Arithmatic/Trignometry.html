Measure Of an Angle

There are three system for measuring angles.

  1. Sexagesimal System (English System) In this system, angle is measured in degrees.
    \( 1 \ right \ angle = 90^{\circ}, \ 1^{\circ} = 60' \ and \ 1' = 60" \)
  2. Centesimal System (French System) In this system, angle is measured in grades.
    \( 1 \ right \ angle = 100^g, \ 1^g = 100' \ and \ 1' = 100'' \)
  3. Circular Measure In this system, angle is measured in radians.

The angle subtended at the centre of a circle by an arc of length equal to its radius is 1 radian, as \( 1^c \) .
On comparing all the above three measuring systems, we have
\( \pi^c = 180^{\circ} = 200^g = 2 \ right \ angles \)

Relation Between Radius and Arc

Let l be the length of an arc of circle of radius r. This arc subtends an angle \( \theta \) (in radian) at the centre of circle, then \( l = r \theta \) .

Trigonometric Ratios

In \( \triangle \) ABC, \( H^2 = P^2 + B^2 \)

\( \begin{align} sin \ \theta = \frac{P}{H} \\\\[6pt] cos \ \theta = \frac{B}{H} \\\\[6pt] tan \ \theta = \frac{P}{B} \\\\[6pt] cot \ \theta = \frac{B}{P} \\\\[6pt] sec \ \theta = \frac{H}{B} \\\\[6pt] cosec \ \theta = \frac{H}{P} \end{align} \)

Identities

  1. \( sin^2 \theta + cos^2 \theta = 1 \)
  2. \( 1 + tan^2 \theta = sec^2 \theta \)
  3. \( 1 + cot^2 \theta = cosec^2 \theta \)

Some Important Formulae

  • \( sin \theta = \frac{1}{cosec \theta} \Rightarrow cosec \theta = \frac{1}{sin \theta} \)
  • \( cos \theta = \frac{1}{sec \theta} \Rightarrow sec \theta = \frac{1}{cos \theta} \)
  • \( tan \theta = \frac{1}{cot \theta} \Rightarrow cot \theta = \frac{1}{tan \theta} \)
  • \( tan \theta = \frac{sin \theta}{cos \theta} \Rightarrow cot \theta = \frac{cos \theta}{sin \theta} \)

Solved Examples

Example 1:

If \( sin \theta = \frac{8}{17} \) , then the value of \( cos \theta \) and \( tan \theta \) is

Solution:

we have, \( sin \theta = \frac{8}{17} = \frac{P}{H} \)

\( \begin{align} \because PQ^2 + QR^2 = PR^2 \\\\[6pt] \therefore PQ = \sqrt{PR^2 - QR^2} \\\\[6pt] = \sqrt{289 - 64} = \sqrt{225} = 15 \\\\[6pt] \therefore cos \theta = \frac{B}{H} = \frac{PQ}{PR} = \frac{15}{17} \\\\[6pt] tan \theta = \frac{P}{B} = \frac{RQ}{PQ} = \frac{8}{15} \end{align} \)

Example 2:

If \( Cot A = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2} - 1} \) , then sin A . cos A is equal to

Solution:

\( \begin{align} Cot A = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2} - 1} \\\\[6pt] \Rightarrow tan A = \frac{\sqrt{2} - 1}{1} \\\\[6pt] \therefore AC^2 = AB^2 + BC^2 \\\\[6pt] \therefore AC = \sqrt{AB^2 + BC^2} \\\\[6pt] = \sqrt{(1)^2 + (\sqrt{2} - 1)^2} \end{align} \)
Now, \( = \sqrt{1 + 2 + 1 - 2 \sqrt{2}} \)
\( \begin{align} = \sqrt{4 - 2 \sqrt{2}} \\\\[6pt] \therefore sin A = \frac{BC}{AC} = \frac{\sqrt{2} - 1}{\sqrt{4 - 2 \sqrt{2}}} \ and \ cos A = \frac{AB}{AC} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{4 - 2 \sqrt{2}}} \\\\[6pt] \therefore sin A . cos A = \frac{\sqrt{2} - 1}{\sqrt{4 - 2 \sqrt{2}}} . \frac{1}{\sqrt{4 - 2 \sqrt{2}}} = \frac{\sqrt{2} - 1}{\sqrt{4 - 2 \sqrt{2}}} \\\\[6pt] = \frac{\sqrt{2} - 1}{2 \sqrt{2}(\sqrt{2} - 1)} = \frac{1}{2 \sqrt{2}} . \frac{\sqrt{2}}{\sqrt{2}} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{4} \end{align} \)

Example 3:

The value of \( (tan 1^{\circ} tan 2^{\circ} tan 3^{\circ}...... tan 89^{\circ}) \) is

Solution:

\( (tan 1^{\circ} tan 2^{\circ} tan 3^{\circ}...... tan 89^{\circ}) \)
Now, take \( tan 1^{\circ} tan 89^{\circ} \)
\( \begin{align} = tan 1^{\circ} [ cot ( 90^{\circ} - 89^{\circ}) ] \\\\[6pt] = tan 1^{\circ} cot 1^{\circ} \\\\[6pt] = tan 1^{\circ} \times \frac{1}{tan 1^{\circ}} = 1 \end{align} \)
\( \therefore \) All terms upto \( tan 44^{\circ} \) will be equal to 1 and \( tan 45^{\circ} = 1 \)
\( \therefore tan 1^{\circ} tan 89^{\circ} tan 2^{\circ} tan 88^{\circ}...... tan 45^{\circ} = 1 \)

Example 4:

If \( cos \theta + sin \theta = \sqrt{2} cos \theta \) , then \( cos \theta - sin \theta \) is

Solution:

\( cos \theta + sin \theta = \sqrt{2} cos \theta \)
On squaring both sides, we get
\( \begin{align} (cos \theta + sin \theta)^2 = (\sqrt{2} cos \theta)^2 cos^2 \theta + sin^2 \theta + 2 sin \theta cos \theta = 2 cos^2 \theta \\\\[6pt] 2 sin \theta cos \theta = (cos \theta - sin \theta)(cos \theta + sin \theta) \\\\[6pt] cos \theta - sin \theta = \frac{2 sin \theta cos \theta}{(cos \theta + sin \theta)} = \frac{2 sin \theta cos \theta}{\sqrt{2} cos \theta} = \sqrt{2} sin \theta \end{align} \)
