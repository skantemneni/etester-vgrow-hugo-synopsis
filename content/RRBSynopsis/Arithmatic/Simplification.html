Introduction

For simplifying an expression containing various types of operation, the order of operating various operations, should be strictly followed as accordance with.

VBODMAS Rule

This rule gives the correct order in which various operations regarding simplification are to be performed. so as to find out the values of given expressions in simple ways. Let us see what these letters mean. Clearly, the order will be as follows.
First Vinculum bracket is solved,
[Remember - 5 - 10 = -15 but - \( \overline{5 - 10} \) = - (- 5) = 5]
Second Brackets are to be solved in order given above,
[first, then second, then third and then fourth]
Third Operation of 'Of' is done,
Fourth Operation of 'Division' is done,
Fifth Operation of 'Multiplication' is done.
Sixth Operation of 'Addition' is done and
Seventh Operation of 'Subtraction' is done.

Important Formulae

  • \( (a + b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2 \)
  • \( (a - b)^2 = a^2 - 2ab + b^2 \)
  • \( (a + b)(a - b) = a^2 - b^2 \)
  • \( (x + a)(x + b) = x^2 + (a + b)x + ab \)
  • \( (a + b + c)^2 = a^2 + b^2 + c^2 + 2ab + 2bc + 2ca \)
  • \( (a + b)^3 = a^3 + b^3 + 3ab(a + b) \)
  • \( a^3 + b^3 = (a + b)^3 - 3ab(a + b) \)
  • \( (a - b)^3 = a^3 - b^3 - 3ab(a - b) \)
  • \( a^3 + b^3 = (a + b)(a^2 - ab + b^2) \)
  • \( a^3 - b^3 = (a - b)^3 + 3ab(a - b) \)
  • \( a^3 - b^3 = (a - b)(a^2 + ab + b^2) \)

Solved Examples

Example 1:

Simplify the expression \( \left [ 11 \div 2 \frac{1}{5} \right ] \div \frac{11}{5} \ Of \ 2 \frac{1}{2} - 2 \)

Solution:
Applying VBODMAS rule,
\( \begin{align} \left [ 11 \times \frac{1}{2 \frac{1}{5}} \right ] \div \frac{11}{5} \ Of \ 2 \frac{1}{2} - 2 \\\\[6pt] = 5 \div \frac{11}{5} \times 2 \frac{1}{2} - 2 \\\\[6pt] = 5 \div \frac{11}{5} \times \frac{5}{2} - 2 \\\\[6pt] = 5 \times \frac{5}{11} \times \frac{5}{2} - 2 \\\\[6pt] = \frac{125}{22} - 2 = 3 \frac{15}{22} \end{align} \)

Example 2:

Simplify the expression \( \frac{14 \times 14 - 46}{11 \times 6 - (4)^2} \) = ?

Solution:
Putting x for (?) and applying VBODMAS rule, we get
\( \Rightarrow x = \frac{196 - 46}{66 - 16} \Rightarrow x = \frac{150}{50} \Rightarrow x = 3 \)

Example 3:

Simplify the expression \( 11 \frac{1}{3} \times 4 \frac{8}{10} \div x = 22 \frac{2}{3} \)

Solution:
\( \begin{align} \Rightarrow 11 \frac{1}{3} \times 4 \frac{8}{10} \div x = 22 \frac{2}{3} \\\\[6pt] \Rightarrow 11 \frac{1}{3} \times 4 \frac{8}{10} = 22 \frac{2}{3} \times x \\\\[6pt] \Rightarrow x = \frac{1}{2} \times 4 \frac{8}{10} \\\\[6pt] \Rightarrow x = \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{48}{10} \\\\[6pt] \Rightarrow x = 2.4 \end{align} \)
