Ratio

Comparison of two quantities of same kind by division is known as ratio.
If a and b are two non-zero numbers of same kind, then \( \frac{a}{b} \) or a : b represents their ratio.
i.e., Rs 5 : Rs 10
Rs 5 : 80 paise is not a ratio.

Types Of Ratio

Duplicate Ratio It is the ratio of the squares of two numbers
e.g., Duplicate Ratio of 3 : 4 = \( 3^2 : 4^2 = 9 : 16 \)
Sub-duplicate Ratio It is the ratio of the square roots of two numbers
e.g., Sub-duplicate ratio of 4 : 9 = \( \sqrt{4} : \sqrt{9} = 2 : 3 \)
Triplicate Ratio It is the ratio of the cubes of two numbers
e.g., Triplicate ratio of 2 : 3 = \( 2^3 : 3^3 = 8 : 27 \)
Sub-triplicate Ratio It is the ratio of the cube roots of two number.
e.g., Sub-triplicate ratio of 64 : 27 = \( \sqrt[3]{64} : \sqrt[3]{27} = 4 : 3 \)

Proportion

When two ratios are equal then it is said to proportion.
i.e., a : b = c : d
proportion is represented by (::)
ie., a : b :: c : d
Example An object 1.6 m long casts a shadow 1.4 m long. At the same time another object kept nearby casts a shadow 6.2 m long. Find the length of the second object.
Sol. Ratio of length of the object to its shadow would be same.
\( \begin{align} \therefore 1.6 : 1.4 = x : 6.2 \\\\[6pt] \Rightarrow x = \frac{1.6 \times 6.2}{1.4} = 7.08 \ m \end{align} \)

Some Results on Ratio and Proportion

  1. Invertendo - If a : b :: c : d, then b : a :: d : c
  2. Alternendo - If a : b :: c : d, then a : c :: b : d
  3. Componendo - If a : b :: c : d, then (a + b) : b :: (c + d) : d
  4. Dividendo - If a : b :: c : d, then (a - b) : b :: (c - d) : d
  5. Componendo - If a : b :: c : d, then
    and Dividendo (a + b) : (a - b) :: (c + d):(c - d)

Equating the Components of Two and More Ratios

If \( \frac{a}{b} = \frac{2}{3}, \ \frac{b}{c} = \frac{4}{5} \) . Then, find a : b : c.
To equate, common component b in the two ratios, take the LCM of 3 and 4 which is 12. Hence, the new ratios are obtained as
\( \frac{a}{b} = \frac{2 \times 4}{3 \times 4} = \frac{8}{12}, \ \frac{b}{c} = \frac{4 \times 3}{5 \times 3} = \frac{12}{15} \)
Now, since the common component b in the two ratios has the equal values,
a : b : c = 8 : 12 : 15
Therefore, if \( \frac{a}{b} = \frac{n_1}{d_1}, \ \frac{b}{c} = \frac{n_2}{d_2} \)
\( \therefore a : b : c = n_1 \times n_2 : d_1 \times n_2 : d_1 \times d_2 \)

Note

If \( \frac{a}{b} = \frac{n_1}{d_1}, \ \frac{b}{c} = \frac{n_2}{d_2} \ and \ \frac{c}{d} = \frac{n_3}{d_3} \)
Then, a : b : c : d = \( n_1 \times n_2 \times n_3 : d_1 \times n_2 \times n_3 : d_2 \times d_1 \times n_3 : d_3 \times d_2 \times d_1 \)

Mixture

The new product obtained by mixing two or more ingredients in a certain ratio is called a mixture of those particular ingredients.

Rules of Mixture or Alligation

  • This rule of mixture is used to determine the mean price of the mixture, when the prices of the individual items being mixed together and the proportion in which they are being mixed are given.
  • When two ingredients at given prices are known, then the ratio in which these two are mixed to obtain a mixture of known price is given by
    \( \frac{Amount \ of \ cheaper}{Amount \ of \ dearer} = \frac{Cost \ price \ of \ dearer - Mean \ price}{ Mean \ price - Cost \ price \ of \ cheaper} \)
    It is also known as rule of alligation. It can also be expressed, as
    Hence, Amount of cheaper :
    Amount of dearer = (D - M) : (M - C)

Partnership

Partnership is an association of two or more persons who invested their money on a certain business. It can be classified in two ways which are given below

  • If there is profit in the business run by P and Q, then
    \( \begin{align} \frac{Amount \ of \ investment \ of \ P}{Amount \ of \ investment \ of \ Q} \times \frac{Number \ of \ months \ invested \ by \ P}{Number \ of \ months \ invested \ by \ Q} \\\\[6pt] = \frac{Profit \ of \ P}{Profit \ of \ Q} \\\\[6pt] \Rightarrow \frac{P's monthly equivalent of investment (MEI)}{ Q's monthly equivalent of investment (MEI)} = \frac{Profit \ of \ P}{Profit \ of \ Q} \end{align} \)
  • If more than two persons invest money in a business, then MEI of P : MEI of Q : MEI of R = Profit of P : Profit of Q : Profit of R
  • If there is loss in business, then
    MEI of P : MEI of Q : MEI of R = Loss of P : Loss of Q : Loss of R
  • If the number of months invested or period of investment is the same for each partner, then
    P's investment : Q's investment : R's investment = P's profit : Q's profit : R's profit

Solved Examples

Example 1:

What number should be added or subtracted from each term of the ratio 17 : 24, so that it becomes equal to 1 : 2?

Solution:

Let the number be x, then \( \frac{17 - x}{24 - x} = \frac{1}{2} \)
\( \begin{align} 34 - 2x = 24 - x \\\\[6pt] \therefore x = 10 \end{align} \)

Example 2:

A person distributes his pens among four friends A, B, C and D in the ratio \( \frac{1}{3} : \frac{1}{4} : \frac{1}{5} : \frac{1}{6} \) . What is the minimum number of pens that the person should have?

Solution:

The ratio among A, B, C and D = \( \frac{1}{3} : \frac{1}{4} : \frac{1}{5} : \frac{1}{6} \)
On rearranging the ratio
= \( \frac{60}{3} : \frac{60}{4} : \frac{60}{5} : \frac{60}{6} = 20 : 15 : 12 : 10 \)
So, minimum number of pens can be when the common ratio is 1.
So, minimum number of pen = 20 + 15 + 12 + 10 = 57

Example 3:

A can contains a mixture of two liquids A and B in the ratio 7 : 5. When 9 L of mixture are drawn off and the can is filled with B, the ratio of A and B becomes 7 : 9. Litres of liquid A contained by the can initially was

Solution:

Let the mixture of two liquids be 7x L and 5x L. If 9 L of mixture be drawn, then
Liquid A, \( 7x - 9 \times \frac{7}{7 + 5} = 7x - \frac{63}{12} = 7x - \frac{21}{4} L \)
and Liquid B, \( 5x - 9 \times \frac{7}{7 + 5} = 5x - \frac{45}{12} = 5x - \frac{15}{4} L \)
When 9 L mixture is mixed in B, then the quantity of
\( B = \left ( 5x - \frac{15}{4} + 9 \right ) L \)
According to the given condition,
\( \begin{align} \frac{ \left ( 7x - \frac{21}{4} \right )}{\left ( 5x - \frac{15}{4} + 9 \right )} = \frac{7}{9} \Rightarrow 63x - \frac{189}{4} = 35x - \frac{105}{4} + 63 \\\\[6pt] \Rightarrow 63x - 35x = \frac{-105}{4} + 63 + \frac{189}{4} \Rightarrow 28x = \frac{-105 + 252 + 189}{4} \\\\[6pt] \Rightarrow 28x = \frac{336}{4} = 84 \ \therefore x = \frac{84}{28} = 3 \\\\[6pt] \therefore \ quantity \ of \ A = 7x = 7 \times 3 = 21 L \end{align} \)

Example 4:

A and B are partners in a business. A contributes \( \frac{1}{4} \) of the capital for 15 months and B received \( \frac{2}{3} \) of the profit. Find for how long B's money was used?

Solution:

Let B contributer \( \frac{3}{4} \) of the capital for x months.
According to the question, \( 15 \times \frac{1}{4} : x \times \frac{3}{4} = \left ( 1 - \frac{2}{3} \right ) : \frac{2}{3} \)
\( \Rightarrow \frac{5}{x} = \frac{1}{2} \)
\( \therefore \) x = 10 months
