Interest

If we borrow money from a bank, then we return the sum after sometime with some extra money. This extra amount paid is known as interest.
There are two types of interest

  1. Simple Interest
  2. Compound Interest

Simple Interest

If on a sum borrowed for a certain period, interest is reckoned uniformly, interest is called simple interest.

Important facts
  • The amount borrowed is called principal or sum denoted by P.
  • Extra amount is interest denoted by I.
  • Time for which amount is borrowed is denoted by T.
  • Amount = Principal + Interest
  • Simple Interest (SI) = \( \frac{P \times R \times T}{100} \)

Compound Interest

If the principal does not remain the same for the entire loan period, due to addition of interest to the principal after a certain interest of time to form the new principal, then the interest obtained is called compound interest.

Important Formulae (Rules)

  • Consider P = Principal (amount borrowed)
    A = Amount (principal + interest)
    T = Time
    R = Rate of interest
    n = Number of time for which interest is calculated.
    When interest is compounded annually
    \( Amount = P \left ( 1 + \frac{R}{100} \right )^n \)
    When interest is compounded half-yearly
    Here, as interest would be calculated after every six months.
    Then, \( n = T \times 2 \ and \ rate = \frac{R}{2} \)
    So, \( A = P \left ( 1 + \frac{ \frac{R}{2}}{100} \right )^n \)
    when rate of interest are different for different years say \( R_1 \%, \ R_2 \%, \ R_3 \% \) for first, second and third year respectively, then amount
    \( A = P \left ( 1 + \frac{R_1}{100} \right ) \left ( 1 + \frac{R_2}{100} \right ) \left ( 1 + \frac{R_3}{100} \right ) \)
  • If A and B are the amounts of a certain sum for two consecutive year, then simple interest for 1 yr = B - A
Note
  • When interest is compounded yearly, then on the same sum and at the same rate, then
    Compound interest for the 1st year = Simple interest for the 1st year
  • If the difference between the simple interest and compound interest on the same sum Rs. P at r% per annum for 2 yr is Rs. d, then \( d = P \left ( \frac{r}{100} \right )^2 \)
  • If the difference between the SI and CI on the same sum Rs.P at r % per annum for 3 yr is Rs.d then \( d = \frac{P \times r^2 \times (300 + r)}{(100)^3} \)

Solved Examples

Example 1:

The sum required to earn a monthly interest of Rs. 400 at 10% per annum at simple interest is

Solution:

Total interest needed in a year \( Rs. 400 \times 12 = Rs. 4800 \)
\( \therefore P = \frac{100 \times SI}{R \times T} = \frac{100 \times 4800}{10 \times 1} = Rs. 48000 \)

Example 2:

The difference between the compound interest and the simple interest on a certain sum at 12% per annum for 2 yr is Rs. 90. The sum is equal to

Solution:

Let the sum is Rs. P
Given, rate of interest is 12% and time period is 2 year.
Clearly, difference = \( P \left ( \frac{r}{100} \right )^2 \)
\( 90 = P \left ( \frac{12}{100} \right )^2 ; \ 90 = P \left ( \frac{144}{10000} \right ) \)
\( P = \frac{900000}{144} = Rs. 6250 \)

Example 3:

A sum of money at compound interest doubles itself in 15 yr. It will become eight times of itself in

Solution:

Amount = \( Principal \left ( 1 + \frac{Rate}{100} \right )^{time} \)
A sum of money at compound interest doubles itself in 15 yr. It will become \( 8 = 2^3 \) times of itself = 15 \( \times \) 3 = 45 yr

Example 4:

A person takes a loan of Rs. 10000 partly from a bank at 8% per annum and remaining from another bank at 10% per annum. He pays a total interest of Rs.950 per annum. Amount of loan taken from the first bank ( in Rs) is

Solution:

Let, amount of loan taken from the first bank be Rs.x
According to the question,
\( \frac{x \times 8 \times 1}{100} + \frac{(10000 - x) \times 10 \times 1}{100} = 950 \)
\( 1000 - \frac{2x}{100} = 950 \Rightarrow 2x = 50 \times 100 \Rightarrow x = Rs. 2500 \)
