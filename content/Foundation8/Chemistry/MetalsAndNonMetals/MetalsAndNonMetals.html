Common misconception Fact
1 All metals are solids and hard. Metals like sodium and potassium are soft and can be cut with a knife. Mercury is the liquid metal.
2 Non-metals are bad conductors of heat and electricity. Graphite a carbon form, is a good conductor of electricity.
3 All metals and non-metals have different, distinctive properties and hence can be differentiated easily. Elements like antimony and arsenic have properties of both metals and non-metals and such substances are called metalloids.

Introduction

All matter in the nature is made from only a few elements. These elements are made up of smallest particles called atoms that display properties of that element. Hence, every element displays a definite set of properties. Yet, elements have their properties either similar or different from one another. Based on this elements can be classified mainly into metals and non-metals apart from a group of elements having both characters of metals and non-metals generalised as metalloids.

Metals

Metal is an element which can form a positive ion by losing electrons. Hence they are also known as electropositive elements.
eg: Al, Cu, Fe, Zn etc.,

Occurrence of Metals

Metals can occur both in free and combined states.

Free state

When a metal is found in free state, then it is in uncombined state.
Example
Gold, Silver, Platinum

Combined state

Reactive metals like Fe, Al, Ca, Mg, etc., generally occur in the form of their compounds such as oxides, sulphides, sulphates, carbonates, nitrates, chlorides etc.

Non-Metals

Non-metal is an element which can form a negative ion by gaining electrons. Hence they are also known as electronegative elements.
eg: C, P, Cl etc.,

Occurrence of Non-metals

Non-metals occur both in free and combined state.

Free state

However in free state, they occur in very small amounts.
Example
Oxygen and nitrogen exist in elemental form as gases.

Combined state

HIn combined state, they occur along with metals and non-metals.
Example
Oxides - \( SO_2 \) (Combination of sulphur with a non-metal)
Sulphides - \( Na_2S \) (Combination of sulphur with a metal)

Metalloids

Elements which show characteristics of both metals and non-metals are called metalloids.
Example
Be, Si, Ge etc.,

Chemical Properties of metals and non-metals

Metals Action with oxygen

\( \begin{align} Metals + Oxygen \rightarrow Metal \ oxide \\\\[3pt] \underset{Calcium}{2Ca} + O_2 \rightarrow \left. \underset{ Calcium \ oxide}{2CaO}\right\rbrace \ Basic \ Oxides \\\\[3pt] \underset{Potassium}{4Al} + 3 O_2 \rightarrow \left. \underset{ Aluminium \ oxide}{2Al_2 O_3}\right\rbrace \ Amphoteric \ Oxides \\\\[3pt] \underset{Zinc}{2 Zn} + O_2 \rightarrow \underset{ Zinc \ oxide}{2ZnO} \end{align} \)
Note
The metal oxides are basic or amphoteric oxide.

Non-Metals Action with oxygen

\( \begin{align} Non-Metals + Oxygen \rightarrow Non-metal \ oxide \\\\[3pt] \left. \begin{array}{rcl} C + O_2 \rightarrow CO_2 \\\\ S + O_2 \rightarrow SO_2 \end{array}\right\rbrace Acidic \ Oxides \end{align} \)
The oxides No and \( N_2 O \) of nitrogen are neutral oxides.
\( N_2 O \) is known as laughing gas.
Note
Non metals react with oxygen to form oxides of acidic nature

Metals Non - metals
Action with litmus paper
Magnesium ribbon is burnt and the residue is collected in glass tumbler. A little quantity of water is added to it and is tested with both red and blue litmus papers. Sulphur is heated in a spoon and transferred to glass tumbler. Water is added to it and it is tested with both red and blue litmus papers.
The blue litmus paper remains unaffected whereas the red litmus paper changes to blue indicating it is a basic oxide. The red litmus paper remains unaffected. Whereas blue litmus paper changes to red indicating it is acidic oxide.
Metals Non - metals
Action with water
Very reactive metals like potassium, calcium, sodium react with water and displace hydrogen by forming metal hydroxide.
\( \begin{align} Reactive metal + Water \rightarrow metal \ hydroxide + Hydrogen \\\\[3pt] \underset{potassium}{2K} + 2H_2 O \rightarrow \underset{Potassium \ hydroxide}{2 KOH} + H_2 \\\\[3pt] \underset{calcium}{Ca} + 2 H_2 O \rightarrow \underset{Calcium \ hydroxide}{Ca(OH)_2} + H_2 \\\\[3pt] \underset{Sodium}{2 Na} + 2 H_2 O \rightarrow \underset{Sodium \ hydroxide}{2 NaOH} + H_2 \end{align} \)
Other metals like magnesium, zinc and iron react with steam and not with cold water. They form the respective oxides and liberate hydrogen.
\( \begin{align} Reactive metal + \underset{steam}{Water} \rightarrow metal \ oxide + Hydrogen \\\\[3pt] \underset{Magnesium}{Mg} + 2H_2 O \rightarrow \underset{Magnesium \ Oxide}{MgO} + H_2 \\\\[3pt] \underset{Zinc}{Zn} + H_2 O \rightarrow \underset{Znic \ Oxide}{ZnO} + H_2 \\\\[3pt] \underset{Iron}{3 Fe} + 4 H_2 O \rightarrow \underset{Iron \ Oxide}{Fe_3 O_4} + 4 H_2 \end{align} \)
Metals like gold, silver and platinum do not react with water.		 Non-metals do not react with water. In fact, they are kept in water to prevent the action of air on them.
Metals Non - metals
Action with Acids
Metals such as sodium, magnesium, zinc and iron react the dilute hydrochloric acid and sulphuric acid to form salt and hydrogen.

Other metals like magnesium, zinc and iron react with steam and not with cold water. They form the respective oxides and liberate hydrogen.
\( \begin{align} Reactive metal + \underset{steam}{Water} \rightarrow metal \ oxide + Hydrogen \\\\[3pt] \underset{Magnesium}{Mg} + 2H_2 O \rightarrow \underset{Magnesium \ Oxide}{MgO} + H_2 \\\\[3pt] \underset{Zinc}{Zn} + H_2 O \rightarrow \underset{Znic \ Oxide}{ZnO} + H_2 \\\\[3pt] \underset{Iron}{3 Fe} + 4 H_2 O \rightarrow \underset{Iron \ Oxide}{Fe_3 O_4} + 4 H_2 \end{align} \)
Metals like gold, silver and platinum do not react with water.		 Nonmetals react with to form stable hydrides
\( \begin{align} Metal + Acid \rightarrow Salt + Hydrogen \\\\[3pt] \underset{Sodium}{2 Na} + 2HCl \rightarrow \underset{Sodium \ chloride}{2 Na Cl} + H_2 \\\\[3pt] \underset{Magnesium}{Mg} + 2 HCl \rightarrow \underset{Magnesium \ Chloride}{MgCl_2} + H_2 \\\\[3pt] \underset{Zinc}{Zn} + H_2SO_4 \rightarrow \underset{Znic \ Sulphate}{ZnSO_4} + H_2 \\\\[3pt] \underset{Iron}{Fe} + 2 HCl\rightarrow \underset{Ferrous \ chloride}{FeCl_2} + H_2 \end{align} \) \( \underset{Sulphur}{S} + \underset{Hydrogen}{H_2} \rightarrow \underset{Hydrogen \ sulphide}{H_2 S} \)

Displacement reactions(reactive series of metals)

A more reactive metal displaces a less reactive metal from its salt solution.
But, less reactive metals cannot displace more reactive metals.
Based on their reactivity metals are placed in decreasing order of reactivity as follows.
In this reactivity series metals are arranged in the decreasing order of their reactivity.

Metals activity series

K > Na > Ca > Mg > Al > Zn > Fe > Sn > Pb[H]Cu > Hg > Ag >Au
\( \overset{\text{(Metals more reactive than hydrogen)}}{\text{Most reactive metals}} \rightarrow \overset{\text{(Metals less reactive than hydrogen)}}{\text{Least reactive metals}} \)

Noble metals

The noble metals are group of metallic elements consisting of gold, platinum, iridium, rhodium, osmium, ruthenium and palladium.
These metals are called noble because noble metals are non reactive hence occur free in nature. They maintain their metallic lustre for a longer time. The availability of these metals is very scarce.

Uses of Metals

The uses of common metals are as follows.

  1. Copper and aluminium metals are used to make wires to conduct electric current.

  2. Copper, iron and aluminium metals are used to make household utensils and factory equipments.

  3. Iron is used as a catalyst in the preparation of ammonia gas by Haber's process.

  4. Zinc is used for galvanising iron to protect it from rusting.

  5. Chromium and nickel metals are used for electroplating and in the manufacturing of stainless steel.

  6. The aluminium foils are used in packaging of medicines, cigarettes and food materials.

  7. Silver and gold are used to make jewellery and ornaments.

  8. Sodium, Titanium and Zirconium are used in projects related with atomic energy and space science.

  9. Zirconium is also used for making bullet proof (alloy steel).

  10. Copper and zinc are constituents of enzymes which act as biological catalysts. Iron, Cobalt, Magnesium are constituents of haemoglobin, vitamin \( B_{12} \) and Chlorophyll

Uses of non-metals

The uses of common non metals are as follows

  1. Silicon is used for making silicon steel alloy and in the preparation of a kind of polymer called silicon.

  2. Phosphorus is used in manufacturing phosphoric acid, and super phosphate fertilizer.

  3. Phosphorus is used for making phosphorus - bronze alloy which can resist corrosion.

  4. Phosphorus is used in the preparation of fire works, smoke screens and in match stick industry.

  5. Sulphur is used for making sulphuric acid which in turn is used for making plastics, synthetic fibres, dyes, drugs, detergents, explosives and fertilisers.

  6. Sulphur is used to make soft rubber hard. This is called vulcanization of rubber.

  7. Sulphur is used as a germicide for killing germs and as an antiseptic in skin ointments.

  8. Powdered graphite is used as a dry lubricant.

  9. Graphite is used for making graphite electrodes in dry cells and electric arcs.

  10. Silicon is used in making semiconductor devices such as transistors.

Corrision

The wastage of metal layer by layer due to formation of metal compounds on the surface is called corrosion. The rate of corrosion varies with different metals.

Methods to prevent corrosion

Galvanisation

Coating the surface of the metal with a non-corrosive metal like zinc is called galvanisation.

Electroplating

Plating of metal surface by less corrosive metal by using electricity is known as electro plating. It involves a cathode and an anode. Deposition or plating takes place on the cathode.

Alloying

The process of mixing certain metals with other metals to form alloys which are resistant to corrosion is called alloying.

Amalgam

  1. An amalgam is an alloy of mercury and one or more metals.

  2. Most of the metals form amalgam with mercury. But iron and platinum are notable exceptions.

Solved examples

Example 1:

Give the names of the following metals:

  1. A metal which is in liquid state at the room temperature.

  2. The best conductor of heat and electricity.

  3. A metal commonly used for preventing rusting of iron.

  4. One metal which is found free or native in nature.

Answer:

  1. The metal is mercury

  2. Silver

  3. Zinc metal is commonly used for preventing rusting of iron.

  4. Gold is found free or native in nature.

Example 2:

Name the non-metals which exhibit the following properties:

  1. A non-metal having lustre and is a good conductor of electricity.

  2. A non-metal, which is the hardest naturally occurring substance.

  3. A non-metal which is essential for organic compounds.

  4. A non-metal which is essential for respiration.

  5. A non-metal which is the source of energy in the sun and stars.

Answer:

  1. Graphite (an allotrope of carbon).

  2. Diamond (an allotrope of carbon).

  3. Carbon is the non-metal.

  4. Oxygen is the non-metal.

  5. Hydrogen is the non-metal.

Example 3:

Usually, metals are shiny, but articles like aluminium kettle, copper utensils or iron pipes are not very shiny. Why?
Answer:
We see that aluminium kettle, copper utensils or iron pipes are dull and not very shiny. This is due to the fact that there is a formation of a thin film of oxide, carbonate or sulphide on the surface of metals. But when they are rubbed with sand paper, the shining metallic lustre is visible.

Example 4:

Why do metals acquire a dull appearance on exposure to air for a long time?
Answer:
The metals form a protective coating of metal carbonates or metal sulphides on reaction with the moisture of air, which makes the metals appear dull.

Example 5:

Why are metals good conductors of electricity?
Answer:
Metals are good conductors of electricity because they have free electrons.

Example 6:

In the laboratory, metallic sodium is stored in kerosene. Give reason.
Answer:
Sodium is so reactive that it immediately catches fire in air. That is why, it is stored in kerosene. Since sodium is more denser than kerosene.

Example 7:

Name the element in each case which has the following descriptions:

  1. The burning metal which combines directly with nitrogen.

  2. The non-metal which forms two compounds on reaction with chlorine.

Answer:

  1. Magnesium

  2. Phosphorus

Example 8:

Account for the following fact:
Though lead is above hydrogen in the activity series, it does not react with dilute hydrochloric acid or sulphuric acid.
Answer:
On reaction of lead with dil. HCl or dil. \( H_2SO_4 \) a protective layer of lead (II) chloride or sulphate forms on the surface of lead and reaction stops.

Example 9:

Name the element in each case which has the following descriptions:

  1. What is an alloy?

  2. A light and strong alloy is required for making the bodies of aircrafts. What should be its constituents?

Answer:

  1. An alloy is an intimate mixture of a metal with other metals or non-metals, which yields a substance with metallic properties.

  2. The alloy is duralumin and its constituents are aluminium and copper containing traces of manganese and magnesium.

Example 10:

Name the element in each case which has the following descriptions:

  1. aluminium used in aircraft construction

  2. lead used in electrical wiring or electrical work in joining metals.

  3. Copper in electrical appliances or household vessels.

Answer:

  1. Duralumin

  2. Solder

  3. Brass

Example 11:

Why is aluminium, and not iron, used for making cooking coil?
Answer:
Aluminium is used for making cooking coil as it is very light weight metal, resistant to corrosion and is a good conductor of heat when compared to iron.

Example 12:

Define

  1. galvanised iron

  2. passive iron

Answer:

  1. When an iron sheet is covered with a thin layer of zinc on its surface, it is called galvanised iron .

  2. When iron is treated with concentrated nitric acid, a protective film of the oxide formed on. its surface prevents further action. This is known as passive iron.
