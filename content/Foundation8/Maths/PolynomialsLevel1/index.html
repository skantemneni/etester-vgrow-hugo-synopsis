Synopsis

General Terms

Constant

A symbol having a fixed numerical value is called a 'constant'. Constant are parts of algebraic expression that doesnot change.
Example : '2' has a definite value. So, it is a constant.

Variables :

A symbol which takes on various numerical values is called a 'variable'. A variable is a quantity that may change with in context of a mathematical problem or experiment.
Typically, we use a single letter to represent a variable
The latters x, y and z are common generic sysbols used for variable.
Example : x, y........

Coefficient :

In the product of a constant and a variable, which is called the coefficient of the other.
Example : In 6x, 6 is the cofficient of x.

Algebraic expression :

A algebraic expression is an expression built up from integer constant, variable, and \( (+ ), \ (-), \ ( \times), \ ( \div) \) is called an 'Algebraic expression'.
Example : 17 - x, \( 3x^2 - 4x + 12, \) etc.

Equation :

Two expressions combined with equality symbol (=) is called an equation.
Example : 17 - x = 0, \( 3x^2 - 4x + 12 = 2x^2 - 3x, \) etc

Degree of an expression :

The highest number of times the variable is present in the terms of an expression is the degree of an expression.
Note : If the expression in one variable then, the highest power of the variable in an expression is known as the 'degree of an expression or equation'.
Example : \( 3x^2 - 4x + 12 = 0 \)
\Rightarrow Degree is 2
\( 6x^4 - 3x^2 + 2x \Rightarrow Degree \ is \ 4 \ etc. \)

Polynomial :

Any expression having the non-negative integral powers for the variables is known as a 'polynomial'.
General form of a polynomial :
An expression \( p(x) = a_n x^n + a_{n - 1} x^{n - 1} + a_{n - 2} x^{n - 2} +.......+ a_2 x^2 + a_1 x^1 + a_0 \) is said to be a polynomial if

  1. \( a_0, \ a_1, ........a_n \) are rationa numbers.

  2. 'n' is a non-negative integer

Note : A polynomial is a rational integer expression

Types of polynomials :

Polynomials can be classified based on two categories:

Based on number of terms :

Monomial : Expression having only one term.
Example : 6x, \( 15y^3 \) etc.
Binomial : Expression having only two terms.
Example : \( 6x^3 + 24, \ x - y \) etc
Trinomial : Expression having only three terms.
Example : \( 6x^2 - 15x + 24, \) etc

Based on degree :

Linear polynomial : A polynomial whose degree is 1 is said to be a linear polynomial.
Example : 2x + 3
Quadratic polynomial : A polynomial whose degree is 2 is said to be a quadratic polynomial.
Example : \( 4x^2 + 2x + 3 \)
Cubic polynomial : A polynomial whose degree is 3 is said to be a cubic polynomial.
Example : \( x^3 - 1 \)
Biquadratic polynomial : A polynomial whose degree is 4 is said to be a biquadratic polynomial.
Example : \( 3x^4 - 100 \)
\( n^{th} \) degree polynomial : A polynomial whose degree is n is said to be a \( n^{th} \) degree polynomial.
Example : \( a_n x^n + a_{n - 1} x^{n - 1} + a_{n - 2} x^{n - 2} + ..... + a_2 x^2 + a_1 x^1 + a_0 \)
Difference between the multinomial and the polynomial:
Polynomial contains only one variable where as multinomial contains one or more than one variables

Arithmetic Operations over Polynomials:

Addition : Addition of all like terms in given polynomials gives the sum of polynomials.
Subtraction: The difference between the like term in given polynomials is known as subtraction of the given polynomials.
Multiplication of polynomials: Multiply each term of the multiplicand by each term of the multiplier and take the algebraic sum of the products. This gives the product of the given polynomials.
Division of polynomials: Divide the first term of the dividend by the first term of the division to obtain the first term of the quadrant.
As \( 1, \ a_1, \ a_2, \ a_3 .........a_{n - 1} \) are the roots of \( x^n - 1 = 0 \)
\( \Rightarrow (x^n - 1) = (x - 1) (x - a_1) .....(x - a_{n - 1}) \) (since, degree of the product of the factors of a polynomial is equal to the degree of the polynomial)
\( \Rightarrow \frac{x^n - 1}{x - 1} = (x - a_1) (x - a_2).....(x - a-{n - 1}) \)
\( x^{n - 1} + x^{n - 2} + ..... + 1 = (x - a_1) (x - a_2)....(x - a_{n - 1}) \)
In order to find the value of \( (x - a_1) (x - a_2) .....(x - a_{n - 1}) \)
We have to put x = 1 in the above equation i.e., when x = 1 we get,
\( 1 + 1 + .... + 1 \) (n times) = \( (1 - a_1) (1 - a_2) .....(1 - a_{n - 1)) \)
\( \Rightarrow (1 - a_1) (1 - a_2) .....(1 - a_{n - 1}) = n \)

Example 4:

Which of the following are not polynomials ?

  1. \( 2 - 3x \)

  2. \( \sqrt{2y^3} + \sqrt{3y} \)

  3. \( x^2 + x \sqrt{x} \)

  4. \( x^2 + \frac{7}{6} x^2 - 9 \)

  5. \( u^{ \frac{-1}{2}} + 3u + 2 \)

Solution:
Let us recall the definition pf polynomial.
If \( f(x) = a_0 + a_1 x + a_2 x^2 + ..... + a_n x^n \)
Where \( a_0, \ a_1, \ a_2, ...... a_n \) are real numbers and n is non negative integer (i.e., 'n' should be positive integer) is called a polynomial in x, of \( n^{th} \) degree.
The \( 2^{nd} \) term in (iii) i.e.
\( x \sqrt{x} = x, \ x^{ \frac{1}{2}} = x^{ \frac{3}{2}} \) here the power of 'x' is not a positive integer. Hence it cannot be a polynomial.
Also \( 1^{st} \) of (v) is \( u^{ \frac{-1}{2}} \) , here the power of 'u' is not a positive integer.
Hence it can not be a polynomial.

Example 9:

  1. What should be added to \( 2x^2 + 6x - 5 \) to get \( 3x^2 - 2x + 6 \) ?

  2. What must be subtracted from \( a^2 + b^2 + c^2 - 3abc \) to get \( 2a^2 - b^2 - 3c2 + abc \)

Solution:

  1. Let 'y' be added to \( 2x^2 + 6x - 5 \) to get \( 3x^2 - 2x + 6 \)
    \( \begin{align} \therefore y + (2x^2 + 6x - 5) = 3x^2 - 2x + 6 \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow y = 3x^2 - 2x + 6 - (2x^2 + 6x - 5) \\\\[3pt] = 3x^2 - 2x + 6 - 2x^2 - 6x + 5 \\\\[3pt] = x^2 - 8x + 11 \\\\[3pt] \therefore x^2 - 8x + 11 \ must \ be \ added \ to \ 2x^2 + 6x - 5 \ to \ get \ 3x^2 - 2x + 6 \end{align} \)

  2. Let 'y' be subtracted from
    \( \begin{align} a^2 + b^2 + c^2 - 3abc \ to \ get \ 2a^2 - b^2 - 3c^2 + abc \\\\[3pt] (a^2 + b^2 + c^2 - 3abc) - y = 2a^2 - b^2 - 3c^2 + abc \\\\[3pt] \therefore - y = 2a^2 - b^2 - 3c^2 + abc - (a^2 + b^2 + c^2 - 3abc) \\\\[3pt] = 2a^2 - b^2 - 3c^2 + abc - a^2 - b^2 - c^2 + 3abc \\\\[3pt] = a^2 - 2b^2 - 4c^2 + 4ab \\\\[3pt] \therefore y = -a^2 + 2b^2 + 4c^2 - 4bc \end{align} \)
    \( \therefore - a^2 + 2b^2 + 4c^2 - 4abc \) be subtracted from \( a^2 + b^2 + c^2 - 3abc \) to get \( 2a^2 - b^2 - 3c^2 + abc \)

Example 11:

Example 14:

Factorise : \( 36x^2 + 25 + 60x \)

Solution:
Given \( 36x^2 + 25 + 60x \)
Rearranging the terms, we get,
\( 36x^2 + 25 + 60x = 36x^2 + 60x + 25 \)
\( = (6x)^2 + 60x + (5)^2 \)
On considering a = 6x and b = 5
\( 36x^2 + 60x + 25 = 6x^2 + 2 \times 6x \times 5 + (5)^2 \)
\( 36x^2 + 60x + 25 \) is of the form \( (a + b)^ = a^2 + 2ab + b^2 \) \( 36x^2 + 60x + 25 = (6x + 5)^2 \)
So the factors of \( 36x^2 + 25 + 60 x \) are (6x + 5) and (6x + 5)

Example 15:

Factorise : \( x^4 + x^2 y^2 + y^4 \)

Solution:
Given \( x^4 + x^2 y^2 + y^4 \)
Here adding and subtracting \( x^2 y^2 \) , the result remains the same we get,
\( \begin{align} x^4 + x^2 y^2 + y^4 = x^4 + x^2 y^2 + y^4 + x^2 y^2 - x^2 y^2 \\\\[3pt] = x^4 + 2x^2 y^2 + y^4 - x^2 y^2 \\\\[3pt] = [(x^2)^2 + 2 \times x^2 \times y^2 + (y^2)^2] - (xy)^2 \\\\[3pt] = (x^2 + y^2)^2 - (xy)^2 \end{align} \)
Using the identity \( a^2 - b^2 = (a + b) (a - b) \) and here we can write \( a = (x^2 + y^2) \) and b = xy
\( x^4 + x^2 y^2 + y^4 = (x^2 + y^2 + xy) (x^2 + y^2 - xy) \)
The factors of \( x^4 + x^2 y^2 + y^4 \) are \( (x^2 + y^2 - xy) \)

Example 17:

Factorise : \( x^2 + 14x + 45 \)

Solution:
Given \( x^2 + 14x + 45 \)
Here constant = 45 and coefficient of 'x' = 14
The possible factors of 45 are \( 1 \times 45, \ 3 \times 15, \ 5 \times 9. \)

Now adjusting the middle term 14x as 9x + 5x
\( \begin{align} x^2 + 14x + 45 = x^2 + 9x + 5x + 45 \\\\[3pt] = (x^2 + 9x) + (5x + 45) \\\\[3pt] = x(x + 9) + 5(x + 9) \\\\[3pt] = (x + 9) (x + 5) \end{align} \)
\( \therefore \) The factors of \( x^2 + 14x + 45 \) (x + 9) and (x + 5)

Example 19:

Factorise : \( 12(a^2 + 7a)^2 - 8(a^2 + 7a) (2a - 1) - 15(2a - 1)^2 \)

Solution:
Given \( 12(a^2 + 7a)^2 - 8(a^2 + 7a) (2a - 1) - 15(2a - 1)^2 \)
Put \( (a^2 + 7a) = x \) and 2a - 1 = y
\( \therefore \) Given expression reduces to \( 12x^2 - 8xy - 15y^2 \)
Here \( (12) \times (- 15) = - 180 = - 18 \times 10 \) and - 8 = - 18 + 10
\( \begin{align} \therefore 12x^2 - 8xy - 15y^2 = 12x^2 - 18xy + 10xy - 15y^2 \\\\[3pt] = 12x^2 - xy(18 - 10) - 15y^2 \\\\[3pt] = 12x^2 - 18xy + 10xy - 15y^2 \\\\[3pt] = 6x(2x - 3y) + 5y(2x - 3y) = (6x + 5y)(2x - 3y) \\\\[3pt] \text{[putting values of x and y]} \\\\[3pt] = [2(a^2 + 7a) - 3(2a - 1)] [6(a^2 + 7a) + 5(2a - 1)] \\\\[3pt] = (2a^2 + 14a - 6a + 3) (6a^2 + 42a + 10a - 5) \\\\[3pt] = (2a^2 + 8a + 3) (6a^2 + 52a - 5) \end{align} \)
\( \therefore \) The factors of \( 12(a^2 + 7a)^2 - 8(a^2 + 7a) (2a - 1) - 15(2a - 1)^2 \) are \( (2a^2 + 8a + 3) \) and \( (6a^2 + 52a - 5) \)

Example 20:

Factorise : \( x^2 + \frac{1}{x^2} - 3 \)

Solution:
\( \begin{align} x^2 + \frac{1}{x^2} - 3 = \left[x^2 + \frac{1}{x^2} - 2 - 1 \right] \\\\[3pt] As \ 2 = 2 \times x \times \frac{1}{x}, \ thus \ replacing \ 2, \ we \ get \\\\[3pt] R.H.S = \left[x^2 + \frac{1}{x^2} - 2x \frac{1}{x} \right] - 1^2 = \left[x - \frac{1}{x} \right]^2 - 1^2 \\\\[3pt] This \ is \ of \ the form \ a^2 - b^2 = (a + b) (a - b) \\\\[3pt] where \ a = x - \frac{1}{x} \ and \ b = 1 \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow x^2 + \frac{1}{x^2} - 3 = \left[x - \frac{1}{x} + 1 \right] \left[x - \frac{1}{x} - 1 \right] \\\\[3pt] \therefore The \ factors \ of \ x^2 + \frac{1}{x^2} - 3 \ are \ \left[x - \frac{1}{x} + 1 \right] \ and \ \left[x - \frac{1}{x} - 1 \right] \end{align} \)

Example 21:

Divide \( x^4 - y^4 \) by x + y

Solution:
\( \frac{x^4 - y^4}{x + y} = \frac{(x^2)^2 - (y^2)^2}{x + y} \)
Applying the identity
\( a^2 - b^2 = (a + b) (a - b) \ to \ (x^2)^2 - (y^2)^2, \ where \ a = x^2, \ b = y^2, \ we \ get \)
\( R.H.S = \frac{(x^2 + y^2) (x^2 - y^2)}{x + y} = \frac{(x^2 + y^2) (x - y) (x + y)}{x + y} \)
\( \frac{x^4 - y^4}{x + y} = (x^2 + y^2) (x - y) \)

Example 22:

Divide \( x^3 + 8y^3 \) by x + 2y

Solution:
\( \frac{x^3 + 8y^3}{x + 2y} = \frac{(x)^3 +(2y)^3}{(x + 2y)} \)
Applying the identity
\( a^3 + b^3 = (a + b) (a^2 - ab + b^2) \ to \ (x)^3 + (2y)^3, \)
where a = x and b = 2y, we get
\( R.H.S = \frac{(x + 2y) [x^2 - x \times 2y + (2y)^2]}{x + 2y} \)
\( \therefore \frac{x^3 + 8y^3}{x + 2y} = x^3 - 2xy + 4y^2 \)

Example 23:

Divide \( a^3 + a^2 + 2a + 8 \) by a + 2

Solution:
Given \( \frac{a^3 + a^2 + 2a + 8}{a + 2} \)
Rearranging the terms and simplifying by applying the identity
\( \begin{align} \frac{a^3 + a^2 + 2a + 8}{a + 2} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{(a^3 + 8) + (a^2 + 2a)}{a + 2} = \frac{(a^3 + 2^3) + a(a + 2)}{a + 2} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{(a + 2) (a^2 - a \times 2 + 2^2) + a(a + 2)}{a + 2} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{(a + 2) [(a^2 - 2a + 2^2) + a]}{a + 2} \\\\[3pt] = a^2 - 2a + 4 + a = a^2 - a + 4 \end{align} \)

Example 24:
