Ratio and Proportions:

Ratio :

A ratio is a comparison of two numbers by division. The ratio of x to y is written as x : y.
In the ratio a : b, "a" is the antecedent and "b" is the consequent.

Proportion :

A proportion is an expression which states that two ratios are equal.
Example : \( \frac{4}{16} = \frac{1}{4} \) is a porportion
Each quantity in proportion is called proportional. The first and the last terms are called the extremes, whereas the second and third terms is called means.
When four quantities are in proportion, then
product of means = product of extremes.

Properties of Proportion :

If \( \frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}, \) then

  1. \( \frac{b}{a} = \frac{d}{c} \)

  2. \( \frac{a}{c} = \frac{b}{d} \)

  3. \( \frac{c}{a} = \frac{d}{b} \)

  4. \( \frac{a + b}{b} = \frac{c + d}{d} \)

  5. \( \frac{a - b}{b} = \frac{c - d}{d} \)

  6. \( \frac{a + b}{a - b} = \frac{c + d}{c - d} \)

  7. \( \frac{a}{b} = \frac{a + c}{b + d} = \frac{a - c}{b - d} \)

Continued Proportion :

Three quantities a, b and c are said to be continued proportion, if the ratio between the first and the second is equal to the ratio between the second and the third.
i.e.a:b = b:c

Relation among more than two quantities

  1. Number of quantities are three
    If \( \frac{A}{B} = \frac{N_1}{D_1} \) and \( \frac{B}{C} = \frac{N_2}{D_2} \) then
    \( A : B : C = (N_1 \times N_2) : (D_1 \times N_2) : (D_1 \times D_2) \)

  2. Number of quantities are four
    If \( \frac{A}{B} = \frac{N_1}{D_1}, \ \frac{B}{C} = \frac{N_2}{D_2} \) and \( \frac{C}{D} = \frac{N_3}{D_3} \) then
    \( A : B : C : D= (N_1 \times N_2 \times N_3 ) : (D_1 \times N_2 \times N_3) : (D_1 \times D_2 \times N_3) : (D_1 \times D_2 \times D_3) \)

Percentages

Percent :

The word percent means per hundred. Thus 12% means 12 parts out of 100 parts.

Some important points to remember

  1. If x% of a number (N) is y then
    \( N = \frac{y}{x} \times 100 \)

  2. If \( x_1 \% \ and x_2 \% \) of a number (N) are \( N_1 \) and \( N_2 \) respectively, then \( N = \frac{N_1 - N_2}{x_1 - x_2} \times 100 \)

  3. If a number increased by x% and then decreased by x% or is decreased by x% and then increased by x%, then the original number decreases by \( \frac{x^2}{100} \% \)

  4. If "a" exceeds "b" by x%, then "b" is short of "a" by \( \frac{100 \times x}{100 + x} \% \)

  5. If "a" exceeds "b" by x%, then "b" is exceeds of "a" by \( \frac{100 \times x}{100 - x} \% \)

  6. If a number (N) can be split into two parts such that one part is x% of the other, then the two parts are \( \frac{100}{100 + x} \times N \) and \( \frac{x}{100 + x} \times N \)

  7. % error = \( \frac{False \ measurement - Actual \ measurement}{Actual \ measurement} \times 100 \)

  8. If the circumference of a circle is increased or decreased by x% then the radius of that circle increases or decreases by x%.

  9. If the radius of a circle or diameter of a circle or side of a square is increased by x%, then area will increase by
    \( \left[2x + \left( \frac{x}{10} \right)^2 \right] \% \)

  10. If the radius of a circle or diameter of a circle or side of a square is decreased by x%, then area will decrease by
    \( \left[2x - \left( \frac{x}{10} \right)^2 \right] \% \)

Solved examples

Example 1:

If (5x + 3) : (3x +1) is the triplicate ratio of 4 : 3, then find x

Solution:
The triplicate ratio of 4 : 3 is \( 4^3 : 3^3 \)
\( \begin{align} \therefore (5x + 3) : (3x + 1) = 4^3 : 3^3 \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow \frac{5x + 3}{3x + 1} = \frac{4^3}{3^3} = \frac{64}{27} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow 27(5x + 3) = 64(3x + 1) \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow 135x + 81 = 192x + 64 \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow 192x - 135x = 81 - 64 \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow 57x = 17 \\\\[3pt] \therefore x = \frac{17}{57} \end{align} \)

Example 2:

If y(3x - y) : x(4x + y) = 5 : 12, then find the ratio \( (x^2 + y^2) : (x + y)^2. \)

Solution:
\( \begin{align} Given, \ \frac{y(3zx - y)}{x(4x + y)} = \frac{5}{12} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow 12y(3x - y) = 5x(4x + y) \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow 36 xy - 12y^2 = 20x^2 + 5xy \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow 20x^2 - 31xy + 12y^2 = 0 \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow 20 \left( \frac{x}{y} \right)^2 - 3 \left( \frac{x}{y} \right) + 12 = 0 \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow 20k^2 - 31k + 12 = 0 \ \ \left( Let \ \frac{x}{y} = k \right) \\\\[3pt] \therefore k = \frac{-(-31) \pm \sqrt{ (-31)^2 - 42.20.12}}{2.20} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{31 \pm \sqrt{961 - 960}}{40} = \frac{31 \pm 1}{40} = \frac{4}{5} \ or \ \frac{3}{4} \\\\[3pt] \therefore (x^2 + y^2) : (x + y)^2 = \frac{x^2 + y^2}{(x + y)^2} = \frac{y^2 k^2 + y^2}{(yk + y^2)} \end{align} \) \( \begin{align} = \frac{y^2 (k^2 + 1)}{y^2 (k + 1)^2} = \frac{k^2 + 1}{(k + 1)^2} \\\\[3pt] If \ k = \frac{4}{5}, \ then \ (x^2 + y^2) : (x + y)^2 = \frac{ \left( \frac{4}{5} \right)^2 + 1}{ \left( \frac{4}{5} + 1 \right)^2} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{ \frac{16}{25} + 1}{ \left( \frac{9}{5} \right)^2} = \frac{ \frac{41}{25}}{ \frac{81}{25}} = \frac{41}{81} \\\\[3pt] If \ k = \frac{3}{4}, \ then \ (x^2 + y^2) : (x + y)^2 = \frac{ \left( \frac{3}{4} \right)^2 + 1}{ \left( \frac{3}{4} + 1 \right)^2} = \frac{ \frac{25}{16}}{ \frac{49}{16}} = \frac{25}{49} \end{align} \)

Example 3:

The ratio of the sum of money in three bags A, B and C is 4 : 3 : 2. If Rs.50 is added to each of the bags, the ratio becomes 14 : 13 : 12. Find the sum of money in each of the bags.

Solution:
Let the sums of money in the bags be Rs.x, Rs.y, Rs.z respectively.
As these are on the ratio 4 : 3 : 2, we get
x = 4k, y = 3k, z = 2k.
Rs.50 in added to each bag. So, the sums are now 4k + 50, 3k + 50, 2k + 50 respectively.
But these are in the ratio 14 : 13 : 12.
\( \therefore \) 4k + 50 = 14p .......(1)
3k + 50 = 13p ......(2)
2k + 50 = 12p .......(3)
Subtracting equation (2) from equation (1) [or equations (3) from (2) or (3) from (1)] we get, k = p
\( \therefore \) 4k + 50 = 14p \( \Rightarrow \) 4k + 50 = 14k \( \Rightarrow \) 10 k = 50 \( \Rightarrow \) k = 5
\( \therefore \) The sums in the bags are,
\( Rs.4 \times 5, Rs.3 \times 5,Rs. 2 \times 5 \)
i.e., Rs. 20, Rs. 15, Rs. 10.

Example 4:

Example 5:

If a, b, c, d are in continued proportion, then prove that \( a:d::(ma^3 + nb^3 rc^3) : (mb^3 + nc^3 - rd^3) \) .

Solution:
a, b, c, d are in continued proportion
\( \begin{align} \Rightarrow \frac{a}{b} = \frac{b}{c} = \frac{c}{d} = k \ (say) \\\\[3pt] Then, \ c = dk, \ b = ck = (dk) (k) = dk^2 \\\\[3pt] a = bk = (dk^2) k = dk^3 \\\\[3pt] \therefore \frac{a}{d} = \frac{dk^3}{d} = k^3 \\\\[3pt] \frac{ma^3 + nb^3 rc^3}{mb^3 + nc^3 - rd^3} = \frac{m (dk^3)^3 + n(dk^2)^3 - r(dk)^3}{m(dk^2)^3 + n(dk)^3 - rd^3} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{md^3 k^9 + nd^3 k^6 - rd^3 k^3}{md^3 k^6 + nd^3 k^3 - rd^3} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{d^3 k^3 (mk^6 + nk^3 - r)}{d^3 (mk^6 + nk^3 - r)} = k^3 \\\\[3pt] \therefore \frac{a}{d} = \frac{ma^3 + nb^3 rc^3}{mb^3 + nc^3 - rd^3} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow a:d::(ma^3 + nb^3 rc^3) : (mb^3 + nc^3 - rd^3) \end{align} \)

Example 6:

If \( \frac{x}{a} = \frac{y}{b} = \frac{z}{c} \) then prove that \( \frac{x^3}{a^3} + \frac{y^3}{b^3} + \frac{z^3}{c^3} = \frac{3 xyz}{abc} \) .

Solution:
Here,
\( \begin{align} \frac{x}{a} = \frac{y}{b} = \frac{z}{c} = k \\\\[3pt] So, \ x = ak, \ y = bk, \ z = ck \\\\[3pt] \therefore \frac{x^3}{a^3} + \frac{y^3}{b^3} + \frac{z^3}{c^3} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{(ak)^3}{a^3} + \frac{(bk)^3}{b^3} + \frac{(ck)^3}{c^3} \\\\[3pt] = k^3 + k^3 + k^3 = 3k^3 \\\\[3pt] and \ \frac{3 xyz}{abc} = \frac{3.ak.bk.ck}{abc} = 3k^3 \\\\[3pt] So, \ \frac{x^3}{a^3} + \frac{y^3}{b^3} + \frac{z^3}{c^3} = \frac{3 xyz}{abc} \end{align} \)

Example 7:

If \( \frac{a}{b + c} = \frac{b}{c + a} = \frac{c}{a + b} \) then prove that each ratio is equal to \( \frac{1}{2} \) or -1.

Solution:
\( \begin{align} \frac{a}{b + c} = \frac{b}{c + a} = \frac{c}{a + b} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow Each \ ratio = \frac{Sum \ of \ numerators}{Sum \ of \ denominators} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{a + b + c}{(b + c) + (c + a) + (a + b)} \\\\[3pt] \therefore Each \ ratio = \frac{a + b + c}{2(a + b + c)} = \frac{1}{2} \\\\[3pt] Again \ \frac{a}{b + c} = \frac{b}{c + a} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow Each \ ratio = \frac{Difference \ of \ numerators}{Difference \ of \ denominators} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{a - b}{(b + c) - (c + a)} \\\\[3pt] \therefore Each \ ratio = \frac{a - b}{b - a} = \frac{a - b}{-(a - b)} = -1 \\\\[3pt] Thus, \ each \ ratio = \frac{1}{2} \ or \ -1 \end{align} \)

Example 8:

If \( x = \frac{ \sqrt{2a + 3b} + \sqrt{2a - 3b} }{\sqrt{2a + 3b} - \sqrt{2a - 3b} } \) then prove that \( 3bx^2 - 4ax + 3b = 0 \)

Solution:
\( \begin{align} Here, \ \frac{x}{1} = \frac{ \sqrt{2a + 3b} + \sqrt{2a - 3b} }{\sqrt{2a + 3b} - \sqrt{2a - 3b} } \\\\[3pt] \text{By componendo dividendo, we get} \\\\[3pt] \frac{x + 1}{x - 1} = \frac{ \sqrt{(2a + 3b)} + \sqrt{2a - 3b} + (2a + 3b) - \sqrt{2a - 3b}}{ \sqrt{2a + 3b} + \sqrt{2a - 3b} - 2a + 3b - \sqrt{2a - 3b}} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow \frac{x + 1}{x - 1} = \frac{2 \sqrt{(2a + 3b}}{2 \sqrt{(2a - 3b}} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow \left ( \frac{x + 1}{x - 1} \right)^2 = \frac{2a + 3b}{2a - 3b} \end{align} \)
Again by applying componendo-dividendo,
\( \begin{align} \frac{(x + 1)^2 + (x - 1)^2}{(x + 1)^2 - (x - 1)^2} = \frac{(2a + 3b) + (2a - 3b)}{(2a + 3b) - (2a - 3b)} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow \frac{x^2 + 2x +1 + x^2 - 2x + 1}{x^2 + 2x + 1 - (x^2 - 2x + 1)} = \frac{4a}{6b} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow \frac{2x^2 + 2}{4x} = \frac{2a}{3b} \\\\[3pt] or \ \frac{x^2 + 1}{2x} = \frac{2a}{3b} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow 3b(x^2 + 1) = 4ax \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow 3bx^2 - 4ax + 3b = 0 \end{align} \)

Example 9:

If \( \frac{x}{b + c - a} = \frac{y}{c + a - b} = \frac{z}{a + b - c} \) then prove that \( \frac{x + y + z}{a + b + c} = \frac{x(y + z) + y(z + x) + z(x + y)}{2(ax + by + cz)} \)

Solution:
Each of the given fractions = \( \frac{sum \ of \ numerators}{sum \ of \ denominators} = \frac{x + y + z}{a + b + c} ....(1) \)
Again if we multiply both numerator and the denominator of the three given fractions by y + z, z + x, x + y respectively, then we get,
\( \frac{x(y + z)}{(y + z) (b + c - a)} = \frac{y(z + x)}{(z + x) (c + a - b)} = \frac{z(x + y)}{(x + y) (a + b - c)} \)
Each fraction = \( \frac{sum \ of \ numerators}{sum \ of \ denominators} = \frac{x(y + z) + y(z + x) + z(x + y)}{2ax + 2by + 2cz)} ....(2) \)
\( \therefore \) from (1) and (2),
\( \frac{x + y + z}{a + b + c} = \frac{x(y + z) + y(z + x) + z(x + y)}{2(ax + by + cz)} \)
Hence it is proved

Example 13:

A number is increased by a% and then b%. By what p.c. the original number is changed.

Solution:
Let the original number 100x
First time, when increased by a%, it becomes
100x + a% of 100x = 100x + ax
2nd time, when increased again by b%, it becomes
\( \begin{align} = (100x + ax) + \frac{b}{100} (100x + ax) \\\\[3pt] = 100x + ax + bx + \frac{abx}{100} \\\\[3pt] \therefore \ Net \ increase = \left(100x + ax + bx + \frac{abx}{100} \right) - 100x \\\\[3pt] \therefore Increase \ p.c = \frac{Net \ increase}{original} \times 100 \\\\[3pt] = \frac{ax + bx + \frac{abx}{100}}{100 x} \times 100 \\\\[3pt] = \frac{ x \left( a + b + \frac{ab}{100} \right)}{100 x} \times 100 \\\\[3pt] = (a + b) + \frac{ab}{100} \end{align} \)

Example 14:

The ratio of bus and train fares from Calcutta to a certain place is 3 : 4. If the train fare increases by 20% and bus fare by 10% then what will be new ratio of bus and train fares?

Solution:
Let the bus and train fares be Rs.3x and Rs.4x respectively.
As the bus fare increases by 10%, the new bus fare is:
\( = Rs \left(3x + \frac{3x.10}{100} \right) = Rs. \frac{33 x}{10} \)
And the train fare increases by 20%, the new fare is
\( = Rs \left(4x + \frac{4x.20}{100} \right) = Rs. \frac{24 x}{5} \)
Hence the new ratio of bus and train fares
\( = \frac{33 x}{10} : \frac{24 x}{5} = 33 : 48 = 11 : 16 \)

Example 17:

Forty per cent of the employees of a certain company are men and 75% of the men earn more than Rs. 25,000 per year. If 45% of the company's employees earn more than Rs.25,000 per year, what reaction of the women employed by the company earn Rs. 25,000 or less per year?

75% of men or 30 men get more than Rs. 25,000.
45% of total employees or 45 employee get more than Rs. 25,000.

Therefore, the fraction of women = \( \frac{45}{60} = \frac{3}{4} \)
