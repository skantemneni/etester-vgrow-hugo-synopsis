Common misconception Fact
1 Electricity is flow of current Electricity is flow of charges.
2 Liquids do not conduct electricity. Most liquid which are solutions of acids, bases and salts are good conductors of electricity.
3 Electric current doesn't react chemically with a conducting solution Passage of electrical current through a conducting solution causes chemical reactions.

Introduction

IWhen electric current passes through some conducting fluids, chemical action takes place at the electrodes. This effect is known as chemical effect of electrical current. The passage of an electric current through a conducting solution causes chemical reactions. As a result any of the following activities can be observed.

  1. formation of gas bubbles

  2. deposit of metals on electrodes

  3. change in colour of the solution.

Good Conductors of Electricity

  1. Materials, which allow electric current to pass through them are good conductors of electricity.

  2. All metals are good conductors of electricity.

  3. Human body is a good conductor of electricity. Thus we should take precautions while handling electrical appliances.

Bad Conductors of Electricity

  1. Materials which don't allow electric current to pass through them are bad conductors of electricity. Ex.: Wood, Plastic etc.

  2. All liquids are bad conductors of electricity except mercury which is a liquid metal.

  3. Pure water is a bad conductor of electricity. But impure water conducts electricity.

  4. All gases are bad conductors of electricity.

Insulators

The liquids which do not allow passage of electric current through them.
Eg: Vegetable oils, Distilled water.

Electrolyte

The liquids which allow the passage of electric current through them and dissociate into ions.
Eg: acids, bases and salts.

Electrodes

  1. Metal plates partly immersed in the electrolyte to pass current though them are called electrodes.

  2. Negative terminal is called cathode and positive terminal is called anode.

  3. In solids, conduction is due to the motion of free electrons and in electrolytes conduction is due to ions.

  4. Positive ions are called cations and negative ions are called anions.

Electrolysis

When electricity is passed through some substances they decompose such process is called electrolysis.

Electrolysis of Water

The process of splitting liquid water into its two component gases is called electrolysis of water.
Chemically water is a compound of hydrogen and oxygen gas. It is stable compound and cannot be separated into hydrogen and oxygen on heating or other process than electrolysis on passing electric current in impure water, water splits up into its component gas, hydrogen and oxygen.

Application of electrolysis

Two important uses of electrolysis are in electroplating and extraction of some metals.

Electroplating

The process of depositing a layer of any desired metal on another metal by means of electricity is called electroplating.

Uses of electroplating

  1. The process of depositing a layer of any desired metal on another metal by means of electricity is called electroplating.

  2. Chromium plating is done to make the object scratch proof and appear shiny.

  3. Tin cans, used to preserve food items, soft drinks are made by electroplating tin onto iron.

  4. A coating of zinc is deposited on iron to protect it from corrosion and formation of rust.
