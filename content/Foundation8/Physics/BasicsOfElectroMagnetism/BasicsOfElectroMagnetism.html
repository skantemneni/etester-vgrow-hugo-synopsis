Introduction

An electric bell, a motor or a generator used in our daily life works on the principles based on the electromagnetism. Electro magnetism is one of the branch of physics which deals with the magnetic effects produced by a current carrying conductor.

Magnets

Magnets are solid objects of stone, metal or other materials which have the property of attracting certain substances like iron. This attracting property is either natural or induced. The phenomenon of attracting magnetic substances like iron, nickel, cobalt etc., is called magnetism.

Properties of magnets

The properties of magnets are.

Attractive property

Magnets possess the property of attracting small pieces of a objects made of metal like iron, steel, nickel and cobalt.
Magnetic material
Substancee which are easily attracted by a magnet are called magnetic substances.
Example: Iron, steel, nickel and cobalt.
Non-magnetic material
Substances which are not attracted by a magnet are called non-magnetic substances.
Example: Copper, brass, paper, plastic, wood, etc.

Directive property

Suspend a magnet by a thread attached to a wooden stand push the magnet. The magnet will comes to rest in north-south direction. This property is called directive property.

Like poles repel and unlike poles attract

If two magnets are brought neai.er such that two north poles face each other or two south poles face each other, they repel one another. On the other hand a north and a south pole always attract one another.

Poles exists in pairs

If a magnet is broken into two parts, instead of obtaining separate N-pole and S-pole, each of the two parts is found to possess both the polarities, that is, each piece is still a magnet.
The new ends formed at the place where the magnet is cut acquire polarities opposite to what the ends of the pieces possess.

Properties of magnets

Magnets are classified into two types:

Natural magnets

  1. The magnets found in nature are called natural magnets.
    eg:lode stones

  2. Natural magnets are of irregular shapes and behaves like a weak magnet.

Artificial magnets

  1. Magnets produced from magnetic materials are called "ARTIFICIAL MAGNETS".

  2. This magnets can be made in a variety of shapes and sizes.

  3. They are extensively used in electrical instruments.

  4. Artificial magnets generally made from special forms or steel alloys which are magnetised electrically or a piece of lode stone stroked with a lode stone.

Permanent magnets

Magnets made from substances such as hard-steel and certain alloys which retain their magnetic properties over a long of time are called permanent magnets.

Temporary magnets

The magnets which retain their magnetism only under certain conditions like presence of strong magnets or an electric current in the vicinity are called temporary magnets.

Testing a magnets

A magnet attracts a piece of iron as well as the opposite pole of another magnet. Therefore, an object cannot be identified as a magnet simply because it is getting attracted towards a magnet. It could just be a piece of iron.
However, if the object is repelled by a magnet, then it must also be a magnet, since only like poles of two magnets repel.
Thus, repulsion and not attraction, is the sure test for polarity of a magnet.

Terms Used in the study of Magnets

Magnetic axis

The line joining the poles of a magnet is called magnetic axis.

Equatorial line

The line passing through the mid point centre of the magnet and perpendicular to the magnetic axis is called the equatorial line.

Magnetic length

The distance between the magnetic poles is called magnetic length.

Magnetic meridian

It is the imaginary vertical plane passing through the north-south direction of a magnet.

Electrical Method

A magnet can be produced by passing current through a coil bound over a steel rod or a soft iron rod.

Magnetic field

  1. A magnetic field is the region around a magnet where magnetic forces act.

  2. Although magnetic fields are invisible, you can see their effect on iron fillings, compass needles and other magnets.

  3. To represent a magnetic field, we draw magnetic field lines or magnetic lines of force.

The Earth as a Magnet (Terrestrial Magnetism)

The Earth behaves like a huge magnet with its north pole near the geographic South pole and south pole near the geographic North pole. This is the reason why a freely suspended magnet comes to rest in the north - south direction. The north pole of the suspended magnet is attracted by the south pole of the Earth's magnet, and hence points to the geographic north.

Compass

A compass consists of a needle-shaped magnet which can rotate freely on a pivot. The needle always rests in the north - south direction.
Compasses help hikers, sailors and soldiers to find their way.

Electromagnets

  1. An electromagnet is made by winding a coil of wire around a soft iron core.

  2. Soft iron and not steel is used as the core because soft iron loses its magnetism when the current is switched off. Steel retains its magnetism and becomes a permanent magnet.

  3. The strength of an electromagnet increases
    significantly with the use of soft iron core.
    when the number of turns per unit length of the coil is increased.
    when the current in the coil is increased.

  4. Electromagnets are used in electric bells, circuit breakers, electromagnetic relays and telephone earpieces.

Electric Bell

Electric bell is based on the principle of an electromagnet, "a temporary strong magnet acquires magnetic properties when an electric current flows through it, and loses its magnetic properties as soon as the current is stopped".

Magnetic field due to current in a straight conductor

When current flows in a long straight wire, the magnetic field produced can be observed by sprinkling iron filings around the wire on a piece of cardboard as shown in figure.

The direction of the magnetic filed produced can be obtatined by moving a small plotting compass around the wire.
Without the plotting compass, the direction of the magnetic field is obtatined using the right-hand grip rule.

Right-hand grip rule

Grip the wire with the right hand and with the thumb pointing in the direction of the current. The fingers point in the direction of the magnetic field.

The two-dimensional view of the magnetic field due to current in a straight wire is easier to draw, and is shown in figure.

As the distance from the wire increases, the magnetic field becomes weaker. This is shown by the increasing distance between the lines.

Magnetic field due to current carrying circular coil

The magnetic field due to current carrying in a circular coil is almost equal to that of a bar magnet.

Solenoid

A solenoid is an electric device consisting of a coil of insulated wire wound around a cylinder whose diameter is less in comparison to the length.
When current passes through the wire, it behaves like a magnet with opposite poles at the end.

Electromagnetic Induction

Electromagnetic induction is the production of an induced e.m.f. in a conductor when there is a relative motion between the conductor and the magnetic field.

Direct current and Alternating current

If the current flows always in the same direction, it is called a direct current.
If the current changes direction after equal intervals of time, it is called alternating current.

Electric Generator

The electric generator (or dynamo) converts mechanical energy (or kinetic energy) into electrical energy.

Principle of Electric Generator

The e electric generator works on the principle that when a straight conductor is moved in a magnetic field, then current is induced in the conductor.

Tranformer

It is an electric device by which e.m.f. of an alternating current can be changed as per the need of a particular situation.

Example 2:

You are given two similar unmarked bars. One of them is a magnet while the other is a magnetic material. Without any other aid, how would you deduce which one of them is magnet.
Solution:
Magnets attract any magnetic materials by magnetic induction and can only do so at its ends. Place the tip of one of the bars (X) near the midpoint of the other bar (Y). If X attracts, X must be the magnet. If X does not attract, Y must be the magnet.
