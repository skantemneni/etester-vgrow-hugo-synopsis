Trigonometric Identities

Angle:

  1. An angle is the

  2. amount of rotation of a

  3. revolving line with

  4. respect to a fixed line

Note: If the rotation is in clockwise sense, the angle measured is negative and it is positive if the rotation is in anti-clockwise sense.

Different Units for Measuring Angles:

Sexagesimal System (or) British System

In sexagesimal system, a right angle is divided into 90 equal parts called degrees. Further, each degree is divided into sixty equal parts called minutes and each minute is divided into sixty equal parts called seconds.
Thus, 1 right angle = 90 degrees \( (90^{\circ}) \)
\( \begin{align} 1^{\circ} = 60 \ minutes(60') \\\\[3pt] 1' = 60 \ seconds(60'') \end{align} \)

Centesimal System or French System

1 right angle is divided into 100 equal parts. Each part is called a grade.
Thus, 1 right angle = 100 grades \( (100^g) \)
\( \begin{align} 1 \ grade = 100 \ minutes(100') \\\\[3pt] 1 \ minute = 100 \ seconds(100'') \end{align} \)

Radian Measure:

An angle made by an arc of length equal to radius of a given circle at its centre is called one Radian.

Relation between Degree and Radius

If D is the degree measure of an angle and R is its measure in Radians then
\( \begin{align} \frac{D}{90} = \frac{2R}{ \pi} \\\\[3pt] \therefore 1 \ Radian = \frac{180^{\circ}}{ \pi} \ degrees \\\\[3pt] = 57^{\circ}17'45''(approximately) \\\\[3pt] and \ 1 \ degree = \frac{ \pi}{180} \ Radian \\\\[3pt] = 0.01746 \ Radian(approximately) \end{align} \)

Radians \( \frac{ \pi}{6} \) \( \frac{ \pi}{4} \) \( \frac{ \pi}{3} \) \( \frac{ \pi}{2} \) \( \pi \) \( \frac{3 \pi}{2} \) \( 2 \pi \)

Trigonometric Ratio's:

  1. In a right angled triangle
    \( \begin{align} ON = x, \ NP = y \ and \\\\[3pt] OP = r, \ \angle PON = \theta, \ \angle PNO = 90^{\circ} \\\\[3pt] sin \theta = \frac{Opposite \ side}{Hypotenuse} = \frac{NP}{OP} = \frac{y}{r} \\\\[3pt] cos \theta = \frac{Adjacent \ side}{Hypotenuse} = \frac{ON}{OP} = \frac{x}{r} \\\\[3pt] tan \theta = \frac{Opposite \ side}{Adjacent \ side} = \frac{NP}{ON} = \frac{y}{x} \\\\[3pt] cosec \theta = \frac{Hypotenuse}{Opposite \ side} = \frac{OP}{NP} = \frac{r}{y} \\\\[3pt] sec \theta = \frac{Hypotenuse}{Adjacent \ side} = \frac{OP}{ON} = \frac{r}{x} \\\\[3pt] cot \theta = \frac{Adjacent \ side}{Opposite \ side} = \frac{ON}{NP} = \frac{x}{r} \end{align} \)

  2. \( sin \theta . cosec \theta = 1 \Rightarrow sin \theta = \frac{1}{cosec \theta} \Rightarrow cosec \theta = \frac{1}{sin \theta} \)

  3. \( cos \theta . sec \theta = 1 \Rightarrow cos \theta = \frac{1}{sec \theta} \Rightarrow sec \theta = \frac{1}{cos \theta} \)

  4. \( tan \theta . cot \theta = 1 \Rightarrow tan \theta = \frac{1}{cot \theta} \Rightarrow cot \theta = \frac{1}{tan \theta} \)

  5. \( tan \theta = \frac{sin \theta}{cos \theta} \)

  6. \( cot \theta = \frac{cos \theta}{sin \theta} \)

For All Values of \( \theta \)

  1. \( sin^2 \theta + cos^2 \theta = 1 \Rightarrow 1 - sin^2 \theta = cos^2 \theta \Rightarrow 1 - cos^2 \theta = sin^2 \theta \)

  2. \( sec^2 \theta - tan^2 \theta = 1 \Rightarrow 1 + tan^2 \theta = sec^2 \theta \Rightarrow sec^2 \theta - 1 = tan^2 \theta \)

  3. \( cosec^2 \theta - cot^2 \theta = 1 \Rightarrow 1 + cot^2 \theta = cosec^2 \theta \Rightarrow cosec^2 \theta - 1 = cot^2 \theta \)

Trigonometric Ratios of Allied Angles:

Two angles are said to be allied when their sum or difference is a multiple of \( 90^{\circ} \) .
Thus \( \theta \) and \( - \theta \) ; \( \theta \) and \( 90^{\circ} - \theta \) ; \( \theta \) and \( 90^{\circ} + \theta \) ; \( \theta \) and \( 180^{\circ} - \theta \) ; \( \theta \) and \( 180^{\circ} + \theta \) ; \( \theta \) and \( 270^{\circ} - \theta \) ; \( \theta \) and \( 270^{\circ} + \theta \) ; \( \theta \) and \( 360^{\circ} - \theta \) ; \( \theta \) and \( 360^{\circ} + \theta \) are called Allied angles, it is assumed that \( \theta \) is measured in degrees.

Allied Angles:

\( \begin{align} sin(- \theta) = - sin \theta, \ cos(- \theta) = cos \theta, \ tan(- \theta) = - tan \theta \\\\[3pt] cot(- \theta) = - cot \theta, \ sec(- \theta) = sec \theta, \ cosec(- \theta) = - cosec \theta \\\\[3pt] Ex: \ sin(- 60^{\circ}) = - sin \ 60^{\circ} = \frac{- \sqrt{3}}{2}; \ cos(- 60^{\circ}) = cos \ 60^{\circ} = \frac{1}{2} \end{align} \)

Complementary Angles:

Two angles are said to be complementary when their sum is \( 90^{\circ} \) .
Thus \( \theta \) and \( 90^{\circ} - \theta \) are complementary angles.
Ex: \( cos \ 30^{\circ} = cos(90^{\circ} - 60^{\circ}) = sin \ 60^{\circ} = \frac{ \sqrt{3}}{2} \)

Supplementary Angles:

Two angles are said to be supplementary when their sum is \( 180^{\circ} \)
Thus, \( \theta \) and \( 180^{\circ} - \theta \) are supplementary angles.
Ex: \( sin \ 120^{\circ} = sin(180^{\circ} - 60^{\circ}) = sin \ 60^{\circ} = \frac{ \sqrt{3}}{2} \)

Note 1: If the allied angle is \( 90^{\circ} \pm \theta, \ 270^{\circ} \pm \theta \) then trigonometric ratios changed as sin \( \leftrightarrow \) cos, tan \( \leftrightarrow \) cot, sec \( \leftrightarrow \) cosec.

Note 2: If the allied angle is \( - \theta, \ 180^{\circ} \pm \theta, \ 360^{\circ} \pm \theta \) then trigonometric ratio is not changed.

Note 3: If the allied angle is in the form of \( (n. \ 360^{\circ} \pm \theta) \) where 'n' is an integer, then trigonometric ratio is not changed.
Ex: \( sin \ 750^{\circ} = sin(2.360^{\circ} + 30^{\circ}) = sin \ 30^{\circ} = \frac{1}{2} \)

Compound Angles

A compound angle is that which is made up of algebraic sum of two or more angles.

Addition and Subtraction of two Angles:

  1. \( sin(A + B) = sin A . cos B + cos A . sin B \)

  2. \( sin(A - B) = sin A . cos B - cos A . sin B \)

  3. \( cos(A + B) = cos A . cos B - sin A . sin B \)

  4. \( cos(A - B) = cos A . cos B + sin A . sin B \)

  5. \( tan(A + B) = \frac{tan A + tan B}{1 - tan A \times tan B} \)

  6. \( tan(A - B) = \frac{tan A - tan B}{1 + tan A \times tan B} \)

  7. \( cot(A + B) = \frac{cot A \times cot B - 1}{cot A + cot B} \)

  8. \( cot(A - B) = \frac{cot A \times cot B + 1}{cot A - cot B} \)

  9. \( sin(A + B)sin(A - B) = sin^2 A - sin^2 B = cos^2 B - cos^2 A \)

  10. \( cos(A + B)cos(A - B) = cos^2 A - sin^2 B = cos^2 B - sin^2 A \)

  11. \( tan(A + B)tan(A - B) = \frac{tan^2 A - tan^2 B}{1 - tan^2 A \times tan^2 B} \)

  12. \( cot(A + B)cot(A - B) = \frac{cot^2 A \times cot^2 B - 1}{cot^2 B - cot^2 A} \)

Trigonometric Ratios of the Sum of three Angles:

  1. sin(A + B + C) = sin A.cos B.cos C + cos A. sin B.cos C + cos A.cos B.sin C - sin A.sin B.sin C
    or sin(A + B + C) = cos A.cos B.cos C(tan A + tan B + tan C - tan A.tan B.tan C)

  2. cos(A + B + C) = cos A.cos B.cos C - sin A. sin B.cos C - sin A.cos B.sin C - cos A.sin B.sin C
    or cos(A + B + C) = cos A.cos B.cos C(1 - tan A.tan B - tan B.tan C - tan C.tan A)

  3. \( tan(A + B + C) = \frac{tan A + tan B + tan C - tan A.tan B.tan C}{1 - tan A.tan B - tan B.tan C - tan C.tan A} \)

Some Important Results:

  1. \( tan \left( \frac{ \pi}{4} + A \right) = \frac{1 + tan A}{1 - tan A} = \frac{cos A + sin A}{cos A - sin A} \)

  2. \( tan \left( \frac{ \pi}{4} - A \right) = \frac{1 - tan A}{1 + tan A} = \frac{cos A - sin A}{cos A + sin A} \)

Trigonometric Ratios of Multiple and Submultiple Angles:

If A is an angle, then the angles 2A, 3A, 4A etc., are called multiple angles of A. And the angles \( \frac{A}{2}, \ \frac{A}{3}, \ \frac{A}{4} \) etc., are called submultiples angles of A.

  1. \( sin 2A = 2 sin A.cos A = \frac{2 tan A}{1 + tan^2 A} \)

  2. \( cos 2A = cos^2 A - sin^2 A = 1 - 2 sin^2 A = 2 cos^2 A - 1 = \frac{1 - tan^2 A}{1 + tan^2 A} \)

  3. \( tan 2A = \frac{2 tan A}{1 - tan^2 A} \)

  4. \( cot 2A = \frac{cot^2 A - 1}{2 cot A} \)

  5. \( sin 3A = 3 sin A - 4 sin^3 A \)

  6. \( cos 3A = 4 cos^3 A - 3 cos A \)

  7. \( tan 3A = \frac{3 tan A - tan^3 A}{1 - 3tan^2 A} \)

  8. \( cot 3A = \frac{cot^3 A - 3 cot A}{3 cot^2 A - 1} = \frac{3 cot A - cot^3 A}{1 - 3 cot^2 A} \)

Heights and Distances

Angle of Elevation:

\( \angle XOM \) i.e., the angle in which the line joining the object and the eye makes with the horizontal through the eye is called the angle of elevation of M as seen from O.

Angle of Depression:

\( \angle XOP \) i.e., the angle in which the line joining the object and the eye makes with the horizontal through the eye is called the angle of depression of P as seen from O.

Formulae:

  1. The angle of elevation measured from the points A, B on a horizontal line from the floor of the tower are \( \alpha \) and \( \beta \) if AB = d then the height of the tower 'h' is

    \( \therefore h = \frac{d tan \alpha.tan \beta}{tan \beta - tan \alpha} \ (or) \ h = \frac{d sin \alpha.sin \beta}{sin( \beta - \alpha)} \ (or) \ h = \frac{d}{cot \alpha - cot \beta} \)

  2. The angle of elevation measured from the points A, B on a horizontal line either side to the floor of the tower are \( \alpha \) and \( \beta \) if AB = d. Then the height of the tower 'h' is

    \( \therefore h = \frac{dsin \alpha.sin \beta}{sin( \beta + \alpha)} \ (or) \ h = \frac{d}{cot \alpha + cot \beta} \ (or) \ h = \frac{dtan \alpha.tan \beta}{tan \alpha + tan \beta} \)

  3. The angle of elevation of the top of a tower from the bottom and top of a building of height 'd' metres are \( \beta \) and \( \alpha \) respectively. The height of the tower is

    \( \therefore h = \frac{dsin \beta.cos \alpha}{sin( \beta - \alpha)} \ (or) \ h = \frac{dcot \alpha}{cot \alpha - cot \beta} \)

  4. The angle of elevation cloud from a height 'd' metres above the level of water in a lake is \( \alpha \) and the angle of depression of its image in the lake is \( \beta \) . The height of the cloud from the water level in metres is:
    \( \frac{dsin( \beta + \alpha)}{sin( \beta - \alpha)} \ (or) \ h = \frac{d(tan \beta + tan \alpha)}{tan \beta - tan \alpha} \ (or) \ h = d \left[ \frac{cot \alpha + cot \beta}{cot \alpha - cot \beta} \right] \)

  5. The angle of elevation of a hill from a point C is \( \alpha \) . After walking to some point D at a distance 'a' metres from C on a slope of inclination \( \beta \) , the angle of elevation was found to be \( \gamma \) , then
    \( h = \frac{a sin \alpha.sin( \gamma - \beta)}{sin( \gamma - \alpha)} \)

  6. A ballon is observed simultaneously from the three points P, Q, R on a straight road directly beneath it. The angular elevation at Q is twise taht at P and the angular elevation at 'R' is thrice that at P. If PQ = a and QR = b then the height of the ballon 'h' in terms of a and b is,
    \( h = \frac{a}{2b} \sqrt{(3b - a)(a + b)} \)

  7. A round ballon of radius r subtends an angle \( \alpha \) at the eye of an observer, while the angle of elevation of its centre is \( \beta \) . Then the height of the centre of the ballon is
    \( h = r sin \beta. cosec \left( \frac{ \alpha}{2} \right) \)

  8. A flag staff stands on the top of a tower of heights h metres. If the tower and flag staff subtended equal angles at a distance 'd' metres from the foot of the tower, then the height of the flag staff in metres is
    \( h \left[ \frac{d^2 + h^2}{d^2 - h^2} \right] \)

Solved examples

Example 1:

Question:
If \( tan \theta + sin \theta = m \) and \( tan \theta - sin \theta = n \) , show that \( m^2 - n^2 = 4 \sqrt{mn} \)

Solution:
\( \begin{align} LHS = m^2 - n^2 = (m + n) \times (m - n) \\\\[3pt] = \lbrace(tan \theta + sin \theta) + (tan \theta - sin \theta) \rbrace \times \lbrace(tan \theta + sin \theta) - (tan \theta - sin \theta) \rbrace \\\\[3pt] = 2 tan \theta + 2 sin \theta = 4 tan \theta sin \theta \\\\[3pt] = 4 \sqrt{tan^2 \theta sin^2 \theta} = 4 \sqrt{(sec^2 \theta - 1)sin^2 \theta} \\\\[3pt] = 4 \sqrt{sec^2 \theta sin^2 \theta - sin^2 \theta} = 4 \sqrt{ \frac{sin^2 \theta}{cos^2 \theta} - sin^2 \theta} \\\\[3pt] = 4 \sqrt{tan^2 \theta - sin^2 \theta} = 4 \sqrt{(tan \theta + sin \theta)(tan \theta - sin \theta)} \\\\[3pt] = 4 \sqrt{mn} = RHS \end{align} \)

Example 2:

Question:
\( cosec \theta - sin \theta = m \) and \( sec \theta - cos \theta = n \) , prove that \( (m^2n)^{ \frac{2}{3}} + (mn^2)^{ \frac{2}{3}} = 1 \)

Solution:
\( \begin{align} cosec \theta - sin \theta = m \ and \ sec \theta - cos \theta = n \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow \frac{1}{sin \theta} - sin \theta = m \ and \ \frac{1}{cos \theta} - cos \theta - n \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow \frac{1 - sin^2 \theta}{sin \theta} = m \ and \ \frac{1 - cos^2 \theta}{cos \theta} = n \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow \frac{cos^2 \theta}{sin \theta} = m \ and \ \frac{sin^2 \theta}{cos \theta} = n, \ i.e., \ m = \frac{cos^2 \theta}{sin \theta} \ and \ n = \frac{sin^2 \theta}{cos \theta} \end{align} \)
\( \begin{align} \Rightarrow m^2n = \frac{cos^4 \theta}{sin^2 \theta} \times \frac{sin^2 \theta}{cos \theta} \ and \ mn^2 = \frac{cos^2 \theta}{sin \theta} \times \frac{sin^4 \theta}{cos^2 \theta} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow m^2n = cos^3 \theta \ \ and \ \ mn^2 = sin^3 \theta \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow (m^2n)^{ \frac{2}{3}} = cos^2 \theta \ and \ (mn^2)^{ \frac{2}{3}} = sin^2 \theta \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow (m^2n)^{ \frac{2}{3}} + (mn^2)^{ \frac{2}{3}} = cos^2 \theta + sin^2 \theta = 1 \end{align} \)

Example 3:

Question:
Prove that \( \frac{cot \theta + cosec \theta - 1}{cot \theta - cosec \theta + 1} = cosec \theta + cot \theta = \frac{1 + cos \theta}{sin \theta} \) .

Solution:
\( \begin{align} LHS = \frac{cot \theta + cosec \theta - 1}{cot \theta - cosec \theta + 1} = \frac{(cot \theta + cosec \theta) - (cosec^2 \theta - cot^2 \theta)}{cot \theta - cosec \theta + 1} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{(cot \theta + cosec \theta)(1 - cosec \theta + cot \theta)}{(cot \theta - cosec \theta + 1)} \\\\[3pt] =cot \theta + cosec \theta = \frac{cos \theta}{sin \theta} + \frac{1}{sin \theta} = \frac{1 + cos \theta}{sin \theta} = RHS \end{align} \)

Example 4:

Question:
Prove that \( \frac{sin \theta}{cot \theta + cosec \theta} = 2 + \frac{sin \theta}{cot \theta - cosec \theta} \)

Solution:
The identity is established if we can prove that
\( \begin{align} \frac{sin \theta}{cot \theta + cosec \theta} - \frac{sin \theta}{cot \theta - cosec \theta} = 2 \\\\[3pt] LHS = \frac{sin \theta. cot \theta - sin \theta. cosec \theta - sin \theta. cot \theta - sin \theta. cosec \theta}{(cot \theta + cosec \theta)(cot \theta - cosec \theta)} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{-2 sin \theta. cosec \theta}{cot^2 \theta - cosec^2 \theta} = \frac{-2}{-1} \ (\because sin \theta. cosec \theta = 1, \ cosec^2 \theta - cot^2 \theta = 1) \\\\[3pt] = 2 = RHS \end{align} \)

Example 5:

Question:
If (1 - sinA)(1 - sinB)(1 - sinC) = (1 + sinA)(1 + sinB)(1 + sinC) then prove that each side is equal to cosA cosB cosC

Solution:
Let (1 - sinA)(1 - sinB)(1 - sinC) = (1 + sinA)(1 + sinB)(1 + sinC) = k
then (1 - sinA)(1 - sinB)(1 - sinC)(1 + sinA)(1 + sinB)(1 + sinC) = k.k
\( \begin{align} (1 - sin^2A)(1 - sin^2B)(1 - sin^C) = k^2 \ or \ cos^2A cos^2B cos^2C = k^2 \\\\[3pt] \therefore k = cosA \ cos B \ cos C \end{align} \)
Hence the identity given in the problem is established.

Example 6:

Question:
If \( tan( \pi cos \theta) = cot( \pi sin \theta) \) , prove that \( cos \left( \theta - \frac{ \pi}{4} \right) \)

Solution:
We have,
\( \begin{align} tan( \pi cos \theta) = cot ( \pi sin \theta) \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow tan( \pi cos \theta) = tan \left( \frac{ \pi}{2} - \pi sin \theta \right) \Rightarrow \pi cos \theta = \frac{ \pi}{2} - \pi sin \theta \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow cos \theta + sin \theta = \frac{1}{2} \Rightarrow \frac{1}{ \sqrt{2}} cos \theta + \frac{1}{ \sqrt{2}} sin \theta = \frac{1}{2 \sqrt{2}} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow cos \theta cos \frac{ \pi}{4} + sin \theta sin \frac{ \pi}{4} = \frac{1}{2 \sqrt{2}} \Rightarrow cos \left( \theta - \frac{ \pi}{4} \right) = \frac{1}{2 \sqrt{2}} \end{align} \)

Example 7:

Question:
Prove that: \( \frac{1 + sin 2 \theta + cos 2 \theta}{1 + sin 2 \theta - cos 2 \theta} = cot \theta \)

Solution:
We have,
\( \begin{align} LHS = \frac{1 + sin 2 \theta + cos 2 \theta}{1 + sin 2 \theta - cos 2 \theta} = \frac{(1 + cos 2\theta) + sin 2 \theta}{(1 - cos 2 \theta) + sin 2 \theta} \\\\[3pt] = \frac{2 cos^2 \theta + 2 sin \theta cos \theta}{2 sin^2 \theta + 2 sin \theta cos \theta} = \frac{2 cos \theta(cos \theta + sin \theta)}{2 sin \theta(cos \theta + sin \theta)} = \frac{cos \theta}{sin \theta} = cot \theta = RHS \end{align} \)

Example 8:

Question:
Prove that \( sin^2 \left( \frac{ \pi}{8} + \frac{A}{2} \right) - sin^2 \left( \frac{ \pi}{8} - \frac{A}{2} \right) = \frac{1}{ \sqrt{2}} sin A \)

Solution:
\( \begin{align} LHS = sin^2 \left( \frac{ \pi}{8} + \frac{A}{2} \right) - sin^2 \left( \frac{ \pi}{8} - \frac{A}{2} \right) \\\\[3pt] = sin \left\lbrace \left( \frac{ \pi}{8} + \frac{A}{2} \right) + \left( \frac{ \pi}{8} - \frac{A}{2} \right) \right\rbrace sin \left\lbrace \left( \frac{ \pi}{8} + \frac{A}{2} \right) + \left( \frac{ \pi}{8} - \frac{A}{2} \right) \right\rbrace \\\\[3pt] = sin \frac{ \pi}{4} sin A \\\\[3pt] = \frac{1}{ \sqrt{2}} sin A = RHS \end{align} \)

Example 9:

Question:
If \( tan ( \alpha + \theta) = n \ tan( \alpha - \theta) \) show that \( (n + 1) sin 2 \theta = (n - 1) sin 2 \alpha \)

Solution:
We, have
\( \begin{align} tan ( \alpha + \theta) = n \ tan( \alpha - \theta) \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow \frac{tan ( \alpha + \theta)}{tan ( \alpha - \theta)} = \frac{n}{1} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow \frac{tan ( \alpha + \theta) + tan ( \alpha - \theta) }{tan ( \alpha + \theta) - tan ( \alpha - \theta)} = \frac{n + 1}{n - 1} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow \frac{sin ( \alpha + \theta) cos ( \alpha - \theta) + cos ( \alpha + \theta) sin ( \alpha - \theta) }{sin ( \alpha + \theta) cos ( \alpha - \theta) - cos ( \alpha + \theta) sin ( \alpha - \theta) } = \frac{n + 1}{n - 1} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow \frac{sin ( \alpha + \theta + \alpha - \theta)}{ sin ( \alpha + \theta - \alpha + \theta)} = \frac{n + 1}{n - 1} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow \frac{sin 2 \alpha}{sin 2 \theta} = \frac{n + 1}{n - 1} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow (n + 1) sin 2 \theta = (n - 1) sin 2 \alpha \end{align} \)

Example 10:

Question:
If an angle \( \alpha \) be divided into two parts such that the tangent of one part is \( \lambda \) times the tangent of the other part, then prove that their difference \( \theta \) is given by \( sin \theta = \frac{ \lambda - 1}{ \lambda + 1} sin \alpha \)

Solution:
Let two parts of the angle \( \alpha \) be A and B. Then, A + B = \( \alpha \) and A - B = \( \theta \) .
It is also given that
\( \begin{align} tan A = \lambda tan B \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow \frac{tan A}{tan B} = \frac{ \lambda}{1} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow \frac{tan A + tan B}{tan A - tan B} = \frac{ \lambda + 1}{ \lambda - 1} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow \frac{sin A \ cos B + cos A \ sin B}{sin A \ cos B - cos A \ sin B} = \frac{ \lambda + 1}{ \lambda - 1} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow \frac{sin (A + B)}{sin (A - B)} = \frac{ \lambda + 1}{ \lambda - 1} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow \frac{sin \alpha}{sin \theta} = \frac{ \lambda + 1}{ \lambda - 1} \\\\[3pt] \Rightarrow sin \theta = \frac{ \lambda - 1}{ \lambda + 1} sin \alpha \end{align} \)

Example 11:

Question:
If tan A - tan B = x and cot B - cot A = y, prove \( cot(A - B) = \frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{y} \)

Solution:
We have,
tan A - tan B = x and cot B - cot A = y
Now, cot B - cot A = y
