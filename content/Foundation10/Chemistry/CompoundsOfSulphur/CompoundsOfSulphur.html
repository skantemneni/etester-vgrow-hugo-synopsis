Introduction

Sulphur belongs to 3rd period and VI A group of the periodic table. Sulphur forms a hydride \( H_2S \) , two oxides, \( SO_2 \) and \( SO_3 \) . Sulphur forms a number of oxy acids out of which \( H_2SO_3 \) and \( H_2SO_4 \) are prominent ones.

Hydrogen Sulphide

Hydrogen sulphide is one of the compounds of sulphur also known as sulphuretted hydrogen.

Preparation:

  1. Treatment of Fes with \( H_2SO_4 \) .
    \( FeS + H_2SO_4 \rightarrow FeSO_4 + H_2S \uparrow \)

  2. By passing \( H_2 \) gas through boiling S or over heated \( Sb_2S_3. \)
    \( \begin{align} H_2 + S \rightarrow H_2S \\\\[3pt] 3H_2 + Sb_2S_3 \rightarrow 2Sb + 3H_2S \end{align} \)

  3. By the action of pure HCl on \( Sb_2S_3. \)
    \( Sb_2S_3 + 6HCl \rightarrow 2SbCl_3 + 3H_2S \)
    \( H_2S \) thus obtained is pure.

Note:

\( H_2S \) cannot be dried by conc. \( H_2SO_4 \) because it oxidezes \( H_2S \) to sulphur.
\( H_2S + H_2SO_4 \rightarrow SO_2 + S + 2H_2O \)
It is therefore dried by passing through \( CaCl_2 \) or \( P_2O_5. \)

Laboratory preparation

Kipp's apparatus.
Passing \( H_2 \) gas through boiling sulphur.
\( H_2 + S \rightarrow H_2S \)

Physical properties:

  1. Colourless, poisonous gas with rotten egg smell.

  2. Moderately soluble on cold water.

  3. Inhalation leads to headache and in large quantities may become fatal.

  4. Easy liquefaction under high pressure.

  5. e) Its boiling point is \( 60^{\circ}C \) and freezing point is \( - 85.6^{\circ}C \) at which it freezes to a transparent solid.

Chemical properties:

Combustion:

Burns with blue flame
\( \begin{align} 2H_2S + 3O_2 \rightarrow 2H_2O + 2SO_2 \\\\[3pt] Limited \ supply \ of \ O_2 \\\\[3pt] 2H_2S + O_2 \rightarrow 2H_2O + 2S \end{align} \)

Reducing agent:

Acts as only reducing agent since sulphur is in lowest oxidation state(-2)

  1. Reduction of halogens
    \( H_2S + X_2 \rightarrow 2HX + S \)

  2. Reduction of \( SO_2 \)
    \( 2H_2S + SO_2 \rightarrow 3S + 2H_2O \)

  3. Reduction of \( O_3 \)
    \( H_2S + 2O_3 \rightarrow H_2SO_4 \)

  4. Reduction of \( HNO_3 \)
    \( 2HNO_3 + H_2S \rightarrow 2NO_2 + S + 2H_2O \)

  5. Reduction of \( H_2SO_4 \)
    \( H_2SO_4 + H_2S \rightarrow 2H_2O + SO_2 + S \)

  6. \( KMnO_4 \) (Acidified)
    \( 3H_2SO_4 + 2KMnO_4 + 5H_2S \rightarrow K_2SO_4 + 2MnSO_4 + 8H_2O + 5S \)

  7. Acidified \( K_2Cr_2O_7 \)
    \( K_2Cr_2O_7 + 4H_2SO_4 + 3H_2S \rightarrow K_2SO_4 + Cr_2(SO_4)_3 + 7H_2O + 3S \)

Acidic nature:

  1. It is weak dibasic acid
    \( \begin{align} H_2S \rightarrow H^{+} + HS^{-} \\\\[3pt] H_2S \rightarrow H^{+} + HS^{-2} \end{align} \)

  2. Forms two types of salts - bisulphides, Sulphides

Reaction with metals/metal oxides/metal salts:

\( \begin{align} 2Na + H_2S \rightarrow Na_2S + H_2(No \ precipitate) \\\\[3pt] Cu + H_2S \rightarrow CuS + H_2(black \ precipitate) \\\\[3pt] 2Ag + H_2S \rightarrow Ag_2S + H_2(white \ precipitate) \\\\[3pt] Pb + H_2S \rightarrow PbS + H_2(Black \ precipitate) \end{align} \)

Sulphur dioxide

Sulphur dioxide is an anhydride of sulphurous acid.

Preparation methods:

  1. Metals with conc. \( H_2SO_4 \)
    \( \begin{align} Cu + 2H_2SO_4 \rightarrow CuSO_4 + 2H_2O + SO_2 \\\\[3pt] Ag + 2H_2SO_4 \rightarrow AgSO_4 + 2H_2O + SO_2 \end{align} \)

  2. Non metals with conc. \( H_2SO_4 \)
    \( C + 2H_2SO_4 \rightarrow CO_2 + 2SO_2 + 2H_2O \)

  3. Sulphite salts with dil. \( HCl \)
    \( Na_2SO_3 + 2HCl \rightarrow 2NaCl + H_2O + SO_2 \)

Physical properties:

  1. Colourless pungent gas.

  2. It can be easily liquefies to a colourless liquid(b.pt - \( 10^{\circ}C \) ) and into a snow like solid (f.pt - \( 72.7^{\circ}C \) ).

Chemical properties:

Combustion:

It neither combustible nor supporter of combustion. However, burning magnesium and potassium continue to burn in its atmosphere. It is due to the fact that burning of metal liberates heat which decomposes \( SO_2 \) to \( O_2 \) .
\( \begin{align} SO_2 + 3Mg \rightarrow 2MgO + MgS \\\\[3pt] 3SO_2 + 4K \rightarrow K_2SO_3 + K_2S_2O_3 \end{align} \)

Thermal decomposition:

\( SO_3 \) and S are formed.
\( 3SO_2 \xrightarrow{1200^{\circ}C} 2SO_3 + S \)

Acidic nature:

It is an acidic oxide(anhydride of sulphurous acid).
\( SO_2 + H_2O \rightarrow H_2SO_3 \)
Its acidic reactions are given below:

  1. Action with caustic alkalies:
    It reacts to form two types of salts - Bisulphites and sulphites
    \( \begin{align} SO_2 + NaOH \rightarrow \underset{Sod. \ bisulphite}{NaHSO_3} \\\\[3pt] SO_2 + 2NaOH \rightarrow \underset{Sod. \ sulphite}{Na_2SO_3} + H_2O \end{align} \)

  2. Action with lime water:
    \( SO_2 \) when passed through lime water gives ppt. of \( CaSO_3. \)
    \( SO_2 + Ca(OH)_2 \rightarrow \underset{milky}{CaSO_3} + H_2O \)

Addition reactions:

  1. Reaction with \( O_2 \) :
    \( 2SO_2 + O_2 \xrightarrow{Catalyst(platinised \ asbestos)} 2SO_3 \)

  2. Action of halogen:
    Sulphuryl halides are formed
    \( SO_2 + Cl_2 \rightarrow \underset{Sulphuryi \ chloride}{SO_2Cl_2} \)

Bleaching action:

In presence of moisture \( SO_2 \) acts as a bleaching agent, due to its reducing nature
\( \begin{align} SO_2 + 2H_2O \rightarrow H_2SO_4 + 2H \\\\[3pt] Coloured \ matter + H \rightarrow Colourless \ matter \end{align} \)
The bleaching by reduction is always temporary. The bleached matter regains its colour when exposed to air due to oxidation.

Uses

  1. In the manufacture of \( H_2SO_4. \)

  2. As a bleaching agent, a disinfectant.

  3. In petroleum refining.

  4. As a antichlor to remove chlorine from fabrics after bleaching.

  5. As a refrigerant(liquid \( SO_2 \) ).

  6. In the manufacture of paper from wood pulp.

  7. As bleaching agent in sugar industry to decolorise molasses.

Structure:

Sulphur undergoes \( sp^2 \) hybridisation. The molecule is 'V' shaped with a blood angle of \( 119.5^{\circ}. \)

Sulphur trioxide

Preparation:

  1. Dehydration of \( H_2SO_4 \) with \( P_2O_5 \)
    \( H_2SO_4 + P_4O_{10} \rightarrow H_3PO_4 + SO_3 \)

  2. Oxidation of \( SO_2 \) in presence of catalyst
    \( 2SO_2 + O_2 \overset{Pt}{ \rightleftarrows} 2SO_3 \)

Properties:

Acidic nature:

On dissolution in water, \( H_2SO_4 \) is formed. Therefore it is called anhydride of \( H_2SO_4. \)
\( SO_3 + H_2O \rightarrow H_2SO_4 \)

Formation of oleum:

\( SO_3 \) dissolves in \( H_2SO_4 \) forming \( H_2S_2O_7 \) (oleum)
\( H_2SO_4 + SO_3 \rightarrow H_2S_2O_7 \)

Oxidising agent:

\( \begin{align} SO_3 + 2HBr \rightarrow H_2O + Br_2 + SO_2 \\\\[3pt] 5SO_3 + 2P \rightarrow 5SO_2 + P_2O_5 \\\\[3pt] SO_3 + PCl_5 \rightarrow POCl_3 + SO_2 + Cl_2 \end{align} \)

Uses:

  1. In the manufacture of \( H_2SO_4 \) and \( H_2S_2O_7 \) .

  2. As a drying agent.

Structure:

'S' atom in \( SO_3 \) undergoes \( sp^2 \) hybridisation. It has trigonal planar structure with \( 120^{\circ} \) bond angle.

Oxyacids of sulphur:

Sulphur forms two important oxy acids namely sulphurous acid( \( H_2SO_3 \) ) and sulphuric acid( \( H_2SO_4 \) ).

Sulphurous Acid

Preparation:

Dissolution in water:

\( SO_2 + H_2O \rightarrow H_2SO_3 \)
The solution smells like \( SO_2 \) which on heating is evolved completely. The acid present in solution shows this equilibrium with free gas.
\( H_2SO_3 \rightarrow H_2O + SO_2 \)

Properties:

  1. It is strong dibasic acid and so forms two series of salts.[bisulphites, ( \( HSO_3^{-} \) ) sulphites,( \( SO_3^{2-} \) )]
    \( H_2SO_3 \rightarrow H^{+} + HSO_3^{-} \rightarrow 2H^{+} + SO_3^{-2} \)
    These salts are fairly stable.

  2. Attempts to isolate sulphurous acid in the free state leads the condensation of \( HSO_3^{-} \) ion to pyrosulphate ions.
    \( 2HSO_3^{-} \rightarrow S_2O_7^{2-} + H_2O \)

  3. Action of Fe: \( FeSO_3 \) and \( FeS_2O_3 \) are formed.
    \( 2Fe + 3H_2SO_3 \rightarrow FeSO_3 + FeS_2O_3 + 3H_2O \)

  4. Reducing nature: \( H_2SO_3 \) is easily oxidised and thus it acts as a reducing and bleaching agent.(similar way as \( SO_2 \) )
    \( 2H_2SO_3 + O_2 \rightarrow 2H_2SO_4 \)

  5. Oxidising nature: With strong reducing agents, it also acts as oxidant e.g,
    \( H_2SO_3 + 2H_2S \rightarrow 3H_2O + 3S \)

Structure:

\( SO_3^{-2} \) ion has pyramidal structure as sulphur undergoes \( sp^3 \) hybridisation and has a low pair of electrons.

Sulphuric Acid

Commonly called oil of vitriol or king of chemicals. Due to its extensive use in the manufacture of fertilizers, dyestuffs, paints, pigments, explosive, plastic, detergents etc. It is considered as the king of chemicals (or oil of vitriol).

Manufacture by contact process:

  1. Oxidation of \( SO_2 \)
    \( 2SO_2 + O_2 \overset{V_2O_5 \ or \ Pt}\longleftrightarrow 2SO_3 \ \ \ \ \ \ \Delta H = - 47 \ k.cal \)

  2. Dissolution in \( H_2SO_4 \)
    \( SO_3 + H_2SO_4 \rightarrow H_2S_2O_7 \)

  3. Dilution of oleum with water
    \( H_2S_2O_7 + H_2O \rightarrow 2H_2SO_4 \)

Physical properties:

  1. Colourless, syrupy oily liquid having corrosive nature.

  2. \( 98 \ \% \ H_2SO_4 \) is called constant boiling mixture

  3. Fumes in moist air and soluble in water.

  4. Forms hydrates during dilution.

  5. Dehydrating agent

Chemical properties:

Dissociation:

At \( 444^{\circ}C \) the vapours of \( H_2SO_4 \) dissociates into steam and sulphur trioxide.
\( H_2SO_4 \xrightarrow{444^{\circ}C} H_2O + SO_3 \)

Acidic nature:

It is strong dibasic acid and ionises in dilute solutions.
\( H_2SO_4 \rightarrow HSO_4^{-} + H^{+} \rightarrow SO_4^{2-} + 2H^{+} \)
It forms two series of salts bisulphate( \( HSO_4^{-} \) ) and sulphates( \( SO_4^{2-} \) ) with bases.

  1. Action on alkalies:
    \( \begin{align} H_2SO_4 + NaOH \rightarrow \underset{Sodium \ hydrogen \ sulphate}{NaHSO_4 + H_2O} \\\\[3pt] H_2SO_4 + 2NaOH \rightarrow \underset{Sodium \ sulphate}{Na_2SO_4} + 2H_2O \end{align} \)

  2. Reaction with electropositive metals: \( H_2 \) is evolved.
    \( \begin{align} H_2SO_4 + Zn \rightarrow ZnSO_4 + H_2 \\\\[3pt] H_2SO_4 + Fe \rightarrow FeSO_4 + H_2 \end{align} \)

  3. Action on carbonates and bicarbonates: \( CO_2 \) is evolved.
    \( \begin{align} H_2SO_4 + Na_2CO_3 \rightarrow Na_2SO_4 + H_2O + CO_2 \\\\[3pt] H_2SO_4 + NaHCO_3 \rightarrow NaHSO_4 + H_2O + CO_2 \end{align} \)

  4. Action on salts: More volatile acids are liberated.
    \( \begin{align} H_2SO_4 + 2NaCl \rightarrow Na_2SO_4 + 2HCl \\\\[3pt] H_2SO_4 + CaFe_2 \rightarrow CaSO_4 + 2HF \end{align} \)

Dehydration nature:

It is powerful dehydrating agent due to capability to H - bonds extensively. Its dehydrates organic compounds also and that is why causes corrosive action on skin.
\( \begin{align} \underset{Glucose}{C_6H_{12}O_6} \xrightarrow{H_2SO_4} 6C + 6H_2O \\\\[3pt] \underset{Cane \ sugar}{C_{12}H_{22}O_{11}} \xrightarrow{H_2SO_4} 12C + 11H_2O \end{align} \)

Sulphonation:

It is sulphonates aromatic rings.
\( H_2SO_4 + \underset{Benzene}{C_6H_6} \rightarrow \underset{Benzene \ sulphonic \ acid}{C_6H_5SO_3H} + H_2O \)

Reaction:

With \( KClO_3 \)
On heating with conc. \( H_2SO_4, \ KClO_3 \) gives chlorine dioxide with explosion.
\( 3H_2SO_4 + 3HClO_3 \rightarrow 3KHSO_4 + HClO_4 + 2ClO_2 + H_2O \)

Structure:

\( sp^3 \) hybridisation of sulphur. Tetrahedral structure. Two double bonds with oxygen atoms.

Solved examples

Example 1:

Question:
An acidified \( K_2Cr_2O_7 \) paper turns green when exposed to \( SO_2 \) . Explain.

Solution:
\( SO_2 \) reduces \( K_2Cr_2O_7 \) to green chromium sulphate.
\( 3SO_2 + K_2Cr_2O_7 + H_2SO_4 \rightarrow K_2SO_4 + \underset{green}{Cr_2(SO_4)_3} + H_2O \)

Example 2:

Question:
Write most favourable conditions for the conversion of \( SO_2 \) into \( SO_3 \) .

Solution:
The reaction is \( 2SO_2 + O_2 \rightarrow 2SO_3; \ \ \Delta H = -ve. \)
According to Le Chatelier principle, high pressure, low temperature( \( 450^{\circ}C \) ) will favour the conversion of \( SO_2 \) and \( SO_3 \) . Also the presence of catalyst will enhance the rate of reaction.

Example 3:

Question:
Sugar turns black on addition of conc. \( H_2SO_4. \) Why?

Solution:
Conc. \( H_2SO_4 \) has great affinity for \( H_2O \) as it is strong dehydrating agent. It dehydrates sugar leaving carbon behind.
\( C_{12}H_{22}O_{11} \xrightarrow{Conc. \ H_2SO_4} 12C + 11H_2O \)

Example 4:

Question:
Dry \( SO_2 \) does not bleach dry flowers. Explain.

Solution:
Only in presence of moisture, the nascent hydrogen responsible for bleaching(reduction) is produced.
\( \begin{align} SO_2 + 2H_2O \rightarrow H_2SO_4 + 2[H] \\\\[3pt] Coloured \ flower + [H] \rightarrow Colourless \ flower \end{align} \)

Example 5:

Question:
\( H_2S \) is better reducing agent than water or \( H_2S \) is thermally less stable than \( H_2O \) . Explain.

Solution:
\( S - H \) bond is longer and so weaker than \( O - H \) bond, hence \( H_2S \) is better reductant.

Example 6:

Question:
Conc. \( H_2SO_4 \) cannot be used for drying up \( H_2S \) . Explain.

Solution:
Conc. \( H_2SO_4 \) oxidises \( H_2S \) to S and thus cannot be used as for drying up \( H_2S. \)
\( H_2SO_4 + H_2S \rightarrow 2H_2O + SO_2 + S \)

Example 7:

Question:
\( H_2S \) acts only as reducing agent while \( SO_2 \) can act both as reducing agent and oxidising agent. Explain.

Solution:
S in \( SO_2 \) has oxidation state +4. It lies betweenthe minimum oxidation state(-2) and maximum oxidation state(+6) of S. Thus S in \( SO_2 \) can show an increase in its oxidation number(i.e, acts as reductant) or can show a decrease in its ox.no.(i.e, acts as oxidant). On the other hand in \( H_2S, \ S \) is in -2 oxidation state and can only increase its oxidation state to act as reductant.

Example 8:

Question:
Conc. \( H_2SO_4 \) is used as dehydrating agent. Explain.

Solution:
Conc. \( H_2SO_4 \) has a strong affinity for water and it prevents the reversibility of the reaction where water is formed. Thus, it is used as dehydrating agent.

Example 9:

Question:
\( KMnO_4 \) should not be dissolved in conc. \( H_2SO_4 \) . Explain.

Solution:
\( KMnO_4 \) forms explosive covalent compound \( Mn_2O_7 \) with conc. \( H_2SO_4 \) .
\( 2KMnO_4 + H_2SO_{4(conc.)} \rightarrow K_2SO_4 + Mn_2O_7 + H_2O \)
However, it can be dissolved in dil. \( H_2SO_4 \) to give nascent oxygen if it is to be used as oxidant.

Example 10:

Question:
\( H_2SO_4 \) is used as pickling agent. Explain.

Solution:
Pickling is an industrial process for removing layers of basic oxides from metals like Fe and Cu before electroplating, enamelling, galvanising and soldering. \( H_2SO_4 \) being strong acid dissolves all the basic oxides from the surface.
